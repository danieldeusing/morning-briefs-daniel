Open Weights · Qwen Conference — 3.7 stays closed [1][2]

The question we'd been building toward for days got its answer today: at the Qwen Conference 2026 in Singapore (Marina Bay Sands), Alibaba made its flagship Qwen3.7-Max official — and is keeping it closed. Local users' hope for open 3.7 weights doesn't materialize today. I re-checked exactly the question that landed today.[1]

Checked today: still no open 3.7

huggingface.co/Qwen still carries only the 3.5 and 3.6 families — the model filter on qwen3.7 returns 0 hits, and Qwen/Qwen3.7-Plus replies straight with 401 (doesn't exist publicly). ollama.com/library the same: newest Qwen entries are qwen3.6 and qwen3.5, no 3.7. Qwen3.7-Max therefore stays preview/API-only (1M-token context, extended thinking) — confirmed at the conference, but without a weights drop.[1][2]

preview/API-only vs. open weights: "Preview/API-only" means you may use the model through a cloud endpoint — like renting a car. "Open weights" means you get the model file itself and run it on your own machine — like buying the car. For Daniel's local Ollama/llama.cpp stack, only the latter counts.

Reporting still holds that a Plus variant (and smaller 27B/35B open weights) are planned while Max stays closed — but as of tonight, nothing has landed on HuggingFace. As long as no repo exists, I treat it as a promise, not an artifact.

The spectacular conference claims (very long autonomous runs, thousands of tool calls to optimize a kernel) are vendor demo, not an independently measured result — interesting to watch, not to rely on. MCP · new attestation proposal, RC date stands [7][8]

SEP-2787 · signed tool-call envelopes

The most interesting MCP proposal of the week is open, not merged: SEP-2787 "Tool-Call Attestation" (PR opened 2026-05-25) proposes signed audit envelopes for tool calls — intent, agent identity, tool name, arguments and execution proof bound into a verifiable audit trail, explicitly aimed at EU AI Act Art. 12 (logging obligations). Status: open, Extensions Track — not an accepted spec part yet, but a signal of where the compliance discussion is heading.[7]

Attestation (tool call): Like a stamped delivery note for every action an agent takes — instead of just "tool X was called", there's a signed receipt of who did what with which intent, and that it really happened that way. Tamper-proof and auditable later, in case a regulator asks.

It's Tuesday, May twenty-sixth. And today is finally the day of the big stage: the Qwen Conference took place in Singapore. For days the question was whether local users would get open weights for Qwen three-point-seven there. The answer is no.

Alibaba made its flagship Qwen three-point-seven Max official — but it stays closed, usable only through the cloud, with no file to download. I checked it myself today: HuggingFace still carries only the older three-point-five and three-point-six families, and the filter for three-point-seven returns zero hits. Ollama has nothing new either. Reporting says an open Plus variant is planned — but as of tonight, nothing has landed. A promise, not an artifact.

Alongside the model, Alibaba showed Qoder, an agentic coding platform — an agent you hand a task to, rather than just autocomplete. But the spectacular claims about very long autonomous runs are vendor marketing, not an independently measured result. Wait until it runs off-stage.

Among the tools there was real movement. Cline brought its VS Code extension to version three-point-eighty-five and added three new models to the menu, including GPT five-point-five and DeepSeek V four. Zed and JetBrains Junie pushed small builds. The big command lines, by contrast, are all resting: Claude Code, Codex and the Gemini command line have no new build.

For the Model Context Protocol there's an interesting new proposal: tool-call attestation. The idea is to give every action an agent takes a signed, tamper-proof receipt — who did what with which intent. Explicitly meant for the logging obligations of the EU AI Act. For now it's only an open proposal, not an accepted standard.

On price the good news holds: DeepSeek is making its seventy-five-percent discount on the V four Pro model permanent — now confirmed by three outlets. Only the official pricing page still shows the old text with the May thirty-first end date. So check it yourself before budgeting.

And the benchmarks stand still as ever. SWE-Bench Verified holds at seventy-nine point two percent, the most recent verified submission is from February twenty-sixth, and not a single entry is above eighty percent. The brand-new Qwen star isn't there at all — the conference numbers are vendor-reported and partly contradict each other. I deliberately cite none here.

So today is mostly about patience on the local stack and an eye on the tools. Tomorrow we'll see whether open weights still follow after the stage. Until then.
