SpaceX buys Cursor: $60B, the largest coding-tool deal ever

On June 16, SpaceX signed a merger agreement to acquire Anysphere — the company behind the AI editor Cursor — for $60B in a pure stock deal.[1] According to CBS, it's the largest acquisition of an AI coding-tool company ever recorded. Each Anysphere share will be exchanged for SpaceX Class A common stock;[2] closing is expected in Q3 2026, subject to antitrust approvals.[1]

The timing context: SpaceX went public on June 12 and raised $75B — four days later, part of that goes toward the Cursor acquisition. An options agreement had existed since April 2026; yesterday the option was exercised.[1] Cursor CEO Michael Truell (founded the company in 2022 with three MIT classmates) spoke of working together on "frontier AI capabilities."[1] The stock (Nasdaq: SPCX, IPO price $135) closed on its first trading day at $160.95 — +19%; intraday as high as ~$176 (~20%). The valuation reached roughly $2.1 trillion at close.[3]

What this means for you Cursor is one of the most-used agentic coding tools — and now belongs to a rocket company, not an independent startup. Short-term, nothing changes in the editor. Medium-term, three things are open: Does Cursor keep model neutrality (you can choose Claude, GPT, Gemini today) or does SpaceX swing it toward proprietary/exclusive models? Will pricing shift? And does engineering drain from product features into SpaceX-internal applications? For your daily driver, it's worth keeping a model-agnostic alternative (Claude Code, Cline, Aider) warm.

Numbers on Cursor user count or revenue were not published in available sources — not reliable, so omitted here.

GitHub Copilot: usage billing burns budget on day one

Since June 1, GitHub Copilot charges usage-based via "AI Credits."[7] The reaction was fierce: a community thread on the switch gathered 958 downvotes against 24 upvotes.[8] One Pro+ user watched 82% of their monthly quota evaporate on the first day — a 7,000-unit quota, estimated burned in under two days.[9]

Usage-based billing: Instead of a flat rate, you pay per "consumption" — like a taxi with a meter instead of a monthly pass. Each agentic run (the model edits files, calls tools, iterates) burns more credits than a simple autocomplete query. It was exactly these agentic workflows that emptied the quotas surprisingly fast.[10]

The kicker for the tool landscape: flat-rate agentic use is expensive, and pricing becomes a competitive factor. Cursor and Claude Code are seen as "agentic-native" challengers — except, see above, Cursor is itself going through a $60B acquisition and Claude Code is hit by the Fable 5 shutdown. If you run Copilot agentic, watch your credit dashboard closely this week.

Stack Overflow Survey 2025: 84% use AI — and trust it less

A data note, not fresh 2026: The Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2025 (conducted May–Aug 2025, published late December 2025) shows 84% of respondents using or planning AI tools — up from 76% the year before.[11] Among professional developers, 50.6% use it daily.[11]

The more interesting number is trust: More developers distrust AI accuracy (46%) than trust it (33%); only 3% "fully trust" it.[11] The most-cited frustration (66%): "Solutions that are almost right, but not quite."[11] Usage is climbing, trust is falling — fitting the agentic reality: tools do more, so each "almost right" mistake stings harder. (The separate 2026 survey has not yet shipped.)

Claude Fable 5 + Mythos 5: offline per U.S. order [4][5][6]

Anthropic's two strongest models, Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 (launched June 9), have been shut down since June 12 — and even now, five days later, they're not back.[5] The trigger was a U.S. export-control order, received June 12 at 17:21 ET, that forced Anthropic to block access for every foreign national — inside and outside the US, including their own foreign employees.[4] Because Anthropic can't separate foreign from U.S. users in real-time, both went offline for everyone. (Opus 4.8 and other models continue to run normally.)

Export-control order: A government directive banning the export of certain technology to foreigners — like a customs agent suddenly locking down a tool because it could be misused as a weapon. Here, the government claims a jailbreak was found: a trick to bypass the model's safety guardrails. Sticky for us: the jailbreak revolved around making the model read a codebase and find software vulnerabilities — Fable 5 was especially strong at that.[6]

Anthropic pushes back publicly: a "narrow, non-universal" potential jailbreak doesn't justify recalling a model deployed to hundreds of millions; that standard would halt "every new model deployment" industry-wide. Trump advisor David Sacks counters that they warned and Anthropic failed to close the gap.[12] There's no official return date — Anthropic says only "as fast as possible."[5]

Practical If you've pinned Fable 5 or Mythos 5 in your harness: they're unreachable and there's no ETA. Fall back to Opus 4.8 (running) or an open-weight model and don't plan on them returning until Anthropic announces a date.

Benchmark check: the top is vendor-reported [13]

Anthropic promoted Fable 5 at launch with 80.3% on SWE-Bench Pro (Opus 4.8: 69.2%, GPT-5.5: 58.6%).[14] On the public SWE-Bench-Verified leaderboard (as of 06-17) Fable 5 leads with 0.950, ahead of Mythos Preview (0.939) and Opus 4.8 (0.886).[13]

Skepticism These Verified numbers are not a measured value you should rely on: on the board, only 1 of 101 entries is independently verified — the other 99 were self-submitted by vendors.[13] Anthropic itself cited SWE-Bench Pro (80.3%), not Verified. Treat the 0.950 as a marketing number until an independent run confirms it.

Open-weights coding models

MiniMax M3 (launched June 1) leads open models on SWE-Bench Pro with 59.0% try — worth exploring if you want to keep agentic coding local or self-hosted.[17] DeepSeek V4 Pro (1.6T parameters MoE, 49B active, MIT license) leads LiveCodeBench at 93.5%.[18]

MoE (Mixture of Experts): Instead of running the whole giant model for every query, the model activates only the few "expert" pieces relevant to the task — like a hospital that doesn't send all doctors to every patient, just the right specialist. With DeepSeek V4 Pro: 1.6T parameters total, but only 49B active per query — big capacity, affordable inference.

Tool roll-call

Claude Code 2.1.178 (06-08): new `/cd` command for switching session directory, nested sub-agents (agents spawning their own agents), and a `--safe-mode` for debugging broken configs.[19]

Windsurf → Devin Desktop: Cognition renamed Windsurf to Devin Desktop on June 2. "Devin Local" replaces Cascade (up to 30% more token-efficient, Rust rewrite, sub-agents); Agent Command Center is the new default screen, plus the open ACP protocol.[20]

Still in the shadows: Aider still on v0.86.0 (Aug 2025) — cadence noticeably slowed, the leaderboard correspondingly stale and deliberately not cited as current here. Cline, Continue, OpenHands, Plandex, Bolt, JetBrains AI: nothing notable this week.
