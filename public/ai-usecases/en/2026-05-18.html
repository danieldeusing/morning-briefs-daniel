Standing Thread · Regulation

Watermarking deadline pushed — 3-month grace instead of 6

Detail in the Digital Omnibus that yesterday's brief didn't unpack: the watermarking obligation under Art. 50(2) (every GenAI output must be machine-readable as AI) moves from 2 Aug 2026 to 2 Dec 2026; at the same time, provider grace shrinks from 6 to 3 months. Practically: four extra months to prepare, less tolerance afterward. The consultation on the implementing guidelines runs until 3 Jun. What follows is not trivial — watermarking still lacks a robust standard solution; C2PA and SynthID compete on different assumptions about re-encoding robustness. (digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu)

C2PA vs. SynthID: Two rival "digital postage stamps" for AI content. C2PA (Adobe, Microsoft, BBC, Sony) attaches a signed metadata provenance to the file — losable on re-encoding. SynthID (Google) bakes the signature into the image/audio/video itself — robust against compression, but proprietary. Whichever format wins decides which detector works.

BaFin "IT Spotlight Division": targeted audits from Q3/26

BaFin president Mark Branson (May statement): a new division for IT spotlight inspections, because AI-driven risks emerge too fast to catch via broad routine audits. In parallel: the BSI enforcement mandate (NIS-2 + CRA + DORA interface) goes operational in May; first audits in Q3/26 across energy, healthcare, public admin, banking, telco, and industrial machinery. For German mid-caps in regulated sectors: time to compile an AI inventory and risk profile now. (hipther.com)

Methodology flag BaFin "IT Spotlight" so far is a Branson statement, not an official document. Division formation will be observable via job postings, org-chart changes, and first audit actions — the next solid signal likely arrives with the BaFin annual report in early June.

MedTech Europe protests publicly against the dual regime

Published 7 May: MedTech Europe publicly criticizes that the Digital Omnibus gave industrial AI (Machinery Directive) a single pathway — medical AI got nothing. Class IIb/III medical AI must therefore by 2 Aug 2027 satisfy both MDR (conformity assessment, clinical evidence, PMS, notified-body audit) and the AI Act (high-risk, GPAI obligations). Consolidation in favor of the large vendors (Siemens Healthineers, Philips, GE Healthcare) becomes nearly unavoidable. (medtecheurope.org, osborneclarke.com)

EU Tech Sovereignty Package 27 May — the real lever

CNBC and Channel Web deliver the timetable: Commission publishes on 27 May the Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA) plus Chips Act 2.0. Behind the scenes the Commission examines restrictions on US cloud for sensitive government data; a €180M tender for sovereign cloud providers (OVHcloud, STACKIT, Scaleway, S3NS, Mistral-side) ran in Q1. AWS European Sovereign Cloud is live in 2026 under separate EU governance. If you're rolling out AI in regulated sectors in Germany from 2026, the sovereign layer belongs in the architecture before the pilot, not after. (cnbc.com, euronews.com)

Methodology update on the studies from last week

If you're still bringing the 7-day brief to the strategy table: three corrections. The Gartner Hype Cycle for Agentic AI 2026 was published in April (not 13 May as said yesterday); the 17%-deployment figure comes from the CIO survey, no telemetry. The BCG AI Radar 2026 sample is not a pure CEO sample — 5% C-suite, 27% direct C-suite reports, 68% "other executives." The PwC CEO Survey was collected 30 Sep–10 Nov 2025, so >6 months old — fine as sentiment indicator, not as May-2026 statement. To convince a board with these numbers, pair the surveys with hard customer stories (Bosch / Bradesco / UnitedHealth) — that combination holds in 2026.

Methodology flag Run-rate ≠ ARR. Survey number ≠ telemetry number. Vendor PR ≠ third-party reporting. Especially this week, with three conferences running synchronized PR, it pays to interrogate every cited number on methodology.
