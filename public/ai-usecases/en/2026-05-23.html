Top Story · The deployment market consolidates

Three billion-dollar players, one turf: AI from pilot to production

Within two weeks, three heavily funded players have bought their way into the same business — helping companies move AI from pilot into production. On May 21, EY and Microsoft announced a 5-year pact worth >$1bn: Microsoft's "Forward Deployed Engineers" plus EY industry teams to move clients "beyond experimentation" to "measurable, enterprise-wide outcomes" across finance, tax, risk, HR, and supply chain. news.microsoft.com · bloomberg.com

In parallel, OpenAI stood up its "DeployCo" (OpenAI Deployment Company) with $4bn in initial capital and acquired the London consultancy Tomoro (~150 deployment specialists) as its operational core. And the $1.5bn enterprise-services firm backed by Anthropic, Blackstone, and Hellman&Friedman, unveiled in May, made its first acquisition on May 21: the AI consultancy Fractional AI — explicitly to help mid-sized firms deploy Claude. constellationr.com · blackstone.com

Forward Deployed Engineer: An engineer who doesn't sit in-house working on the product, but at the customer's site — like a plumber who doesn't sell pipes but installs them in your home. This "hands-on at the customer" model is exactly what all three are now buying at scale — and exactly what twiceD already lives.

What this means for a solo consultancy

For context: Accenture booked $5.9bn in gen-AI orders and $2.7bn in AI revenue (~3× the prior year) in FY2025 alone. The deployment market is long past being a niche. ciodive.com

My take Three vendor billions pushing into mid-market deployment is, at first glance, a threat to a solo shop — but read it closely. All three sell platform-locked scaling: DeployCo pulls toward OpenAI models, the Anthropic firm toward Claude, EY/Microsoft into the Azure-Copilot stack. None offers model neutrality, EU sovereignty, or on-prem, and none goes below enterprise pricing. But the real line of defense is elsewhere: all of them promise "measurable outcomes" — none ships a measured before-and-after. That's exactly where twiceD plays.

Counter-check · Nearly half fail

HCLTech: ~43% of major AI projects will fail

The "AI Impact Imperatives 2026" report from HCLTech (May 20) surveyed 467 executives at firms with >$1bn revenue and concludes: ~43% of major AI initiatives are expected to fail — not for lack of tools, but because firms can't translate ambition into enterprise-wide outcomes under time pressure. prnewswire.com

Methodology flag Vendor-funded: HCLTech sells exactly the AI transformation whose failure the report forecasts — a "you'll fail without us" frame. And "expected to fail" is an executive expectation, not a measured outcome. Still useful: even the industry that sells the deployment puts the failure rate high.

Gartner: layoffs ≠ AI ROI

A Gartner survey (n=350 executives at firms ≥$1bn) finds no correlation between layoffs and AI ROI: firms reporting strong returns cut staff at the same rate as those with weak returns. 80% reported workforce reductions that had nothing to do with returns. gartner.com · fortune.com In a separate Gartner survey (782 infrastructure & operations leaders), only 28% of AI use cases there meet their ROI expectation. gartner.com (Apr 7)

METR: the measurement lab distrusts its own survey

The same lab whose controlled developer experiment was a topic yesterday asked tech workers how much AI helps them — and then took the result apart itself. Respondents claim a median ~2× value gain; but METR points out that its own 2025 RCT showed developers overestimated AI's time effect by 40 percentage points. metr.org

Methodology flag Self-report, n=349, ~70% paid to respond. METR says it itself: "Survey results are not necessarily grounded in reality." That's the point of the day — feeling replaces measurement, and that very gap is the business case for a measurement offering.

Standing Thread · Regulation

EU: The Digital Omnibus moves the clock — a correction to yesterday

Council and Parliament reached a provisional political agreement on the "Digital Omnibus on AI" on May 7 — the first amendment to the AI Act since 2024. It moves several deadlines that read differently in this brief yesterday: Annex III high-risk slips from Aug 2, 2026 to Dec 2, 2027, product-embedded high-risk (Annex I) to Aug 2, 2028, and the provider duty to apply machine-readable watermarking (Art. 50(2)) from Aug 2, 2026 to Dec 2, 2026. Newly prohibited as of Dec 2, 2026: AI "nudify" apps and CSAM generation. The deal is still provisional — the formal votes are expected in June/July. cms.law · euronews.com

Important What was not moved: the deployer-facing disclosure obligations under Art. 50 still apply from Aug 2, 2026 — users must be able to tell they're talking to an AI (50(1)); deepfakes (50(4)) and emotion/biometric systems (50(3)) must be labeled. Only the provider watermarking slips. For a company with a customer chatbot, the August deadline stands.
Digital Omnibus: An "omnibus amendment" — like a single repair order that fixes ten small things in an existing law at once, instead of ten separate procedures. Here Brussels pushes the AI Act's deadlines back to give industry more room.

EU: Art. 50 consultation closes in eleven days; CRA deadline in September

The draft guidelines on the Art. 50 transparency obligations (May 8) are in consultation until Jun 3. In parallel, the Cyber Resilience Act runs: from Jun 11 the conformity-assessment-body provisions apply, and from Sep 11, 2026 the reporting duties for vulnerabilities/incidents (24h early warning / 72h / 14-day report) via the ENISA platform. digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu · cra-reporting

Brazil: ANPD sandbox in live testing, three firms named

At the TeleBrasil Summit (May 19), the data-protection authority ANPD confirmed that its regulatory sandbox is now in the live-testing phase under direct supervision through Dec 2026; the three approved firms — Metatext IA, Synapse AI, IA Greenworld — have begun. The framework bill PL 2338/2023 remains stuck in the Câmara special committee. gov.br/anpd

Project ideas for twiceD

Researched proposals — with the market gap and an honest solo-feasibility read

1 · AI deployment with a built-in measurement harness for the Mittelstand

ELI5 & value: Most AI consultancies deliver a solution and then claim the benefit ("saves 15% of the time") — it's rarely measured. This idea flips that: before the AI arrives, you instrument the work step (processing time, error rate, throughput), then deploy the AI and measure the same things again. Value to the customer: a real before-and-after instead of a gut feeling — they know whether the AI project pays off and can justify the next euro. Like an electricity meter before and after installing the new heater: not "feels warmer," but kilowatt-hours read off the dial.
Prototype (V0): (1) One real, tightly scoped workflow at a pilot customer (e.g. quote generation, complaint triage). (2) Two weeks of baseline measurement without AI — time per item, error rate, volume, via simple telemetry + a few manual logs. (3) AI deployment using existing building blocks (a hosted model, or an open model on-prem; for the trace/eval layer LangSmith or Datadog LLM Observability, not built in-house). (4) Four weeks of after-measurement, same dataset, a before-and-after report with a confidence note. MVP deliberately narrow: one workflow, one customer, human stays in the loop — no in-house measurement platform.
Market & gap: The AI-consulting market is growing at ~26–27% CAGR (2026 base $11–14bn depending on source),[1] and three fresh billion-dollar vehicles are pushing into deployment — EY+Microsoft, OpenAI DeployCo, the Anthropic/Blackstone firm.[2] But the measurement tools alongside them (Arize, Galileo, Fiddler, LangSmith, Datadog LLM Observability — the LLM-observability market ~$2.7bn in 2026, ~36% CAGR)[3] all sit at the technical layer: token cost, latency, drift, eval scores — developer self-service, not a business outcome and not hands-on. A category of "AI ROI measurement" doesn't even exist as a market yet — only as a problem statistic (Gartner: 28% of AI use cases in infrastructure & operations meet ROI).[4] Gap for twiceD: the combination of measured before-and-after + hands-on delivery + Mittelstand fit + EU/on-prem sovereignty is occupied by no one today — the giants promise "measurable outcomes" but ship no standardized telemetry. [1] businessresearchinsights.com · [2] news.microsoft.com · [3] researchandmarkets.com · [4] gartner.com
Feasibility (solo + AI): High. The heavy model and the trace layer are bought, not built — twiceD builds the measurement design, the baseline logic, and the report. V0 solo in 4–6 weeks per customer; sold as a fixed-price "measurement pilot," not as hosted standing infrastructure, so low maintenance load. Scaling: one contractor per engagement, the measurement framework is reusable. Sovereignty lever: the on-prem / open-model variant for customers with sensitive data, where the US platform consultancies don't reach. Honest limit: clean measurement demands discipline from the customer (holding to two weeks of baseline) — the sale must frame that as part of the value, not as a burden.
