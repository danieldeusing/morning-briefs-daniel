AWS Opens the Template for Every Retailer

Agentic Shopping Assistant: Kate Spade Is Just the First Visible Piece

On 05.28, AWS released the Agentic Shopping Assistant toolkit via the Generative AI Innovation Center to every retailer — architecture, starter code and expert guidance, validated against "billions of real shopping interactions." First visible piece: Kate Spade AI Gift Concierge from parent company Tapestry (live since 04.13), built on Anthropic's Haiku 4.5 with Amazon Bedrock AgentCore for observability, auth and evals. Tapestry CIO Yang Lu: "AWS brought the recipe, but together we built the customization our consumers needed." Contract→production: ~2.5 months, same length test phase on top. New retailers should go live in ~60 days; additional brands are in tests. Amazon's justification number: 53% report stress buying gifts. aboutamazon.com · itbrief.asia

Agentic Shopping Assistant / AgentCore: An AI advisor in the shop that asks questions conversationally (occasion, recipient, style) instead of just setting filters. AgentCore is the plumbing: AWS services that give the agent memory, tools, identity, runtime and policy — the tedious parts without which a production chatbot doesn't hold up. Like the difference between a plastic espresso cup at home and a café machine that runs eight hours straight.
Our take Most important use-case story of the week because it sets a pattern: hyperscaler supplies a pattern pack (architecture + model connection + pre-release pricing), retailer supplies brand voice, data, rules. That's the horizontal template that wires the consulting pyramid vertically in enterprises. For mid-market, the bottleneck remains: the toolkit exists, but someone has to wire it tight — and that's where Amazon stops.
Methods flag The 2.5 months is Amazon's number for exactly one pilot (Tapestry); the ~60 days a sales promise, not an average. No published conversion or GMV effect. Real: a pre-cooked toolkit with named AWS components and a named live customer — more than most hyperscalers show today.

Platform Competition: Salesforce, Shopify, Klevu, OTTO/Google

The AWS toolkit meets a platform market with clear price tiers: Salesforce Agentforce Commerce enterprise-priced (~$3,600/user/year + $2/conversation, Data Cloud on top); Shopify Sidekick in plan from $29/month; Klevu from ~$449 (Search) and ~$549 (Merchandising). Protocol-wise two camps: ACP (Stripe + OpenAI, 09/2025) and UCP (Shopify + Google) — UCP with Walmart, Target, Etsy, AmEx, Mastercard, Stripe, Visa. In DE, OTTO went live on 05.03 with its Gemini assistant across 18 million items — proof that the AI advisor tier is already happening in DE large-scale retail. alhena.ai · shopify.com · ecommercegermany.com

ACP / UCP (Agentic Commerce Protocols): Standards for how an AI agent (like the ChatGPT or Gemini shopper acting on a buyer's behalf) can search the shop, fill a cart and pay — without the shop and agent needing custom wiring first. Like the ISO-standard coupling on a trailer: two different trailers, same socket.

AI Search Tips to Default Tier

Gemini 3.5 Flash GA + AI Mode at 1 Billion MAU

On 05.19, Google lifted Gemini 3.5 Flash to GA at I/O — standard in search's AI Mode, in the Gemini app, in Antigravity and in the enterprise agent platform. Pichai in keynote language: "AI Mode … in just a year, it's already surpassed 1 billion monthly active users." AI Overviews at "over 2.5 billion MAU". Bench-wise, 3.5 Flash partially replaces its Pro sibling (Terminal Bench 2.1 76.2%, MCP Atlas 83.6%, CharXiv 84.2%) — methodology in-house. Price-wise the tier has moved: $1.50/million input tokens, $9/million output — 6× the price of 3.1 Flash-Lite, near 3.1 Pro ($2 / $12). blog.google (Pichai) · blog.google (3.5 Flash) · simonwillison.net

AI Mode (Google Search): This is no longer "blue links + answer box on top," it's a conversational search mode where Gemini researches in the background and builds a connected answer — with sources, follow-up questions, sometimes small interactive UIs. Like the difference between a library card catalog (you find the books yourself) and a librarian opening three right pages and answering follow-ups.
Our take 1 billion MAU is a watershed — AI search is no longer pilot. Consequence one: retailer SEO becomes "AEO" (Answer Engine Optimization), product feeds and structured data become mandatory. Consequence two: model costs rise in tiers that used to be "cheap." $9/million output is not the rate at which 2024's help-center bot economics can be carried forward.
Methods flag 1 billion MAU is Google's own number, "MAU" Google's definition. Magnitude plausible (AI Overviews 2.5 billion, search standard for over a year). Bench values in-house; LMArena/Artificial Analysis need time to weigh in.

The Consulting Pyramid Wires Itself Vertically

KPMG Switches 276,000 Employees to Claude

On 05.19, KPMG announced a global alliance with Anthropic — Claude embedded in KPMG Digital Gateway (delivery platform running on Azure), reach 276,000+ employees in 138 countries. First wave: Tax/Legal ("tools that previously required weeks … now takes minutes"), Cybersecurity, plus PE portfolio modernization with Claude Code via KPMG Blaze. Anthropic calls KPMG preferred PE partner. Lead-in: two years adoption in KPMG US. On 05.26, the KPMG Tax Summit Miami reframed the story. anthropic.com · kpmg.com

OpenAI DeployCo: The Pyramid Funds Its Own Middle

On 05.11, OpenAI spun off the Deployment Company — OpenAI-controlled majority, 4 billion dollars external funding at 10 billion dollars pre-money / 14 billion dollars post-money. 19 external partners, lead TPG; co-leads Advent, Bain Capital, Brookfield; plus Goldman, SoftBank, BBVA, Warburg Pincus. Notable: McKinsey & Co., Bain & Co., Capgemini are on the list — consulting firms whose middle delivery tier the vehicle is supposed to cannibalize. Operating core: Tomoro acquisition (forward-deployed engineering). Guaranteed minimum return over five years 17.5%, profits capped — credit structure more than classic VC. axios.com

Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE): Instead of a slide consultant, a software engineer sitting with the customer for weeks, building the AI system into their databases and tools. Palantir made the role famous — the job lives off the last mile between model and customer system.
Our take Three announcements in 14 days: KPMG/PwC to Anthropic, OpenAI building a delivery subsidiary with McKinsey/Bain/Capgemini as co-investors, EY sticking with Microsoft. This isn't a tooling choice, it's the delivery tier of consulting rewiring itself — and deliberately not for 200-person firms. For twiceD: the vacuum below remains and gains a blueprint from the AWS template that a small shop can deliver.

Regulation: First Shifted High-Risk Deadlines

Article 6 Consultation Running, High-Risk Dates Slip

With high-risk classification guidelines (Article 6 consultation until 06.23 22:00 CET), the Commission has shifted delivery dates: standalone high-risk obligations not until 12.02.2027 (was 08.02.2026), embedded in products 08.02.2028. Article 50 transparency consultation closes 06.03, hard lever remains 08.02.2026 (GPAI + Article 50). For users: high-risk booking theater gets a year of air, transparency obligation stays summer deadline. hunton.com · ec.europa.eu

Our take Shifted deadlines reorder pressure. High-risk enterprise projects get air, transparency obligations don't — in a week when AI search cracks 1 billion MAU. For DE mid-market rollouts, compliance pressure migrates from "clarify high-risk" to "disclose chatbot/deepfake" — exactly the tier everything hangs on right now.

Project Ideas for twiceD

Researched proposals — with market gap and honest solo feasibility

1 · Agentic Commerce Delivery Block for DE Mid-Market Retailers

ELI5 & Value: A DE mid-market retailer (50–500 employees, Shopware/Adobe Commerce/Shopify) sees the Kate Spade Concierge in the press, wants a conversational advisor in their own shop and realizes: Salesforce Agentforce is enterprise-priced, AWS Agentic Shopping Assistant is a toolkit (not a product), Shopify Sidekick doesn't do the brand-advisor job. A twiceD delivery block combines Bedrock AgentCore (or an EU-sovereignty variant, see idea 2) with product feed and brand voice, hands over a productive advisor plus maintenance block after 6–8 weeks. Value: no platform subscription, no generic chatbot, an advisor trained on this inventory with concrete conversion argument.
Prototype (V0): (1) Engagement: 2 weeks discovery + 4 weeks build + 2 weeks handover, fixed price. (2) Stack: Haiku 4.5 over Bedrock (or Anthropic EU tenant); AgentCore runtime + memory + gateway; LlamaIndex/LangGraph for retrieval; vLLM + Qwen/Mistral for the on-prem variant. (3) Three pre-cooked modules: "Gift Advisor" (occasion/recipient/style), "Product Detail Companion," "Size Recommendation from Returns Data." (4) Handover: architecture, data contract, eval set with 50 real questions, runbook. MVP tight: one module, one inventory, every external effect behind a release gate.
Market & Gap: GenAI in ecommerce for 2026 estimated at $1–1.24 billion, CAGR 18.5–29%.[1] Platform prices clear: Salesforce Agentforce $3,600/user/year + $2/conversation, Shopify Sidekick from $29/month, Klevu from $449/$549/month,[2] AWS Agentic Shopping Assistant is a toolkit with no list price.[3] In DE, OTTO ships its own Gemini assistant,[4] Adobe Commerce/Spryker/Shopware offer integrations but no brand-deep blueprint. Gap for twiceD: the tight custom brand advisor under Salesforce price and over the platform default — plus GDPR/sovereignty story for Article 50 obligation from 08.02.
Feasibility (Solo + AI): High. V0 solo in 6–8 weeks per customer — the building blocks are documented. Maintenance moderate: 2–4 hrs/week per productive customer, as a paid block. Scaling via contractor pool from customer three on. Risks: (a) Bedrock costs on high traffic — token caps + model switching in V0; (b) acquisition via Chambers/retail trade associations and shop-system partner programs, not LinkedIn. 2 · EU Sovereignty Wrapper over Hyperscaler Agent Stacks

ELI5 & Value: Hyperscaler agent stacks (Bedrock AgentCore, Azure AI Foundry, Vertex AI Agent Builder) are workable — but give a DE compliance officer three problems: (a) data residency and sub-processor list, (b) Article 50/Article 6 classification and documentation, (c) a second model eye for content marking and hallucination filter. The twiceD wrapper is a thin layer in front of the agent stack — compliance adapter, not platform — that logs inputs, classifies high-risk per workflow, couples audit trails to regulators and pulls a second EU-hosted model pass. Value: cloud stack stays, compliance officer sleeps, the 08.02 deadline gets prepared.
Prototype (V0): (1) Policy Gateway (FastAPI) in front of the agent endpoint with JSON schema and automatic Article 6 classification against Annex III. (2) Eval/Guard Layer with second model pass — Mistral Large 2 on OVHcloud/IONOS or vLLM + Llama-3.x/Qwen on-prem — against three checks (PII, hallucination score, content marking). (3) Append-only audit log (Postgres + WORM-S3 or MinIO) with workflow ID, I/O hash, classification. (4) Optional Article 50 marking module + C2PA stamp on images. MVP tight: one workflow, one backend (AgentCore or Azure AI Foundry). Delivery as open-source repo + Docker Compose + 4-week setup contract.
Market & Gap: DE AI market $12.18 billion 2025 → $54.71 billion 2032 (CAGR 23.9%),[5] Europe AIaaS $8.2 billion 2026 → $98.5 billion 2034.[6] On the compliance side, EU AI Act pushes mid-market into classification theater; high-risk standalone deadlines just shifted to 12.02.2027 — more time for proper build plus hard transparency obligation from 08.02.2026.[7] Existing solutions: compliance SaaS (Credo AI, Holistic AI, Fairnow, Saidot) — governance documentation but not in agent data path — or platform add-ons (Azure Content Safety, Bedrock Guardrails) — generic, US-sovereign. Gap for twiceD: narrow wrapper in data path, specific to EU compliance and DE mid-market reality, as a build service.
Feasibility (Solo + AI): Moderate. V0 solo in 8–10 weeks (FastAPI, OPA, OpenTelemetry, vLLM/Mistral). Maintenance higher than idea 1 because rules move — 4–6 hrs/week, plus paid maintenance block per customer. Scaling via open-source community + paid setup contracts, not hosted service (else liability shifts to twiceD). [5] fortunebusinessinsights.com · [6] marketdataforecast.com · [7] hunton.com

It's Friday, May 29th. The week has acquired two poles — above, the consulting pyramid, below, the platform tier — and yesterday added the connector between them.

The most important use-case story of the week came on May 28th from Amazon Web Services. AWS released the toolkit for an agentic shopping assistant — in plain language, a conversational advisor in the shop — to every retailer. The first visible piece is the Kate Spade gift concierge from parent Tapestry, built on Anthropic's Haiku 4.5 and the plumbing called Bedrock AgentCore. From contract to live was about two and a half months, and AWS promises new retailers a launch in around sixty days. That is the horizontal template that wires the consulting pyramid vertically in enterprises.

This pyramid reshaped itself over the fourteen days before. On May 19th, KPMG announced a global alliance with Anthropic — Claude will be embedded in their Digital Gateway, accessible to two hundred seventy-six thousand employees in one hundred thirty-eight countries. Eight days earlier, on May 11th, OpenAI had founded the Deployment Company, a majority-controlled subsidiary with four billion dollars in external funding at ten billion dollars pre-money. On the investor list sit TPG, Bain Capital, Brookfield, Goldman, SoftBank — and notably McKinsey, Bain & Company and Capgemini. That is precisely the consulting firms whose middle delivery tier the subsidiary could cannibalize in time.

In parallel, Google on May 19th lifted Gemini 3.5 Flash to general availability at I/O. Sundar Pichai called a watershed: AI Mode, the conversational search tier, has hit one billion monthly active users. AI Overviews sit even higher, at two and a half billion. This means two things for applied AI. First, AI search is no longer pilot — discoverability shifts from search engine optimization to answer engine optimization, with clear consequences for product feeds and structured data. Second, the hyperscalers tested their API customers' price tolerance at the same moment: Gemini 3.5 Flash costs one dollar fifty cents per million input tokens and nine dollars per million output tokens. That is six times the prior generation's rate.

On regulation, a first shift: the high-risk classification guidelines are in consultation from May 19th through June 23rd. The high-risk obligations for standalone systems move from August 2nd, 2026 to December 2nd, 2027 — more time to build properly — but embedded systems move to August 2nd, 2028. What stays: the Article 50 transparency requirement — chatbot and deepfake disclosure — starts August 2nd, 2026. Mid-market rollouts in Germany need exactly that now, cleanly.

From this picture, two project ideas for twiceD. First: an agentic commerce delivery block for German mid-market retailers. AWS has the toolkit, Salesforce is enterprise-priced, Shopify only for Shopify shops — the gap is the tight custom brand advisor under the platforms, delivered on fixed price in six to eight weeks. Second: an EU sovereignty wrapper over the hyperscaler agent stacks. Not a platform itself, but a thin layer in front of Bedrock AgentCore or Azure AI Foundry, bringing high-risk classification, audit trail and a second EU-hosted model pass. Both solo-feasible, both hookable to the GDPR sensitivity of German mid-market operators.

The next brief will watch whether the Anthropic consolidation wave continues at Deloitte or EY, which additional retailers AWS names for its agentic shopping toolkit, and how first submissions to the Article 6 consultation shape up. Until then the filter stays the same: a measured impact number beats an announced delivery capability, and "wired at big companies" is nowhere near "affordable for mid-market."
