Why the Week Rose — and Friday Retraced — the Mechanism

Two forces carried the week, one reversed it on Friday. Drivers up (Mon–Thu): ECB rate-hike repricing for June 11 solidified at ~91% — Lagarde's inflation revision (May 23) and Schnabel's open hike call pulled the euro leg hard higher, and a stronger euro mechanically lifts EUR/BRL. Repricing (Rate Expectations): On futures markets, banks constantly bet what the central bank will do next with rates. "Repricing" means this bet shifts because new data came in. Like a betting shop adjusting the odds for a soccer match when the star striker limps off injured. When Lagarde raised her inflation forecast, the bet on an ECB hike June 11 shifted from 76% to 91% — and that's exactly what makes the euro more expensive, so EUR/BRL higher, before the central bank even decides.

PCE Split (YoY vs. Monthly): Inflation is measured two ways — month-to-month (what happened in April concretely) or year-over-year (where we stand vs. April a year ago). Like two speedometers: one shows your current speed, the other shows your average over twelve months. If current speed drops but the yearly average is still high, you're going slower — the stretch was just fast on average. That's exactly what PCE delivered: April's monthly was cool (0.2%), but because earlier months were hot, the yearly rate climbed to a three-year high (3.3% core).
