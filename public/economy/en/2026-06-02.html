FX in detail — what shifted since Monday

Update since Mon Monday's brief was built on hope that today's Euro May inflation would show cooling energy and weaken the euro. The opposite happened. The flash came in at 3.2% — higher than April's 3.0%, not lower.[1] Energy stayed at +10.9% as the main driver, but now services picked up too, from 3.0% to 3.5%, and the core rate (ex-energy & food) stands at 2.5%. This is broader, not just an oil effect.

EUR/BRL reacts mildly: The ECB-Fix from 01.06 stands at 5.8515, intraday today ~5.8514 (−0.18%) — virtually unchanged from Monday.[2] The cross-check is clean: USD/BRL 5.0245 × EUR/USD 1.1646 = 5.8515, divergence 0.001%, well below the 0.5% threshold. The anchors are consistent.[3]

Core inflation: Inflation without the two most volatile items — energy and food. Like your car's tachometer without a downhill roll: it shows how fast the engine is really spinning. If core inflation rises, price pressure is broad-based and not just a one-time oil shock — exactly what forces a central bank to act.

Monday's tension was: Does the ECB hike because of inflation whose driver (oil) is falling away? Today's number defuses that tension — against the doves. First, inflation came in hot, not cool. Second, oil didn't keep falling: Brent stands at ~93.19 $ (02.06), after nearly touching 96 $ on 01.06 — from May's low (~92.56 $) bouncing back up, choppy, without a clear peace trend.[4] My revised view: The hike was secure anyway; what's new is that the path after it looks less dovish than I thought on Monday. Broadening core inflation (services!) gives the ECB arguments for more than one step — the market is already pricing in at least one more in 2026.[5]
