FX in detail — where we stand & why

This week, the story proved itself. Still on Jun 3, EUR/BRL fixed at 5.8284 — the real firm, the euro weak. On Jun 4, the rate jumped to 5.8951, a gain of 1.14% in a single day. This is not real weakness: the real only weakened slightly (USD/BRL 5.0184 → 5.0645). It is a euro-strength story. EUR/USD rose in parallel from 1.1614 to 1.1640 — the market is buying the euro because the ECB is almost certain to hike on Jun 11.[1]

The causal path of yesterday's move — left to right:

May eurozone inflation 3.2%→ ECB hike Jun 11 nearly certain→ EUR/USD +0.2%→ EUR/BRL +1.14%

Why does 0.2% in EUR/USD lift EUR/BRL by over one percent? Because at the same time, the real weakened slightly against the dollar — both legs point upward for EUR/BRL. The euro gets more expensive and the real gets a bit cheaper; the effects add together.[1][2]

Priced in: The market doesn't bet only at the event — it bets beforehand. If everyone knows Monday that it will rain Friday, the umbrellas are already sold out by Thursday — not when the first drops fall. Likewise: the euro rose yesterday, not on Jun 11. If the ECB then truly hikes and nothing surprises, the euro can even fall slightly ("buy the rumour, sell the news"). The actual move happens in the expectation.

The cross-check discipline: The ECB fix (5.8951) and the independently calculated cross rate (USD/BRL 5.0645 × EUR/USD 1.1640 = 5.8951) match at 0.0% — the anchors are clean today.[1]

The causal path, should the hike come as expected on Jun 11:

Read it backward: the last arrow is the point. Even if the real sits completely still, euro strength sustains the EUR/BRL rate — and thus the real value of a euro invoice. This week, that is no longer an abstract risk, but has largely already happened.

Positioning & BR rates

Positioning remains fundamentally real-friendly, despite yesterday's wobble. The dollar lost roughly 10% against the real in 2026, driven by carry — the high Selic rate of 14.50% attracts foreign money. USD/BRL fell to early May as low as ~4.91 (lowest level since early 2024), since then a gentle counterMove to now 5.06.[2]

BRL carry trade: Borrow where it's cheap (e.g., euros at 2%), invest in Brazil at 14.5%. Like renting a bike for almost nothing and subleasing it dearly — as long as no one steals the bike. The "theft" would be a real collapse. As long as the Selic path doesn't drop abruptly and oil stays tame, this trade has no obvious trigger to unwind.

The Focus survey (BCB economists poll) sees USD/BRL at year-end 2026 at ~R$ 5.16 — second consecutive cut, so a slightly real-optimistic consensus.[3]

Brazil's 5-year CDS today not cleanly verifiable through usual sources (last level ~135–139 bps, end of March); the dated Focus consensus serves as mood proxy. CDS moves slowly.

What this means for Daniel's timing

The operating question remains: convert euro invoice to real now or wait? Daniel's chain is euro in → real spend. A higher EUR/BRL is good for him: more reais per euro. This week, the euro has already gained 1.14% — someone converting yesterday would get more reais today. The setup continues to favor the euro side, but part of that move is now in the rate.

The mechanics for the next few days: If the ECB only confirms the expected on Jun 11, the euro can pull back on "sell the news" — then the day before the meeting would have been better. If it surprises hawkish (e.g., hints at further steps), the euro climbs even higher. Today, the US jobs report gives the first signal: a weak number (~under 50k) pushes the dollar down, making the real firmer and USD/BRL lower — but EUR/BRL barely, because the euro dominates there. No verdict, just the mechanics.
