FX in Detail — Where We Stand & Why

The week had one euro engine; Friday brought a second from Washington. The ECB day fix for EUR/BRL stood at 5.8897 — marginally below the prior day (5.8951). But that's misleading, because the fix is drawn ~14:15 CET, and the day's pivotal data point came after: At 14:30 CET the US Department of Labor released the May jobs report with 172,000 new positions instead of the expected ~80,000.[1][2]

The reaction was textbook. A strong labor market → the market strikes Fed rate-cut hopes, even starts talking about hikes → US bond yields up, dollar firm. The DXY jumped Friday by 0.66% to 100.07 — the first close above 100 in two months. A strong dollar pressures the real, so EUR/BRL spiked intraday to 5.956, well above the 14:15 fix.[1][3][4]

Friday's move's causal chain — left to right:

US jobs +172k (vs 80k)→ Fed hike bets ↑→ DXY above 100→ Real weaker→ EUR/BRL to 5.956

So this week both legs for EUR/BRL stacked. Early week the near-certain ECB hike pushed the euro (EUR/USD leg), Friday the strong dollar pushed the real weaker (BRL leg). Rare to see both drivers point so clearly the same way up.[1][2]

Sampling Timing (why fix ≠ close): The ECB "fix" is a snapshot taken at the same time each day (~14:15 CET). But the market keeps running after. Like a traffic photo taken at 8 AM — by 8:30 the situation could be totally different. Friday the biggest news (the jobs report, 14:30 CET) fell exactly after the photo. So the fix shows 5.8897, but the real day run went up to 5.956. Monday's next fix catches up.

The cross-check discipline: The ECB fix (5.8897) and independently calculated cross-rate (USD/BRL 5.0599 × EUR/USD 1.1640 = 5.8897) match to 0.0% — the anchors are clean.[1]

Positioning & Brazil Rates

Positioning stays fundamentally real-friendly, but Friday dented it. The carry — high Selic rate at 14.50% draws foreign capital — has held the real through 2026: the euro lost roughly 8.3% y/y against the real.[5] But this exact carry advantage melts if Copom cuts further on 06/17 as expected and Fed/ECB tighten — the rate gap narrows.

BRL carry trade: Borrow cheap (say euros at 2%), lend in Brazil at 14.5%. Like renting a bike for almost nothing and renting it out dear — as long as no one steals it. The "theft" would be a real crash. Shrink the rate gap (Brazil cuts, euro/US hike) and the rent shrinks — the incentive to stay in reals drops.

The Focus Survey (BCB economists' poll) last saw USD/BRL at year-end 2026 near ~R$ 5.16 — slightly real-bullish.[6] Brazil's 5-year CDS doesn't cleanly verify via standard sources today (last level ~135–139 bps, end of March); CDS moves slowly; dated Focus consensus serves as sentiment proxy.

What It Means for Daniel's Timing

The operational question stays: convert euro to real now or wait? Daniel's flow is euro in → real spend. A higher EUR/BRL is good for him: more reais per euro. This week both ran for him — euro firm, real weak Friday afternoon. Anyone hitting Friday afternoon got close to 5.95 instead of 5.83 Wednesday.

The mechanics for the next few days, without a call: if the ECB on 06/11 merely confirms expectations, the euro could ease on "sell the news." The new wild card is the dollar: as long as the US labor market stays firm and Fed hike talk continues, the real has a headwind that holds EUR/BRL up even without euro support. The EUR/BRL downside would be an oil crash (eases Brazil inflation, lets Copom cut faster — paradoxically first supporting real via lower risk premium) or sudden Iran ease that flips the safe-haven trade.
