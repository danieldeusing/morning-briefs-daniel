Central Bank Week — tone counts, not the hike

The week brings three decisions in four days, and the first is the most important for Daniel.

ECB, Thursday 06-11: A hike of the deposit rate to 2.25 % (from 2.00 %) is priced at ~99 % — this is no surprise risk anymore.[3] The surprise potential lies entirely in the tone of Lagarde's press conference. The latest Reuters poll shows a hawkish shift: 49 of 80 economists now see two more hikes in 2026 (probably September), driven by energy inflation stoked by the Iran conflict.[4]

"Sell the news" (why a hike can weaken the euro): When everyone already expects a move, its arrival is no buy signal anymore — the buyers are in and taking profits. Like a long-awaited concert: excitement drives ticket prices, but on the night itself they fall. So if ECB merely confirms what's expected, the euro may briefly pull back. But if Lagarde hints at a second hike, that's the real surprise — and the euro keeps climbing.

FOMC & Copom, Tue/Wed 06-16/17: The Fed should hold at ~98 % (3.50–3.75 %) — Kevin Warsh's first meeting as chief. The strong jobs report takes away his cut option, which many had expected; a hawkish debut tone supports the dollar further.[5] In Brazil, the market is pricing ~80 % for another Selic cut (from 14.50 %). Each cut shrinks the real's carry advantage — real-burdening for EUR/BRL.[6]

Positioning & BR Rates

Positioning remains broadly real-friendly, but Friday dented it. The carry — the high Selic at 14.50 % attracts foreign money — has carried the real to ~−8 % euro weakness YTD (the euro lost ~8.3 % against the real over the year).[7] This exact edge melts if Copom cuts as expected on 06-17 and Fed/ECB tighten — the rate gap narrows.

BRL Carry Trade: Borrow where it's cheap (say, in euros at 2 %) and lend in Brazil at 14.5 %. Like renting a bike for next to nothing and re-renting it dear — as long as nobody steals it. The "theft" would be a real crash. When the gap shrinks (BR cuts, euro/US hike), the rent gets smaller — the incentive to stay in real assets falls.

The Focus survey (BCB economist poll) sees Selic at year-end 2026 at ~13.25 % and IPCA inflation at 5.09 % — the latter has now risen for 12 weeks in a row, driven by energy and sticky service prices.[8] There's the tension in the BR picture: the market expects cuts even as inflation expectations drift higher. Brazil's 5-year CDS is not cleanly verifiable today (last reads ~135–139 bps, end March); CDS moves slowly, so dated Focus consensus serves as sentiment proxy.

On the other side, the dollar, which after one hundred seventy-two thousand new US jobs has pushed the dollar index above one hundred for the first time in two months. A strong dollar weakens the real. Both legs point upward — and the real gets squeezed in the middle.

The heart of the week is the European Central Bank Thursday. A hike to two point two five percent is priced at around ninety-nine percent — that is no longer a surprise.
