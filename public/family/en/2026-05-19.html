Prova Paraná running today — and yes, our daughter is in scope [4][5]

Plainly missed in Sunday's and Monday's briefs: SEED-PR is running today and tomorrow, 2026-05-19 and 2026-05-20, the Prova Paraná 2026 diagnostic assessment — statewide learning-level evaluation for Year 2 and Year 5 elementary in the municipal network. Today: Portuguese; tomorrow: math. About 1.1 million students PR-wide, including 1,773 with specialised resources (57 Braille, 1,692 low-vision).

Concretely for us: our 7-year-old daughter is in Year 2 — she's in scope. The 8-year-old brother is in Year 3 depending on enrolment year (so not directly in scope), but worth a quick WhatsApp ping to the class chat or teacher this afternoon: Was the test held today? In which period? Did the kids need a specific pencil/eraser kit? Some schools post these in the weekly agenda, others announce same-day.

Prova Paraná: Not a graded class test — a standardised diagnostic, similar to VERA-3 in Germany. SEED-PR wants to know where children stand in Portuguese and math; results feed school development planning, not the report card. For the daughter that means: no pressure, this is for the school, not against her.

If the school takes the diagnostic seriously, the upside is a clear reading and arithmetic snapshot, refreshed two or three times a year (May + September). Results land in the school-pedagogy meeting, not in the class chat — if parents want to see the level, ask the head teacher.

Dengue: Ribeirão Claro at 0 — but Jacarezinho is PR's #4 [10][11][12]

Yesterday's framing was "PR state: 19th RS Jacarezinho stable, no fresh spike in SE 18." Sharper stat: the 19th RS Jacarezinho has 4,633 cumulative confirmed dengue cases in 2026 — the 4th-highest regional in all of Paraná, behind 17th RS Londrina (15,954), 14th RS Paranavaí (11,291) and 15th RS Maringá (8,689). "Stable" was too soft; more accurate: "no longer rising, but at a high level".

The good half: Ribeirão Claro itself holds 0 cases for 2026. The Vigilância home-visit routine is working. PR overall is radically better than 2025 — −96.1% YoY in cases (52,193 → 2,028), one death statewide vs. 90 last year. Dry season helping.

What it means for us in practice: any trip into Jacarezinho or further toward Londrina sees higher Aedes pressure than at home. Standard prevention is enough — repellent with Icaridin or DEET from age 2, lightweight long sleeves for the kids in late afternoon, no standing water in the yard. With the 2-year-old, especially careful: thin layer of repellent, avoid face and hands.

Boletim Epidemiológico: Weekly bulletin from Sesa-PR (state health secretariat), short like a weather report: current cases per disease, per regional. Comes out Tue/Wed, covers the epidemiological week (SE Mon–Sun). SE 19 ended Sat 2026-05-09; SE 20 ends this coming Saturday 2026-05-23.
