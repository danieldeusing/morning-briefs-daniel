InfoGripe correction: Paraná is now in the growth group [9][10]

The most important caution, and it corrects yesterday's. Yesterday the brief relied on InfoGripe EW 17/2026 with the line "Paraná and São Paulo are the exceptions." The newer edition EW 18/2026 is now out (data through 2026-05-09, published 2026-05-14) — and it says the opposite: PR and SP are now explicitly on the list of the 16 states with a growth trend in SRAG over the last six weeks. All states are at alert, risk, or high-risk level. A worsening, not an all-clear.

SRAG: "Síndrome Respiratória Aguda Grave" — severe acute respiratory illness that lands you in hospital; InfoGripe counts these weekly per state. RSV: respiratory syncytial virus — like a heavy cold that in babies can clog the small airways. In a school kid usually just a runny nose, in a one-month-old dangerous.

Why it was different yesterday: not because anyone was wrong, but because the bulletin updates weekly. Yesterday EW 17 was current; today EW 18 is the fresh truth. That's exactly why we stamp every health figure with week + year — otherwise you carry an outdated all-clear too long.

Operationally for the baby — and for the event plans: RSV is driving the rise in children under two (41.5% of the 4-week mix, influenza A 27.2%, rhinovirus 25.5%). Cocooning one notch stricter: handwashing before every baby contact by the three siblings, no kisses from sniffly noses, mask when holding if congested. At the events: avoid full closed halls with the baby, prefer outdoor programming + wrap + distance. Baby alarm thresholds: > 60 breaths/min, retractions between the ribs, blue lips, any fever under 3 months = emergency care immediately.

Open family question (unchanged): whether your wife got the maternal Abrysvo RSV shot from week 28 of pregnancy. If yes: baby protected ~6 months via the mother. If no: cocooning carries it alone — with the situation rising, all the more urgent to clarify today.
