School: Prova Paraná is done — looking ahead to the Corpus Christi bridge [8][9]

The Prova Paraná 2026 diagnostic assessment is complete (Portuguese + Math, May 19/20, ~1.1M students statewide, incl. 2nd/5th grade). Our 7-year-old took part. Planning anchor: the next holiday is Corpus Christi on Thursday June 4 — nationwide, and in many PR municipalities Friday June 5 too as a ponto facultativo, so potentially a 4-day weekend. Whether the school in Ribeirão Claro grants the Friday "bridge" depends on the municipal calendar — clarify in good time. Ponto facultativo: not a true holiday but an "optional day off" for government offices/schools — each municipality decides whether to close. Like a bridge day in Germany, only without using up vacation.

Health & baby: situation unchanged, all the more important during indoor crowding [10][11]

No new bulletin today — the status stays InfoGripe SE 18/2026 (data through May 9, published May 14). An SE 19 hasn't come out yet. So substantively unchanged since midweek: Paraná remains on the list of 16 states with growing SRAG, all states at alert, risk or high-risk level, and the rise in children under two is driven by RSV. No all-clear — but we're not extrapolating either, we're waiting for the next bulletin.

SRAG: "Síndrome Respiratória Aguda Grave" — severe acute respiratory illness with hospital involvement; counted weekly per state. RSV: respiratory syncytial virus — for the older kids usually just a cold, but dangerous for the 1-month-old baby, because it can clog the tiny airways.

Why it matters today: the wet weekend pushes everyone into enclosed spaces — theatre hall, shopping, packed halls at the Virada. That's exactly where respiratory viruses circulate. So for the baby, cocooning a notch stricter: hand-washing before every baby contact by the three siblings, no kisses from runny noses, avoid full enclosed halls with the baby, prefer short outdoor windows with a carrier + distance. Warning signs in the baby: > 60 breaths/min, rib retractions, blue lips, any fever under 3 months = emergency service immediately. The open family question remains: whether the mother got the maternal Abrysvo RSV vaccine from week 28 of pregnancy (if so: baby protected ~6 months).

Dengue (status unchanged): Sesa Informe nº 16 (SE 01–19/2026) — 19th RS Jacarezinho 32 cases / 0 deaths, statewide 3,552 / 2 deaths, −94.8% YoY. The "6,593 / 17,768" figures floating around in search results are from earlier high-incidence seasons (2024/25) — don't confuse them. With the weekend rain: after showers, empty standing water (buckets, saucers, tarps).

Flu vaccine: 8 days left to the deadline [12][13]

The flu campaign in Paraná runs until Saturday May 30, 2026 — today 8 days, across more than 1,800 vaccination points in 399 municipalities. So far 1,535,863 doses (Sesa, as of May 15); a new delivery of 880,000 doses keeps the UBS stocked. Antibodies take about two weeks — go this week and you're protected for the season ahead.

Who's eligible where: at the UBS Ribeirão Claro the priority groups apply — children 6 months to < 6 years (our 2-year-old clearly qualifies), pregnant women, over-60s, the chronically ill, teachers. The school actions (ações itinerantes) were expanded to the whole school community of any age — students and staff — but only while the team is on-site at the school; that does not extend to students' families. So for the older two, most likely via a school action, otherwise the UBS.
