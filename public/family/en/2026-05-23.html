Weather: storm smaller than expected — but the wet weekend stays [1][2][3]

The honest correction to yesterday: the media reports about an orange INMET storm warning with gusts up to 100 km/h for Norte Pioneiro were overblown. The officially active warning (checked via the INMET Avisos interface) is yellow today — "Perigo Potencial," thunderstorms, valid 05/23 00:01 to 05/24 09:00, for Paraná and several neighboring states. Content: rain of 20–30 mm/h or up to 50 mm/day, gusts of 40–60 km/h (not 100), hail possible, low risk of damage/power outages. The orange level applied only to Friday 05/22 — and even then not to our region, but to western/southwestern/central-southern Paraná.

What remains: Saturday will be very wet. All the checked portals agree on the rain — ~90% (climatempo 90%, otempo/Jacarezinho similar), 21/15 °C, thunderstorm risk. So the plan stays indoors, but without the storm drama: no 100 km/h gusts for us, no top-level hail alert.

Mon/Tue now uncertain Yesterday it was "Mon/Tue dry, 0% rain." That no longer holds cleanly: Sunday stays wet at 73–90%, Monday is disputed (tempo.com 0%, Climatempo 55%), Tuesday 40–60%. So: keep outdoor plans flexible and recheck Sunday evening, rather than locking onto Monday.
Yellow vs. Orange (INMET): INMET color-codes its warnings like a traffic light. Yellow = "Perigo Potencial," watch out, usually just inconveniences. Orange = "Perigo," real danger (strong gusts, flooding). Today it's yellow — the difference from last night is real, not just splitting hairs.

Health & baby: new InfoGripe bulletin — trend still rising [8][9]

Real news today: the InfoGripe bulletin SE 19/2026 (Fiocruz, SE 19 = 05/10–05/16) is out — the first update since SE 18. Content: Paraná remains among the 18 states with a long-term rising SRAG trend (severe respiratory illnesses), with all states except Rondônia at alert, risk or high-risk level. New and specifically relevant to us: Influenza A hospitalizations are currently still rising in Paraná (alongside RS and TO). Among the under-twos, VSR (RSV) continues to drive the increase — over the last four weeks 44.5% of positive samples were RSV.

SRAG: "Síndrome Respiratória Aguda Grave" — severe respiratory illness requiring hospitalization; counted weekly per state. VSR/RSV: Respiratory Syncytial Virus — for the older ones usually just a runny nose, dangerous for the 1-month-old because it can clog the tiny airways.

Why it matters today: the wet weekend pushes everyone into enclosed spaces — the theater hall, the mall, packed venues at the Virada. That's exactly where respiratory viruses circulate. So for the baby, cocooning a notch stricter: hand-washing before every contact with the baby by all three siblings, no kisses from runny noses, avoid full enclosed halls with the baby, prefer short windows with a baby sling + distance. Warning signs in the baby: > 60 breaths/min, rib retractions, blue lips, any fever under 3 months = ER immediately. An open family question remains whether the mother got the maternal Abrysvo RSV vaccine from the 28th week of pregnancy (if so: baby protected for ~6 months).

Dengue (slightly updated): Sesa Informe nº 16 (SE 01–19/2026, published 05/19) — 19ª RS Jacarezinho 32 cases / 0 deaths, statewide now 4,010 cases / 2 deaths (previously 3,552 in Informe nº 15), still −94.8% YoY. Informe nº 17 isn't out yet. The "6,593 / 17,768" figures circulating in search results come from earlier high-incidence seasons — don't confuse them. With this rain: after showers, empty standing water (buckets, saucers, tarps).

Flu shot: still 7 days to the deadline [10][11]

The flu vaccination campaign in Paraná runs through Saturday 2026-05-30 — so today, 7 days. So far 1,535,863 doses (Sesa, as of 05/15); of the 2.2M doses received, an 880,000-dose batch was distributed to the 22 Regionais, which keeps the UBS stocked. Antibodies take about two weeks — whoever goes this week has protection for the rest of the season. With the still-rising Influenza A situation in PR described above, this isn't something to put off.

Who's eligible where: at the UBS Ribeirão Claro the priority groups apply — children 6 months to < 6 years (our 2-year-old clearly qualifies), pregnant women/new mothers, those over 60, the chronically ill, teachers/school community. For the two school-age kids, most likely via a school campaign, otherwise the UBS.
