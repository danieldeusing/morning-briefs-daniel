Health & baby: Paraná in the respiratory growth group [10][11]

The latest bulletin remains InfoGripe SE 19/2026 (Fiocruz, SE 19 = 05-10 to 05-16) — SE 20 isn't out yet. The situation stays serious: Paraná is in the growth group of states with rising severe respiratory illness (a long-term trend over several weeks). Specifically: Influenza A hospitalizations are still rising in PR, and among the under-twos, RSV (VSR) continues to drive the rise.

SRAG: "Síndrome Respiratória Aguda Grave" — severe respiratory illness with hospital involvement, counted weekly per state. RSV: respiratory syncytial virus — for the older kids usually just a runny nose, dangerous for the 1-month-old baby because it can clog the tiny airways.

Why it matters today: the wet Sunday pushes everyone into enclosed spaces — theatre hall, shopping. That's exactly where respiratory viruses circulate. So for the baby, cocoon one notch tighter: handwashing before every baby contact by the three siblings, no runny-nose kisses, avoid crowded enclosed halls with the baby, prefer short windows with sling + distance. Baby warning signs: > 60 breaths/min, rib retractions, blue lips, any fever under 3 months = emergency service immediately.

Dengue (holding): Sesa Informe nº 16 (SE 01–19/2026, published 05-19) — 19ª RS Jacarezinho 32 cases / 0 deaths, PR-wide 4,010 cases / 2 deaths, still −94.8% YoY. Informe nº 17 isn't out yet. Search hits with "6,593"/"17,768" are from earlier high-incidence seasons — don't mix them up. With this rain: after showers, empty standing water (buckets, saucers, tarps).
