Health & baby: Paraná in the respiratory growth group [7][8]

No new bulletin since yesterday, but the situation stays serious: the latest is still InfoGripe SE 19/2026 (Fiocruz, published 2026-05-21, SE 19 = 05-10 to 05-16) — SE 20 hasn't appeared yet. Paraná is in the growth group of states with rising severe acute respiratory illness: Influenza A hospitalizations are still rising in PR, and among the under-2s RSV (VSR) keeps driving the increase (nationally RSV dominates at ~44% of positive samples).

SRAG: "Síndrome Respiratória Aguda Grave" — severe acute respiratory illness with a hospital link, counted weekly per state. VSR/RSV: respiratory syncytial virus — for the big kids usually just a runny nose, but dangerous for the 1-month-old because it can clog the tiny airways.

Why it matters today: a wet start to the week pushes everyone into enclosed spaces — theater hall, shopping. That's exactly where respiratory viruses circulate. So for the baby, cocooning one notch stricter: handwashing before every baby contact by the three siblings, no kisses from runny noses, avoid full enclosed halls with the baby, prefer short windows with sling + distance. Baby red flags: > 60 breaths/min, rib retractions, blue lips, any fever under 3 months = emergency care immediately.

Dengue (holding): Sesa Informe nº 16 (SE 01–19/2026, published 2026-05-19) — 19ª RS Jacarezinho 32 cases / 0 deaths, PR-wide 4,010 cases / 2 deaths, still −94.8% YoY. Informe nº 17 isn't out yet. Search hits with "6,593"/"17,768" are from earlier high-incidence seasons — don't confuse them. With this rain: after showers, empty standing water (buckets, saucers, tarps).
