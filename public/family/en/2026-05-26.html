Health & baby: Paraná in the respiratory-growth group [7][8]

No new bulletin since yesterday, but the picture stays serious: the latest is still InfoGripe SE 19/2026 (Fiocruz, published 2026-05-21, SE 19 = 2026-05-10 to 16) — SE 20 isn't out yet. Paraná is in the growth group of states with rising severe acute respiratory illness: influenza A hospitalizations are still rising in PR, and among the under-twos, RSV keeps driving the increase (nationally RSV dominates with ~44.5% of positive samples).

SRAG: "Síndrome Respiratória Aguda Grave" — severe acute respiratory illness requiring hospital care, counted weekly per state. RSV: respiratory syncytial virus — for the older kids usually just a runny nose, but dangerous for a 1-month-old because it can clog the tiny airways.

Why it matters today: the storm day pushes everyone into enclosed spaces — exactly where respiratory viruses circulate. So for the baby, cocooning one notch stricter: hand-washing before every baby contact by the three siblings, no sniffly kisses, avoid full enclosed halls with the baby, prefer short windows with a carrier + distance. Baby warning signs: > 60 breaths/min, rib retractions, blue lips, any fever under 3 months = emergency service immediately.

Dengue (figures held): Sesa Informe nº 16 (SE 01–19/2026, published 2026-05-19) — 19ª RS Jacarezinho 32 cases / 0 deaths, PR-wide 4,010 cases / 2 deaths, still −94.8% YoY. Informe nº 17 isn't out yet. Search hits with "6,593"/"17,768" are from earlier high-incidence seasons — don't mix them up. After the showers, empty standing water (buckets, saucers, tarps).
