Health & baby: fresh dengue numbers, respiratory illness still watched [7][8][9]

Dengue (NEW): Sesa-PR published Informe nº 17 yesterday (26.05., covering SE 01–20/2026). For our region the picture stays mild: the 19ª RS Jacarezinho counts 39 confirmed cases / 0 deaths (from 32 in nº 16). PR-wide 4,411 cases / 2 deaths (+401 week-on-week), still −94.5% versus 2025. Search hits showing "6,705" (Jacarezinho) or "17,768" are from the 2025 high-incidence season — don't confuse them. After the rains, empty standing water (buckets, saucers, tarps).

Respiratory (status held): No new bulletin — the latest remains InfoGripe SE 19/2026 (Fiocruz, published 21.05.); SE 20 is not out yet. Paraná stays in the growth group: influenza A hospitalizations still rising in PR, and among the under-twos, RSV (VSR) continues to drive the increase (~44.5% of positive samples nationally).

SRAG & RSV/VSR: SRAG = "severe respiratory illness requiring hospitalization," counted weekly per state. RSV/VSR = respiratory syncytial virus — for the older kids usually just a runny nose, but dangerous for a 1-month-old baby because it can clog the tiny airways.

Why it matters this week: The cool evenings push everyone back indoors more — exactly where respiratory viruses circulate. So keep cocooning the baby: hand-washing before every contact by the three siblings, no kisses from sniffly noses, ration full enclosed halls. The outdoor zoo this weekend is good precisely for that — fresh air instead of a crowded hall. Baby warning signs: >60 breaths/min, rib retractions, blue lips, any fever under 3 months = emergency care immediately.

Flu shot: only 3 days to the deadline [9]

Paraná's flu campaign runs through Saturday 30.05.2026 — so 3 days from today, with no extension announced. So far 1,535,863 doses (Sesa, as of 15.05.); Sesa just distributed a new 880,000-dose lot to municipalities to keep supplies up. Antibodies take about two weeks — go this week and you're protected for the season's course. With influenza A still rising in PR, this isn't one to put off.

Who's eligible where: At the UBS Ribeirão Claro the priority groups apply — kids 6 months to < 6 years (our 2-year-old clearly qualifies), pregnant/postpartum women, those over 60, the chronically ill, teachers/school community. For the two school-age kids, most likely through a school drive, otherwise the UBS.
