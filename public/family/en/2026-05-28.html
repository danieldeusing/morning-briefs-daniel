Paraná stays in long-term growth group: influenza-A hospitalizations continue rising in PR, and in under-4s (so explicitly also the 2-year-old and baby) RSV is driving the rise (nationally ~44.5% of positive samples last four weeks).

SRAG & RSV — quick refresh: SRAG = "severe respiratory illness with hospital tie-in," Fiocruz counts it weekly per state. RSV = respiratory syncytial virus — for older kids mostly just a runny nose, for a 1-month-old dangerous because it can clog tiny airways. Fever, >60 breaths/min, visible rib retractions, blue lips, or any fever under 3 months old: go to ER immediately.

What this means today: Cool nights (lows 12–14 °C) push everyone back into enclosed spaces — where respiratory viruses circulate densest. Baby cocooning stays strict: hand-wash before every baby contact by all three siblings, no sniffly kisses, dose closed spaces. Exactly why Triolé on Saturday works so well: open-air on the Pedreira is precisely the right respiratory hygiene. Indoor slots like EnCena tonight are okay, but then not mall + indoor play place the same day.
