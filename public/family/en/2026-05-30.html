School & the Corpus Christi bridge day [3]

Thursday June 4 is free — and likely Friday too

Corpus Christi falls on Thursday, June 4, 2026. In Paraná, it's a ponto facultativo (discretionary "can-close" day for government offices, not a mandatory holiday), and many schools close both Thursday and Friday — making a four-day weekend.[3]

ponto facultativo: not a required holiday, but a day when offices and many businesses can choose to close. Think of it as a "discretionary bridge day": whether your child's school or your workplace participates is up to them — so check with the school calendar first.

Planning tip: The Thursday-to-Sunday window is perfect for one of the Londrina trips below. If you're going, check your kids' school calendar ahead to confirm Friday is actually free.

Health & Safety [4][5]

Dengue 2026: sharply down — good news for a house with a newborn

Good news with perspective: Paraná recorded 2,028 confirmed dengue cases and 1 death so far in 2026 — a decline of 96.1% and 98.8% respectively compared to the same period in 2025 (52,193 cases, 90 deaths).[4] This is the cumulative figure for epidemiological weeks 1–14/2026 (January through April 11), published April 15, 2026.

Epidemiological week (EW): Health agencies count not by calendar month but in numbered weeks from the year's start — like pages in a diary. "EW 21/2026" is the 21st week of this year. This allows clean week-to-week comparison across years.

Regionally, the 17ª Regional de Saúde Londrina leads the statistics with 534 confirmed cases — it covers our Norte Pioneiro area.[4] The freshest weekly bulletin is Informe Epidemiológico nº 17 from May 26, 2026.[5] Despite the decline, the old rule applies: empty standing water (buckets, saucers, tires) — the Aedes mosquito needs only a bottle cap's worth to breed.

Weekend & bridge day plans (Greater Londrina) [6][7][8]

Expo Japão 2026 — Japanese culture for the whole family

From June 3 to 7, ACEL Londrina transforms its grounds into one of Southern Brazil's largest Japan celebrations: Oriental cuisine, traditional and modern dance, sports, plus anime and K-pop corners. Organizers expect over 40,000 visitors.[6]

What works
  • Admission affordable: R$ 14 (half R$ 7)
  • Children under 3, 65+, and disabled free
  • Thursday (holiday) 10 am–10 pm — plenty of time
  • School kids love anime and food stands
Concerns
  • Large crowds — with baby, go early maybe
  • ~2 h 40 drive each way
  • Evening is loud and crowded for the 2-year-old

Suitability: School kids (8 & 7 yrs) excellent; toddler (2 yrs) fine during the day; newborn (1 month) only briefly and early morning, baby carrier instead of stroller in crowds.

Paraná Junino — free festa junina at Sesc Londrina Norte

On Saturday, June 6 from noon, Sesc hosts its Junina festival — free, at six PR locations at once (including Londrina; Jacarezinho is not included).[7][8] On offer: quadrilha dancing, fishing games, clown mouth, knock-down-the-cans, sack races, plus quentão (hot sugarcane punch), green corn, canjica, and pamonha (cornmeal treats). Closing show: Banda Alma Serrana.

Festa Junina / Arraiá: Brazil's June summer festival — colorful bunting, bonfire atmosphere, folk dances (quadrilha) and lots of cornmeal snacks. Like a harvest fair, but with checkered shirts and straw hats.

Suitability: ideal for school kids (supervised games), good for the 2-year-old, baby-friendly thanks to seating and daytime slot. The top pick of the week (see dashboard).

It's Saturday, May 30th. Today the local area stays quiet, but the weather cooperates: dry conditions with light clouds and no rain — perfect for a playground or a walk along the river. With a newborn at home, that's exactly the most relaxed plan.

The real momentum comes next week. Corpus Christi falls on Thursday, June 4th — in Paraná, what's called a ponto facultativo or discretionary holiday. Many schools close both Thursday and Friday, which can make a four-day weekend. It's worth a quick check with the kids' school calendar to confirm Friday is actually off.

Two highlights in Londrina fall right into this window — about two and a half hours' drive away. From June 3rd through 7th, Expo Japão features Japanese culture, food, traditional and modern dance, and an anime corner. Admission runs fourteen reais; children under three are free. For your two school kids, it's a home run. With the newborn, go early and keep it short.

The second highlight is my weekend pick: Paraná Junino on Saturday, June 6th, at Sesc Londrina Norte. Free entry, starting at noon, with supervised children's games — fishing games, sack races, quadrilha dancing — and a live band at the end. Supervised games means your older kids can jump in while you stay at the table with the toddler and baby.

On the health front, some reassuring news: dengue cases in Paraná are down ninety-six percent this year — good when a newborn is in the house. In search results, you may still find older outbreak numbers from last year; those no longer apply. Still, keep emptying standing water — the mosquito only needs a bottle cap full to breed.

If you're up for a longer drive, São Paulo's big Junina festivals are running in Villa-Lobos Park and Ibirapuera Park, some with free entry days. And close by, there's a Moto Fest near the end of June in Santo Antônio da Platina — more geared toward school kids.

Next week we'll see what small neighborhood festivities pop up in Ribeirão Claro and Jacarezinho — those community schedules usually go live at the last minute. Until then: enjoy the long weekend.
