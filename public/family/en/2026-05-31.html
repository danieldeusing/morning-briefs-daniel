School & the Corpus Christi bridge day [3]

Thursday June 4 off — and likely Friday too

Corpus Christi falls on Thursday, June 4, 2026. In Paraná it's a ponto facultativo (per Decree 12.134/2025), and many schools close both Thursday and Friday — making it a four-day weekend.[3]

ponto facultativo: not a mandatory holiday, but a day when offices and many businesses may voluntarily close. Think of it as a "wish bridge day": whether your school or workplace joins in is their decision — so check with your kids' school beforehand.

Planning tip: The Thu–Sun window fits perfectly with the Junina weekend plans below. If you're planning a Londrina trip, check the school calendar beforehand to see if Friday is really off — that makes the overnight trip worthwhile instead of a same-day round trip.

Health & Safety [4][5][6]

Respiratory season ramping up — important with newborn in the house

Fiocruz reports in the InfoGripe bulletin week 19/2026 (May 10–16, released May 21, 2026) a nationwide rise in severe respiratory infections (SRAG). In young children, the main driver is RSV: in the past four weeks, 44.5% of positive samples were RSV versus 24.5% influenza A. Paraná is among 18 states with long-term upward trend; influenza A hospital admissions are also rising here.[4]

RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus): a cold virus that's usually harmless in adults but can clog the tiny airways in babies (bronchiolitis). Think of drinking straws that swell shut — in infants they're already tiny, so RSV hits the youngest hardest. Brazil's winter (May–August) is peak season.

Practical for a 1-month-old: no need to panic, but a good reason for the basics. Wash hands before holding, keep the sick away from the infant, in crowded indoor spaces (festival pavilions!) keep the baby close in a carrier and keep visits short, avoid large crowds with the newborn. That's exactly why outdoor festivals and early visits are better than the full evening crowd.

agencia.fiocruz.br

Dengue 2026: still very low

The good news holds: Paraná counted 2,028 confirmed dengue cases and 1 death in 2026 so far — a decline of 96.1% and 98.8% respectively versus the same period in 2025 (52,193 cases, 90 deaths).[5] This is the cumulative count for epidemiological weeks 1–14/2026, released April 15, 2026; the most recent weekly bulletin is Epidemiological Report no. 17 from May 26, 2026.[6]

Epidemiological week (EW): the health authority counts not by calendar month but in numbered weeks from the start of the year — like pages in a diary. This lets weeks be cleanly compared with prior years.

Regionally, the 17th Health District Londrina leads with 534 confirmed cases — it covers our Norte Pioneiro area.[5] Despite the decline, the classic rule applies: empty standing water (buckets, saucers, tires) — the Aedes mosquito needs just a bottle cap of water to breed.

Junina weekend — from nearby to Londrina [7][8][9]

Festa Junina Cornélio Procópio — short drive, full festival

The closest major Junina option: Saturday, June 6 from 4 PM at Praça Botafogo in Cornélio Procópio — free, just ~80 km / about one hour's drive.[7] Offerings: quadrilhas from local schools, fishing game and children's games, typical food (proceeds go to schools), and evening sertanejo with Marcus & Dalto.

Festa Junina / Arraiá: Brazil's June folk festival — colorful pennants, bonfire atmosphere, folk dances (Quadrilha), and lots of corn pastries. Like a harvest fair, only with checked shirts and straw hats.

Suitability: school-age children (8 & 7 yrs) excellent, toddler (2 yrs) good, infant (1 month) early part and short — open plaza, so nursing space and shade at the edge. With the baby, better aim for the early part before the stage gets crowded in the evening.

Expo Japão 2026 — Japanese culture for the whole family

From June 3–7, ACEL Londrina (rural headquarters, Rua Major Achiles Pimpão Ferreira 2300) transforms its grounds into one of southern Brazil's biggest Japan celebrations: oriental cuisine, traditional and modern dances, Taiko drums, plus anime and K-pop corners and the 63rd Agricultural Expo.[8]

Paraná Junino — free Junina festival at Sesc Londrina Norte

On Saturday, June 6, Sesc hosts its Junina festival (Rua Cegonha 100, Sesc Londrina Norte) — free.[9] Offerings: Quadrilha, fishing game, clown's mouth, knock-the-cans, sack races, plus hot sugarcane punch, fresh corn, corn mush and pamonha, plus crafts fair.

Suitability: ideal for school-age kids (supervised games), good for the 2-year-old, baby-friendly thanks to seating and daytime hours. Practical: if you were already in Cornélio Procópio that same day (closer), you don't need to add Londrina — both run on June 6 in parallel.

If the long drive appeals: São Paulo festivals [10][11]

São João de São Paulo & Arraiá do Ibira

For a bigger long-weekend adventure (4–6 h drive): In Parque Villa-Lobos runs São João de São Paulo — free (ticket pickup via Sympla), weekends through June 21 (incl. Jun 4–7). Highlight for the kids: performances by Turma da Mônica and Chico Bento, plus forró, Quadrilha, and children's workshops.[10] From June 13, Arraiá do Ibira in Parque Ibirapuera (free, Jun 13/14, 20/21, 27/28).[11]

Worth it if: you're using the long weekend for a São Paulo trip anyway — for the single purpose of "festival," the nearby Cornélio Procópio option with 1 h drive is clearly more relaxed, especially with baby and toddler.

It's Sunday, May thirty-first. Today stays quiet in the core radius, but the weather cooperates: dry twenty-three degrees with some clouds, no rain — and both weather portals agree. A good day for the playground, the plaza, or a walk along the river. With the newborn in the house, that's exactly the most relaxed plan.

The real momentum comes next week. Corpus Christi falls on Thursday, June fourth — in Paraná what they call an optional holiday, and many schools close Thursday and Friday. That could make a four-day weekend. Best to check your kids' school calendar to see if Friday is really off.

Right in that window falls the Junina weekend. My tip this time is the closest option: the free Festa Junina in Cornélio Procópio on Saturday, June sixth, from four PM at Praça Botafogo. Only about an hour's drive — much more relaxed than Londrina, with Quadrilha, fishing games, and children's games, sertanejo in the evening.

If you want to drive farther: in Londrina from June third to seventh runs Expo Japão — Japanese culture, food, drums, and an anime corner. Entry costs fourteen reais, children under three are free. And that same Saturday, Sesc Londrina Norte runs its free Paraná Junino festival with supervised children's games. Both Londrina festivals run in parallel — one is enough.

On health, an important note: respiratory season is ramping up. Fiocruz reports a rise in severe respiratory infections, in young children mainly from respiratory syncytial virus, or R S V. In the past four weeks, nearly half of positive samples were R S V, and Paraná shows an increasing trend. With a one-month-old baby that means: wash hands, keep the sick away, in crowded festival pavilions keep the baby close in a carrier and stay short and early.

On dengue the news stays good: in Paraná this year cases have dropped ninety-six percent versus last year. Still empty standing water though, the mosquito needs just a bottle cap full.

If you fancy a bigger drive: in São Paulo the big Junina festivals run in Park Villa-Lobos and from mid-June in Ibirapuera, some with Turma da Mônica and Chico Bento for the kids. But it's worth it only if you're already using the long weekend for a São Paulo trip.
