Festa Junina season kicks off — and a dry, cool winter opening

Monday, June 1, 2026 · Ribeirão Claro / Norte Pioneiro do Paraná

June is Festa Junina month — the most family-friendly month on the Brazilian calendar. Quadrilha dances, fishing games, quentão for the grownups and hot chocolate for the kids flow through the whole region this week. Plus a textbook winter start: dry, cool, no rain until the weekend. One thing clouds the picture — upper-respiratory-virus season is climbing in the South, and with a one-month-old baby, a second look makes sense.

June = Festa Junina — right at your doorstep

June is the family month par excellence in Brazil: quadrilha dances (the signature pair-dances in check shirts and braids), bonfire atmosphere, fishing games for the kids, and traditional food — boiled corn, popcorn, pé-de-moleque, and quentão for the grownups. SESC runs its statewide "Paraná Junino" program in Norte Pioneiro too: in Jacarezinho there's the 2º Arraiá do Sesc Jacarezinho with the I Festival de Quadrilha Junina dos Comerciários — free entry, starting at 6 p.m., with games and stage performances.[3] The exact date wasn't posted on the SESC website yet — check sescpr.com.br/cidade/jacarezinho shortly before.

Weekend Plans

Sat: Festa Junina Cornélio Procópio (our pick)

On Saturday, June 6, from 4 p.m., Praça Botafogo in Cornélio Procópio (~80 km, roughly an hour via BR-369) turns into an arraiá: school classes dance their choreographed quadrilhas, the duo Marcus & Dalto plays sertanejo, and food stands offer corn, popcorn, pastel, hot chocolate and quentão. Entry is free; proceeds from school stands go directly to the schools.[4] Perfect for the 8- and 7-year-olds (fishing games, ring toss, dance shows), the 2-year-old gets along well. Practical: open-air, dry, ~22°C — but cool by evening, so bring jackets and a blanket for the baby; the announcement doesn't mention a changing room, so plan a backup (car/blanket).

Pro: free, classic Festa Junina, short drive, all ages, perfect dry weather.
Con: cool evening for the baby, no changing room mentioned, plaza gets crowded — arrive early.

Wed–Sun: Expo Japão 2026 in Londrina

From Wednesday June 3 to Sunday June 7, Expo Japão 2026 runs on the ACEL grounds (Rua Archiles Pimpão Ferreira 2300, Jardim Vale Verde, Londrina; ~110 km, ~1.5 hours). Five days of Japanese culture: taiko drums, dance, pop culture (cosplay, anime), a tea ceremony in the cultural pavilion, and a food plaza with 2,200 seats full of Japanese restaurants and food trucks.[5] Entry R$ 14 (discounted R$ 7 for students and teachers; kids under 3, seniors 65+ and people with disabilities free). For school-age kids a must-see (the stage show and food draw them in), with a baby and toddler keep it short and go early while it's less crowded and brighter. Worth it if you're heading to Londrina anyway — as a standalone trip for the 1.5-hour drive with four kids, better to save it for a weekend day, not evening.

Health & Safety

Heads up — Baby Upper-respiratory-virus season is climbing in the South. The latest InfoGripe report from Fiocruz (Epidemiological Week 20/2026, May 17–23, published May 28) reports rising SRAG cases across all age groups; RSV accounts for roughly 47.6% of the latest virus detections and is currently the leading cause of respiratory hospitalization in young children. Influenza-A hospitalizations are also explicitly rising in Paraná.[6] With a one-month-old baby, this means concretely: avoid close contact with anyone sick, wash hands before touching the baby, weigh whether large crowds are wise — and if there's labored breathing, rapid breathing, or poor feeding, don't hesitate to head to urgent care.
RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus): a cold virus that's mostly harmless for adults — but in infants it can block the tiny airways (bronchiolitis). Think of a baby's airway as thin as a straw; a little mucus makes breathing hard. That's why so many tiny babies end up in the hospital in winter with it.
Context The good news first: in early May, Fiocruz did not classify Paraná as a state with alarm-level SRAG incidence (InfoGripe SE 17/2026) — the rise is real, but PR was below the alarm threshold then. The national trend, though, has been up for six straight weeks, so no all-clear yet. Watch it, don't hide. jmonline.com.br — InfoGripe SE 20

Dengue: the quietest season in years

Real relief: in Paraná, confirmed dengue cases in the first weeks of 2026 have crashed by 96.1% — 2,028 instead of 52,193 cases in the same period in 2025, deaths down from 90 to 1 (−98.8%), measured across epidemiological weeks 1–14/2026.[7] The latest Epidemiological Report (no. 17, May 26, 2026) confirms the quiet trend. Most cases still lie in the North/Northwest (17ª Regional Health Office, Londrina, leading), one death was reported in Andirá in Norte Pioneiro. Despite the good numbers: clearing standing water stays essential — the mosquito doesn't vanish just because the stats look good.

Longer Trips & Reminders

São Paulo range: Arraiá do Ibira (Ibirapuera)

If you're planning a long weekend or a bridge day: at Parque Ibirapuera the Arraiá do Ibira runs six days — June 13, 14, 20, 21, 27 and 28 — as a major outdoor arraiá with traditional food, live music, quadrilhas and a kids' program. In Parque Villa-Lobos there's concurrently the Arraiá do Família no Parque through most of June, with slides, a little train, fishing games and a kids' traditional-dress parade.[8] Time: a full day (4–6 hours driving each way), rough family budget ~R$ 150–300 plus gas. Only worthwhile as a planned weekend or holiday trip — for an afternoon, the Cornélio arraiá is the smarter choice.

Norte Pioneiro: Moto Fest in Santo Antônio da Platina

Late in the month, June 26–28, the 1º Moto Fest Norte Pioneiro runs in Santo Antônio da Platina (~70 km). More suited to older kids and motorcycle fans than a toddler event, but flagged as a regional program point — details come closer to the date.[9]

School & Education

Winter break nearing: July 13–24

The state PR school year 2026 has its July recess from July 13 to 24 (Resolution 6.494/2025; teaching ends December 18).[10] That means: roughly six more regular school weeks for the 8- and 7-year-olds, then two weeks off. Good time to rough out a bigger vacation trip (e.g., one of the São Paulo arraiás or a Curitiba day trip) now while the festivals are running.
