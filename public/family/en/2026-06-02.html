Weekend Plans — the Big Arraiá Saturday

Sat: Festa Junina Cornélio Procópio (our pick)

Saturday, June 6, from 4 p.m., Praça Botafogo in Cornélio Procópio (~80 km, just over an hour via BR-369) turns into an arraiá: school classes dance rehearsed Quadrilhas (the traditional paired dances in plaid shirts and braids), plus country music, and the food stalls serve corn, popcorn, pastel, hot chocolate, and mulled drink. Entry free; proceeds from school food stalls go straight to the schools.[4] Perfect for the 8- and 7-year-olds (fishing games, ring toss, dance show), the 2-year-old does well. Practical: open-air, dry, ~22 °C — but chilly in the evening, so bring jackets and a blanket for the baby; no changing station mentioned in the announcement, so have a backup plan.

Pros: free, classic-junino, short drive, all ages, perfect dry weather.
Cons: cold in evening for baby, no changing station listed, plaza gets crowded — arrive early.

Sat: Paraná Junino at SESC Londrina — the more kid-friendly option

That same Saturday, June 6, the statewide Paraná Junino runs at SESC across eight cities — nearest is SESC Londrina Norte (Av. Saul Elkind 1555), 12 to 8 p.m., free entry.[5] The big plus with little kids: a dedicated play block — fishing games, clown mouth, ring toss, three-legged race, ring-throwing — plus Quadrilhas, forró music, and a food fair with mulled drink, corn, manioc cake, cornmeal snack, and cornmeal cake.[6] Because it starts at noon, you can time it for daylight hours instead of the chilly evening — easier on the baby. The downside: Londrina at ~110 km is the longer drive. Rule of thumb: if the expanded kids' play block matters, Londrina pays off; if it's about shortest distance, Cornélio stays the smarter bet.

Health & Safety

Heads up — Baby Respiratory virus season is running nationwide. The latest available Fiocruz InfoGripe snapshot (most recent regional through Epidemiological Week 20/2026, May 17–23) reports rising SRAG cases across all age groups; RSV currently accounts for roughly 38–48% of virus detections and is the leading reason for respiratory hospitalizations in young children. With a one-month-old baby, that means concretely: avoid close contact with anyone sick, wash hands before touching the baby, weigh large gatherings — and if the baby shows shortness of breath, fast breathing, or poor feeding, don't hesitate to head to the ER.
RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus): a common cold virus that's usually harmless in adults — but in babies it can clog the tiny airways (bronchiolitis). Think of a baby's airway like a straw; even a bit of mucus makes breathing hard. That's why so many tiny children end up in the hospital during winter with it.
Context Important update: in Bulletin SE 17/2026, Fiocruz counted Paraná not among the states with alert-level SRAG incidence ("with the exception of Paraná and São Paulo"). In the latest update SE 20/2026 (May 28), that exception has fallen away: Paraná now ranks among states with rising alert/risk incidence, and the statewide trend has climbed for several consecutive weeks (RSV and influenza A). No alarm, but heightened caution now — with the 1-month-old, avoid close contact with anyone sick and monitor carefully. agencia.fiocruz.br · jmonline.com.br — SE 20

Dengue: the quietest season in years

Real relief: Confirmed dengue cases in Paraná have crashed 96.1% in the first weeks of 2026 — 2,028 instead of 52,193 cases in the same stretch of 2025, deaths dropping from 90 to 1 (−98.8%), measured across epidemiological weeks 1–14/2026.[8] Most cases still cluster in the north/northwest (Londrina's 17ª RS region leading with 534 cases). Despite strong numbers, the health department keeps warning: drain standing water remains a must — the mosquito doesn't vanish just because the stats look good.
