Today & the long weekend

High pressure: dry, sunny, cold nights

Brazil is in the grip of winter, and a stable high-pressure system is parked over Norte Pioneiro. Today 22°/13°, then through Saturday more of the same each day: plenty of sun, morning fog, daytime highs 22–24 °C, nighttime lows a crisp 11–14 °C—and a solid 0% rain all week. Climatempo and HG Brasil agree; showers don't return until Monday (6/8) with a slight chance (~20%).[1][2] For outdoor time: sunscreen during the day, but bundle the little ones up mornings and evenings—nights are cool.

Current: 06/03/2026 morning BRT · climatempo.com.br · hgbrasil.com

Corpus Christi: bridge-day logic for the weekend

Thursday, June 4, is Corpus Christi. In Paraná state, it's a ponto facultativo (bridge day—government offices closed, not a legal holiday), and in Curitiba and many municipalities it's a full holiday.[3] In practice, many families add Friday too (emenda) and get four days off in a row. If you're planning a bigger outing with all four kids, this weekend is your window—the SP area Festa Junina option below only makes sense with that kind of time block.

Weekend plans

1 · Expo Japão Londrina—indoors, varied, baby-friendly

Five days of Japanese culture on the ACEL grounds in Londrina (today through Sunday). Taiko drums, Bon-Odori and Matsuri dances, kimono workshops, daily tea ceremony (3–7 p.m.), and martial arts demos in karate, kendo, and kung-fu. School kids get intro classes in baseball, table tennis, and archery; everyone enjoys the massive food-court hall with 2,000 seats.[4] Entry R$ 14 (reduced R$ 7), children under 3 free. The big win with a baby: covered and full of seating.

Pros: covered (weatherproof), tons of seating for nursing, wide age range, affordable.
Concerns: ~110 km drive; drums/crowds loud for baby—protect ears, find a quiet corner in the food hall.

2 · Paraná Junino at SESC—free & very family-friendly

Saturday from noon at SESC Londrina Norte: a full arraiá with fishing game (pescaria), clown mouth, can toss, sack race, ring toss—all staffed, plus participatory quadrilha dancing, regional food, and Banda Alma Serrana in the evening.[5] Free admission. This is the perfect time slot for your 8-year-old and 7-year-old; your 2-year-old will love the fishing game and dancing too. Daytime is great for the baby too—just secure a shaded spot.

If you stretch Corpus Christi into a 4-day block: the greater São Paulo area (4–6 hours' drive) has free Festa Junina arraiás in parks—like Arraiá do Família no Parque in Parque Villa-Lobos with quadrilha performances and kid activities, free entry.[6] Worth it if: you're heading SP-way anyway and plan a full day outdoors. Health & Safety [7][8]

Heads up — relevant to baby Respiratory season in the Brazilian winter has begun. According to Fiocruz InfoGripe (bulletin week 20/2026, data through 5/23/2026), severe respiratory cases (SRAG) are rising nationwide in both long and short-term trends—and Paraná ranks among 20 states with sustained increases, at alert to high-risk level. Drivers in the South: mainly RSV and influenza A.
RSV (respiratory syncytial virus): a cold virus that's usually mild in adults—but can be dangerous for infants because their tiny airways clog easily. Think of a straw: when an adult gets a cold, the swelling barely matters. When a baby gets it, that swelling blocks most of the air.

RSV accounts for roughly 48% of all positive respiratory samples in the past four weeks and is rising in every Southern state; it's the top driver for children under 4.[7] With a 1-month-old and a 2-year-old in the house, this is the most important health news of the week.

Practical steps: For the baby, follow the simplest rule—keep visitors with cold symptoms away, wash hands before holding, avoid crowds and indoor spaces full of coughing people, have older siblings wash up after school/events before they touch the baby. If the infant shows rapid breathing, chest retractions, trouble feeding, or fever, don't wait—call the doctor right away.

Fiocruz InfoGripe, Summary week 20/2026 · agencia.fiocruz.br
Good news — Dengue Dengue is not a concern right now: 2026 shows Paraná with roughly 96% fewer cases than 2025 (April: ~2,028 vs. 52,193)—typical for dry winter, when the Aedes mosquito is barely active.[8] A note on context: High regional dengue numbers circulating online for "Jacarezinho" (e.g., 6,655 cases) are from the 2025 season—not 2026. Don't mix them up.

Region & Culture

Festa Junina season is open

June is festa junina high season. The regional "Rota do Arraiá"—hundreds of parish, community, and school arraiás—has been running since late May and goes through end of July.[9] In the core Norte Pioneiro region, specific dates usually pop up last-minute on parish and school social-media channels; Ribeirão Claro's municipal calendar doesn't list firm dates yet.[10] Translation: check the community WhatsApp and Instagram groups this week—an arraiá in your own town is by far the most kid-friendly (and cheapest) option with zero drive time.
