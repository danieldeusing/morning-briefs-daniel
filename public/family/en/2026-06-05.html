1 · Festa Junina Weekend in Londrina — the season has arrived

This weekend, Londrina hosts the first major Arraiá cluster of the season: multiple parish and community festivals simultaneously — Paróquia Nossa Senhora da Glória (R. Togo 60), São Lourenço (R. Augusto Galo 88), Arraiá da Auxiliadora (R. Pros: kid-friendly, affordable, many options — one nearby is almost guaranteed. Perfect for outdoor fun in this dry weather.

Health & Safety [7][8]

Heads up — relevant for baby Respiratory season is climbing further. The fresh Fiocruz InfoGripe bulletin (summary week 21/2026, published June 3, 2026) reports ongoing increases in severe respiratory cases (SRAG) nationwide — 18 states trending upward, Paraná among them. RSV is the main driver in the south, along with influenza A.
RSV (respiratory syncytial virus): a cold virus that is usually harmless in adults — but can become dangerous for infants because their tiny airways clog easily. Imagine a straw that swells when you have a runny nose: barely noticeable for an older child, but nearly blocks the baby's airflow.

Over the past four weeks, RSV accounts for roughly 48.5% of all positive respiratory samples (influenza A 21.9%, rhinovirus 24.3%), and according to Fiocruz, SRAG incidence is "highest in young children and especially associated with this virus".[7] With a 1-month-old and a 2-year-old in the house, this is the week's most important health alert — especially now during the Festa weekend with many people around.

Practical steps: For baby, the simplest protection rule applies — keep away visitors with cold symptoms, wash hands before holding baby, avoid crowds and crowded indoor spaces with lots of coughing people; have older siblings wash hands after festivals and school before touching the baby. If you go to a festival: don't bring baby into the crush, keep baby in a carrier at the edge in a quiet spot. If you notice rapid breathing, chest retractions, weak feeding, or fever in your infant, don't wait — see a doctor right away.

Fiocruz InfoGripe, summary week 21/2026 · fiocruz.br
All clear — Dengue Dengue remains not an issue right now: in the dry winter, the Aedes mosquito is barely active, Paraná has recorded far fewer cases in 2026 than in 2025, and there is no current dengue alert in the region.[8] Note for context: High dengue numbers circulating online for Norte-Pioneiro cities usually come from the 2025 season — don't confuse them with 2026.

June is festa junina peak season, and this weekend is the first major push. Parish, community, and school festivals run through the end of July, with the next busy weekend on June 13–14.[3] In the Norte-Pioneiro core, specific local dates usually pop up on social media only at the last minute — the city of Ribeirão Claro's calendar doesn't yet list fixed festival dates. Bottom line: this week, check the WhatsApp and Instagram groups of your local community; a festival right in your town is by far the most kid-friendly and cheapest option, with no long drive.

The highlight is the Festa Junina season, which really gets rolling this weekend. In Londrina, multiple parish festivals run at the same time — with Quadrilha dancing, fishing games for the kids, and traditional food like pamonha and green corn. Admission is usually free; you just pay for the food.

The best option for the family is Paraná Junino at SESC Londrina Norte, Saturday from noon through the evening, completely free. There are supervised game booths, sack racing, ring toss, Quadrilha to join in, and live music in the evening. Perfect for your eight-year-old and seven-year-old, and your two-year-old will love the fishing game. During the daytime in this sunshine, it's also doable with the baby — just find a shaded spot and a quiet corner.

If you make a long weekend of it and are already heading to São Paulo, you'll find two big free festivals there: São João de São Paulo in Parque Villa-Lobos with Turma da Mônica and Quadrilha, and the quieter Arraiá do Burle Marx in the green park — that one is nicer with a baby and toddler. But with the long drive, it only makes sense if you're already in that area.

The most important health news this week concerns the baby. The new Fiocruz InfoGripe bulletin for week twenty-one continues to report rising severe respiratory cases — eighteen states showing increases, Paraná among them. The main culprit is RSV, which accounts for nearly half of all positive samples. For adults it's usually just a cold, but for a one-month-old infant it can become dangerous because tiny airways clog easily. So especially at the festival with lots of people around: don't take the baby into the crowds; wash your hands before holding the baby; keep away visitors with a cold; and if you notice rapid breathing or feeding troubles, see a doctor right away.
