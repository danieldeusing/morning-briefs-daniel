Health & Safety [7][8]

Heads up — relevant for the baby Respiratory season is rising. The current Fiocruz InfoGripe bulletin (week 21/2026 summary, published 06/03/2026 — the latest so far) reports continued increase in severe respiratory cases (SRAG) nationwide — 18 states with upward trend, Paraná among them. The main driver in the south is RSV, plus Influenza A.
RSV (respiratory syncytial virus): a cold virus that's usually harmless in adults — but can turn dangerous for infants because their tiny airways clog quickly. Imagine a straw swelling during a cold: barely noticeable on a grown-up, but almost blocks an infant's air.

In the past four weeks RSV accounts for roughly 48.5% of all positive respiratory samples (rhinovirus 24.3%, Influenza A 21.9%), and the increase is driven mainly by RSV-related hospital admissions, worst in small children, per Fiocruz.[7] With a 1-month-old and a 2-year-old in the house, this is the top health info of the week — especially now for the festa weekend with crowds.

Practical: For the baby the simplest rule applies — keep visitors with cold symptoms away, wash hands before holding, avoid dense crowds and cramped indoor spaces with lots of coughing, have older siblings wash hands after parties/school before touching the baby. If you go to the festa: better the baby stays out of the crush, in a carrier at the edge in a quiet corner. If the infant shows fast breathing, rib retractions, difficulty feeding, or fever, don't wait — see a doctor right away.

The Corpus Christi holiday on Thursday (6/4) and the bridge day Friday many schools took are behind us. From Monday (6/8) onward, school runs normally in Paraná. So the weekend is free for family and festa — and the next regular week starts with no holiday break.

Festa Junina peak season is running — and what comes next

June is festa junina peak season; this weekend is the first big push, the next busy weekend comes 6/13–6/14.[4] Parish, community, and school arraiás run through end of July. Beyond the festa weekend, June has one date for older car fans: Armageddon Londrina (NOPREP drag race at Autódromo Ayrton Senna, 6/19–6/20) — a loud motorsport spectacle with Brazil's, Argentina's, and Paraguay's fastest cars, better for older school kids than toddlers. For a day-trip in the core area, weather stays reliably dry the next few days — a good time for the playground or park too.
