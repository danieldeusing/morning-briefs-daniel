Region & culture

Festa Junina season is underway. All of Paraná celebrates São João in June — quadrilha dancing, fishing games, corn cakes. The big Paraná Junino (SESC + RPC) was on Sat 06.06. in eight cities, including SESC Londrina Norte (free) — that edition has wrapped, but smaller town and school festas run all month. Worth asking locally in Ribeirão Claro/Jacarezinho about church and community celebrations (often advertised only via posters and Instagram).

FesCafé draws near. Home game — Ribeirão Claro's big coffee, culture, and amusement park fair (~50,000 visitors) — traditionally comes early July. The 2025 edition ran July 3–6; the official 2026 date (28th) hasn't been confirmed yet. Already mark it down as a family weekend at your doorstep.

Health & safety

No acute regional alert confirmed in today's research. Two grounded tips for Brazilian winter:

RSV/flu watch — because of the 1-month-old baby. June is peak season for respiratory viruses. RSV is a cold virus that can narrow a baby's tiny airways — like a straw getting too thin. For a one-month-old, it's a real risk. In practice: wash hands before holding the baby, keep sick people away, avoid large crowds with the infant. German-Brazilian notes

Word of the week for family Portuguese-Brazilian around Festa season: "arraiá" (colloquial for arraial) — the Festa Junina celebration with bonfires, paper streamers, and quadrilha dancing. When kids bring home "vamos no arraiá?" from school, that's what they mean. To go with it: pescaria (fishing-game booth) and quentão (hot spiced drink — the grown-up version).
