Close by: FILO festival in Londrina (from Fri 6/12)

The FILO — Festival Internacional de Londrina is one of Brazil's largest theater festivals, running this year from June 12 to 28. For families, the highlight is a dedicated "theater for childhood" program — plus free open-air performances at public spaces (Calçadão, Zerão, Concha Acústica). Worth the drive? Festa Junina in Greater São Paulo

Planning a weekend getaway: SP is currently packed with free arraiás. São João de São Paulo in the sprawling Parque Villa-Lobos runs every weekend through June 21 (free, pre-book your ticket). On the weekend of June 20–21, the Memorial da América Latina (11am–9pm, pet-friendly) and the Arraiá do Parque in Parque Burle Marx (10am–7pm, also June 27–28) join in — both free.[5]

Makes sense if you're planning a longer SP weekend anyway: 4–6 hours driving with four kids justifies a park-plus-arraiá combo more than a single arraiá visit. The parks offer space, shade, and room to run — the morning slot (Burle Marx from 10am) is most relaxed with a baby.

Health & safety

Dengue in Paraná: staying very quiet

Good news from the latest state bulletin. The Informe Epidemiológico nº 18 from SESA (as of June 2, 2026, epidemiological weeks 1–21/2026) counts 4,742 confirmed dengue cases statewide and 2 deaths — a drop of roughly 94% compared to the same period in 2025.[7]

Dengue: a virus spread by the Aedes aegypti mosquito — the same mosquito that breeds in standing water (saucers under pots, old tires, clogged gutters). Fewer mosquitoes = fewer cases; Brazil's dry winter weather right now slows breeding.

Directly relevant to us: our Regional, the 19ª Regional de Saúde Jacarezinho, reports in the same bulletin only a low double-digit number of confirmed cases and no deaths. Chikungunya, Zika, and Oropouche play virtually no role in Norte Pioneiro. Still: the simple routine (no standing water in the yard) remains the best protection.[7]

BR winter: cool, dry nights with a newborn at home

We're in the middle of dry Southern winter — tonight 13°C, coming nights 14–16°C. It's respiratory season (colds, flu, and in infants especially RSV). With a 1-month-old baby, that's not cause for panic, but two practical reflexes are worth it:

RSV: a common respiratory virus. In older kids it's just a runny nose, but in very young babies it can narrow the tiny airways. Protection is unglamorous: wash hands, keep sick visitors away, avoid large crowds with the newborn.

Practical steps: keep baby warm when going out (day/night temperature swings are big), track your older child's school-cough cycle, and for arraiás and Fescafé with the baby, lean toward short visits on the edges rather than standing in the crowd up front.

School & region

Festa Junina fever spreads through schools

June is Festa Junina month in northern Paraná. In the Londrina school network alone, over 100 arraiás are planned through July — with quadrilha dances, traditional food, and family afternoons.[8] Even though that's a big-city number: Norte Pioneiro follows suit, and your children's schools will have their own Festa dates. Worth actively asking the class teacher or school in the coming days for the date, time, and whether parents should help or bring anything — these dates rarely show up in official online calendars.
