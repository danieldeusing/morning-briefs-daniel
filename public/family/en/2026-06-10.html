For a long São Paulo weekend the city is full of free Arraiás in big parks — best with four kids because of space, shade and room to run. São João de São Paulo at Parque Villa-Lobos runs on weekends through June 21 (free, ticket in advance). On the weekend 20./21. June add the Arraiá do Ibira at Parque do Ibirapuera (Gate 10), the Festa Junina at Memorial da América Latina (11am–9pm, pet-friendly) and the Arraiá do Parque at Parque Burle Marx (10am–7pm) — all with free entry.[7]

Makes sense if you're already planning a São Paulo weekend: the 4–6 hour drive justifies the combo of park + festa more than a festa alone. The early slot (Burle Marx from 10am, Ibira mornings) is most relaxed with a toddler.

Health & safety

Heads up — Respiratory The latest InfoGripe bulletin from Fiocruz (dated 21.05.2026, epidemiological week 19) shows flu-A clinic admissions rising in Paraná; Curitiba is among 16 state capitals on "Alert/Risk." Nationwide, RSV (44.5% of positive respiratory samples) is driving severe cases — especially in children under 2 years. With a 1-month-old this is not cause for panic, but the simple precautions matter now.

Respiratory winter ramping up — what it means for baby

A month ago (week 17) Paraná wasn't on the InfoGripe charts; the week-19 bulletin now lists the state explicitly in the rise.[4] It's seasonal expectation — we're in dry southern winter with cool nights (13°C today) and lots of virus circulating — but exactly why the sober look matters.

RSV / VSR: a very common respiratory virus. In older kids and adults often just a cold — but in very small babies it can narrow the fine airways and is the top reason for hospital stays under 2 years. "SARS" is Brazil's term for severe acute respiratory infection, meaning exactly these more serious cases.

Practical and straightforward: wash hands (including older siblings bringing home the cough carousel from school), keep sick visitors away from the newborn, avoid big crowds with the baby — so Arraiás and Fescafé with the infant stay short and at the edge, not front and center. And when you go out, bundle up: the swing between 22°C daytime and 13°C night is big.

Dengue in Paraná: still very calm

The good news holds: dry winter keeps the Aedes mosquito down, and state numbers are way below last year — over –96% confirmed dengue cases 2026 versus the same period 2025 (2,028 confirmed instead of 52,193, –96.1% per SESA).[5]

Dengue: a virus spread by the Aedes aegypti mosquito — the same one that breeds in standing water (saucers, old tires, clogged gutters). Less standing water = fewer mosquitoes = fewer cases.

For us directly: our 19th Regional de Saúde Jacarezinho reports in the latest verified state bulletin (SESA briefing no. 18, dated 02.06.2026) only low case numbers and no deaths. Simple yard routine (no standing water) stays your best defense — especially since Wed–Fri rain will create new puddles that should dry up after the weekend clears.[5]

Festa Junina wave: over 100 festivals in June/July

June is Festa Junina month in northern Paraná. Just in Londrina alone dozens of Arraiás are planned — in schools, parishes and cultural centers, almost all free or low cost; the city culture office counts over 40 festivals for 2026 just in city limits.[6] The Norte Pioneiro region joins in, and our schools will have their own Festa dates. These rarely show up in online calendars — they live on school and parish Instagram channels. It's worth asking the class teacher / school directly over the next few days for dates, times and whether parents need to bring something or help out.
