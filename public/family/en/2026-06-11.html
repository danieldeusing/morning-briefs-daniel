Saturday is your window

The weather pattern this week is clear cut: Thursday and Friday bring steady rain — Climatempo lists 97% rainfall chance Friday in Jacarezinho, theweather.com confirms with 90% and thunderstorms. Then it shifts: Saturday stays nearly dry (Climatempo: 0 mm, ~21° high; theweather: fog, no rain listed), before rain returns Sunday with ~77–90%.[1]

Festa Junina peak season — and it's mostly free

June in Brazil is Arraiá month: Quadrilha (the traditional paired dance in plaid shirt and straw hat), Pescaria (fishing game for kids), Quentão (hot spiced wine for adults), and a bonfire all go together. The good news: nearly everything on the event radar above is free — you only pay for the food.

In Londrina, the culture secretary has announced over 40 festivals for June and July, spread across parishes, schools, and cultural venues.[2] Two dates are locked in for this weekend: the Festa do Padroeiro at the Metropolitan Cathedral (12th/13th) and the Arraiá at Parish N. Sra. da Paz in Vila Recreio (Sat from 5 PM, Sun Prize Show at 3 PM).[3] Recurring but not yet confirmed for 2026 is the Festival Alvorecer Primeira Infância, built for young kids (games, stilt walkers, bonfire, forró) — the date on the event radar comes from the 2025 calendar; check locally.[2]

On-site in the core zone — June runs, but verify dates

Within the 50-km core (Ribeirão Claro, Jacarezinho, Norte Pioneiro), somewhere in June there's an Arraiá almost every weekend — usually at parishes and community festivals. But there's no single, dated online calendar for it; dates mostly show up through the city or the local church.

Honest For this week we couldn't find a single core event with a second source confirming the date — so we're not making one up. Tip: check quickly with the city (ribeiraoclaro.pr.gov.br) and your local parish about the weekend Arraiá.

Health & Safety

Heads up — Respiratory season In the Brazilian winter, severe respiratory infections spike. The latest Fiocruz bulletin (InfoGripe) reports a nationwide rise in SRAG, driven largely by RSV — and children under 2 are hit hardest. Paraná is among states showing an upward trend.
RSV: a cold virus that usually runs mild in adults — like a regular cold. But in babies, it can clog those tiny airways and cause severe breathing trouble (bronchiolitis). Think of a straw that clogs easily with a little mucus: that's what happens in a baby's lungs. So with a 1-month-old and a 2-year-old, it's not a minor detail.

According to the bulletin (Epidemiologic Week 21, released June 3rd), RSV made up 48.5% of positive samples recently; in the South region — including Paraná — RSV-related SRAG cases keep rising.[4] A later reading suggests a strong uptick in the weeks after; the exact number isn't primary-sourced yet, but the upward trend is clear.

Practical for the baby: Avoid crowds at Arraiás or bring the newborn only briefly and close to your body in a carrier. Wash hands before touching, keep sick visitors away.
Watch for: rapid/labored breathing, chest retractions, trouble feeding, bluish lips → see a doctor, don't wait.

Dengue: all-clear for now

Unlike 2025, dengue in Paraná is quiet right now: cases and deaths have dropped over 96% in the first months of 2026.[5] No acute Aedes alert for the Norte Pioneiro — but staying careful with standing water in the yard still makes sense.

The Big Trip: São João in São Paulo

If you're already planning a weekend getaway, this is a good one: across greater São Paulo several big free Arraiás kick off. The Arraiá da Família with Hello Kitty in Parque Villa-Lobos (our weekend pick above) and the Arraiá do Ibirapuera both start Saturday, each with Quadrilhas, live music, traditional food, and kids' activities like slides, a mini train, and fishing games.[6]

The following weekend (20th/21st), the Festa Junina Memorial da América Latina opens — 11 AM to 9 PM, pet-friendly, also free.[6] All three are city-park events: lots of space, shade, easy metro access (Memorial is right at Barra Funda station).

Worth it if …you go overnight. 4–6 hours each way is too much for a day trip with four kids; as a planned São João weekend with a central park as your anchor, very doable — and entry is free.

German-Brazilian

Tomorrow is Dia dos Namorados

Friday, June 12th, is Brazil's Valentine's Day — different from the European February 14th. For German families here, a nice double tradition: plan a quiet evening together when the two older ones (8 and 7) can watch the little ones, or make it a family date — pancakes, cocoa, a film. With Friday's rain anyway, that fits better than any outdoor plan.

Festa Junina explained — for the German ear

If Arraiás are new: Festa Junina is Brazil's mix of harvest festival and village fair, in June around Saints Anthony, John, and Peter. Plaid shirts, straw hats, painted freckles, Quadrilha dancing, and a bonfire — for kids mostly games and sweet corn bread. A perfect, low-barrier entry into local culture, and almost always free.
