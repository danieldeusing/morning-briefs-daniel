São João High Season — and it's mostly free

June is Arraiá month in Brazil: Quadrilha (the traditional pair dance in checked shirt and straw hat), Pescaria (fishing game for the kids), Quentão (hot spiced wine for the adults), and a bonfire are part of it. The good news: nearly everything on the Event Radar above is free — you only pay for food.

In Londrina (~110 km) there are three weekend dates firmly confirmed this time: the Festa do Padroeiro at the Catedral Metropolitana (Fri from 8 am, Sat from 11 am), the Festa do Quentão of Paróquia N. Sra. da Paz in Vila Recreio (our weekend pick) and the Paróquia Santo Antônio in Cafezal 4 with Quadrilha and children's games (Fri 8 pm, Sat 6 pm).[2] A second wave follows next weekend (19–21) with Boa Viagem and Coração de Maria.

Right in the Core — June is running, but check the dates

In the 50-km core (Ribeirão Claro, Jacarezinho, Norte Pioneiro) there's an Arraiá somewhere every June weekend — typically in the parishes and community festivals. A central, dated online agenda for it doesn't exist; dates usually only show up on the City Hall or individual church sites.

Health & Safety

Heads up — Respiratory Season The latest Fiocruz bulletin (InfoGripe, SE 22/2026, published 11.06.) reports continuing rising hospital admissions for RSV and flu. Children under 4 are hardest hit, the driver in them is RSV. Paraná ranks among the eleven states with SRAG values at alert/risk level and upward trend.
VSR / RSV: a cold virus that usually runs mild in adults — like a normal cold. But in babies, it can clog the tiny bronchioles and lead to severe breathing trouble (bronchiolitis). Picture a straw that clogs with just a bit of mucus: that's exactly what happens in a baby's lungs. That's why with the 1-month-old and 2-year-old it's not a minor detail.

In the current bulletin, SRAG (severe acute respiratory infections) is rising nationwide, driven in the Northeast, Southeast and South regions — including Paraná — mainly by RSV.[3] Across the country, 2026 so far counts 82,544 SRAG cases and 3,591 deaths.[3] For your family the message is simple: be extra careful with the baby and toddler, especially in crowds.

Practical for the baby: Avoid crowds at the Arraiá or take the newborn only briefly and close to your body in a carrier. Hand hygiene before handling, keep sick visitors away.
Warning signs: fast/labored breathing, rib retractions, poor feeding, bluish lips → check with a doctor, don't wait.

Dengue: still all clear

Unlike 2025, the dengue situation in Paraná is calm right now: cases and deaths fell over 96 % in the first months of 2026.[4] No acute Aedes alert for the Norte Pioneiro — the usual care with standing water in the yard still makes sense, especially after today's rain.

The Big Trip: São João in São Paulo

If you're planning a weekend trip anyway, this is a good one: across the São Paulo area a whole wave of free Arraiás is opening. The Arraiá do Ibirapuera runs from Saturday (through 28.06) with Quadrilhas, Shows, and kids' activities; plus the Arraiá dos Jardins (plaza in Jd. Paulistano, 1–10 pm), the Festa Junina CERET in the East Zone, and the Festa Junina at Centro Cultural Fiesp right on Paulista Avenue.[5] All free, all easy to reach by Metro.

For the smallest ones there's even something tailor-made: the Arraiá Pequenininho by Orquestra Modesta at Sesc Bom Retiro (Sun 14.06, 4 pm) — a gentle, kid-friendly version, perfect for the 2-year-old and the baby.[5] The next weekend (20./21.) brings the Festa Junina do Memorial da América Latina — 11 am to 9 pm, pet-friendly, right by the Metro Barra Funda.

Worth it if …you stay overnight. 4–6 hours each way is too much for a day trip with four kids; as a planned São João weekend with a central park as the anchor, though, it's very doable — and admission costs nothing.

German-Brazilian

Today is Dia dos Namorados

Friday, June 12, is Brazil's Valentine's Day — different from the European February 14. For German families here a nice double tradition: plan a quiet evening for two if the two older ones (8 and 7) can watch the little ones for a bit, or turn it into a family date — pancakes, cocoa, a movie. With all this rain today, it fits better than any outdoor plan.

Festa Junina explained — for a German ear

If the Arraiás are new: Festa Junina is Brazil's blend of harvest festival and village fair, in June around the saints Anthony, John, and Peter. Checked shirts, straw hats, Quadrilha dance, and a bonfire — for the kids mostly games and sweet corn cakes. A low-barrier entrance to local culture, almost always free — and exactly right for the two schoolkids to pick up words like "quadrilha" and "pescaria" right in the original.
