Health & Safety — the baby-protection point

Heads up Paraná at SRAG alarm level (InfoGripe SE 22/2026, published June 11). The state is one of eleven showing in the past two weeks an incidence at "alarm, risk, or high-risk level with rising long-term trend." The driver is RSV, which causes the most SRAG hospitalizations in children under four years. With a one-month-old infant in the house, that's this week's most relevant number. [1]
SRAG & RSV: SRAG (síndrome respiratória aguda grave) is bureaucratic-speak for "severe respiratory infection requiring hospitalization." The main trigger in the Brazilian winter is RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus). Think of it as a particularly stubborn cold: harmless in older kids and adults, but in an infant, the tiny airways can swell shut because they're barely thicker than a straw. That's why the smallest babies end up in the hospital.

What that means in practice

No cause for panic, but reason to tighten the routine while the wave runs. Concretely for a household with a newborn:

Protects the baby
  • Wash hands before anyone touches the baby — visitors included.
  • Anyone coughing/sniffling keeps distance or wears a mask around the infant.
  • Take sibling daycare/school sniffles seriously: don't go near the crib.
  • Avoid crowded spaces and crowds with the infant (FesCafé, celebrations).
  • Breastfeeding continues — passes antibody protection along.
Warning signs → doctor
  • Fast, labored breathing; ribs visibly drawing in.
  • Noticeably less feeding / fewer wet diapers.
  • Bluish lips, fever in infant under 3 months.
  • Lethargy, hard to wake.

InfoGripe recommends: eligible groups vaccinate against influenza and RSV to prevent severe illness. Ask your pediatrician / health clinic about protection options for the baby (Nirsevimab / monoclonal antibody). [1]

Dengue: far below epidemic threshold

The good news to take home: Paraná's 2026 dengue season is drastically quieter than 2025. Through SE 14/2026 (Jan–March 14), the state had 2,028 confirmed cases versus 52,193 in the same prior-year period (−96.1%), deaths 1 instead of 90 (−98.8%). The Londrina region (17th RS) leads with 534 cases. [2]

It's Tuesday, the sixteenth of June, and today's briefing starts with health instead of an outing — for good reason.

The state of Paraná ranks at alarm level in the nationwide respiratory statistics. That comes from the InfoGripe report for epidemiological week twenty-two, published on the eleventh of June. The driver is RSV — a cold that's harmless in older kids but can put babies under four in the hospital because their airways are so narrow. With a one-month-old daughter in the house, that's this week's most important number. No cause for panic, but wash hands before every contact, avoid sick visitors and crowds, and go straight to the doctor if you see fast, labored breathing.

One bit of good news: dengue is far quieter in Paraná this year than in twenty twenty-five — over ninety-six percent fewer cases. That's the figure for week fourteen; the latest bulletin from the ninth of June is only available as a PDF.

The weekend splits clearly. Saturday will be the wet day with eighty-one percent rain chance and thunderstorms — stay indoors. Sunday opens a dry, sunny window: that's the day to go outside. And month-end brings a cold snap with lows to five degrees and frost risk, so get warm layers ready.

For Ribeirão Claro and Jacarezinho themselves, no local celebration was findable online; it's worth asking the City Hall or the municipality directly.
