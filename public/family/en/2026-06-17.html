Health & Baby Protection

Heads up — Respiratory Season Brazil is at peak winter for severe respiratory infections (SRAG). According to InfoGripe/Fiocruz (as of week 21/2026, May 24–30), 18 states show an upward trend, Paraná among them. Of positive samples, roughly one-third tested positive for RSV — and RSV hits especially children under 2 years old. With a one-month-old baby, this is the week's most relevant concern.
SRAG / RSV: "SRAG" is the Portuguese term for a severe respiratory illness requiring hospitalization — like flu that gets so bad breathing becomes difficult. "RSV" (known as respiratory syncytial virus in English) is one of the most common culprits in very young children: in older kids and adults often just a cold, but in an infant it can narrow the airways. That's exactly why the current wave is more serious for a one-month-old than for the rest of the family.

What the numbers show

InfoGripe reports roughly 77,153 SRAG cases through week 21, about 48% testing positive for respiratory viruses; in positive samples, RSV was about 31.6%, rhinovirus 33.1%, influenza A 25%.[1] In the updated count (week 22, May 31 – June 6), national reports listed 16 to 18 states in growth trend, Paraná still on the list.[2] The official main recommendation stays simple: vaccination and contact hygiene.

fiocruz.br
Baby Protection, Practical Steps
  • Avoid crowded indoor spaces with the infant (including the clown show — weigh the risk).
  • If anyone has a cold or cough: keep distance from the baby, wash hands before picking them up.
  • Older kids after school/festival: wash hands first, then approach the baby.
  • Check the family's flu vaccination status — recommended primary prevention.
Warning Signs in Baby
  • Fast, labored breathing or rib retractions.
  • Poor feeding, fewer wet diapers.
  • Fever in an infant under 3 months → always get medical evaluation.
  • Bluish lips → emergency.

Dengue: currently quiet

The latest published comparison (saude.pr.gov.br, published 15.04.2026, data from weeks 1–14/2026) reports 2,028 confirmed cases and 1 death — down 96.1% and 98.8% from the same 2025 period.[3] A newer bulletin (no. 20, June 16, 2026) has been released, but detailed breakdowns aren't available to me here — so the April figure remains the cited, dated value. Region & Culture

June Festivals — what's behind them

The June Festivals are Brazil's June festival season around saints Anthony, John, and Peter — with bonfires, quadrilha (the traditional group dance), corn dishes, decorative flags, and often costumes in the "caipira" style. The peak is São João on June 24. For kids, it's the year's most family-friendly season: practically every weekend something's happening somewhere.

In the wider radius, the 1st Moto Fest North Pioneer in Santo Antônio da Platina (~70 km) runs June 26–28 on the state's tourism calendar — more for older kids/adults.[8]
