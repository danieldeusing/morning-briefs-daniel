Health & Safety

RSV Alert · Baby Brazil is in the midst of winter respiratory season, and Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) is driving it: it currently accounts for 48.5% of severe respiratory cases in young children (InfoGripe SE 21/2026, Fiocruz). Paraná is one of 18 states with an ongoing rising trend. For a one-month-old baby, this is the most relevant risk this week.
RSV: A common cold virus — for adults and older kids mostly just a runny nose, but dangerous for infants because their airways are tiny. Imagine a straw that clogs up with just a little mucus: in a baby, that same thing can lead to severe breathing trouble (bronchiolitis). That's why the younger they are, the more carefully you need to shield them.

Specifically for the 1-month-old this peak period:

What protects
  • Wash hands before anyone touches the baby.
  • Avoid crowds — festas, FILO & SESC for the older kids, baby stays at a distance or at home.
  • Keep sick contacts (even sniffly older siblings) away from the baby, skip face kisses.
  • Keep breastfeeding — passes along antibodies.
Warning signs → doctor
  • Fast, labored breathing, flaring nostrils, rib retractions.
  • Poor feeding, fewer wet diapers.
  • Bluish lips, fever in a very young infant.

National context: Cases in children under two are rising across all of Brazil, per the Health Ministry ("across all of Brazil," SE 18/2026, Agência Brasil); nationally over 77,000 severe respiratory reports YTD. In Paraná, April saw "on average one new severe respiratory case per hour."

Unknown Whether the RSV antibody Nirsevimab (Beyfortus) is currently available for term babies in Paraná's SUS couldn't be confirmed — nationally it has been mainly recommended for premature infants. Worth a quick question for your pediatrician.
Sources: fiocruz.br (InfoGripe SE 21/2026) · agenciabrasil.ebc.com.br · bemparana.com.br

Dengue: All Clear, But Stay Alert

Good news for this season: dengue in Paraná has dropped dramatically. For SE 1–14/2026, the Health Secretary reports 2,028 confirmed cases and one death — a decline of −96.1% vs. 52,193 cases in the same period 2025, and just 1 instead of 90 deaths. No epidemiological alarm, no reason to worry about the weekend.

Regionally, the 17th health region (Londrina) carries the highest load in the state with 534 cases — manageable, but reason enough to avoid standing water in the yard for the Aedes mosquito (drain saucers, buckets, gutters). The latest report (Bulletin 20 from 6/16) came only as an unreadable binary PDF; the numbers here are the officially confirmed figures from the authorities.

German-Brazilian Tie

The Festa Junina is the perfect bridge for the kids: here in June they celebrate what in Germany would be harvest festival / summer fair — with bonfires, quadrilha dancing, and corn dishes (corn bread, corn pudding, popcorn). A nice chance to practice a few Portuguese words around the party with the older kids: fogueira (bonfire), quentão (hot spiced drink, for adults), bandeirinhas (the colorful pennant strings), pescaria (the fishing kids' game). Daniel can use that right away while you stroll around.
