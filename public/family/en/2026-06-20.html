Health & Safety

Heads up — Infant in house Paraná currently ranks among roughly 14 states with elevated, rising trends in severe respiratory infections (SRAG) — RSV is the most common pathogen right now. With a four-week-old baby, some caution is worth it.

Respiratory wave (SRAG/RSV): why it matters

SRAG & RSV: SRAG is Brazil's term for "severe acute respiratory infection" — a respiratory infection so severe it requires hospital care. RSV is one of the viruses behind it: for older kids and adults usually just a nasty cold, but for babies dangerous because their airways are still tiny — like a straw that clogs with a little mucus.

The national InfoGripe bulletin (epidemic week 23/2026, data from June 7–13) reports rising SRAG numbers again. Nationwide: 89,725 cases since start of year, 44,485 lab-confirmed. Over the last four weeks RSV at 51.4% was the most common detected virus, ahead of rhinovirus (23.9%) and influenza A (19.1%). Fourteen states — including Paraná — are at alarm level with rising trend over six weeks.[9][10]

Key for us: the rising trend applies to the general population; for very young children nationally, the picture is actually slowing per the bulletin. That's no cause for panic, but good reason to take simple protection rules seriously over the coming weeks.[9]

correiobraziliense.com.br
Practical for baby Not a directive, just standard guidance during RSV wave: wash hands before anyone touches baby; keep distance from infant if you have cold symptoms and skip kisses; have older siblings wash up after daycare/school first; seek pediatrician promptly if shortness of breath, fast or wheezy breathing, poor feeding or fever. Ventilate rooms regularly.

Dengue: the good news

Dengue: a viral disease spread only by the Aedes mosquito — the one that breeds in standing water (saucers, tires, clogged gutters). No person-to-person transmission; fewer breeding sites = fewer cases.

Paraná saw dengue cases crash in 2026: −96.1% vs. 2025 (2,028 vs. 52,193 confirmed cases in weeks 1–14) and only 1 death vs. 90. The latest state bulletin is Informe No. 20 from 16.06.2026. No acute alert level then — keep the routine (no standing water on property) anyway, especially in this wet week.[12][13]
