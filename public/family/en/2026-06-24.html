Health first — RSV wave and the baby

Heads up · Respiratory Brazil's winter is RSV peak season, and Paraná is currently in alert according to Fiocruz InfoGripe. With a one-month-old baby in the household, this is the point that matters most today.
RSV (VSR): a cold virus that usually causes only a runny nose in adults — but in very small babies it swells the tiny airways (bronchiolitis). Imagine a straw that swells on the inside: no problem for a big kid, but for a newborn breathing becomes work. That's exactly why the season is serious for infants, harmless for everyone else.

The Fiocruz report InfoGripe (week 22/2026, May 31–June 6, published June 11) lists Paraná among eleven states with severe respiratory infections (SRAG) in alert or high-risk zones with rising trends. In children under four, RSV drives most hospital admissions; influenza B is also rising in ages 5–14 and 15–49. Nationwide, the report counts 3,591 SRAG deaths in 2026 through week 22.[1]

Data note Latest available is week 22 (published June 11) — so data lags by about two weeks. No newer Fiocruz report was available today; once week 23+ is released, it should be added.

Practical tips for a household with an infant:

Protects
  • Keep visits small; anyone with a runny nose or cough stays away.
  • Wash hands before anyone holds the baby.
  • Breastfeeding — passes antibodies to baby.
  • Siblings coming home from daycare/school: wash hands, don't cough on the baby.
Warning signs → doctor
  • Fast, labored breathing; flaring nostrils, chest retractions.
  • Poor feeding, noticeably fewer wet diapers.
  • Bluish lips, fever in a very young infant.

Cold wave & frost — what the polar air means

An intense polar air mass is over Paraná. INMET has activated a cold/frost warning for 135 municipalities, June 23–26. Rain reached Norte Pioneiro Tuesday afternoon/evening; frost with lows near 0 °C is expected Wed–Fri, and cities in the state's south could break their 2026 cold record.[2]

For us in Ribeirão Claro, tomorrow early morning (Thu 25.06) is the week's coldest point around 9 °C — temperatures rise after that. For Norte Pioneiro as reference: at a previous cold event in May, Cornélio Procópio reported 6.4 °C, Guarapuava in the highlands even −2.4 °C.

Heads up · Warm at night For baby and toddler, plenty of layers at night — but no loose blankets over an infant (sleep sack instead of comforter). Never leave a space heater unattended in the nursery; watch ventilation if running a gas/wood stove (CO risk).

Dengue — at an all-time low, especially now

Good news to take with you: Paraná reports a dengue collapse at year-start 2026 of 96.1% compared to the same period 2025 — from 52,193 to 2,028 confirmed cases, one death. The count covers weeks 1–14 (January through March 14).[3]

Season context These 2,028 are a count through mid-March; the current Informe Epidemiológico no. 21 (June 23) is PDF-only and wasn't analyzed — the year total has likely risen since. More important for your gut: dengue is seasonal, and winter is the quietest Aedes time. Acute risk is low — the reflex of dumping standing water (before spring) stays good practice.

Big weekend ahead: 28th FESCAFÉ

Thursday July 2 through Sunday July 5 (Thu 8 AM to Sun 10 PM), the 28th FESCAFÉ turns Ribeirão Claro into a street fair — right on your doorstep, with 50,000 guests expected. For families, there's most to do in the daytime: amusement park, food court, exhibits, and Sunday the Cavalgada plus the children's show "Sonhos e Magia." Evenings feature Sertanejo concerts (including Edson & Hudson, Israel & Rodolffo, Fernando & Sorocaba) and an indoor rodeo.[4]

For older kids: FIML Londrina (July 10–19)

A bit farther, but worth it for school kids: the 46th Festival Internacional de Música de Londrina (~180 km) runs July 10–19. Alongside symphony concerts (including Mônica Salmaso & André Mehmari), there are children's courses: music theory, recorder, children's choir, and "Banda de Garagem." Concert tickets cost R$ 25–70 (2025), much is free; course sign-ups go through fiml.art.br.[5]

This is more of a school-break plan than a spontaneous day trip — but if a kid loves music, a course is a nice holiday activity, and a family concert pairs well with a Londrina day (zoo, mall).
