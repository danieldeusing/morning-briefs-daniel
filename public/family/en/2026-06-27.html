Health & Safety

RSV Alert · Paraná Paraná has been on InfoGripe's list of states with sustained rising severe respiratory cases for six weeks. Nationally, RSV now accounts for 53.1% of positive severe respiratory cases — with the highest burden on infants and young children. With a one-month-old girl in the house, this is the number of the week.[3]
RSV: A respiratory virus that starts like a bad cold — but in babies it can move deep into the lungs and make breathing harder. Think of it like a normal cold that's annoying in adults but serious in a newborn, whose airways are still very narrow.

SRAG (severe acute respiratory infection) has logged 82,544 cases and 3,591 deaths in Brazil this year through epidemiological week 24; influenza A dominates deaths (41.9%, mostly elderly), RSV dominates cases (mostly young children).[3] It's BR winter — the season typically runs May through August.

Practically, calmly, no lockdown: Fresh air outdoors is good. The risk is crowding and tight indoor spaces. For the Festa, concretely:

Do
  • Keep baby in carrier/stroller with distance, not in strangers' arms
  • Wash hands before every contact with baby; siblings too
  • Go during the day, avoid evening/night air (15 °C)
Don't
  • Don't jostle the baby through dense crowds
  • Don't let sick people contact the baby
  • Don't wait if baby has a runny nose/fever — see a pediatrician

Warning signs in baby that warrant emergency care: rapid or labored breathing, retracted ribs, blue lips, refusing feeds. When in doubt, head to the ER or pediatrician.

Dengue: all-clear here

Unlike RSV, the dengue situation in the region is reassuringly low. The 19th Regional Health Unit in Jacarezinho has logged only 73 confirmed cases with 0 deaths year-to-date through week 24 (autochthonous incidence 19.65/100,000). Across Paraná, the state stands at 5,251 confirmed cases — a drop of −94% versus the same window last year.[4]

Autochthonous incidence: Cases acquired locally via mosquito here (not brought in from elsewhere), counted per 100,000 residents. Low means the Aedes cycle is barely running in this region right now.

Dry season and low numbers take pressure off — the standard routine (no standing water in pots, old tires, gutters) remains the cheapest insurance. dengue.pr.gov.br · Notice nº 21, 23.06.2026

It's Saturday, the twenty-seventh of June, in Ribeirão Claro. A dry, clear winter weekend — and two days of Festa Junina waiting in Londrina. A really good family Saturday, with just one caution because of the baby.

The weekend tip: the Quermesse Junina at Santuário Nossa Senhora Aparecida in Londrina, roughly one hundred ten kilometers away. It runs today and tomorrow, and again on July fourth and fifth. Admission is free. There's a small petting farm with ponies, oxen and goats, carousels, a viola caipira orchestra, a raffle, plenty of food stands — and new this year, World Cup matches on the big screen. At the amusement park you pay per ride: six reais today, seven from tomorrow.

The weather is cooperating: no rain Saturday and Sunday, daytime highs of twenty-four to twenty-six degrees, cool fifteen at night. So go during the day, dress the baby in layers, and be back home before sunset.

The biggest health story this week is RSV rolling through Paraná. Nationwide, according to InfoGripe, it now accounts for fifty-three point one percent of severe respiratory cases, and it hits infants hardest. With a one-month-old girl in the house, that's the number of the week. It doesn't mean stay home — it means plan smart: outdoors is good, crowding and tight indoor spaces are the risk. Wash hands before touching the baby, keep the little one in a carrier with distance, and don't let strangers pass her around. If she has trouble breathing, retracted ribs, or won't eat, head straight to the pediatrician.

There is good news: dengue here is very low. The Jacarezinho region has logged only seventy-three confirmed cases this year and not a single death — a drop of ninety-four percent from last year.

Looking ahead: next weekend the JuninFest in Londrina with its own kids zone and a gluten-free stand, then an Arraiá at Concha Acústica on July eleventh — and starting July thirteenth comes the three-week school recess.
