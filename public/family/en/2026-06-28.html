Health & Safety

Heads up — Respiratory season Paraná appears in the InfoGripe Monitor (SE 22/2026, May 31–June 6) among the 11 states with SRAG incidence at alert, risk or high-risk level. Hospital admissions from RSV and flu (A/B) are rising, 3,591 SRAG deaths nationwide so far in 2026. With a 1-month-old baby, this is today's most important line.
SRAG & RSV: SRAG ("Síndrome Respiratória Aguda Grave") is Brazil's umbrella term for severe respiratory infections requiring hospitalization. RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus) is one of the main drivers — for adults usually a harmless cold, but for infants under 6 months the #1 reason for winter ER visits. Winter means everyone indoors, air is dry — and viruses jump easiest then.

What that means in practice — calm, not alarmist:

  • Vaccinate the close circle. InfoGripe cites flu and RSV vaccination for vulnerable groups as the key protection. A baby under 6 months can't protect themselves — protection comes via adults and siblings around them (cocoon strategy).[7]
  • Limit crowd exposure. FesCafé and Festas are great for bigger kids — but don't spend hours with baby in dense crowds on show nights. Brief, daytime, at the edge.
  • Hands & cough etiquette. Wash hands before touching baby; anyone with a cold keeps distance. Sounds basic, but it's winter's most effective lever.

Dengue: still very low — don't let protection slip

Good news: Paraná reports only 2,028 confirmed dengue cases (weeks 1–14/2026) with 1 death — a 96.1% drop vs. the same period in 2025 (52,193 cases). The Londrina region (17th RS) leads with 534 cases, but still low.[8]

Dry winter keeps the Aedes mosquito down; still: empty standing water in yard, watch baby for light, long-sleeve protection. Note: Latest confirmed data is weeks 1–14 (from April 15). It's Sunday, the twenty-eighth of June. Brazil is in the middle of winter, and that means: respiratory season. Today the newsletter opens not with fun, but with health — because there's a one-month-old baby in the house.

The national flu monitor InfoGripe lists Paraná among eleven states where severe respiratory infections are at alert, risk or high-risk level. Hospital admissions for Respiratory Syncytial Virus and flu are rising. For an adult it's often just a cold; for an infant under six months it's the #1 reason for winter emergency care. Protection comes via the close circle: vaccinate adults and siblings, limit crowds with baby, wash hands before touching them.

The good health news: dengue is very low this winter. Paraná counts around two thousand confirmed cases in the first fourteen weeks — nearly ninety-six percent fewer than last year. Mosquito protection in the yard still matters.

And now for the fun: Festa Junina season is in full swing. Today in Londrina is the last day of the Festa at Royal Plaza, until four in the afternoon, free entry. Plus the Quermesse at the sanctuary with an amusement park and a little petting farm — perfect for the two-year-old and school kids.

But the big weekend is next: from Thursday through Sunday the twenty-eighth FesCafé rolls into town — rodeo, Sertanejo shows, and Sunday the kids show "Dreams and Magic". Around fifty thousand visitors are expected. With the baby: brief, daytime, away from the crowds. Admission prices for the shows aren't officially confirmed yet — call ahead before you go.

If you'd rather head to Londrina: Saturday and Sunday the JuninFest from São Vicente runs with its own kids area — bounce house, ball pit, slide, free entry.

Weather stays sunny and mild through Thursday, around twenty-five degrees and dry. Friday a cold front arrives with thirty percent chance of rain and down to eighteen degrees high. Saturday clears up again. There's no official storm warning for the region.

To mark your calendar: Paraná school break starts July thirteenth. And in São Paulo at month's end runs the Kidzhouse Festival with Bluey and Paw Patrol — including nursing area and changing tables if you're planning a bigger break trip.
