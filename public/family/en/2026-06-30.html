Health & Safety

Heads up — baby context Paraná ranks among eleven states in the latest report (InfoGripe SE 22/2026) with severe respiratory hospitalizations at alert/risk level. The clear driver in young children is RSV — nationally, 48.5% of recent positive SRAG samples. With a one-month-old baby in the house, this is the most important line of the week.
RSV: A cold virus that's usually harmless for adults and older kids — like a normal head cold. For infants, though, it's often not: their airways are thin as a straw, and if they swell, breathing gets hard. That's exactly why the tiniest ones end up in the hospital with it during Brazil's winter season (May–August).

RSV season: what protects the infant

"SRAG" (síndrome respiratória aguda grave) is Brazil's term for severe respiratory hospitalizations. The national InfoGripe network reports 3,591 SRAG deaths for 2026 so far and a sustained rise; in children under 4, "mainly RSV" is the driver, per Fiocruz.[1] Paraná is at risk level.[2]

The practical takeaway for a household with a newborn: wash hands before handling the baby. If someone's coughing or has a cold, keep distance or wear a mask near the baby. Explain to the 8- and 7-year-olds why they can't kiss their baby sibling right now. Avoid large crowds with the infant — that rules out Anime Friends with the baby, but not an open-air, cool Festa field. If baby shows rapid breathing, rib retractions, or bluish lips, get to a doctor immediately.

Dengue: quiet, but not gone

Here's good news with numbers: Paraná is far below the severe outbreak year of 2025. In the same period, confirmed cases statewide dropped from 52,193 to 2,028 — a decline of 96.1%, deaths fell from 90 to 1.[3] Over the full year (weeks 01–24/2026), the state bulletin #21 counts 5,251 confirmed cases and 2 deaths so far.

In our region (19ª RS Jacarezinho, which covers Ribeirão Claro) there are 73 confirmed local cases and zero deaths, incidence 19.65 per 100,000 — low.[4] A figure for Ribeirão Claro municipality alone wasn't available in the bulletin; the regional number is the reliable one. Still: empty any standing water around the house after rainy days — the circulating serotype in the region is DENV-2.

Tuesday, June thirtieth. The cyclone that passed over the Norte Pioneiro overnight Monday is through — and starting Wednesday it'll be dry here for seven days straight. But before we talk about the weekend, one thing first, and it's the most important this week.

Respiratory hospitalizations are climbing across Paraná right now, especially in very young children. In kids under four, the main driver is RSV — a cold virus that's harmless for us grown-ups but can get serious for babies because their airways are as thin as a straw. Nationally, according to Fiocruz, nearly half of all positive samples come back for this virus. With a one-month-old baby in the house, that means: wash your hands before touching the little one, if you have a cold keep your distance, and skip the big crowds with the baby. If you see fast breathing or bluish lips, get to a doctor right away.

The good news on weather. Climatempo does have a heavy rain alert through Saturday for southern Brazil, but the main hit is in southern Paraná, not our area. For Jacarezinho and Ribeirão Claro the forecast shows zero percent rain for all seven days from Wednesday on. The weekend will be cool — twelve to twenty degrees — so pack a jacket, especially for the baby.

And now the weekend: it's Festa Junina season. Two free family festivals are happening in Londrina on Saturday and Sunday — the Quermesse at Santuário Nossa Senhora Aparecida with a petting zoo of miniature horse, cattle, and goats, and the JuninFest at São Vicente parish. Both about a hundred ten kilometers away, both free. The petting zoo is the big draw for the kids.

If you're planning a bigger trip over vacation: Parque Villa-Lobos in São Paulo is running Família no Parque through early August with what the organizers say is Latin America's biggest inflatable slide — free admission. And for the eight-year-old anime fan, Anime Friends is July second through fifth at Anhembi — but that's not for the baby, there are too many people in the wrong season.

For dengue, we can breathe easy: Paraná is down nearly ninety-six percent from the bad year before, in our region seventy-three confirmed cases and not a single death. Still dump out standing water after the rains though.
