Today & this weekend — Fescafé

Four days of festival, right at your doorstep

The 28th Fescafé — Ribeirão Claro's culinary and agricultural festival — runs from today (Thu 02.07) through Sunday (05.07) at Centro de Eventos Barão Victor Von Rainer Harbach. This is the rare stroke of luck: the week's highlight happens right in town, no drive needed with four kids. The program is explicitly "for the whole family," according to the announcement: shows, talks, an indoor rodeo, exhibits, a horse parade, a kids' show, carnival rides (parque de diversões), and a big food court.

For concrete family planning: the kids' show "Sonhos e Magia" is scheduled for Sunday — the best day for the 8- and 7-year-olds. The carnival and food court run all four days. Earlier editions drew about 50,000 visitors, so it will be crowded — with the 1-month-old baby, better to go in the morning than on cold evenings.

Weather — dry start, wet weekend

Winter's opening is pleasant: today 28°/14° with 0% rain, sunny with light early morning fog — a perfect first Fescafé day. From Friday, cloud cover increases, and Friday and Saturday each have 40% shower probability, mostly afternoon and evening. Saturday will be noticeably cooler at 22°. Sunday stays dry (23°/13°), but cloudy with some sunny breaks.

Practice: if you want to head out on the weekend, plan activities for the morning — it's drier and warmer. For cool evenings (lows around 13–15°), pack jackets, especially for the baby.

Health & safety

RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus): a respiratory virus that's usually just a cold for adults, but can be serious for infants — like a normal cold that easily affects a baby's small airways. It circulates most in the Brazilian winter (May–August).

We're in the BR winter, the peak season for RSV, flu, and respiratory infections. With a 1-month-old baby in the household, that's the key point this week — especially at big Fescafé crowds. No current dated alert number for Ribeirão Claro/PR was available by press time; this is not an active outbreak alert, but normal seasonal caution: keep the baby away from dense crowds, wash hands before handling, avoid sick visitors.

Baby context At the festival: baby in the morning, not cold evenings, distance from coughing people, dress warmly (nights 13–15°). If fever, rapid breathing, or poor feeding in the infant, seek medical care immediately.
