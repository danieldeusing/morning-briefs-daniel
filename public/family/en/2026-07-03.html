Festival of the week: 31st FETEXAS Jacarezinho [1][2]

July 9–12, free entry, ~12 km from home

The big event on the calendar: from Thursday July 9 through Sunday July 12, the 31st FETEXAS ("Feira e Exposição Agropecuária") runs at the Centro de Eventos on PR-431 in Jacarezinho — practically at our doorstep. Entry is free ("entrada franca"), and besides the agricultural part there's an amusement park, a food court, and big rodeo/concert action.

For kids, the daytime draw is the amusement park; the big sertanejo concerts at night (Leonardo on Saturday 11, Sorriso Maroto for the close on Sunday 12) are rowdy and geared toward adults. Tip: go early while it's light and quieter — with a newborn and toddler, the late loud evening isn't your scene. Check exact attraction times on-site or via official channels.

Health & Safety

Good news: dengue in Paraná down 94% year-over-year

Dengue: a virus spread by the Aedes aegypti mosquito — the same one that breeds in standing water in buckets, tires, and saucers. Fewer breeding sites = fewer mosquitoes = fewer cases. That's exactly what we're seeing now.

According to the latest SESA-PR Epidemiological Report No. 21 (state health secretariat, published June 23, 2026, data from SINAN, weeks 01–24/2026), Paraná reports 5,251 confirmed dengue cases and 2 deaths statewide. In the same period in 2025 there were 87,896 confirmed cases — a decline of −94%. The season is comparatively quiet.

For our two home regions it breaks down like this:

  • 17th Regional Health Londrina: 1,521 confirmed cases, 1 death, incidence 120.79 per 100,000.
  • 19th Regional Health Jacarezinho (ours): 73 confirmed cases, 0 deaths, incidence 19.65 per 100,000 — very low.

Despite the good numbers, basic prevention stays essential, especially with a newborn in the house: empty standing water weekly, cover rainwater barrels, and for young children use repellent only age-appropriately (for babies under 6 months, mosquito netting over topical products).
