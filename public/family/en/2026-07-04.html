It's rare that the weekend highlight sits zero kilometers away: The 28th Festival do Café (Fescafé) has been running since Thursday right in the heart of Ribeirão Claro and goes through Sunday evening at 10 pm.[1] Coffee country celebrates coffee — with vendors, regional music and food. Weekend & next week: FETEXAS in Jacarezinho

31st FETEXAS — rodeo, amusement park, free entry

One fest ends, the next one starts: The 31st Festa Texana (FETEXAS) runs from Thursday July 9 through Sunday July 12 at the Centro de Eventos on PR-431 in Jacarezinho — about 30 km and a good half-hour drive away.[3] The best part first: Free admission ("portões abertos").[4] On offer: rodeo, a parque de diversões (amusement park with rides), food stalls, exhibitions and four evenings of live music — including Leonardo (Sat) and Sorriso Maroto (Sun).[5]

For the family, the amusement park is the draw: for the 8-year-old and 7-year-old the rides, for the 2-year-old the carousels and animals. It falls right in school break — ideal for a relaxed evening without school stress the next morning. Rides usually cost per ticket (Fichas/Token); admission itself stays free.

Health & safety

RSV peak season — matters with a newborn Paraná is in the middle of Brazil's winter peak for respiratory viruses. The national surveillance system InfoGripe rates the state at high risk with sustained upward trend (SRAG alert, as of SE 22–23/2026, end May/early June). The main driver right now is RSV at roughly 48–51% of positive samples.[7][8]
RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus): a cold virus that usually just causes sniffles in adults and older kids — but in babies it can clog tiny airways and cause bronchiolitis. Think of a straw you breathe through: a baby's is already tiny, and RSV makes it swell shut. That's why it matters for our one-month-old.

What that means in practice (calmly, not alarmistically): No need to stay home — but a few simple habits cut the baby's risk significantly:

  • Wash hands before anyone touches the baby — guests included.
  • Manage crowds: at Fescafé/FETEXAS skip the dense clusters, pick the edges or go early — or leave the baby home this time.
  • Keep sick people away from the baby; kissing the face/hands is off-limits while someone has a cold.
  • Older siblings home from daycare/school: wash hands after arriving home before going to the baby.

Note on sources: The primary source (Fiocruz InfoGripe) was not directly reachable; the numbers come from two independent reports (O Tempo, Bem Paraná) citing the same InfoGripe bulletins. A bulletin newer than SE 23–24/2026 was not yet available — the trend (RSV dominant, Paraná rising) is consistent across both sources.

Dengue: the good news

On the bright side, here's a welcome number: Paraná is seeing a dramatic dengue drop this year. In the period SE 1–14/2026 (January through mid-March) there were 2,028 confirmed cases versus 52,193 in the same 2025 period — a drop of 96.1% — and 1 death instead of 90.[9]

Data gap, honestly named There's no fresher week-by-week dengue count right now: Paraná switched to annual reporting in 2025 instead of weekly.
