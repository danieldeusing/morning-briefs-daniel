Health & safety

Baby context Brazilian winter (May–Aug) means peak season for respiratory viruses like RSV and flu. With a 1-month-old infant in the house, that matters twice as much.
RSV: a cold virus that just gives adults the sniffles, but in very young babies can clog up the fine airways — like a straw that's too narrow, making it hard for the child to breathe. That's why it calls for extra care in winter.

In practice, that means this week: keep the baby warm (cool mornings!), avoid crowds in enclosed, poorly ventilated spaces with the infant, wash hands if older kids or visitors have a cold. Outdoors in fresh air, on the other hand, is no problem — and the dry weather is inviting exactly that.

Seasonal outlook, not an alert For the 07/05–07/12 window, there is no dated INMET advisory (cold/frost) in effect. The state weather service SIMEPAR only paints a general winter picture: a cool start to July with cold-air incursions, a warmer second half of the month, La Niña at a ~69% probability (Jul–Sep) — a seasonal outlook, not a day-specific warning.
