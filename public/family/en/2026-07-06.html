Weekend plans — from the core ring to São Paulo

FETEXAS 2026 — 4-day festival, free, ~50 km away

The 31st FETEXAS — billed by its organizers as "the biggest Texas-themed party in Brazil" — runs from Thursday Jul 9 through Sunday Jul 12 at the Centro de Eventos FETEXAS in Jacarezinho, with free admission.[3] The music lineup: Thu Ícaro & Gilmar and Panda, Fri Coleto & Gabriel and Jiraya Uai, Sat Leonardo, Sun closing out with Sorriso Maroto.[4] Family-friendly by day (grounds, food stalls, fairground vibe), evenings get louder and later — with a baby and a 2-year-old, better to go early evening. Plan for cold nights.

Motocross on FETEXAS weekend (Jul 11–12)

On the same grounds this weekend, the Camiloti motocross takes place: Saturday practice from 2:30 pm, Sunday practice at 9:00 am and races from 10:30 am, with over R$ 6,000 in prize money across 17 categories.[5] A bonus spectacle for the school-age kids (8 & 7) if you're heading to FETEXAS anyway — loud, but outdoors. For baby and toddler, better to watch from a distance.

Londrina anchor: SESC holiday program from Jul 13

Fitting the start of the break, SESC Londrina Norte runs its supervised holiday program "Brincando nas Férias" from Jul 13–24 for kids 4–12 — play, movement, creative activities.[6] (~110 km, primary radius.) Health & safety

Baby context Brazilian winter (May–Aug) means peak season for respiratory viruses like RSV and flu. With a 1-month-old in the house that matters double — and nights get colder this week.
RSV: a cold virus that just gives adults a runny nose, but in very young babies can clog the tiny airways — like a too-narrow straw the child struggles to breathe through. That's why winter calls for caution.

In practice this week that means: keep the baby warm (cold mornings and single-digit nights from Wed!), avoid crowds in enclosed, poorly ventilated spaces with the infant, wash hands if older kids or visitors have a cold. Fresh air outside is not a problem — and the dry weather makes it easy.

Dengue risk is low anyway in the dry winter; when in doubt, check the current bulletin at saude.pr.gov.br.

Holiday programs are starting up everywhere to match: SESC (Jul 13–24), the children's library (Jul 12–23), and the kids' play areas at Catuaí Shopping Londrina run through Jul 30. This week's rhythm: lots of outdoor time in the dry window, plus FETEXAS on the weekend and, from Jul 13, the supervised program options in Londrina. The weather is making things easy. Ribeirão Claro is looking at about twenty-four degrees during the day and thirteen at night today, dry and sunny with a few clouds. The week stays dry and mild through Friday, perfect for the park, the playground, and bike rides. From Wednesday, though, nights turn cold, sometimes dipping into the single digits, so bundle up.

The highlight of the week is the thirty-first FETEXAS in Jacarezinho, only about fifty kilometers away. This Texas-themed festival runs from Thursday through Sunday, and admission is free. Leonardo plays Saturday, and Sorriso Maroto closes things out on Sunday. The same weekend also brings a motocross race on the grounds. It's great for the whole family during the day, but gets louder at night — so with the baby and the two-year-old, an early visit makes more sense.

For families wanting more, there are several options. In Londrina, once the break starts on the thirteenth, the supervised SESC program kicks off for four- to twelve-year-olds, and the children's library is running a free holiday program too. As a day trip, Ourinhos offers a free music festival from the fourteenth through the eighteenth. And in greater São Paulo, the Hopi Hari theme park is offering free admission all July for kids up to twelve, with no paying companion required — worth it with two school-age kids if a trip is in the cards anyway.

A word on health: it's Brazilian winter, so peak season for respiratory viruses like RSV and flu. With a one-month-old baby at home, that means keeping her warm, especially through the cold nights, avoiding big crowds in enclosed spaces, and washing hands often. Fresh air outdoors is perfectly fine. On dengue, our last confirmed number is from April: cases in Paraná were down more than ninety-six percent from the year before. A newer bulletin exists, but we couldn't pull its figures today, so there's no current number to report.
