Why so cold in July? Unlike in Germany, July is the depths of winter in Brazil. On the Planalto (highlands), nighttime temperatures drop noticeably, while daytime under clear skies still feels pleasant. Clear plus dry means gorgeous sunny days but genuinely cold mornings — the cold snap on the 13th/14th brings lows around 8°C. For the baby, that means: warm indoors, hat and layers outdoors.

Festa das Cerejeiras — Cherry Blossom Festival, Parque do Carmo

The Japanese-Brazilian cherry blossom festival, paired with the Tanabata star festival, at Parque do Carmo (east SP) is a classic: blooming cherry trees, taiko drumming, Japanese food, all outdoors and free admission. For 2026, sources point to the 46th edition on July 24–26 (plus July 31–August 2); the older July 25–27 / 45th-edition figure is left over from 2025. Still, confirm the date on the official site before planning the long drive. Worth it if you want to spend a whole day outside with the kids.[4]

Works well: free, plenty of space, nature + culture, all ages; stroller-friendly on the park paths.

German-Brazilian Corner

Fitting for the cherry blossom festival: Tanabata (七夕, "star festival") is a Japanese holiday that São Paulo's Japanese-Brazilian community celebrates in a big way — Brazil has the largest population of Japanese descent outside Japan. The lovely tradition for kids is writing a wish on a colorful strip of paper (tanzaku) and hanging it on a bamboo branch. A nice little holiday project for home on a cold afternoon — and a good excuse, along the way, to talk with the kids about the three cultures that come together in this family.
