Health & safety

RSV wave: high-risk in Paraná — infant context The InfoGripe bulletin for Epidemiological Week 23/2026 (06/07–13, published 06/20/2026) explicitly rates Paraná: "tanto o Paraná quanto Curitiba têm alerta de alto risco com tendência de crescimento a longo prazo nos casos" ("both Paraná and Curitiba have a high-risk alert with a long-term growth trend in cases"). And further: "a incidência de SRAG é mais elevada nas crianças pequenas e está associada principalmente ao VSR" ("the incidence of severe acute respiratory syndrome is higher in young children and is mainly associated with RSV"). The more recent bulletin for EW 25/2026 (06/21–27, published 07/05/2026) confirms: RSV remains, nationwide, "o principal responsável pelo elevado número de hospitalizações […] especialmente entre crianças pequenas" ("the main driver of the high number of hospitalizations […] especially among young children"). For a household with a one-month-old infant, this is the week's most relevant figure: no crowds, no fairground with the baby, hand-washing before every contact.
RSV: A cold virus that catches almost every child once before their second birthday. For an adult it's a sniffle; in an infant it can turn into bronchiolitis — an inflammation of the very smallest airways. An adult breathes through a drainpipe, a newborn through a drinking straw: a little mucus barely bothers the pipe but clogs the straw. That's why babies with RSV end up in the hospital while older siblings just cough. Portuguese: VSR; the term is the same virus known worldwide as RSV.

Dengue: the figure making the rounds is from the start of the year

The widely quoted −96.1% cases (52,193 → 2,028) and −98.8% deaths (90 → 1) refer to "a semana epidemiológica de 1 a 14 deste ano, entre janeiro e 14 de março" ("epidemiological weeks 1 through 14 of this year, between January and March 14") — published on 04/15/2026. This is a start-of-year seasonal tally, not a statement about the dengue situation in July. There is no active alert: the health department's (Sesa) last statement on the topic (05/05/2026) was a routine reminder — "a Secretaria de Estado da Saúde (Sesa) reforça o alerta para que a população mantenha os cuidados preventivos" ("the State Health Department reinforces the alert for the population to keep up preventive care") — not an escalation. A more recent, EW-dated July bulletin could not be found today.

German-Brazilian corner

Sertanejo & Festa Texana: A Fetexas is essentially a "Festa Texana" — rural, cowboy/rodeo-style festivals that Paraná and southern Brazil borrowed from US ranch culture: line dancing, cowboy hats, rodeo, and the music genre Sertanejo. It's roughly comparable to a German Schützenfest or Volksfest — local tradition, a stage, a family-run affair — just with a lasso instead of a brass band. Leonardo, Saturday's headliner, is something like Sertanejo's counterpart to a German folk-music superstar: on the charts continuously since the 1990s, known by practically everyone in Brazil.
