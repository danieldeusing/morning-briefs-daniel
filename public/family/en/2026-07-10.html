Health & safety

RSV wave turning — but caution stays (baby context) The good news first: the new nationwide respiratory bulletin from Fiocruz/InfoGripe (epi week 26/2026, data through week 26, published 07/09/2026 via Agência Brasil) reports that SARI cases nationally "seguem em tendência de queda" ("continue trending downward") and explicitly lists Paraná among the states showing "indícios de interrupção do avanço ou início de queda" ("signs the increase is stopping or a decline is beginning"). That's a turnaround from the previous bulletin (epi week 23/2026, publ. 06/20), which still classified Paraná as "alerta de alto risco com tendência de crescimento" ("high-risk alert with a growing trend"). Still, the virus hasn't gone away — over the last four weeks, 55.9% of cases nationwide were RSV, followed by rhinovirus (23.3%), influenza A (12.7%), and influenza B (8.4%); and RSV hits the youngest the hardest. For our household with the one-month-old infant, that means: breathe easier, but don't drop your guard — no fairground with the baby, hand-washing before every contact. Epi week 26/2026, publ. 07/09 · agenciabrasil.ebc.com.br · Epi week 23/2026 · portalanativa.com.br
RSV: A cold virus that almost every child catches at least once before turning two. For adults it's just a sniffle; in an infant it can turn into bronchiolitis — an inflammation of the very smallest airways. Picture an adult breathing through a drainpipe and a newborn breathing through a drinking straw: a bit of mucus barely bothers the pipe, but clogs the straw completely. That's why babies with RSV end up in the hospital while older siblings just cough. Portuguese: VSR; English: RSV — same thing.

German-Brazilian corner

Winter break in July: For a German parent, the calendar flips upside down here. In Germany, the big summer break falls in July/August. In southern Brazil, July is the depth of winter — so the two-week mid-July school break goes by the Portuguese name recesso escolar de julho and lands right in the coldest, (normally) driest season. Instead of the outdoor pool and ice cream, there's a winter coat, geada (frost) alerts, and Festa Junina/Julina season with hot quentão (mulled wine). It's the same "finally free" mood as back home — just with the seasons swapped and a cowboy-style county fair standing in for the swimming pool.
