Health & safety

Cold week — keep the one-month-old baby warm Brazilian winter is biting right now in Paraná: last week (Jul 7–9), parts of the country dropped below freezing (General Carneiro −1.3°, Palmas −0.1°), and INMET had issued a yellow frost alert for around 150 PR towns — but it only applied to July 8/9 and has already expired. For today, theweather.com flags a cold-weather notice for Ribeirão Claro; that's an automatic portal notice, not a confirmed INMET warning with a number — we're not inventing an advisory ID for it. The Norte Pioneiro region usually dodges the harshest frost thanks to fog and heavy cloud cover. What matters at home: morning lows drop into the single digits this week — a newborn cools down fast, so warm layers, a hat at night, and a well-heated sleeping spot. Thermoregulation in infants: A newborn can't hold their body temperature as well as an adult yet. Picture a small thermos next to a big one: the small one cools down much faster because it has proportionally more surface area. That's exactly why a one-month-old needs one more layer than the adults when morning temperatures dip into the single digits — but not overheated either, just matched to the weather.

German-Brazilian corner

Winter break in July: For a German parent, the whole calendar flips here. The big school break in the middle of the year is Brazil's férias de julho — and July, in the Southern Hemisphere, is deep winter, not high summer. So instead of the pool and ice cream, it's a winter jacket, cold mornings, and indoor play. Right into this winter vacation window fall the Festas Julinas (the July edition of the Festa Junina) and cowboy-style fairs like Fetexas: bonfire vibes, quentão (mulled wine) for the grown-ups, pastry stalls — Brazil's cozy winter take on a street fair.

It's Sunday, July twelfth. Two things define the day: the big local street fair, the thirty-first Fetexas in Jacarezinho, wraps up its last day today, and starting tomorrow, two weeks of winter break kick off.

At Fetexas, this morning is the real draw. Motocross practice runs from nine, races from ten-thirty, seventeen categories and over six thousand reais in prize money — the real highlight for the seven- and eight-year-olds. Admission is free, and it's only about a twenty-minute drive. In the evening, the band Sorriso Maroto closes out the fair, but that's late and loud, better suited for the grown-ups. With the baby, it's smarter to stay away from a crowded fairground during peak winter respiratory season.

Weather is today's wildcard. Two sources, Climatempo and theweather dot com, agree on the big picture: a high around twenty-four degrees and seventy to seventy-six percent chance of rain — rain to thunderstorms in the morning, then clearing in the afternoon. But they openly disagree on the low: Climatempo says seventeen degrees, the other source says just twelve. We're not quietly picking one over the other — for the baby, we're planning around the colder scenario. The state's primary weather source, Simepar, unfortunately wasn't reachable today.
