Londrina: FIML music festival through Sunday [4]

The 46th Festival Internacional de Música de Londrina (FIML) runs through 19.07 — making it the highlight of the first break week, though a good 2 hours 40 minutes (~187 km) away. Much of it is free, and several evenings suit families with school-age kids who can sit still.

  • Thu 16.07, 20h30 — free: the Orquestra Sinfônica do Paraná plays Mozart and Beethoven at the Teatro Ouro Verde. A genuine symphony orchestra, free of charge.
  • Fri 17.07, 20h30 — free: a youth orchestra from Toledo, Ohio (USA) — kids get to watch peers on stage.
  • Sat 18.07, 20h30 — from R$ 25: "GATOS — O Musical," a musical adaptation made specifically for children.
  • Sun 19.07, 10h30 — free: closing concert of the Orquestra Brasileira de Projetos Sociais. Given the ~2h40 each way, the morning slot is realistically the only one that works as a day trip with a toddler and baby.

Bigger trip: São Paulo school holidays

If you're already weighing a weekend or long-weekend trip to São Paulo (4–6 h): the school-holiday season there is in full swing. Curated picks for what might make the long drive with four kids worthwhile:

  • Disney "Mickey & Amigos" at Shopping Eldorado (Av. Rebouças 3970), through 04.08, free admission — plus a 1,200-m² bounce park with Turma da Mônica.
  • Zoo sleepover "Meu Teto de Estrelas," 17.–19.07, at the Zoológico de São Paulo — an adventure for the older kids, less so with a baby.
  • Patati Patatá Circus Show (Zona Leste), through 26.07, from R$ 63 — kids up to 1 yr 11 mo free.
  • Kidzhouse Festival, 24.–26.07 — one of the country's biggest kids' events, a good pick for the end of the break.

Worth it if an SP trip is on the cards anyway — otherwise Londrina is enough for a full break weekend.

School & break

Winter break starts today: Paraná's state school network sends a good 1.1 million students home for two weeks — the break runs 13 to 24 July, and classes resume, per the Seed-PR calendar, on Monday, 27 July. In the first half of the year (February–July), 101 school days were completed. [5][6]

Health & safety

Heads up · Baby Brazilian winter means respiratory season. According to Fiocruz-InfoGripe (reporting from May 2026), RSV is currently the most commonly detected respiratory pathogen, and Paraná is among the states with steadily rising severe respiratory cases (SARI). With a one-month-old infant in the house: avoid close contact with anyone who has a cold, wash hands before holding the baby, and steer clear of crowded indoor spaces (concert hall!) with the baby where possible.
RSV: a very common cold virus. In older kids and adults it's usually just a runny nose — in infants it can swell up the tiny airways, like the straw for breathing gets too narrow. That's why it's the most common reason babies under six months end up in the hospital in winter.

Note on data availability: A fresh, primarily confirmed InfoGripe release (with epi-week) wasn't directly retrievable this week — the assessment above comes from Fiocruz's reporting, not a live-pulled bulletin. As everyday caution with a newborn, it still applies.

It's Monday, July thirteenth, and today Paraná's winter break kicks off. Around one point one million schoolchildren now have two weeks off, our four right in the thick of it. Classes don't resume until the twenty-seventh of July — a nice long window for planning.

The start coincides with a cold snap. A cold front has swept across southern Brazil, and for today and tomorrow morning the INMET weather service is warning of frost risk in southern and southwestern Paraná. Here in the Norte Pioneiro it stays milder, with lows around nine to thirteen degrees and no rain. From Wednesday the thermometer climbs back up, toward twenty-five degrees by the weekend. Two portals, climatempo and otempo, agree on that.

Locally it's a quiet week. The big rodeo festival in Jacarezinho wrapped up on Sunday, and we didn't find another family event in the core ring for the coming days. The worthwhile stuff is in Londrina.

Down there, the forty-sixth international music festival runs through Sunday. A lot of it is free: Thursday brings a symphony orchestra playing Mozart and Beethoven, Friday a youth orchestra from the United States, and Sunday morning at ten thirty the closing concert — that morning slot is the easiest one with a toddler and a baby. Saturday evening there's also a children's musical called GATOS, starting at twenty-five reais. The theater itself is indoors and warm. But Londrina is a good two hours and forty minutes away — nearly six hours round trip. An evening concert on the same day, with four kids and the infant, in the cold, just isn't realistic; the day trip that actually works is the free Sunday morning concert, while the evening musical is better paired with an overnight stay.

For anyone weighing a bigger trip: in São Paulo, four to six hours away, the school-holiday season is in full swing — a free Disney exhibit, a zoo sleepover, a circus with Patati Patatá, and the big Kidzhouse Festival at the end of the break. Worth it if a São Paulo trip is on the cards anyway.

On health: Brazilian winter is respiratory season. According to Fiocruz, RSV is circulating heavily right now, and Paraná is among the states with rising severe cases. With a one-month-old baby at home, that means avoiding close contact with anyone who has a cold and skipping crowded indoor spaces with the infant where possible. A freshly confirmed bulletin wasn't available this week, but the caution still stands. On dengue, though, it's all clear — cases are down more than ninety percent from last year, as of April.

Tomorrow is the coldest morning of the week, then the warmth returns — and there's an FIML weekend in Londrina I'd genuinely recommend. Until next time.
