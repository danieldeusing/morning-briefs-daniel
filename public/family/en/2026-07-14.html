Two Free Music Festivals in the Region

Ourinhos (~39 km, ~40 min): Tonight the XXIII Festival de Música de Ourinhos opens — from July 14 through 18, an ensemble plays every evening at 8:30 pm at Teatro Roque Quagliato (Colégio Santo Antônio, Rua Arlindo Luz 800, Centro), free admission. Tonight's opener is Quinteto Fronteiras from the OSESP orchestra; the closing act on Saturday (Jul 18) is the well-known bandolim virtuoso Hamilton de Holanda with his trio. For music students, there are also workshops between July 15 and 17. [3]

Londrina (~187 km, ~2h45): In parallel, the 46th Festival Internacional de Música de Londrina (FIML) runs through Sunday with more than 30 program items across the city. The family highlight is the free concert by the Greater Toledo Youth Orchestra from the USA on Friday (Jul 17) at Teatro Ouro Verde — kids get to see peers on stage. There are also free music circuits in bars and squares throughout the day and evening (jazz, choro, MPB). [4]

Honest about the drive Two very different trips: Ourinhos is only ~39 km / ~40 min away, just across the Rio Paranapanema — an 8:30 pm evening concert is a well doable outing from here, even with the kids, no overnight needed. Londrina, on the other hand, is ~187 km / a good 2h45 each way: a same-night concert there means a late drive home deep into the night with four kids and an infant — realistically only workable as an early plan for the two older kids with one parent, or paired with an overnight stay. The upside on both: indoors, warm, and completely free.

Health & Safety

Weather · Cold snap easing The brief cold snap (roughly Jul 11–13, with frost warnings in southern Paraná) has passed; from Wednesday, highs climb back to 24–27°. On the INMET cold-weather advisory: the official advisory page was unreachable, and two news outlets cite slightly different time windows for the same warning — so here we deliberately stick to the rough framing "cold snap around Jul 11–13, warmer again from Jul 15," with no exact validity times and no advisory code.
Heads up · Baby Brazilian winter means respiratory season (May–August). With a four-week-old infant at home, caution pays off: avoid close contact with anyone with a cold, wash hands before holding the baby, and skip crowded indoor spaces — including a concert hall — with the baby when possible. Note: we weren't able to pull a freshly dated InfoGripe/SRAG bulletin (with epi week) this run — this is general winter-season caution, not a current case count.
RSV: a very common cold virus. In older kids it's usually just a sniffle — in infants it can swell the tiny airways shut, like the breathing straw getting too narrow. That's why it's the most common reason babies under six months end up in the hospital during winter.
Dengue · Data status Dengue activity has been very quiet lately — but watch the timeliness. The only cleanly epi-week-dated Sesa-PR figure comes from epi weeks 1–14/2026 (Jan. through Mar. 14, published Apr. 15, 2026): 2,028 confirmed cases and 1 death, a 96.1% drop from the prior year. That figure is now about 14 weeks old and therefore not the current July number. The latest bulletin (Informe Epidemiológico No. 22, listed Jul. 7, 2026) wouldn't open — we'll pass along the fresh figure once it's accessible.
