Health & safety

SARI & RSV: SARI (SRAG in Portuguese) is Brazil's reporting category for "severe respiratory infection that leads to hospitalization" — it's not a single disease, but a catch-all bucket. Picture an ER that puts every patient struggling to breathe into one basket, then checks in the lab afterward which pathogen was actually in there. RSV is one of those pathogens and the most dangerous for infants, because it clogs their finest airways.

Fiocruz's current InfoGripe bulletin, SE 26/2026 (survey week 06/28–07/04/2026, published 07/09), lists Paraná among the states with SARI at alarm, risk, or high-risk level. Nationwide, RSV was responsible for 55.9% of positive SARI cases over the last four epidemiological weeks — so it isn't flu filling the beds, it's RSV.

The good news in the same report: after five months of increases, the national SARI curve is falling again for the first time. For a household with a one-month-old girl, that changes little in practice — she's too young to be vaccinated herself, and her protection comes from the people around her not bringing the virus home. That's why today's vaccination drive-thru is more than a box to check: flu protection for parents and school-age kids is baby protection. And that's why, for this weekend, the rule is to avoid crowded indoor spaces with her — the good options are outdoors anyway.

The Ourinhos trap

Don't go Search these days for family events in the day-trip ring and you'll reliably get the "XXIII Festival de Música de Ourinhos, July 14 to 18" — Teatro Roque Quagliato, free, closing tonight. But the city hall's article is dated 06/30/2025 and describes the 2025 edition: it calls July 14 a Monday and July 18 a Friday. In 2026, July 14 falls on a Tuesday and July 18 on a Saturday — the weekdays only match 2025. A 2026 edition with these dates is not documented.

This is the classic stale-year snippet, and it's a five-second self-check: does the source name a weekday for the date, and does it match the current year? If not, the hit is old — no matter how current the page looks. That goes for festivals just as much as for health figures.
