Health & Safety

Windstorm advisory for western Paraná — not for us

No advisory is in effect for the Norte Pioneiro — but since Friday, 07/17, 00:00, INMET has had an orange windstorm (vendaval) advisory out (gusts 60–100 km/h) for Oeste, Sudoeste, Noroeste and Centro-Sul do Paraná, valid through today, Sunday 07/19, 11:59 pm. Those are the western, southwestern, northwestern and central-southern mesoregions; Ribeirão Claro and Jacarezinho in the Norte Pioneiro are not named and are therefore not covered. Risks listed inside the advisory area: falling trees, damage to buildings, power outages. Anyone heading toward Cascavel, Toledo or Foz do Iguaçu today should take it seriously. If the Wednesday/Thursday thunderstorm scenario does materialize, things could change for us too — and it would show up on the portal the same day.

Heads up: the dengue figure circulating is years old Search results are currently surfacing the headline "Novo boletim da dengue registra 22,2 mil casos e 11 óbitos no Paraná" prominently — as if it were the current count. It isn't. The underlying report refers to the "período epidemiológico que começou em 30 de julho de 2023" and dates from early 2024. These are 2023/24-season numbers, not 2026. Sharing them spreads a two-year-old figure as if it were today's news.

What the current status actually is — and isn't

The state's official bulletin index lists Informe Epidemiológico No. 22 from 07/07/2026 as its latest edition. The actual case and death counts for that edition sit in a linked PDF that couldn't be opened during this run — so no number appears here. Better a gap than a guessed value; the number will follow once it's documented.

Informe Epidemiológico / epidemiological week: Think of it as the state's weekly health "bank statement" — it tallies how many cases of a disease were reported in a given calendar week. Like a bank statement, it always states up top which week and which year it covers. That's exactly why a dengue figure without a week and year is worthless: dengue runs in seasons, and the count from a record season looks like a catastrophe next to a quiet one — even though it simply comes from a different year.
dengue.pr.gov.br
Even so: three dry days are Aedes time Regardless of any statistic — Aedes aegypti needs standing water to lay eggs, and the larvae take about a week to become full mosquitoes. After the rain starting Thursday, the following Sunday is the right moment for the ten-minute round: plant-pot saucers, gutters, buckets, tires, the dog's water bowl. With a one-month-old in the house, that's the cheapest protection there is — and it costs nothing but ten minutes.

Region & Culture

Why the drive to São Paulo almost pays off this time

The coming weekend is the last of the vacation, and in greater São Paulo two free family events run in parallel. The Festa das Cerejeiras at Parque do Carmo (46th edition, plus the 6th Tanabata Star Festival) runs on two weekends only — Fri–Sun 07/24–26 and 07/31–08/02, closed Monday through Thursday. Fridays 12–5 pm, Sat/Sun 9 am–5 pm; it shuts at 5 pm, so an evening visit isn't an option. Japanese culture, food, performances, plenty of park and shade, free admission. The Festival do Milho at the Memorial da América Latina runs Sat/Sun 07/25–26 from 11 am to 9 pm with a country fair, shows and its own kids' area, also free.

For: Both free admission, both family-friendly, both the same weekend — you could do one on Saturday and the other on Sunday. Parque do Carmo has real lawns and trees, so room to run off energy and shade for the baby.
Against: 4–6 hours of driving each way with four kids, one of them a month old. Without an overnight stay, this isn't a day trip, it's an ordeal — and the Monday after is the first day of school. If no lodging is arranged, Londrina is the smarter choice.

It's Sunday, July nineteenth, and this is the second-to-last free week of school vacation. On Monday the twenty-seventh, school starts back up across all of Paraná. Whatever's supposed to happen this week has to happen now.

Weather first, because it decides everything else. Today, tomorrow and Tuesday are sunny and dry, between twenty-eight and twenty-nine degrees, zero millimeters of rain. Climatempo literally says, for today, sun all day, not a cloud in the sky. Anything meant to happen outdoors belongs in this window.

Starting Wednesday, things get murky, and I mean that deliberately. The two weather portals that were reachable today disagree sharply. Climatempo says Wednesday stays dry and puts Thursday's rain chance at just forty percent. Theweather dot com says eighty percent for Wednesday and ninety percent with thunderstorms for Thursday. I'm not averaging that, I'm giving you both. The only thing they agree on is that Thursday will be the wet, cool day, around twenty-three degrees. Simepar, the state weather service that would normally know best, was unreachable today.

On activities, there's one unwelcome piece of news and one very good one. The unwelcome one first. In the core ring around Ribeirão Claro and Jacarezinho, and even out to Ourinhos in the day-trip radius, I found not a single confirmed family event starting today. Fetexas in Jacarezinho ran from the ninth to the twelfth of July, the music festival in Ourinhos through yesterday. Both over. The ring is empty after the big July calendar wound down, and I'm not inventing anything to fill it.

The good news comes from Londrina, about one hundred ten kilometers away, and the city is packed with vacation programming. My pick for today is a double bill at Londrina Norte Shopping. From eleven a-m to two p-m there's a free vacation day-camp with painting, balloon-art and dance workshops for kids up to ten. Spots go by ticket, handed out about half an hour ahead, so get there early. And in the same building, the Teatro do Clubinho is staging Pinocchio at four and at six p-m, for ten reais a person.

For the weekdays there are more options. The city's children's library on Praça Primeiro de Maio runs a free vacation program every day from Monday to Friday at ten a-m, for kids up to twelve with an adult. Sesc Londrina Norte keeps its Brincando nas Férias program going through Friday, daily at half past one — though the age range and price aren't confirmed anywhere, so you'd need to ask ahead. And at Catuaí Shopping there's the Garden Kids day-camp for sixty-nine reais a day, running through Saturday.

Two things on health. First, there's no severe-weather advisory in effect for our part of the state, the Norte Pioneiro. But Inmet has had an orange windstorm advisory running since Friday, with gusts of sixty to one hundred kilometers per hour, covering the west, the southwest, the northwest and the Centro-Sul of Paraná. It stays valid until eleven fifty-nine tonight. Ribeirão Claro and Jacarezinho are not on that list. Second, and this matters more, a dengue headline is circulating in search results right now about twenty-two thousand two hundred cases and eleven deaths in Paraná. That number is old. It belongs to the twenty twenty-three to twenty-four season, not this year. The state's current report is bulletin number twenty-two from July seventh, and I couldn't open its actual numbers today. So I'm giving you no figure here, rather than a wrong one.

Finally, a look ahead to next weekend. In greater São Paulo, two free family festivals run at the same time — the Festa das Cerejeiras at Parque do Carmo starting Friday, and the corn festival at the Memorial da América Latina on Saturday and Sunday. Both free, both kid-friendly. But let's be honest: four to six hours of driving each way with four kids, one of them an infant, and the first day of school right after on Monday. Without an overnight stay, Londrina is the smarter choice. And one more warning: the Kidzhouse Festival in São Paulo, announced for the twenty-fourth through the twenty-sixth, has officially been postponed — don't plan around it.
