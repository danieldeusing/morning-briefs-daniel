Five Mornings at the Library — Free, Indoors, Starting Today

The Biblioteca Infantil de Londrina is running a daily program for the second week of break: Monday the read-aloud story "O Lobo que Caiu do Livro" followed by a mask workshop, Tuesday kirigami and pop-up cards, Wednesday dance and movement, Thursday letter writing, Friday origami. Health & Safety

Dengue: Londrina at a Three-Year Low

From January 1 through 07/15/2026, Londrina counted 10,299 reports, of which 1,292 confirmed dengue cases, 8,079 ruled out, 928 still under review — and 2 deaths so far this year. For comparison: 2024 had 52 deaths, 2025 had 9. This is the third straight year of decline.[6]

At the state level, the most recent status is "Informe Epidemiológico No. 22" from 07/07/2026. We're naming it here only by number and date, because the case figures behind it weren't retrievable today — inventing a number for it would be worse than naming the gap.[7] Likewise: neither of the two dengue sources carries an epidemiological week number, both are dated to the exact day. We therefore deliberately give only the date and do not attach an SE number.

cobranews.com.br · dengue.pr.gov.br
Relevant for the baby In the most recent available InfoGripe bulletin — epi week 26/2026 (week of 06/28–07/04, published 07/09/2026) — 55.9% of all positive respiratory samples over the past four weeks turned out to be RSV, followed by rhinovirus (23.3%), influenza A (12.7%), influenza B (8.4%) and SARS-CoV-2 (2.2%). Paraná is listed as a state at "alert, risk, or high-risk level," and Curitiba is among nine state capitals with elevated and growing activity — concentrated "mainly in children under two to four years old." With a one-month-old in the house, that means: postpone visits from anyone with a cold, wash hands before handling the baby, and keep in mind the two school-age kids as the most likely route in once school resumes 07/27.[8]
RSV (Portuguese: VSR): a cold virus that in adults and school-age kids usually just causes a runny nose, but in infants can swell the fine bronchial tubes and lead to breathing trouble. Like a straw you normally drink through — until someone pinches it partway shut. That's why the same infection is harmless for the eight-year-old and a hospital risk for the youngest.
SRAG: the Brazilian reporting category for "severe acute respiratory syndrome" — the cases that make it all the way to the hospital. Think of it as the tip of the iceberg: not every runny nose gets counted, only the people who actually get seriously sick. When this number rises, the wave underneath it is already large.

German-Brazilian

To a German ear it stays curious: this is winter break, and the thermometer reads 28 °C today. Linguistically, the difference is cleanly captured in Portuguese: what's happening right now is officially called recesso escolar — a break in the middle of the school year — not férias. Férias are the long holidays in December and January. At school, "quando termina o recesso?" is therefore the more precise question than "quando terminam as férias?".

It's Monday, July twentieth, the second and final week of school break — and it opens with something you can use today.

The children's library in Londrina is running a vacation program every morning at ten, Monday through Friday. Today there's a read-aloud story about a wolf that falls out of a book, followed by a mask-making workshop. Tuesday it's kirigami and pop-up cards, Wednesday dance, Thursday letter writing, Friday origami. All of it is free, at Praça Primeiro de Maio, number one-ten, across from the Concha Acústica. Sign up by phone, weekdays between eight a-m and four p-m. Kids up to twelve need an adult along.

On weather, there's one thing today that matters more than any single number. For Monday and Tuesday, both portals we checked agree: sunny, zero percent chance of rain, twenty-eight and twenty-nine degrees. If you want to do something outside this week, do it today or tomorrow.

Starting Wednesday, though, the sources disagree so sharply that no rain call can be trusted. Climatempo puts Wednesday at forty percent, theweather dot com says eighty. For Thursday it's forty-four against ninety. For Friday and Saturday, zero against ninety and eighty percent respectively. We're deliberately not averaging that, and we're not quietly picking one number either. If you're planning anything weather-dependent for the back half of the week, you need an indoor plan B. The state weather service SIMEPAR, normally the most accurate source for Paraná, was unreachable today — which is why we're relying on this second tier of portals. The one thing that is certain is the cooldown: from twenty-nine degrees on Tuesday down to twenty-one by Sunday.

The recesso ends Friday, and on Monday, July twenty-seventh, classes resume across Paraná for roughly one point one million students. The first semester ran one hundred and one school days; the second adds one hundred more.

On health, there's one good story and one to watch. The good one: dengue in Londrina is at its lowest level in years. Through July fifteenth, the city counts twelve hundred ninety-two confirmed cases and two deaths. Two years ago that was fifty-two deaths, last year nine. This is the third year in a row of decline.

The one to watch concerns the youngest one in the house. In the most recent available InfoGripe bulletin, epidemiological week twenty-six, published on the ninth of July, fifty-five point nine percent of all positive respiratory samples were RSV. That's a cold virus that barely registers in adults but can swell an infant's fine bronchial tubes. Paraná is listed as a state at alert level, and cases are concentrated mainly in children under two to four years old. For a one-month-old baby, that means in practice: put off visits from anyone with a cold, wash hands, and once school starts back up, keep in mind the older siblings as the most likely route of transmission.

For a bigger-radius outing, the Hopi Arraiá festival at Hopi Hari in the São Paulo area is still running, extended through the twenty-seventh of July, and the Wet'n Wild water park is running its vacation promotion through the second of August. Both are four to six hours' drive each way — with a newborn in the car, more of an option for the two older kids with one parent than for the whole family.

One honest note to close: the event listing sites for Ribeirão Claro, Jacarezinho, and the day-trip radius out to Ourinhos and Cornélio Procópio couldn't be technically retrieved today.
