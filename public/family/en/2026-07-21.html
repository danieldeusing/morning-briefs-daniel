Relevant for the baby In the most recent available InfoGripe bulletin — epi week 26/2026 (published 07/09/2026) — 55.9% RSV among all positive respiratory samples over the past four weeks. Paraná is listed as a state at "alert, risk, or high-risk level," and Curitiba is among the state capitals with elevated and growing activity — concentrated mainly in children under two to four years old. With a one-month-old in the house, that means: postpone visits from anyone with a cold, wash hands before handling the baby, and keep in mind the two school-age kids as the most likely route in once school resumes 07/27.[8]
RSV (respiratory syncytial virus): a cold virus that usually just gives adults and school-age kids a runny nose, but in infants can swell the fine bronchial tubes and lead to breathing trouble. Like a straw you normally drink through — until someone pinches it partway shut. The same infection is harmless for the eight-year-old and a hospital risk for the youngest.

German-Brazilian

Today's wind advisory doubles as a quick language lesson. INMET works with color-coded warning levels like Germany's DWD: amarelo (yellow, "Perigo Potencial") corresponds roughly to an early advisory, laranja (orange, "Perigo") to a significant weather warning, vermelho (red, "Grande Perigo") to a severe-weather warning. Anyone familiar with a yellow DWD warning back in Germany can read "Perigo Potencial" the same way: take note, don't panic. And today's nice word of the day is vendaval — not the Spanish word for "sale," just a strong burst of gusty wind.
