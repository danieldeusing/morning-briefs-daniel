SC Freiburg · Pre-final overture

Höfler's farewell turned into a complete statement

Sometimes the schedule writes its own scripts. Nicolas Höfler — at Freiburg since 2009, the club's record holder for Bundesliga appearances, midfield metronome in 293 league matches — played his last competitive game as a Sport-Club player in front of the home crowd yesterday. What did the team around him deliver? A 4-1 over the third-placed side, three goals inside 47 minutes, with the kind of tactical calm that hadn't fit the previous four league weeks (one point from the three before).

The shape: Schuster set up in a 4-2-3-1, Atubolu in goal, Treu-Ginter-Lienhart-Makengo in front, Eggestein and Höfler as the double pivot, Beste-Manzambi-Scherhant behind Matanović. Beste opens in the 24th with the 1-0 from a Ginter assist, two minutes later Matanović finishes a Manzambi cross. Leipzig pull one back through Ouédraogo (33'), but right after the break Ginter heads in himself — not against his old club BVB, but against Leipzig. Scherhant ends the discussion in the 75th.

Schuster not rotating is the second message. After the 3-2 in Hamburg a week ago, which Schuster himself called a "hangover" performance, the worry was: would the team find its feet vs Leipzig, or would he cold-rotate for Istanbul? Answer: full team, key players for 90 minutes — and it worked immediately. That's the psychologically strongest possible build-up to Wednesday.

What 7th place is actually worth right now

The standard "what do I get for my club?" question has a pleasingly clear answer: Conference League is locked, automatically via 7th. The mechanism: because both DFB-Pokal finalists on May 23 (Bayern + Stuttgart) are already in the Champions League via the league, the cup-winner's Europa League slot trickles up — Hoffenheim and Leverkusen move into the EL, and Freiburg takes the Conference League slot that would otherwise have gone to 8th. Pure standings mechanics, no extra merit beyond what was earned yesterday.

Champions League is only available via Wednesday. Win in Istanbul = direct CL league-phase spot for 2026/27, plus €6m winner's bonus on top of the €7m finalist guarantee. Loss = Conference League 2026/27 plus €7m. The competition gap matters enormously for a club Freiburg's size — we're talking nine-figure UEFA payouts over the next few years if they qualify and pick up group-stage money.

Conference League (UECL): UEFA's third club competition under CL and EL — essentially the second tier of European club football. Introduced in 2021 as a landing zone for clubs finishing 6th–7th in the big leagues, or cup winners from smaller federations. Sporting weakest of the three, but it brings competitive matches against European opposition, travel, TV money, squad experience. Last winner (2025): Chelsea, who got in because they had nothing else.

UEFA association coefficient: The Bundesliga competes every season with La Liga, the Premier League, etc. for European starting slots. Think of a school ranking — if German clubs rack up CL/EL/Conference points, the league average rises. The two top-performing leagues in a season earn an extra fifth CL direct slot. Falling short in 2025/26 costs Germany a direct CL slot for 2026/27. A Freiburg win Wednesday would bring points into the 2026/27 ranking — i.e. relevant for the 2027/28 slot allocation.

It's Sunday, May seventeenth. The Bundesliga season has been history since yesterday — and for SC Freiburg what's left is a reassuringly clear picture. Four-one over RB Leipzig at the Europa-Park-Stadion, seventh place in the final standings, forty-seven points, Conference League safely in the pocket. Nicolas Höfler played his last home match as a Sport-Club player, and the team gave him the gift he deserved — a game where everything worked. Beste in the twenty-fourth minute, Matanović in the twenty-sixth, Ginter with the header after the break, Scherhant in the seventy-fifth.

More important than the result is the tactical call behind it. Schuster did not rotate. Atubolu, Ginter, Höfler, Matanović, Manzambi, Beste — all on the pitch. Considering that Wednesday in Istanbul is the biggest match in the club's history, a coach could reasonably have said: today's a half-squad day, save the legs. Schuster chose the opposite, and it apparently works better. After the final whistle he said, "We're going to need exactly that kind of performance again to make the last big step."

Correction to yesterday's brief: the Europa League final is being played at Beşiktaş Park — also known as Tüpraş Stadyumu — right on the Bosphorus, capacity forty thousand. Not at the Atatürk Olympic Stadium, which is in western Istanbul and holds seventy-five thousand. It's the second major UEFA event at Beşiktaş, after the 2019 Super Cup between Liverpool and Chelsea. The referee will be François Letexier from France, who whistled the 2024 European Championship final. Aston Villa arrives off a clear four-one aggregate over Nottingham Forest — the form line favors the English Premier League club over the Breisgau underdog.

What seventh place is worth now: via the table Freiburg has automatically secured the Conference League. The mechanism works like this — both DFB-Pokal finalists on May twenty-third, Bayern and Stuttgart, are already in the Champions League via the league. So the cup-winner's Europa League slot trickles up the table, Hoffenheim and Leverkusen slide into the EL, and the seventh-place team takes the Conference League slot. That's mechanics, not merit — the merit was simply holding the table position yesterday. Champions League is only available via Wednesday. Win in Istanbul means CL league phase, plus six million in winner's bonus on top of the seven million finalist guarantee.

In Munich today the championship trophy is presented at Marienplatz. Bayern finished the season on one hundred twenty-two goals — that's a new Bundesliga record, the old mark was one hundred and one. In Brazil the sixteenth Brasileirão round is underway. Palmeiras only managed one-one in Barueri against Cruzeiro, a rainy game that showed the first signs of fatigue at the top. Fluminense beat São Paulo two-one at the Maracanã — that was the two-thousandth match ever at that stadium. Internacional hammered Vasco four-one. Tonight the heavyweight is in Curitiba — Athletico Paranaense against Flamengo at seven thirty local, tickets sold out. For Flamengo it's the chance to jump to the top of the table.

Tomorrow, Monday, Carlo Ancelotti names his Brazil World Cup squad. Five p.m. Brasília time, Museu do Amanhã in Rio. Twenty-six names per FIFA rules — Ancelotti is said to have twenty-four locked in. Rodrygo is out with a torn ACL, Neymar is the open question. Then on Thursday the Nagelsmann squad for the German World Cup team, one p.m., DFB Campus in Frankfurt. Postponed from May twelfth because the Bayern–Stuttgart cup final is on May twenty-third.
