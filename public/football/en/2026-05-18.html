SC Freiburg · Two days before Istanbul

Höfler's farewell moves to the final — and that's Schuster logic

Correction to last few briefs' reading: Nicolas Höfler's last competitive appearance in an SC Freiburg shirt is not the 4-1 against Leipzig — it will be the EL final on Wednesday. The same applies to Daniel-Kofi Kyereh and Maximilian Philipp; all three farewells have been deliberately moved past Istanbul. The coaching staff's rationale: an emotional home goodbye on Sunday would have become competition for the actual decisive game of the week. Höfler, Kyereh and Philipp stay with the club after the final, details to be announced in the coming weeks.

That's the typical Christian Schuster architecture that's been showing through in the briefs of recent weeks: emotion is welcome, but it has to appear in the order that helps the team. Istanbul first, then the reception at the trade fair grounds on Thursday (16:00 doors open, stage 17:00, team ~18:00 — regardless of result), then the goodbyes.

Personnel check: Onana is Villa's big hole

SCF. Yuito Suzuki is out (broken collarbone, surgery after the May 3 1-1 vs Wolfsburg) — season over, no discussion. Atubolu played the EL SF second leg and the Leipzig game in full — fit. Ginter, Höfler, Beste, Matanović, Manzambi: all clean after May 16. Aston Villa. Unai Emery's problem child is Amadou Onana: calf injury since the Forest SF first leg around May 2, Emery has estimated about three weeks out — Wednesday participation highly unlikely. Who steps in? Per UEFA's preview, Victor Lindelöf is the natural Onana replacement in midfield alongside Tielemans. Expected Villa XI per UEFA: Martínez — Cash, Konsa, Pau Torres, Digne — Lindelöf, Tielemans — McGinn, Rogers, Buendía — Watkins. Form context: Villa removed Nottingham Forest 4-1 on aggregate (4-0 in the second leg at Villa Park, McGinn brace), sit in a CL place in the Premier League (62 pts), CL qualification already locked irrespective of Wednesday. No league distraction left, full focus on Istanbul.

Emery + Europa League Unai Emery has won this competition three times with Sevilla (2013/14, 2014/15, 2015/16) and once with Villarreal (2020/21) — plus a lost final with Arsenal in 2019. Four titles in five final appearances is the absolute high-water mark in modern coaching. That's the weight Freiburg carry into Wednesday: a coach who feels no competition-respect left in this trophy.

Beşiktaş Park: 42,590 seats, cashless, on the Bosporus

Capacity correction. The last two briefs (May 16 + 17) had 40,000 — the correct value per UEFA's event guide is 42,590. Hybrid pitch, opened in 2016, right on the north bank of the Bosporus. Cashless: only Visa and Mastercard accepted, no American Express, no cash — irrelevant for Daniel in Brazil, but every travelling fan needs their card ready. Tickets per club ~11,000 (the SCF allocation sold out in minutes).

What Wednesday is worth in euros. 7 million euros are guaranteed as the finalist payment (regardless of win or loss), plus 6 million on a win. The sporting side carries more weight: Champions League League Phase 2026/27 plus participation bonus ~18 million euros + match bonuses. Vs Conference League entry (loss in Istanbul): a two- to three-digit million-euro difference over two years if you make the knockouts once.

Bundesliga · Season's end & cup-final prep

Marienplatz yesterday: trophy at 1:49 PM, Olise, Goretzka, the Kompany anecdote

Bayern's 35th title found its public peak on Sunday. The squad arrived at the town hall around 1:30 PM, Manuel Neuer lifted the trophy at 1:49 PM in front of tens of thousands of fans on Marienplatz, reportedly in an unusually clear bass voice — and probably with half an eye on his own career extension (see transfer block, right column). The anecdote of the day will probably outlive the formal part: Vincent Kompany apparently left the trophy at home after the internal team night party. Wife Carla brought it to the town hall in the last minute — otherwise the handover would presumably have taken place with a replica trophy. Olise and Davies danced wildly on stage, video doing the rounds. Reception in the Great Council Chamber by Mayor Dominik Krause.

Bayern/BVB/Leipzig/Stuttgart locked into the CL. The cup mechanism (both cup finalists are already in the CL via the league) lifts Hoffenheim + Leverkusen into the EL and Freiburg into the Conference League. Tiebreaker between Heidenheim and St. Pauli at 26 pts + −31 GD: goals scored counts more — Heidenheim 41 GF, St. Pauli 29 GF, so Heidenheim 17th, St. Pauli 18th (no practical consequence, both directly relegated).

UEFA association coefficient: Every season the Bundesliga competes with La Liga, Premier League etc. for European places. Like a school ranking — when German clubs score lots of points in CL/EL/Conference, the league average goes up. The two best leagues of a season get a fifth CL slot. As of 2025/26: England 27.125 · Spain 21.781 · Germany 21.214 — the Bundesliga is third and no longer catchable. Consequence: 2026/27 the Bundesliga has 4 CL slots (places 1–4), 2 EL slots (5+6), 1 Conf slot (cup winner or 7th-placed, depending on the constellation). A Freiburg win in Istanbul on Wednesday no longer changes anything for 2026/27 — but it does bring points that count for the 2027/28 season.

Seleção · Ancelotti day

26 names tonight 5:00 PM BRT — and Neymar moves from the "out" pile to the "in" pile

By the time Daniel reads this brief in the morning, the squad reveal is still ~10 hours away. But the trend has flipped notably over the past week. Neymar Jr. (34, Santos) — having missed 65.45% of the World Cup cycle (807 of 1,233 days) to injury — was long considered unselectable; in mid-May the majority media expectation was "out". In the last ten days the consensus has reversed — Goal Brasil headlines "from unlikely to strong candidate", Metrópoles and CNN Brasil now expect him called up. Ancelotti himself on May 13: "Decision 100% professional, no outside pressure." His son and assistant coach Davide confirmed Neymar on the 55-man preliminary list.

Announcement logistics (confirmed): 5:00 PM BRT / 10:00 PM CET, Museu do Amanhã Rio, around 700 accredited journalists, effective list read-out at ~5:45 PM BRT. Live free on Globo, SBT, Xsports and CazéTV (official Brazilian WC rights-holder, YouTube + Twitch + Prime Video), pay on SporTV/ESPN/TNT/CNN Brasil. Player meet-up at Granja Comary (Teresópolis) from May 27.

What the preliminary list reveals: 6 goalkeepers, 20 defenders, 12 midfielders, 17 forwards. Flamengo supply the biggest contingent with 7 players (Léo Pereira, Léo Ortiz, Paquetá, Pedro, Samuel Lino, Danilo, Alex Sandro). In goal: Alisson, Bento (Al-Nassr), Ederson (Fenerbahçe), Hugo Souza (Corinthians), John (Nottingham Forest), Weverton (Grêmio) — six candidates for three spots.

Preliminary list (55): FIFA rule — every World Cup team submits up to 55 names as a pre-selection, from which the final 26 (up from 23) are then called. Works like an insurance pool: anyone on the preliminary list can still be released by their club for game-week purposes if they get selected. Anyone not on the preliminary list can only enter the final squad via injury replacement. Brazil submitted the list on schedule on May 11, 2026.

It is Monday, May 18th. Today is the day of the last training before the big game — and the day of the big list out of Rio. SC Freiburg trains one final time on home turf; tomorrow morning the charter leaves for Istanbul; Wednesday evening at nine PM German time is kickoff at Beşiktaş Park. The stadium capacity we wrote in the last two briefs was wrong — it is forty-two thousand five hundred ninety, not forty thousand. The stadium is also entirely cashless, which is relevant for the traveling fans.

The second correction concerns Nicolas Höfler. His farewell on Saturday was not his last competitive appearance — he, Daniel-Kofi Kyereh and Maximilian Philipp will all three be sent off properly after Istanbul. The coaching staff deliberately moved the ceremonies, so that the actually decisive game of the season is not overshadowed by a home-game emotional rush. On Thursday, the day after the final, there will be a reception at the trade-fair grounds in Freiburg, regardless of the result.

Aston Villa's personnel worries center on one name: Amadou Onana, the former Bochum prospect, is likely out with a calf injury — three weeks out, Emery said most recently. Victor Lindelöf moves up. Tyrone Mings, who was rested against Liverpool last Friday, is considered fit in time. Villa beat Liverpool in that match four to two, Watkins brace — Champions League qualification is already locked via the league, so Wednesday has no additional league significance. Full focus on Istanbul, no insurance policy. Unai Emery, by the way, has won this competition three times with Sevilla and once with Villarreal — four Europa League titles in five final appearances. That is the weight the Sport-Club has to carry on Wednesday.

In Munich there was a title party at Marienplatz yesterday. Manuel Neuer lifted the trophy at one forty-nine PM, Michael Olise was honored as Player of the Season, Leon Goretzka given an emotional send-off after eight years. The anecdote of the day: Vincent Kompany had left the trophy at home after the internal night party — his wife Carla brought it to the town hall in the last minute, otherwise the handover would presumably have happened with a replica trophy.

The cup-final worries for Bayern, though, are not trivial. Alphonso Davies is out for several weeks with a muscle injury and definitely misses the final. Manuel Neuer picked up a calf injury on Saturday — his participation on the coming Saturday is still in question six days out. Sven Ulreich, just extended through twenty-twenty-seven, stands ready. At Stuttgart, captain Karazor is suspended; Finn Jeltsch returns from his abdominal injury.

The relegation play-off is set: Wolfsburg against SC Paderborn, first leg Thursday at eight-thirty PM at the Volkswagen Arena, second leg Sunday or Monday in Paderborn — SAT-one free. A bitter footnote for Brazilian watchers: Rogério injured his knee on the last matchday.

In Brazil, matchday sixteen has finished. Athletico-PR against Flamengo ended one to one in Curitiba — Stiven Mendoza put the home side ahead, Pedro equalized, Danilo saw a second yellow. Flamengo miss the closer on Palmeiras; the gap stays at four points. Botafogo beat Corinthians three to one with a clean Arthur Cabral hat-trick. Bahia and Grêmio shared the points, Santos lost zero to three at home against Coritiba. Important correction to yesterday's brief: the Ceará-Fortaleza match was Série B, not Série A — the newsletter had it incorrectly listed as a top-flight match.

And tonight at ten PM German time, Carlo Ancelotti reads out the Brazil World Cup list at the Museu do Amanhã in Rio. Twenty-six names, FIFA rule. What has flipped in the Brazilian media in the past ten days: Neymar Jr. — who has missed sixty-five percent of the entire World Cup cycle to injury — is now considered a lock by majority. Goal Brasil headlines: "from unlikely to strong candidate". Rodrygo is out with an ACL tear, Estêvão with a hamstring strain — Endrick is considered the likely Estêvão replacement. The last slot is being fought over by Rayan of Bournemouth, Pedro of Flamengo and Andrey Santos of Chelsea. An important correction to yesterday's brief: Kevin Viveros, the Athletico top scorer, is Colombian and does not belong on the Brazilian list — he is on Néstor Lorenzo's pre-list. Side note: Ancelotti extended his contract through twenty-thirty already on May fourteenth — so what he decides tonight also applies after the tournament.
