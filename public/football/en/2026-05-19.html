SC Freiburg · Day −1 Istanbul

Charter, stadium walk, presser 18:15 — the schedule as far as the club talks

SC Freiburg flies out together early in the morning from Basel-Mulhouse; the club has deliberately not published an exact takeoff time, on charter-logistics security grounds. The team moves into the official UEFA team hotel; with the stadium walk at Beşiktaş Park late afternoon, the official UEFA matchday-1 program begins. The press conference with head coach Julian Schuster and captain Christian Günter is set in the UEFA program for 18:15 local (17:15 CET) — sport.de is running a live-stream companion channel. Immediately after, the only closing training session on the Tüpraş Stadyumu pitch — by tradition the first 15 minutes are media-open, after that the coaching staff reserves tactical preparation for the closed setting.

Coach name correction In the briefs from May 16–18, "Christian Schuster" appeared occasionally. The head coach is named Julian Schuster (44, former SCF captain, head coach since 2024 after Streich's departure). "Christian" belongs to Günter, the captain. Correction in force from today.

Expected XI: Atubolu, Ginter, the Schuster combination — and Höfler

UEFA preview and Tipico match almost word-for-word on the lineup forecast: Atubolu — Kübler, Ginter, Lienhart, Treu — Eggestein, Manzambi — Beste, Höfler, Grifo — Matanović. Höfler ends career — the actual date is Wednesday

What appeared scattered on Saturday after the 4:1 against Leipzig is, since the t-online interview on May 17, plainly formulated: Nicolas Höfler is ending his active career after the EL final. 21 years at SCF — from U-15 player in 2005 through his pro debut in 2010, captain of the promotion season in 2016, to 386 competitive matches — the arc is as complete as a club today rarely traces a player's path. The club is planning a further role for Höfler — a concrete function is not public, the official follow-up conversation is scheduled for after the final.

Daniel-Kofi Kyereh and Maximilian Philipp leave the club on free transfers; tribuna.com confirmed on May 15 the farewell of both players after season's end. All three farewells take place after Istanbul — the coaching-staff logic of the past few weeks was consistent in ensuring the emotional home-final against Leipzig would not become sporting competition for the actual end of the season.

Karazor correction: he plays the cup final

Clean cut on two consecutive brief errors: the second yellow Atakan Karazor saw on the final Bundesliga matchday against Hoffenheim brought a two-match suspension only in the Bundesliga — Leverkusen + Frankfurt were affected, the DFB cup final explicitly was not. Vfb.de phrased it precisely in the note from April 28; Sky Sport confirmed on the cup-week ramp-up. Spicy side note: referee Sven Jablonski wrongly sent Karazor off in Wolfsburg in February 2024 — the Stuttgarter Zeitung reports both got in touch beforehand to clear the old episode. A cup final without that background would be Jablonski's premiere anyway — it is his first cup final at all.

Neuer calf: cautiously optimistic, but training status decides

Four days before the final, the most important personnel issue of the Munich prep. Sky Sport reports that Manuel Neuer's calf injury of May 16 shows no structural damage — no fiber tear, no muscle-fiber tear, but an irritation that should subside under normal load buildup. Bayern is running a reduced training program at the start of the week, with developments on May 19/20 decisive. Max Eberl at the May 18 presser: "We hope he'll be in goal on Saturday."

Sven Ulreich (contract extended to 2027) stands ready as backup — Lukas Schenk as third man on the arc, both having logged cup-match minutes recently. Alphonso Davies, on the other hand, is definitively out: fcbayern.com confirmed on May 17 the diagnosis of muscle injury to the rear left thigh (in the CL semifinal return against PSG), expected to be sidelined approximately six weeks. Cup final off the table, and the World Cup buildup with Canada (from June 11) hanging in the balance.

Seleção · Ancelotti list of May 18

26 names — Neymar in, Weverton a surprise, Andrey Santos out

Carlo Ancelotti yesterday at just before 18:00 BRT at the Museu do Amanhã in Rio read out the final list — in front of around 700 accredited journalists, live free on Globo, SBT, Xsports and CazéTV. The central decision: Neymar Jr. (Santos, 34) is back in the squad for the first time in two years and seven months. Olympics.com and Jornal de Brasília confirm the call-up; the Brazilian press mood in the ten days before had swung from "unlikely" to "strong candidate." Ancelotti justified the choice with "physical condition, tactical game intelligence, and the ability to turn a match." Chant-jubilation broke out at the Museu do Amanhã when the name was read — not surprising.

The second big surprise sits in goal: Weverton (Grêmio, 38) displaces Bento (Al-Nassr) and Hugo Souza (Corinthians) from the third-goalkeeper slot. Ancelotti's explanation at the coletiva: "We tested three goalkeepers this year — Bento, Hugo Souza, and John. For this tournament we need a more experienced player, and that can be Weverton." The three goalkeepers are thus: Alisson (Liverpool), Ederson (Fenerbahçe), Weverton (Grêmio).

Last slot — Rayan. In the three-way Rayan / João Pedro / Andrey Santos race, Ancelotti decided for the 19-year-old Bournemouth striker. João Pedro (Chelsea) and Andrey Santos (Chelsea) stay out — the big losers of the Neymar call-up. Endrick (just back to Real Madrid from his Lyon loan) is in as the Estêvão replacement. Confirmed absences: Rodrygo (Real Madrid, ACL tear), Estêvão (Chelsea, severe thigh strain), Éder Militão (Real Madrid, hamstring surgery).

Ancelotti's statement of intent "We want to be the most resilient team in the world. I'm not afraid to say we can win." — At the coletiva, Band broadcast. In combination with the contract extension to 2030 (CBF, May 14), that's a six-weeks-out statement to take unusually clearly from a "we'll see" pragmatist's mouth.

Flamengo with four players the largest club contingent

Four Flamengo players are in the final squad: Danilo, Léo Pereira, Alex Sandro, Lucas Paquetá — flamengo.com confirms the constellation. Two players each come from Arsenal (Gabriel Magalhães, Martinelli), Manchester United (Casemiro, Matheus Cunha), Real Madrid (Vinicius Jr., Endrick), Newcastle (Bruno Guimarães, Joelinton), and Zenit. Pedro (Flamengo, centre forward) is not in — one of the less surprising calls, his form picture for the season was visibly thin.

Flamengo is thus the most strongly represented club in a Brazilian WC squad since 2002. This constellation falls right into the buildup for the R17 top match Flamengo–Palmeiras on Saturday — Filipe Luís has to motivate a team whose key figures, in the most career-defining stretch of their lives, simultaneously have their heads in two spheres.

Granja Comary: the official CBF training base in the mountains above Rio, operating since 1987. Functions like a closed-off high-performance campus with hotels, training pitches, medical facilities — perfect rest-pod for the final weeks before a tournament. What "Camp David for Brazilian football" would be: you drive there, everything is already there, no one walks in unannounced.

At Aston Villa, Onana remains out with the calf injury, Lindelöf steps in.

For Nicolas Höfler, Wednesday will be the last professional match. Twenty-one years at the Sport-Club, twenty-one years — from youth player through pro debut in 2010 to promotion captain in 2016. T-online confirmed the retirement on Sunday. Daniel-Kofi Kyereh and Maximilian Philipp leave the club on free transfers; all three farewells will be officially communicated after Istanbul. In Freiburg the fan mile at the exhibition grounds sold out fifteen thousand tickets within twenty-four hours; the Thursday reception happens regardless of the result.

The second capital-city story comes from Rio. Carlo Ancelotti at just before six in the evening Brazilian time, in front of seven hundred accredited journalists at the Museu do Amanhã, read out the final twenty-six names. Neymar Jr. is in — for the first time in two years and seven months back in the Seleção. Ancelotti justified the choice on the coletiva with physical condition, tactical game intelligence, and the ability to turn a match. There were chants in the Museu do Amanhã when the name fell.

The second surprise sits in goal. Thirty-eight-year-old Weverton of Grêmio displaces Bento and Hugo Souza from the third-goalkeeper slot. Alisson and Ederson are the first two names, Weverton the third. Ancelotti's explanation: for this tournament we need a more experienced man. The fight for the last slot went to Rayan of Bournemouth, nineteen years old, three Premier League goals in recent weeks — João Pedro and Andrey Santos, both of Chelsea, are out. Endrick, just back from his Lyon loan to Real Madrid, replaces the injured Estêvão. Flamengo provides the largest club contingent with Danilo, Léo Pereira, Alex Sandro, and Lucas Paquetá — four players from one club, not seen in a Brazilian World Cup squad since two thousand and two.

Ancelotti's statement of intent at the coletiva: "We want to be the most resilient team in the world. I'm not afraid to say we can win." Remarkably clear for a pragmatist. The CBF extended his contract only on May fourteenth through to twenty-thirty, which gives yesterday's list political weight beyond the tournament — what Ancelotti decides will also be in force after the World Cup.

For Saturday's DFB cup final, an important correction to the past two briefs: Atakan Karazor is not red-card-suspended. The second yellow against Hoffenheim brought him a two-match Bundesliga suspension for Leverkusen and Frankfurt — the cup final is not affected. Vfb.de phrased it unambiguously; we got that wrong in the briefs of May seventeen and May eighteen. Karazor will, with high probability, take the field in Berlin as captain.

The Neuer calf issue in Munich: per Sky Sport no structural injury, no fiber tear. Bayern is running a reduced training program at the start of the week. Max Eberl said at yesterday's presser: we hope he's in goal Saturday. Davies, on the other hand, is definitively out — the season-ending diagnosis has been confirmed since Sunday. Sven Ulreich, freshly extended through to twenty-twenty-seven, stands ready as backup. Finn Jeltsch at Stuttgart is fit but should take a bench seat in favor of Chabot and Hendriks.

In Brazil, round seventeen looks to the Maracanã from Saturday. Flamengo against Palmeiras at twenty-one Brazilian time — that's two in the morning German time on Sunday — Mengão tickets sold out. Palmeiras leads with thirty-five points, Flamengo has a match in hand and is virtually only one point behind. Filipe Luís can hit five trophies in one year with a win — Abel Ferreira collected ten in five years at Palmeiras. Santos is in crisis: head coach Cléber Xavier was let go after the zero-six against Vasco, the club stands at sixteenth, out of the relegation zone only by goal differential. Neymar was subbed off in the zero-three against Coritiba with calf pain — per ESPN without serious diagnosis, which two days before his World Cup call-up nevertheless raises the question whether this is the start of the journey back or the start of a new waiting room.
