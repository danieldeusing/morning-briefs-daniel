SC Freiburg · Final day in Istanbul

What's at stake: a trophy — and the Champions League spot

This isn't "just" a final. The winner of the Europa League final goes straight into the 2026/27 Champions League — the loser lands in the Conference League. For SC Freiburg that's the sharp twist of the evening: via the Bundesliga the club has only reached the Conference League playoff with 7th place (47 points), i.e. the third European tier, and not even a guaranteed group stage. An EL title would lift Freiburg into the top tier in one stroke — financially a low-eight-figure sum, sportingly the first Champions League participation in club history. Rarely have two season outcomes lain so far apart for a German club across 90 (plus a possible 30) minutes.

Kickoff is at 9:00 PM CET — that's 4:00 PM BRT for Daniel in Ribeirão Claro — so 10:00 PM local time in Istanbul, at the Tüpraş Stadyumu, Beşiktaş's stadium on the Bosphorus, 42,590 seats, fully cashless (Visa and Mastercard only). The referee is Frenchman François Letexier (37), who already took charge of the 2024 Euros final; VAR is his compatriot Jérôme Brisard. Broadcast: RTL free-to-air from 8:15 PM CET / 3:15 PM BRT, simultaneously pay on Sky and DAZN.

Expected XI: Schuster stays silent, the forecast holds

At the pre-match press conference at Beşiktaş Park, head coach Julian Schuster deliberately didn't reveal his starting XI — a classic final move that complicates Emery's preparation. The consensus forecast from the UEFA and Tipico previews is in the dashboard card above (4-2-3-1). The one open debate is over Höfler's role: defensively alongside Eggestein/Manzambi in the double pivot, or as a No. 10 behind Matanović. The more emotional read — Höfler as playmaker in his last match — is tactically unusual for Schuster but makes sense as a farewell gesture.

4-2-3-1: the numbers read from defense forward — four defenders, then two more defensive midfielders (the "double pivot," the cover), then three attackers (left/central/right), then one center-forward up top. Like a pyramid narrowing toward the top: broad and secure at the back, the point up front.

Confirmed absentees for Freiburg: Yuito Suzuki (fractured collarbone, operated on), Patrick Osterhage, Max Rosenfelder and Daniel-Kofi Kyereh. The central spine — Atubolu in goal, Ginter and Lienhart inside, Eggestein and Grifo pulling the strings — is fully fit.

Aston Villa: banged up, but the favorites with the final specialist

On the other side stands an Aston Villa that hasn't reached a UEFA final since winning the 1982 European Cup — and has in Unai Emery a coach who knows the competition cold: three wins with Sevilla (2014–2016), one with Villarreal (2021), plus the lost 2019 final with Arsenal. Four final wins, a fifth within reach. The bookmakers and Sports Illustrated tip Villa clearly, with SI calling a 2-0. In this campaign Villa have scored 28:8, Freiburg 25:10 — so Villa bring the more efficient attack and the steadier defense.

However, Villa arrive banged up: Boubacar Kamara is out, Amadou Onana (calf) remains doubtful, as does Emiliano Buendía, and Victor Lindelöf recently came off with a knock but is expected to play. Expected Villa XI (4-2-3-1): Martínez — Cash, Konsa, Pau Torres, Digne — Lindelöf, Tielemans — McGinn, Rogers, Buendía — Watkins. Ollie Watkins and John McGinn top Villa's internal EL scoring chart. If Onana is out, Lindelöf shifts into central midfield — exactly the hole Schuster's pressing could target.

Höfler's last match — and the fan logistics

For Nicolas Höfler, Istanbul is the end: after 21 years at SCF, from U-15 player in 2005 to 386 competitive appearances, the former promotion captain ends his active career tonight. Daniel-Kofi Kyereh and Maximilian Philipp leave the club on free transfers; all three farewells will be made official after the final, so the match keeps the emotional foreground.

In Istanbul the fan meeting point at Beşiktaş Square opens from 10:00 AM local and closes after the final at 3:00 AM — free entry. SCF tickets per club ~11,000, gone within minutes. In Freiburg the badenova fan zone at the trade-fair grounds is sold out with 15,000 tickets, doors from 6:00 PM local. The homecoming reception takes place regardless of the result on Thursday from 4:00 PM local. Weather: in Istanbul cloudy with scattered rain at kickoff at ~16 °C, in Freiburg mostly dry at up to ~20 °C.

DFB-Pokal & national team · the week after Istanbul

Neuer's calf still unclear — four days before Berlin

At Bayern, the most important personnel question before Saturday's cup final (May 23, 8:00 PM CET / 3:00 PM BRT) remains unresolved. Manuel Neuer was substituted in the 60th minute of Saturday's 5-1 win over Köln with discomfort in his left calf and did not train with the team on Tuesday or Wednesday. The internal agreement: if the 40-year-old can join team training on Thursday, he starts in the cup final. Sporting director Max Eberl stays cautiously optimistic: "We hope he might be able to be in goal on Saturday. We still have hope!" Sven Ulreich (under contract to 2027) and the promoted Jonas Urbig are on standby.

Alphonso Davies, by contrast, is definitively out: fcbayern.com confirms a muscle injury in his left thigh (picked up in the CL semifinal second leg against PSG) with a layoff of several weeks — around six weeks of rehab. Cup final scratched, and the World Cup opener with Canada from mid-June is also up in the air. Serge Gnabry has likewise ended his season through injury.

Nagelsmann names the World Cup squad tomorrow — 26 names, 1:00 PM CET / 8:00 AM BRT

Tomorrow it gets real for the German planning: head coach Julian Nagelsmann announces his 26-man squad for the 2026 World Cup on Thursday at 1:00 PM CET (8:00 AM BRT) at the DFB Campus in Frankfurt — live on Sky Sport News and sportschau.de. The date was originally planned for May 12 and pushed back nine days; Nagelsmann cited the tight end-of-season run and the wish for more planning certainty. Notably: even the retired Neuer is on the provisional 55-man list — a final conversation with Nagelsmann is scheduled before the announcement.

The FIFA mechanics behind it: well before the announcement, the DFB had to submit a provisional list of 35 to 55 players (including at least four goalkeepers); the final 26 must be registered with FIFA by June 5 at the latest. So plenty of room for late injury call-ups — for instance if Davies-style cases pile up in the German squad too.
