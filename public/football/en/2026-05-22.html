Freiburg's final dream bursts: 0–3 to Aston Villa

It's over, and it hurts. SC Freiburg lost the Europa League final in Istanbul 0–3 to Aston Villa — the biggest match in the club's 121-year history, and in the end it wasn't even close. Youri Tielemans and Emiliano Buendía turned the game before half-time, Morgan Rogers sealed it in the 58th minute. Instead of the first Champions League appearance in club history, Freiburg are left with the Conference League playoff via 7th place in the league. For Nicolas Höfler it was his last professional match after 21 years — substituted in the 61st minute, then celebrated by thousands of fans at the reception on Freiburg's exhibition grounds. Schuster found the right words: "If we had played like our fans, we'd be holding the trophy now." Elsewhere the German game rolled on: Nagelsmann announced his 26-man World Cup squad (Freiburg's Ginter left out, Neuer recalled), at Bayern Munich Neuer's calf remains a question mark ahead of Saturday's cup final, and in the relegation playoff Wolfsburg and Paderborn drew 0–0. SC Freiburg · The final that wasn't to be

0–3 — Villa were the better team across 90 minutes

The dream lasted about half an hour. SC Freiburg started bravely in the biggest match of their history, even had the first good chance through a Höfler long-range shot in the 17th minute — but then Aston Villa took over, and it became a one-sided evening. Youri Tielemans scored in the 41st minute with a volley after a corner, Emiliano Buendía added another in first-half stoppage time (45+3') from about 18 meters. The one-two punch before the break effectively decided the match. In the 58th minute Morgan Rogers made it 0–3 after a Buendía assist. After that Villa managed the game comfortably. Final score 0–3 in regular time, before 37,324 spectators at the Tüpraş Stadyumu (Beşiktaş Park) on the Bosporus.

xG (Expected Goals): a probability calculation — from this shooting position, on average so many out of a hundred players would have scored, whether or not it actually went in. It measures the quality of the chances. On this night it was 2.08 (Villa) to 0.22 (Freiburg) — Villa created chances almost ten times as dangerous. The 0–3 was no fluke, but deserved.

Head coach Julian Schuster went with the expected XI but surprised in one position: Johan Manzambi played the attacking No. 10, while Nicolas Höfler formed the double pivot with Maximilian Eggestein. So the more emotional reading — Höfler as playmaker in his last game — did not materialize; Schuster chose the more defensively stable option. It didn't help. The xG figures come from English statistics aggregators (ESPN/beIN) and are not cross-checked via kicker — but the magnitude matches the clear flow of the game.

Conference League instead of Champions League — the costly difference

This was the sharp twist of the evening: the winner of the Europa League final would have qualified directly for the 2026/27 Champions League. With the defeat, Freiburg are left with what they earned through the Bundesliga — 7th place (47 points) entitles them to the Conference League playoff, the third-highest European tier and not even a guaranteed group stage. An EL title would have lifted the club into the top competition in one stroke — the first Champions League appearance in club history and a double-digit-million windfall. Rarely have two season outcomes lain so far apart for a German club across 90 minutes. The playoff takes place mid-to-late August, with the draw to follow.

Höfler's farewell — and a reception that outshone the defeat

For Nicolas Höfler (36), Istanbul was his last professional match. In the 61st minute the former promotion captain was substituted (for Lucas Höler), after 21 years at the Sport-Club, from youth player to hundreds of competitive games. He's not stopping entirely: Höfler intends to play one more year in Freiburg's Regionalliga side — but his pro career is over. Together with Daniel-Kofi Kyereh and Maximilian Philipp (both leaving on free transfers) he was sent off at the reception.

And that reception was the day's defining image. On Thursday, thousands of fans celebrated the team on Freiburg's exhibition grounds — despite the 0–3 defeat, with scarves, flags and the club anthem played by the philharmonic orchestra. The team signed the city's Golden Book, mayor Martin Horn paid tribute to the historic season. Captain Christian Günter described the atmosphere as "electrifying"; the celebrations in Istanbul reportedly ran until sunrise over the Bosporus. Schuster summed up the season: "If we had played like our fans, we'd be holding the trophy now. Unfortunately we didn't manage that." On the other side, Villa coach Unai Emery was hailed internationally as the "Lord of the Europa League" for his fifth EL title.

DFB · WC squad set, Neuer's calf open

Nagelsmann's 26: Neuer back, Lennart Karl the surprise — Ginter out

On Thursday at 13:00 CEST (08:00 BRT), national-team coach Julian Nagelsmann announced his 26-man squad for the 2026 World Cup at the DFB Campus in Frankfurt. The biggest call: Manuel Neuer returns despite his retirement from the national team and is penciled in as number one — Nagelsmann brought him back for the task of naming "the country's three best goalkeepers." The biggest surprise in the field: young Bayern player Lennart Karl is in. Left out were Karim Adeyemi and Köln's Said El Mala — and, with a Freiburg connection, Matthias Ginter, who had been in the starting XI in the EL final the day before. Jamal Musiala is included despite a fitness deficit: "Even at 95 percent he's one of the most extraordinary players in the world," Nagelsmann said in substance.

The squad (26) Goalkeepers: Baumann (Hoffenheim), Neuer (Bayern), Nübel (Stuttgart). Defenders: Anton (Dortmund), Brown (Frankfurt), Kimmich (Bayern), Raum (Leipzig), Rüdiger (Real Madrid), Schlotterbeck (Dortmund), Tah (Bayern), Thiaw (Newcastle). Midfielders: Amiri (Mainz), Goretzka (Bayern), Groß (Brighton), Karl (Bayern), Leweling (Stuttgart), Musiala (Bayern), Nmecha (Dortmund), Pavlović (Bayern), Stiller (Stuttgart), Wirtz (Liverpool). Forwards: Beier (Dortmund), Havertz (Arsenal), Sané (Galatasaray), Undav (Stuttgart), Woltemade (Newcastle).

The final 26-man list must be filed with FIFA by June 5 at the latest; until then, injury-related replacements are possible. Neuer's calf before the cup final: 50:50

At Bayern Munich the most important question ahead of Saturday's cup final (May 23, 20:00 CEST / 15:00 BRT, Olympiastadion Berlin, ARD) is open. Manuel Neuer, substituted on May 17 against Köln with calf trouble, again did not train with the team on Thursday, completing only an individual rehab program. By the internal logic ("no Thursday team training = no final"), that would argue against playing — but the decision was officially deferred to Friday, the chances put at 50:50, with a final test before departing for Berlin. Should Neuer be out, Jonas Urbig (22) is the candidate to take the gloves. Alphonso Davies (thigh, several weeks) and Serge Gnabry (season over) are out regardless.

Relegation: Wolfsburg 0–0 Paderborn — VfL waste their first-leg chance

In the relegation playoff first leg for the last Bundesliga spot, VfL Wolfsburg (16th) and second-division third-placed SC Paderborn drew 0–0 on Thursday at the Volkswagen Arena. Wolfsburg pressed for long stretches (17:2 shots by the 70th minute) but failed on their own finishing and on outstanding 20-year-old Paderborn keeper Dennis Seimen — an Eriksen free-kick from 30 meters was saved, Daghim missed when clean through. Paderborn had chances of their own (Castaneda volley off the post, 9'; Bilbija cleared off the line in the 84'). In stoppage time (94') Paderborn's Jonah Sticker saw a second yellow and thus misses the second leg. The goalless draw is a notable success for the second-division side: Wolfsburg must win the second leg on Monday (May 25, 20:30 CEST / 15:30 BRT, in Paderborn) to avoid the first Bundesliga relegation in club history.

It's Friday, May twenty-second, and the day after the big game begins with disappointment. SC Freiburg lost the Europa League final in Istanbul, nil to three, against Aston Villa. It was the biggest match in the club's hundred-and-twenty-one-year history, and in the end it wasn't close.

The dream lasted about half an hour. Freiburg started bravely and even had the first good chance, a long-range shot from Höfler in the seventeenth minute. Then Villa took over. In the fifty-eighth minute, Morgan Rogers made it nil to three. The chance quality, measured as expected goals, was two point zero eight for Villa against zero point two two for Freiburg. The result was deserved.

It was about more than a trophy. The winner would have gone straight into the Champions League. With the defeat, Freiburg are left with the Conference League playoff via seventh place in the league. The dream of a first-ever Champions League appearance has burst.

For Nicolas Höfler, Istanbul was his last professional match. Substituted in the sixty-first minute, he ends his pro career after twenty-one years at the Sport-Club, though he plans to play one more year in the reserve side. And then came the day's defining image: on Thursday, thousands of fans celebrated the team on Freiburg's exhibition grounds, despite the loss, with flags and the club anthem.
