SC Freiburg · The goalkeeper question defines the summer

Record signing incoming: Backhaus in, Atubolu maybe out

The historic season is barely filed away and everything at the Sport-Club already revolves around one position: goalkeeper. Per Bild and the DeichStube (as of 23.05.), SC Freiburg are closing on the most expensive transfer in club history — Mio Backhaus, 21, Germany U21 keeper at Werder Bremen, for around €15m plus bonuses (up to €18m), on a deal to 2031. Werder officially confirmed the departure on 23.05. — Backhaus is leaving, and Estonia's Karl Hein (from Arsenal, ~€3m) becomes Bremen's new number one; a formal presentation at the SC is still to come.

The reason for the expensive move: Noah Atubolu, until now the undisputed number one and an academy product, apparently wants to go. Per kicker and Sport1 (as of 23.05.) his agent is pushing for a transfer; his contract runs to 2027 and he reportedly won't extend. AC Milan, Inter and Atlético Madrid are circling, with fee estimates ranging from €20m to €30m. That, too, is a rumour as of today — but a sale would explain the Backhaus deal. Take: if Freiburg pay a club record for a 21-year-old while letting their young first-choice go for triple that, it's exactly the business model that made the club strong — develop talent, sell at a profit, bring in the next one. It only gets risky if the two deals don't dovetail cleanly and the SC suddenly heads into the Conference League playoff without a proven keeper.

The departures are official — Höfler, Kyereh, Philipp

Almost lost behind the goalkeeper rumours: the long-serving faces have been formally sent off. At the 21.05. reception on Freiburg's exhibition grounds, Nicolas Höfler, Daniel-Kofi Kyereh and Maximilian Philipp were honoured on stage. Höfler ends his pro career after 21 years but will play one more season in Freiburg's reserve (Regionalliga) side; Kyereh (30) and Philipp (32) leave on free transfers. Head coach Julian Schuster stays — no change on the bench has been reported as of today.

DFB-Pokal final · Bayern, the Double — and Urbig in goal

Neuer out, Urbig plays — the World Cup comes first

The question that has hung over the final for days is answered: Manuel Neuer will not be in goal today. The calf problem that flared up against Köln on 17.05. hasn't healed in time; the decision came on Friday. Sporting director Max Eberl, paraphrased: the World Cup is not in danger, but a start today would be "too soon after the injury." With Neuer only surprisingly re-called to the World Cup squad on Thursday, the caution makes double sense — you don't risk a freshly re-named national keeper for a match the 22-year-old Jonas Urbig can also handle. For Urbig it is probably the biggest game of his young career. Alphonso Davies (thigh) and Serge Gnabry (out for the season) are absent anyway.

Champions vs holders — the matchup

Two sides meet under opposite signs. FC Bayern are already German champions — the 2025/26 season, with 89 points and 122 goals, was one of the best in Bundesliga history (a goals record). Under coach Vincent Kompany, Bayern can complete the Double tonight.

Double: when a club wins both the league title and the cup in the same season. Like topping your class and winning the school tournament trophy in the same school year — both big prizes at once.

The VfB Stuttgart (coach Sebastian Hoeneß) are the holders: in 2025 the VfB won the cup with a 4:2 over Arminia Bielefeld. In the league this season was patchy for Stuttgart — and against Bayern they took two clear defeats (0:5 away, 2:4 at home, in Bayern's title-clinching match). The cup is Stuttgart's chance to gild a grinding season — and to snatch a trophy from Bayern. The referee is Sven Jablonski (36), his first DFB-Pokal final. A nice footnote: in Neuer's deputy Urbig and Stuttgart's Alexander Nübel, two keepers who are both in Germany's World Cup squad face off. Confirmed line-ups were not yet out this morning — the names here reflect the pre-match reporting.

It's Saturday, May twenty-third, and it's cup final day. Tonight at Berlin's Olympic Stadium the eighty-third DFB-Pokal final kicks off: FC Bayern Munich against the holders, VfB Stuttgart. Kickoff is at eight in the evening German time, three in the afternoon for you in Brazil, live on ARD.

Bayern have long been German champions, with eighty-nine points and a hundred and twenty-two goals, one of the best seasons in Bundesliga history. Tonight Vincent Kompany's side can complete the Double, that is, league and cup in a single season. Stuttgart, under Sebastian Hoeneß, defend the cup they won in twenty twenty-five and want to gild an uneven league campaign.

The big news landed Friday. Manuel Neuer is out; his calf hasn't healed in time. Since Nagelsmann only surprisingly re-called him to the World Cup squad on Thursday, Bayern won't take any risk. The twenty-two-year-old Jonas Urbig starts in goal instead, the biggest game of his career. Alphonso Davies and Serge Gnabry are out anyway. The referee is Sven Jablonski, his first cup final.

At your SC Freiburg the season is over, but the transfer market is humming, and it's all about goalkeepers. Per Bild and the DeichStube, the Sport-Club are closing on the most expensive transfer in their history: Mio Backhaus from Werder Bremen, for up to eighteen million euros, on a deal to twenty thirty-one. Werder officially confirmed the departure on May twenty-third; the presentation at the Sport-Club follows. The reason for the costly move: first-choice Noah Atubolu apparently wants out, linked with Milan, Inter and Atlético. When Freiburg sell an academy product at a profit and bring in the successor, that's exactly the model that made the club strong.

The farewells to the club's veterans, meanwhile, are official. At Thursday's reception Nicolas Höfler, Daniel-Kofi Kyereh and Maximilian Philipp were given their send-off. Höfler ends his pro career after twenty-one years but will play one more season in the reserve side.

Elsewhere the managerial carousel spins on: Union Berlin sign Mauro Lustrinelli, and Cologne make René Wagner permanent. And on Monday the relegation tie reaches its second act: after a goalless first leg, Wolfsburg must win in Paderborn to avoid the first relegation in their history.

In Brazil round seventeen opens today, and with the clásico of all clásicos: Flamengo against Palmeiras at the Maracanã, second versus first, at nine in the evening Brazilian time. Palmeiras lead with thirty-five points, Flamengo have a game in hand and sit virtually just one point back. A home win tonight would make it very tight indeed.

And eyes are already turning to the World Cup. Germany play Finland in Mainz on May thirty-first, then the USA in Chicago. Brazil say goodbye the same day with a match against Panama at the Maracanã, with Neymar in the squad.
