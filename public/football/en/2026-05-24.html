DFB-Pokal final · Kane delivers the Double — on his own

3:0, a hat-trick, a cup — Bayern crown a record season

In the end it was a one-man show. FC Bayern won the 83rd DFB-Pokal final 3:0 over VfB Stuttgart and completed the Double — to an already historic title-winning season (89 points, 122 goals) they now add the cup. It is Bayern's 21st cup win and their first since 2020. The difference was Harry Kane: at 0:0 at the break, he headed home a cross from Michael Olise in the 55th minute, struck again in the 80th and converted a penalty in stoppage time (90+2') for 3:0. A clean final hat-trick for Kane — and the trophy that crowns his season.

The scoreline hides how open the game was for over an hour: Stuttgart (coach Sebastian Hoeneß) kept pace until the break but found no answer up front against the Bayern defence. Once Kane made it 1:0, the match tipped. Opinion: Stuttgart sold themselves dearly and for long spells did not look like a side losing 0:3 — but when your opponent has a striker in this kind of form, "solid" isn't enough. For the VfB a tough season ends without a title; the 2025 cup is history.

Urbig keeps it clean — the World Cup caution pays off

The pre-match talking point was Manuel Neuer's absence (calf trouble), and the fix worked: 22-year-old Jonas Urbig stood in the Bayern goal and kept a clean sheet — a perfect night for the young keeper in arguably the biggest game of his career. Resting Neuer makes double sense in hindsight: the captain, recalled to the World Cup squad shortly before, did not have to be risked for anything, and Urbig delivered. At the other end, Alexander Nübel — also a World Cup traveller — was in goal; both keepers are in Nagelsmann's squad.

A footnote from Berlin: early in the second half there was a brief interruption when fans of both sides staged a joint pyro protest against the DFB. There were no sporting consequences and play resumed after a few minutes.

SC Freiburg · The record transfer is done

Backhaus official — the most expensive signing in club history

The rumour has become a done deal: both clubs confirmed on 23 May the transfer of goalkeeper Mio Backhaus (21), a Germany U21 keeper, from Werder Bremen to SC Freiburg. The fee is around €15m base, rising to €18m with bonuses — the most expensive signing in Freiburg's history. The contract runs through 2031. At Werder, Estonia's Karl Hein (from Arsenal) steps in as the new number one.

The pricey move is explained by the situation behind Backhaus: Noah Atubolu, until now the undisputed number one and an academy product, reportedly wants to leave this summer, per Sport1 and Goal (as of 23 May) — his contract runs to 2027, an extension is open, and the names circulating are Inter Milan, AC Milan and Atlético Madrid. That is a rumour as of today, but Backhaus is clearly lined up as his successor. Opinion: paying a club-record fee for a 21-year-old while letting the young first-choice keeper leave for potentially double — that is exactly the model that made Freiburg strong: develop, sell at a profit, sign the next one. The only thing that has to be clean is the timing, so the SC isn't left without a proven keeper before August's Conference League play-off.

Bundesliga · Relegation settled on Monday

Paderborn — Wolfsburg, 2nd leg: all open after 0:0

The season's final Bundesliga act comes on Monday, 25 May, at 20:30 CEST / 15:30 BRT (SAT.1, free to air): the relegation 2nd leg SC Paderborn — VfL Wolfsburg. After the 0:0 first leg it is wide open — Wolfsburg (Bundesliga 16th) want to avoid the first relegation in their history, Paderborn (2nd-division 3rd) want to complete promotion.

Relegation play-off: the decider for the last Bundesliga place. The 16th-placed Bundesliga side plays the 3rd-placed 2nd-division side over two legs — whoever scores more across both stays (or becomes) top flight. Like a tie-break when 34 rounds still haven't settled who goes down.

Paderborn must do without Jonah Sticker — he picked up a second yellow in stoppage time of the first leg and is suspended. Another draw over 90 minutes would mean extra time, then a penalty shootout.

Germany NT · The World Cup rehearsals are set

Finland in Mainz first, then the USA in Chicago

The road map to the 2026 World Cup is clear: Germany's squad gathers on 27 May in Herzogenaurach, the last home friendly is Germany — Finland on 31 May (20:45 CEST / 15:45 BRT, MEWA Arena Mainz, sold out, ZDF). Then it's across the Atlantic: on 6 June in Chicago (Soldier Field) comes the final World Cup test against the USA (20:30 CEST / 15:30 BRT). Head coach Julian Nagelsmann has named a 26-man squad, with Joshua Kimmich as captain and Manuel Neuer back as number one. The final list must be submitted to FIFA by 5 June at the latest.

Brazil · Palmeiras beat Flamengo and pull away

Sat 23 May Flamengo 0:3 Palmeiras — a red card tips the clásico early

The top match of round 17 turned one-sided: Palmeiras won 3:0 at Flamengo's Maracanã. Goals: Flaco López 37', Allan 56', Paulinho 90'. The turning point came early — Flamengo's Carrascal saw a straight red around the 20th minute, and the hosts played over an hour a man down, barely threatening up front. Palmeiras (now 38 points) thus stretch their lead to seven points — though Flamengo (31, a game in hand) stay virtually closer than the table shows.

Seleção · Farewell match set, Neymar included

Brazil — Panama on 31 May at the Maracanã

Brazil's last home game before the World Cup is set: on 31 May the Seleção host Panama at the Maracanã (18:30 BRT / 23:30 CEST). It is the farewell on home soil before the tournament opens in June in the USA, Mexico and Canada. The 26-man squad named by coach Carlo Ancelotti stands — the headline remains the return of Neymar Jr. (Santos), called up for the first time in over two years.
