SC Freiburg · the transfer summer revolves around the goal

The Atubolu poker: Backhaus signed, now Freiburg wait for the offer

At Freiburg the loudest summer storyline is a goalkeeping double. Record signing Mio Backhaus (22) from Werder Bremen has been official since 23 May — roughly €12–15m base, with bonuses up to €18m, contract to 2031, the most expensive arrival in club history. That answers the background question before it was even asked aloud: first-choice keeper Noah Atubolu (contract to June 2027) reportedly wants to leave this summer, per Sport1 (as of 23 May), with an extension on ice. A broad list of suitors is doing the rounds — AC Milan, Inter, Brighton, Tottenham, Atlético Madrid — with the asking price said to have slid from ~€30m (February) to ~€20–25m of late. This is still a rumour, not a done deal.

Opinion: This is exactly the Freiburg model — develop, sell at a profit, have the successor already in the building. Signing a 22-year-old for a club record while letting the young first-choice keeper move for potentially double makes sound business sense. The only risk factor is timing: ahead of the Conference League playoff in August, the club mustn't suddenly find itself without a proven keeper if Atubolu leaves late and Backhaus's settling-in period is short.

Höfler leaves the first-team squad — one more year with the reserves

A club institution steps aside: Nicolas Höfler (36), for over a decade the metronome of Freiburg's midfield, reportedly leaves the first-team squad at season's end. Rather than retiring, he is set to stay on one more year with the reserves (Regionalliga) as an experienced guide. The lost Europa League final in Istanbul (0:3 to Aston Villa, 20 May) would thus have been his last professional game — a bitter but grand full stop for a player who carried Freiburg's rise from yo-yo club to European regular over many years.

Bundesliga · the season's final decision

Paderborn — Wolfsburg, second leg: 90 minutes between class and relegation

The very last Bundesliga act of 2025/26 kicks off tonight at 20:30 MESZ / 15:30 BRT (SAT.1, free-to-air): the relegation second leg SC Paderborn — VfL Wolfsburg. After the goalless first leg (0:0, Thu 21 May) the situation is wide open. For Wolfsburg, continuously top-flight since 1997, the first relegation in club history is on the table; Paderborn (third in the 2. Liga) want back into the Bundesliga for the first time since 2014/15.

Relegation playoff: the decisive two-legged duel for the last Bundesliga spot. The 16th-placed Bundesliga side plays the third-placed 2. Liga team home and away — whoever has more goals over the two legs combined is (or stays) in the top flight. Like a tie-breaker when 34 rounds haven't settled who goes down.

Paderborn must do without Jonah Sticker — he picked up a second yellow in stoppage time of the first leg and is suspended. Wolfsburg's Yannick Gerhardt summed up the mood after the 0:0: "We'd have liked to win — now we have a final on Monday." That's exactly it: another draw after 90 minutes means extra time, then penalties. Opinion: A 0:0 first leg is gold for the second-division side — at home Paderborn need only one goal to pile maximum pressure on Wolfsburg. Favourite status helps the VfL little tonight; relegation games live on nerves, not league positions.

Germany NT · World Cup squad set, now the warm-ups

First Finland in Mainz, then the USA in Chicago

The road map to the 2026 World Cup is fixed: head coach Julian Nagelsmann named his 26-man World Cup squad on 21 May — Joshua Kimmich as captain, the returning Manuel Neuer (40) as No. 1, with Nübel and Baumann behind him. Surprises: young Lennart Karl (Bayern) and Leroy Sané (Galatasaray) are in; ter Stegen and Gnabry are out injured, while Füllkrug, Adeyemi and Freiburg's Matthias Ginter were not selected.

The team meets on 27 May in Herzogenaurach — the start was pushed back slightly because of the DFB-Pokal final (23 May, involving Bayern and Stuttgart players). The last home warm-up is Germany — Finland on 31 May (20:45 MESZ / 15:45 BRT, MEWA Arena Mainz, sold out, ZDF). The final World Cup test follows on 6 June against the USA in Chicago (20:30 MESZ / 15:30 BRT, Soldier Field). Both games are pure World Cup warm-ups — the definitive list is due to FIFA by 5 June at the latest.

Brazil · round 17 complete — Athletico push upward

Sun 24 May — Athletico into the G4, Bragantino sweep Vasco aside

The Brasileirão Sunday brought four results and a small shift at the top. Remo 1:2 Athletico Paranaense: the hosts led through Jajá (13'), but Viveros turned it around with a brace (44', 52') — and Remo's Jajá was sent off with a red before half-time. Athletico thus climb to fourth, right into the Libertadores group spots (G4). Vasco 0:3 Red Bull Bragantino: Rodriguinho (45'), Pitta (60') and a late third — a dominant 90-minute display by the visitors at São Januário.

Plus Cruzeiro 2:1 Chapecoense (Kaio Jorge from the spot 26', Sinisterra 72'; a late consolation for the visitors) and Corinthians 1:0 Atlético-MG via a late substitute strike from Zakaria Labyad (87'). Tonight Coritiba — Bahia (20:00 BRT / 01:00 MESZ Tue) close out the round — a direct duel for the upper-mid table, both on 23 points.
