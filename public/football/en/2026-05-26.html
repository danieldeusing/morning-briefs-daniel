SC Freiburg · The Atubolu exit goes on the record

Saier: "We told Noah shortly before the final"

At Freiburg the loudest summer storyline is still the goalkeeper — and yesterday it got an official voice. Sporting director Jochen Saier spoke clearly for the first time on 26 May about the departure of first-choice keeper Noah Atubolu (under contract to June 2027): the club had pushed for an extension for half a year, then chose clarity and told Atubolu "shortly before the Europa League final" that they would let him go this summer. Saier spoke of a "special relationship" and of Atubolu now being able to "plan his future with his advisers."

So the direction is official — the actual market is not. There is no agreed fee and no binding offer (as of 26 May). Reports still cite a wide list of suitors (AC Milan, Inter, Atlético Madrid, Brighton, Tottenham among them) and an asking price now of ~€15–20M (down from ~€30M in February). The successor is long since signed: Mio Backhaus (22, from Werder Bremen, contract to 2031) is locked in as a record signing, reportedly around €15M base, up to ~€18M with bonuses.

Opinion: Saier's candour is smart. Rather than risk a limbo summer with an unhappy first-choice keeper, Freiburg create clarity early — for themselves, for Atubolu, for Backhaus's settling in. It's exactly the Freiburg model: develop, sell at a profit, have the successor already in the building. The one risk factor is timing — the club must not suddenly be without a tested keeper before the Conference League playoff in August.

Höfler out of the pro squad — Philipp and Kyereh too

The squad overhaul has a face: Nicolas Höfler (36), for over a decade the metronome of Freiburg's midfield (383 competitive games), leaves the pro squad and stays a year with the reserves (Regionalliga) as an experienced mentor. He was specially honoured at the fan reception after the EL final. According to the club's official site, Maximilian Philipp and Kofi Kyereh are also leaving the pro squad. The lost final in Istanbul (0-3 to Aston Villa, 20 May) was thus the closing chapter of an era for several veterans at once.

Germany NT · World Cup prep begins

Gather in Herzogenaurach, then Finland and the USA

The road to World Cup 2026 is under way. Head coach Julian Nagelsmann named his 26-man squad on 21 May — Joshua Kimmich as captain, the returning Manuel Neuer (40) as number one, with Nübel and Baumann behind. The team gathers on 27 May in Herzogenaurach; the start was deliberately pushed back because of the DFB-Pokal final (23 May, involving Bayern and Stuttgart players). Kai Havertz joins later after the Champions League final.

The first warm-up is the last home friendly, Germany — Finland on 31 May (20:45 CEST / 15:45 BRT, MEWA Arena Mainz, ZDF). The final test follows on 6 June against the USA in Chicago (20:30 CEST / 15:30 BRT, Soldier Field). Both are pure World Cup warm-ups; the tournament opens on 13 June in the USA, Canada and Mexico.

Bundesliga · Wolfsburg fall, Paderborn rise

Paderborn 2-1 Wolfsburg a.e.t. — the 14th minute cost the VfL their place

The very last Bundesliga act of 2025/26 turned into a small drama. In the relegation second leg in Paderborn the favoured VfL Wolfsburg started perfectly: Dzenan Pejčinović scored as early as the 3rd minute for 0-1. Then everything flipped — Joakim Mæhle was shown a second yellow in the 14th minute, and Wolfsburg had to play more than 75 minutes plus extra time a man down. Filip Bilbija equalised before half-time (38'), and in extra time Laurin Curda sealed the 2-1 and promotion in the 100th minute. No penalty shootout was needed.

Relegation playoff: the decider for the last Bundesliga place. The team 16th in the Bundesliga plays the 3rd-placed side from the 2. Bundesliga over two legs — whoever has more goals across both games is (or stays) in the top flight. Here: first leg 0-0, second leg 2-1 for Paderborn → Paderborn go up, Wolfsburg go down.

For Wolfsburg an era ends: 29 straight years in the Bundesliga (since 1997) are over, and it is the first relegation in the club's history. Paderborn, by contrast, return to the top flight after six years — for the first time since the 2019/20 season. Coach Ralf Kettemann (38), who only took over on 1 July 2025 and goes up in his first pro season, put it simply: "It's just awesome." Opinion: the early lead was almost a trap — the red card shifted the whole game. Wolfsburg were a man short for most of it and had no answer; Paderborn's energy display was deserved in the end. Relegation lives on nerves, not league positions — proof delivered.

Brazil · round 17 complete, cup draw today

Coritiba turn 0-2 into 3-2 against Bahia

The close of round 17 on Monday night (Couto Pereira) had everything: Coritiba 3-2 Bahia. The visitors led through Erick Pulga (27') and were ahead at the break, but Coritiba flipped the game in a strong second half — Bruno Melo (55'), Lavega (64') and Breno Lopes (67') turned 0-2 into 3-2, with Everaldo (90') only pulling one back for Bahia. With their seventh win of the season Coritiba climb to sixth (26 pts), just a point off the Libertadores group places (G4). Bahia stay on 23 points (eighth, a game in hand).

At the top Palmeiras remain comfortably clear (38 pts, +7 on Flamengo, who have a game in hand). At the bottom Chapecoense are stuck on a single win all season (9 pts). At midday today (11:00 BRT / 16:00 CEST) the CBF in Rio draw the Copa do Brasil round of 16 — no seeding, anyone can meet anyone; first legs 1–2 Aug, second legs 5–6 Aug. The ties were not yet set at press time.
