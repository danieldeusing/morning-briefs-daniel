Germany · World Cup camp opens today

Herzogenaurach, then Finland and the USA

Today the hot run-up to the World Cup begins for Germany: the DFB moves into the adidas campus in Herzogenaurach on May 27. Head coach Julian Nagelsmann deliberately pushed the start back two days — the German Cup final on May 23 tied up Bayern and Stuttgart players, and Nagelsmann preferred to give them two extra days with family rather than have them "sit around in Herzogenaurach" with no chance to train. The 26-man squad named on May 21 has Joshua Kimmich as captain and the returning Manuel Neuer (40) as No.1. Kai Havertz joins only after the Champions League final.

The CL final is on Sat May 30 (18:00 CEST / 13:00 BRT, Budapest) between PSG and Arsenal — and since Havertz plays for Arsenal, he arrives at camp later. The first dress rehearsal is the sold-out home friendly Germany vs Finland on Sun May 31 (20:45 CEST / 15:45 BRT, MEWA Arena Mainz, ZDF). On June 2 the party flies to Chicago, where the final test against the USA (Soldier Field) follows on June 6.

Sky Sport (May 25) · dfb.de squad · uefa.com CL final

World Cup Group E: Curaçao, Ivory Coast, Ecuador

In the draw on December 5, 2025, Germany was seeded as group head into Group E — the only European team in the group. The opponents: Curaçao (Sun Jun 14, 19:00 CEST / 14:00 BRT, Houston), Ivory Coast (Sat Jun 20, 22:00 CEST / 17:00 BRT, Toronto) and Ecuador (Thu Jun 25, 22:00 CEST / 17:00 BRT, New York/New Jersey).

Curaçao: a tiny Caribbean island (around 150,000 people, part of the Kingdom of the Netherlands) — and a World Cup debutant. For scale, that's about the population of a mid-size city. It would make Curaçao the smallest nation ever to reach a World Cup finals. Germany has never played a competitive match against them.

SC Freiburg · goalkeeper site and rebuild

Atubolu exit framed, Backhaus is the new No.1

Freiburg's loudest summer storyline is still in goal. Sporting director Jochen Saier said clearly for the first time on May 26 that the club is letting Noah Atubolu (contract to June 2027) go this summer: they had courted an extension for half a year, then told him "shortly before the Europa League final" that he is free to leave. No fee is agreed and no binding offer is on the table (as of May 27). A broad list of suitors (incl. AC Milan, Brighton, Tottenham, Atlético Madrid) and a price tag around €15–20m are still being reported — but those names and figures are rumor (per bulinews/OneFootball, as of late April), not an official process.

The successor was secured long ago: Mio Backhaus (22, Germany U21 keeper, from Werder Bremen) is officially confirmed as the new No.1 (club announcement May 23). The club did not officially state a fee; media (incl. Sky) report a club-record fee of around €15m and a contract to 2031.

fussballtransfers.com (May 26) · Sky Sport · scfreiburg.com

Höfler, Philipp and Kyereh out of the pro squad

The generational change has a face: Nicolas Höfler (36), the metronome of Freiburg's midfield for over a decade, leaves the pro squad and adds one more year with the reserves. Per the official club page (the "Danke Chicco, Milli und Kofi!" note, May 22), Maximilian Philipp and Daniel-Kofi Kyereh also leave the pro squad. The lost EL final in Istanbul (0:3 to Aston Villa, May 20) marked the end of an era for several veterans.

scfreiburg.com · badische-zeitung.de

Transfer ticker: BVB keen on Moreira — no deal done

With the window open (May–Aug) the market is picking up, but mostly at the rumor stage. At Borussia Dortmund, reports (as of May 24–25) say personal terms are agreed with Diego Moreira (Racing Strasbourg); the fee (~€38–43m) is still open — which would be a BVB record. Read: as of today it's a rumor/"agreed", no official deal (no medical, no club announcement). Moreira is under contract at Strasbourg until 2029. The only confirmed Bundesliga move right now is Backhaus to Freiburg.

Bundesliga · final table 2025/26

League season complete, 16th place decided by the relegation playoff: VfL Wolfsburg went down on May 25 after 29 years in the Bundesliga (2:1 a.e.t. for Paderborn in the return leg), and SC Paderborn returns to the top flight for the first time since 2019/20. Daniel's SC Freiburg finishes seventh and is in the Conference League via the cup mechanism.

Brazil · Copa round of 16 drawn, R18 this weekend

Copa do Brasil: Fluminense vs Vasco and Palmeiras vs Fortaleza

On May 26 in Rio, the CBF drew the Copa do Brasil round of 16 — unseeded, anyone could meet anyone. The eight ties (the second-named club hosts the return leg):

  • Fluminense vs Vasco da Gama (Carioca derby)
  • Palmeiras vs Fortaleza
  • Chapecoense vs Cruzeiro
  • Internacional vs Corinthians
  • Mirassol vs Grêmio
  • Athletico-PR vs Vitória
  • Atlético-MG vs Juventude
  • Santos vs Remo

The first legs are on Aug 1–2, the second legs on Aug 5–6. The headline is the Carioca derby Fluminense vs Vasco; title favorite Palmeiras drew an awkward opponent in Fortaleza.

Brasileirão R18: last round before the World Cup break

The weekend brings Round 18 — the last league action before the World Cup break. Leader Palmeiras (38 pts) hosts bottom side Chapecoense on Sun May 31 (21:00 CEST / 16:00 BRT); Flamengo already faces in-form Coritiba on Sat May 30 (21:00 CEST / 16:00 BRT), the side that recently turned a 0:2 into a 3:2 win over Bahia. The late Sunday cracker Cruzeiro vs Fluminense runs to the edge of midnight for Daniel (20:30 BRT / 01:30 CEST on Mon Jun 1).

Seleção · reporting today, farewell match with Neymar

Granja Comary opens, then Panama at the Maracanã

Brazil's World Cup prep also starts today: the Seleção reports to Granja Comary (Teresópolis) for medicals on May 27. The 26-man squad named by Carlo Ancelotti on May 18 brings the big comeback of Neymar Jr. (Santos) — back for the first time since October 2023. Out injured are Éder Militão, Rodrygo and Estêvão, among others; in goal, the call-up of Weverton was a surprise.

Brazil's last home game before the World Cup is Brazil vs Panama on Sun May 31 (18:30 BRT / 23:30 CEST, Maracanã, sold out). The final test follows on June 6 vs Egypt in Cleveland; Brazil's World Cup begins on June 13 vs Morocco (Group C, with Haiti and Scotland).
