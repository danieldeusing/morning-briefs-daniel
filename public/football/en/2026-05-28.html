DFB National Team · Day 1 in Herzogenaurach

11 o'clock, 21 degrees, all 26 — except Havertz

Today at 11:00 CEST / 06:00 BRT, Julian Nagelsmann's 26-man squad took the pitch together as a World Cup collective for the first time — on the training ground right next to their team base Home Ground on the adidas campus in Herzogenaurach. Per DFB team ticker, under sunny 21 °C, partially live on DFB.TV. Complete except for Kai Havertz, who is still in the Champions League final with Arsenal and won't join until after Saturday. Jonas Urbig (Bayern) is the new fourth goalkeeper and is using the training camp as a learning year.

At 14:30 CEST / 09:30 BRT, the opening press conference with Nagelsmann and sporting director Rudi Völler is scheduled — Völler's fifth World Cup in an official capacity. Nagelsmann set the tone yesterday: the camp should behave "like a really good family," an image he deliberately sets against the pressure of the coming weeks. Training happens Thu, Fri, Sat in Herzogenaurach, on Saturday the delegation travels to Mainz / Frankfurt, and on Tuesday 06/02 it's by plane to Chicago.

dfb.de Team Ticker · sportschau.de · bundesliga.com

Neuer rests his calf — Baumann for Finland

The headline of the day is the second one Manuel Neuer has produced in two weeks: after the cancelled cup final due to calf muscle issues, the 40-year-old also won't be in goal Sunday in Mainz against Finland. "We decided in the coaching staff that he won't play on Sunday," WEB.DE live blog quoted Nagelsmann. Instead, Oliver Baumann (Hoffenheim) starts — he was already number one in the cup dress rehearsal and is the nominal backup this summer. Nagelsmann left no doubt about Neuer's role: "With Manu we're better than without Manu" — so clear hierarchy, but rest for the calf. Realistic goal: fit for World Cup start against Curaçao on 06/14; the USA test on 06/06 in Chicago is the next step.

"Calf muscle issues": a small tear or muscle strain in the calf. If you stress it too early, you'll be out for weeks — if you rest it a few days, it's usually quickly able to bear load again. That's exactly what the DFB coaching staff is weighing now: two weeks before the World Cup start, a mandatory appearance in a test against Finland makes no sense if it costs Neuer for Houston.

Heat test in subtext

A detail that could be front-page news in 17 days: today's session ran at ~21 °C, the World Cup opening on 06/14 is in Houston with reported ~40 °C + high humidity. In the DFB apparatus, the hottest talking point right now isn't tactics, but pacing and substitution management — five substitutions + a FIFA cooling break at defined temperature are allowed.

SC Freiburg · Stillness with Substance

Goalkeeper situation sorted, rest waits

After Jochen Saier's unusually frank take yesterday, the situation on Atubolu / Backhaus is clear: Mio Backhaus (22, ex-Werder) has been officially the new number one since 05/23, contract through 2031 — Sky reports a club record fee around ~15 million €, a figure not confirmed by the club. Noah Atubolu is allowed to leave, renewal is off the table, but a binding offer has not arrived. Daniel's reading stays the same: Freiburg isn't a supplicant here — Atubolu has a contract through 2027, and the reported ~15–20 million € are a defensible ask after the Europa League season. Whoever won't pay that can keep scouting.

In the greeting interview from 05/23, Backhaus delivered the sentence that sums up Freiburg logic: "A door has opened, one where I feel I fit exactly right." UEFA Champions League Final · PSG — Arsenal

Budapest on Saturday — and why both World Cup camps care

On Sat 05/30 at 18:00 CEST / 13:00 BRT, the final between Paris Saint-Germain (defending champion) and Arsenal (first final since 2006) kicks off at the Puskás Arena in Budapest. From the DFB perspective, Kai Havertz is the sole directly affected player — he's absent from Nagelsmann's camp until Sunday and won't join until the Mainz leg. From the Brazilian perspective, the overlap is threefold: Marquinhos (PSG captain, third Champions League final appearance) plus Gabriel Magalhães and Gabriel Martinelli (both Arsenal) miss the Maracanã home match against Panama entirely and won't join the Seleção until the Egypt test on 06/06 in Cleveland. For both managers, that means: Sunday test as stress test, not as final form — the key players are absent.

Sportily, Arsenal's weaker form-season stretch meets better defense league-phase stats (per UEFA including a club-record-equaling clean-sheet mark by Raya); PSG has Khvicha Kvaratskhelia (10 goals, 6 assists) as the competition's defining player. For Daniel as a DFB fan, the applause level is clear: an Arsenal triumph arrives with fit Havertz four days later in Mainz.

Seleção · Day 1 Without Neymar

Calf swelling, imaging, no update until results are in

Brazil's World Cup camp started yesterday (Wed 05/27) in Granja Comary (Teresópolis) — and immediately produced the headline Carlo Ancelotti saw coming. Neymar Jr. (Santos) arrived by private helicopter to the training campus but was absent from the first on-pitch session and was immediately sent for imaging exams at a private clinic in Teresópolis. Diagnosis per Brazilian reports: a 2-mm swelling in the right calf — medically classed as "mild," but highly sensitive so close to the World Cup. The CBF has imposed an information blackout: no updates until exams are complete.

His appearance in the home farewell match against Panama on Sun 05/31 (18:30 BRT / 23:30 CEST, Maracanã) is thus open. In an uncomplicated course, it's enough for a substitute role; in any complication, the USA test against Egypt on 06/06 in Cleveland becomes the realistic return date. Ancelotti's line is now known: no PR, no pressure — on 05/15, he'd deliberately nominated Neymar despite his tough Santos season, now he's protecting him. Without Neymar, the dress rehearsal front remains with Vinícius Jr., Raphinha, and Rodrygo stand-in (Estêvão injured) — so much to test without it already being final form.

Three starters missing Panama — arriving from Budapest

Another lineup question resolves itself: Marquinhos (PSG), Gabriel Magalhães, and Gabriel Martinelli (both Arsenal) are bound to the Saturday Champions League final — and travel only after Budapest to the US, arriving no sooner than shortly before the Egypt match on 06/06 to join the Seleção. Consequence: the Maracanã match against Panama runs without the three likely defensive and offensive pillars of the World Cup team — Ancelotti can't read the dress rehearsal as a World Cup starting-formation adaptation test, but rather as a test for the broader squad. For Daniel as a "BR fan from distance," that's the only fair reading of Sunday's 90 minutes: see what the rest of the squad can do, don't expect the full World Cup matchday.

It's Thursday, May twenty-eighth, the first real training day of World Cup preparation. Today at eleven in the morning Central European time, six o'clock in the morning your time, Julian Nagelsmann brought his twenty-six-man squad onto the training pitch in Herzogenaurach for the first time. Under sunny twenty-one degrees. Complete except for Kai Havertz, who plays in the Champions League final with Arsenal on Saturday and won't join until after that.

The headline of the day is Manuel Neuer. The forty-year-old continues to have muscle problems in his calf and won't be in goal Sunday in Mainz against Finland. Instead Oliver Baumann of Hoffenheim starts. Nagelsmann left no doubt about Neuer's role — with Manu they're better than without, he said — but two weeks before the World Cup start against Curaçao, it's simply not smart to risk him now in a test match. The real stress test is Houston in two weeks, where temperatures of forty degrees Celsius are expected in the stadium with brutal humidity.

In parallel, Brazil's World Cup preparation also began yesterday at Granja Comary, in Teresópolis north of Rio. And there too the most important player was immediately absent. Neymar arrived by private helicopter to the training campus but didn't participate in the first on-pitch session. A two-millimeter swelling in his right calf. Imaging at a private clinic in Teresópolis. Carlo Ancelotti is being cautious, the Brazilian football federation has imposed an information blackout until the exams are complete. His appearance in the farewell match against Panama on Sunday at Maracanã is open.

Both camps are waiting for Saturday. In the Champions League final in Budapest, Paris Saint-Germain plays Arsenal — kickoff six in the afternoon Central European time, one o'clock in the afternoon your time. For Germany it's the player Kai Havertz who is tied up there; for Brazil it's three key starters: Marquinhos on the Paris side, Gabriel Magalhães and Gabriel Martinelli with Arsenal. All three miss the Panama match completely and won't join the Seleção until the USA test on June sixth in Cleveland. So for both managers, the dress rehearsals on Sunday are not a lineup test but a stress test for the broader squad.

At your SC Freiburg, after Jochen Saier's statement yesterday, everything is sorted regarding Atubolu and Backhaus. Mio Backhaus has been officially the new number one since May twenty-third, contract through twenty thirty-one, reported fee around fifteen million euros. Noah Atubolu is allowed to leave, but no binding offer has arrived — Freiburg isn't begging here. Otherwise the summer is quiet in Freiburg.

On the Bundesliga transfer market, the lines remain from yesterday. Borussia Dortmund has agreed with Diego Moreira from Strasbourg on personal terms, but the fee remains open — Strasbourg demands thirty-eight million euros base plus five million in bonuses. At Bayern, the Gordon situation is tilting toward Barcelona; the Catalans have the edge on personal conditions. Bayern is holding the pain threshold at seventy million euros.

Over the weekend, round eighteen runs as the last Brasileirão action before the World Cup break. League leader Palmeiras faces last-place Chapecoense on Sunday, Flamengo meets rising-form Coritiba on Saturday. The Sunday showdown Cruzeiro versus Fluminense starts for you at half past eight at night — for German eyes half past one in the morning.
