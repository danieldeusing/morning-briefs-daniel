Seleção · Shock diagnosis Neymar

From "swelling" to Grade 2 tear — and Panama + Egypt ruled out

Yesterday evening (Thu 28.05.), Dr. Rodrigo Lasmar, longtime Seleção physician, addressed the press at Granja Comary. The MRI images from the clinic in Teresópolis were clear: "Not just swelling — a Grade 2 muscle tear" in Neymar Jr.'s right calf. Expected recovery time two to three weeks. That settles the question mark that hung yesterday: Neymar will miss both the farewell home match against Panama (Sun 31.05., 23:30 MESZ / 18:30 BRT, Maracanã) and the USA friendly against Egypt (Sat 06.06., Cleveland). That shifts the one-question season: Can he make it to the World Cup opener against Morocco on 13.06.?

Word from the CBF apparatus: at best a close call. With a three-week rehab starting today, return would fall on 18./19.06. — the day of the second group game against Haiti. FIFA requires final squad submission by Monday 01.06., after which it only allows changes up to 24 hours before the first match. That 24-hour window is precisely Ancelotti's safety net: if Neymar regains fitness by the Morocco date, he'll be added back; if not, CBF can call up a replacement in the final hour without having to cut him beforehand.

exame.com · otempo.com.br · WEB.DE Live Blog

Why CBF keeps him — even with Lasmar's tight timeline

The obvious maximum question — drop him, call up a fit player — CBF answers explicitly with "no". Three reasons showing through in statements from Granja Comary: (1) Ancelotti's style: no media spectacle, no premature pressure that would destabilize the squad. (2) The knockout stage, not the group, is what matters sportively — the group with Morocco, Haiti, and Morocco's second-place opponent is manageable; Neymar won't win it alone, but could make the difference in the round of 16. (3) Squad solidarity: per Exame, teammates stand united behind keeping him. That means for Sunday's dress rehearsal: Ancelotti will test a front with Vinicius Jr., Raphinha, and Rodrygo (Estêvão injured) — without plan-A striker, without the plan-A defensive core Marquinhos / Gabriel Magalhães (tied up in Champions League final), without plan-A winger Martinelli. The 90 minutes against Panama are thus no World Cup lineup test, but a squad depth check.

"Grade 2 injury": Muscle injuries are rated on three levels — Grade 1 is minor fiber strain (1–2 weeks), Grade 2 is partial fiber tear (2–4 weeks), Grade 3 is complete tear (8+ weeks, often requiring surgery). Grade 2 means: noticeable damage, but conservatively treatable — the calf can become match-fit by the knockout stage through rehab, load management, and patience. The risk isn't in healing, but in returning too soon — then Grade 2 quickly becomes Grade 3.

Clinic subplot: Secrecy, locked facility, leaked images

A side note that's raising eyebrows in Brazil: Per Sport1 research, Neymar's examination at the private clinic in Teresópolis yesterday took roughly 90 minutes, the diagnostic center closed early for it, medical staff signed confidentiality agreements — yet the diagnosis leaked by evening. Brazilian press described Neymar after results as "extremely downcast". The question circulating in Comary now is as much political as it is sportive: how much information does CBF want to share before the final squad submission on 01.06.? Today will show whether Lasmar sets up a daily status rhythm — Brazilian press is demanding it, the Comary line so far has been information lockdown.

NADA doping test Thursday evening: ten players tested

What appeared as a note in the team ticker is soberly a routine event yet also a symbolically significant moment: Thursday evening staff from NADA (German Anti-Doping Agency) showed up unannounced at the home ground, the DFB noted it directly in the official ticker. Four players had to provide urine and blood, six others urine only — all tests are part of the standard program before every World Cup. Sportively relevant only because sample collection after intense initial load with exhausted leg muscles can be uncomfortable; medically not risky. The reading: don't fret, just file it away as evidence that DFB is running textbook preparation.

UEFA Champions League Final · PSG — Arsenal (tomorrow)

Budapest on Saturday — and why both World Cup camps are watching

On Sat 30.05. at 18:00 MESZ / 13:00 BRT defending champion PSG faces Premier League champion Arsenal (first final since 2006) in the Champions League final at the Puskás Arena in Budapest. Weather per AOL: dry, ~28 °C — no heat battle, but warm exertion. From DFB perspective, Kai Havertz is the only directly affected player — he'll join the squad after Budapest for the Mainz arrival. From Brazil's perspective, the overlap is triple: Marquinhos (PSG captain, third Champions League final), Gabriel Magalhães, and Gabriel Martinelli (both Arsenal) will completely miss the Maracanã home match against Panama and only rejoin the Seleção for the Egypt friendly on 06.06. in Cleveland.

Sportively: Arsenal comes in with eight wins from eight league phase matches and only four goals conceded — best league defense (26 conceded, Premier League record) with Saliba + Gabriel Magalhães centrally and David Raya with 19 clean sheets (club record-equalling). PSG took a different path to the final: 11th after league phase, then 5–4 vs. Monaco in playoffs, 8–2 vs. Chelsea, 4–0 vs. Liverpool, and 6–5 aggregate in the semifinal vs. Bayern — the defining player was Khvicha Kvaratskhelia (10 goals, 6 assists). For Daniel as a DFB fan, the reading is clear: an Arsenal triumph arrives with a victory-high but empty Havertz four days later in Mainz. A loss produces an empty, frustrated Havertz — both are already factored into the DFB cockpit.

Transfer Market · Gordon to Barcelona, Bayern out

Anthony Gordon: ~80 million euros, 5 years, medical exam Thursday

The headline on the European market Wednesday night, confirmed Thursday: Anthony Gordon (25, Newcastle, English international) moves to FC Barcelona. Per Sky Sports Newcastle and Barcelona agreed on Wed 27.05. to a fee of £69.3 million / ~80 million euros including add-ons (base 70 million euros + 10 million euros add-ons). Gordon underwent medical exam Thursday 28.05. in Barcelona, the contract runs five years, annual salary per weltfussball around 17 million euros — nearly double his Newcastle wage. The announcement comes likely before England's A-team mandatory training camp for the World Cup.

Bayern reading: At around 70 million euros Munich had set the pain threshold — when Barcelona called 80, the club dropped out of the bidding war. The official reason ("no price arms race") aligns with the consistent Eberl/Freund line of years: hard price ceiling, prefer plan B to preparation-killing last-minute coup. Plan B: Per fussballtransfers.com Bayern are checking out Mika Godts (20, left winger, Ajax, contract through 2029) — cheaper, significantly younger, profile fits; per Falk however "not first choice". Daniel's reading: sensible exit, but the problem (elite winger right/left for Champions League readiness) remains — Bayern must deliver by September.

SC Freiburg · Atubolu market intensifies

Premier League + Milan + Atlético — no binding offer yet

Daniel's reading remains yesterday's: the club is no supplicant here. In the background the club holds Vasilios Scherpen (Brighton backup, 2.06 m) as plan-B emergency option on the list — per kicker the most plausible alternative if Backhaus needs a backup keeper under him.
