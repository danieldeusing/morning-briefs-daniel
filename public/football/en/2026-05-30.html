UEFA Champions League final · PSG — Arsenal (today)

Budapest, 18:00 CEST / 13:00 BRT — two contrasting paths to the final

Today Sat 30.05. at 18:00 CEST / 13:00 BRT the Champions League final kicks off at the Puskás Aréna in Budapest: defending champion PSG against the freshly crowned Premier League champion Arsenal. For Daniel a rare luxury — no midnight kickoff, but early Saturday afternoon in Brazil. Weather forecast is dry with ~28 °C, so no heat drama, but still warm for the pre-match.

The two paths to the final could hardly be more different. Arsenal is the only team to have remained unbeaten throughout the entire CL season: eight wins from eight matches in the league phase, plus their first final since losing to Barcelona in 2006 — and that just eleven days after claiming their first English title in 22 years (secured 19.05. under Arteta). PSG, by contrast, came through a gauntlet: 11th place in the 36-club league phase forced Paris into the playoff round, from there through multiple aggregate thrillers until the 6:5 semi-final aggregate against Bayern Munich. A quirk for Daniel as a Bundesliga fan: this very Bayern exit is what made Paris's final ticket possible.

uefa.com Match Center · Al Jazeera Preview · olympics.com

Team news: Hakimi doubtful at PSG, Timber returns for Arsenal

The biggest Paris concern is at right-back: Achraf Hakimi has carried a moderate thigh/hamstring injury since the Bayern semi-final and could miss the match — Warren Zaire-Emery stands by as a makeshift option on the right flank. There's also a fitness question around Ballon d'Or winner Ousmane Dembélé (he stands at 19 goals plus 11 assists across 39 matches), while Nuno Mendes should start despite a thigh niggle. At Arsenal, the day's good news is Jurrien Timber returning from his ankle injury (missed 14 matches since mid-March), trained this week and flew to Budapest. The only certain absence for the Gunners is Ben White.

"League phase" (new CL format): Since 2024, the Champions League no longer plays classic four-team groups but instead one table of 36 clubs — each plays eight different opponents. The top 8 go straight to the round of 16, places 9–24 must go through a playoff round, place 25 and below are out. PSG's 11th place means: two extra rounds of stress compared to Arsenal, who finished first and went straight through.

Why both World Cup camps are rooting — and suffering

From a German perspective, exactly one player takes the field: Kai Havertz (Arsenal). The DFB delegation moves today from Herzogenaurach to Frankfurt and watches the final in the hotel — explicitly to support Havertz and assess his condition. Daniel's reading remains yesterday's: an Arsenal triumph brings a jubilant but empty Havertz into the DFB quarters four days later; a defeat brings a frustrated, empty Havertz — both scenarios are already factored into Nagelsmann's playbook.

From a Brazilian perspective, the intersection is threefold and more painful: Marquinhos (PSG captain, third CL final appearance), Gabriel Magalhães and Gabriel Martinelli (both Arsenal) miss the Seleção home match against Panama entirely and travel to the Egypt test in Cleveland on 06.06. For Ancelotti that means: his center-back pairing will still be makeshift on Sunday (see below). Both national-team coaches therefore treat the friendlies as squad-depth tests, not World Cup lineup rehearsals.

Transfer market · Gordon officially at Barça

Done: Gordon unveiled at Barcelona today, Bayern was never an option

What's been brewing since Wednesday is now official: Anthony Gordon (25, England international) was unveiled at FC Barcelona on 30.05. after clearing his medical upon landing in Catalonia. Per Al Jazeera, the details: transfer from Newcastle, €70 million fixed plus add-ons, five-year contract to 2031. At the press conference, Gordon spoke in Spanish and made clear Barça was the only club he wanted — "no interest in Bayern Munich or Liverpool".

Bayern's take: That aligns with Munich's version, that they exited early once Barça called €80 million. Daniel's reading stays: smart exit, but the real problem (a class winger for UCL readiness) isn't solved — and with Gordon, one option is off the market. The previously rumored Plan B Mika Godts (20, Ajax) is "affordable but not first choice" per Falk. Bayern must deliver by September.

SC Freiburg · Backhaus takes over, Atubolu departs

The goalkeeper question is answered — the price for Atubolu is open

At Daniel's club, the position is settled: Mio Backhaus (from Werder Bremen, contract to 2031) is the new number one. The reported fee — ~€12 million base, with add-ons up to ~€15 million — would be a club record, beating the previous high (Ritsu Doan, €10.4 million from PSV in 2022). With that, Noah Atubolu's departure is effectively sealed: the national goalkeeper won't extend his contract, which has just one year left, and Freiburg are now asking 15–20 million €. There's interest from the Premier League (Brighton, Tottenham, West Ham), from Milan, and from Atlético — but the sporting director has received no binding offer so far.

Daniel's take: SC is not negotiating from weakness here. The sequence is right — first secure the successor (Backhaus), then sell the departing Atubolu to the highest bidder. If no club meets the ask, Atubolu stays one more year as a competitor. That's Freiburg's negotiating position in pure form.

Seleção · Friendly against Panama without Neymar

Neymar confirmed out — Ancelotti's makeshift defense for Sunday

Thursday's diagnosis stands: Neymar has a Grade 2 muscle injury in his right calf (CBF doctor Lasmar, forecast 2–3 weeks; independent physicians estimate 4–6 weeks for Grade 2). He's out for sure against Panama (Sun 31.05., 23:30 CEST / 18:30 BRT, Maracanã) and the Egypt test (06.06., Cleveland); the World Cup opener against Morocco on 13.06. stays the risk date. The CBF keeps him in the squad — they can re-roster him up to 24 hours before match 1.

For the friendly, Ancelotti sketched an XI at Granja Comary: Alisson; Wesley, Bremer, Léo Pereira, Alex Sandro; Casemiro, Bruno Guimarães; Matheus Cunha, Raphinha, Vinicius Jr., Luiz Henrique. Important: this is not yet the World Cup XI. The difference is in the center-back pairing — Bremer and Léo Pereira play as placeholders because the Plan-A axis Marquinhos / Gabriel Magalhães only arrives after the CL final. Once they're there, Bremer/Pereira step out. Plan-A winger Martinelli is also absent (CL final). The 90 minutes against Panama are therefore a squad-depth test, not a World Cup rehearsal.

"Grade 2 injury": Muscle injuries come in three levels — Grade 1 is a minor fiber strain (1–2 weeks), Grade 2 is a partial fiber tear (healable conservatively, 2–6 weeks depending on source), Grade 3 is a complete tear (8+ weeks, often surgery). Grade 2 means: noticeable damage, but healable without a scalpel. The risk isn't in healing, but in returning to load too early — then Grade 2 quickly becomes Grade 3.
