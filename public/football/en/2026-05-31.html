SC Freiburg—Season wrap-up & summer rebuild

For Daniel's club, the season is in the books, and what a season to frame: 7th place in the Bundesliga (47 points, 13 wins, 8 draws, 13 losses, goal difference −6) plus the Europa League final. That they lost 0–3 to Aston Villa on May 20 changes little in the bigger picture—a final in Julian Schuster's second season as successor to Streich is a historic run for a club of this size. Next year's reward: the Conference League playoff in August.[1]

Context 7th place plus EL final means: Freiburg invested its energy in Europe and left points on the table in the league—the −6 goal difference shows the attack wasn't as sharp as it was in Streich's peak years. This summer will show whether Schuster can broaden the squad for two competitions.

The transfer thread (window open until 31.08.)

Freiburg moved early in the market. In goal comes Mio Backhaus from Werder Bremen—per kicker, around €12M base fee, with add-ons up to ~€15M, a club-record transfer.[2] That has a consequence: Noah Atubolu will leave SC in the summer, so the starting keeper job changes hands. Second signing is Yamamoto (~€7–8M), plus Freiburg ties down left-back Berkay Yilmaz (21) long-term after two loan years at Nürnberg.[2]

On the outbound side, the road also ends for veteran Maximilian Philipp and club stalwart Nicolas Höfler; Merlin Röhl doesn't return after his Everton loan (mandatory purchase clause kicked in when Everton stayed up).[2]

Champions League Final—PSG stays on top

It went the distance and stayed open until the last penalty taker: In the Budapest final, Paris Saint-Germain and Arsenal drew 1–1, and on penalties PSG held their nerve with 4–3.[3] Kai Havertz put Arsenal ahead as early as the 6th minute—a deflected effort, three touches, then under the bar. Ousmane Dembélé equalized in the 65th minute from the spot after Mosquera was late against Kvaratskhelia.

The penalty drama: Eberechi Eze and Gabriel missed for Arsenal, David Raya saved against Nuno Mendes—and Lucas Beraldo converted the decisive attempt. For PSG it's the second straight title; after Real Madrid, Paris is only the second club in the Champions League era to defend the trophy.[3] Arsenal, back in a final for the first time since 2006, go home empty-handed again.

DFB & World Cup 2026—tonight's second-to-last test

For Daniel as a German fan, this is now the main thread: The World Cup in the USA, Canada, and Mexico is at the door, and the DFB team is in the thick of their final prep. Tonight Germany tests themselves in the MEWA Arena in Mainz against Finland—kickoff 20:45 MESZ / 15:45 BRT, live on ZDF.[5] It's the last home match before the tournament.

World Cup test / "dress rehearsal": Friendlies right before a tournament don't count for the table—they're the last training session where the coach runs through the lineup, patterns, and fitness before it matters. Like a costume check-in the night before the theater premiere.

After that, it's across the Atlantic: On June 6 (Sat) the dress rehearsal against the USA in Chicago (Soldier Field, 20:30 MESZ / 15:30 BRT), then base camp.[6] The World Cup opener follows on June 14 (Sun) against Curaçao in Houston (19:00 MESZ / 14:00 BRT)—Group E, then Ivory Coast (20.06., Toronto) and Ecuador (25.06., New York/NJ).[7]

Squad is set

Nagelsmann has named the 26 men: Captain is Joshua Kimmich (Bayern), between the posts are Manuel Neuer, Alexander Nübel, and Oliver Baumann. A small surprise: Leroy Sané (Galatasaray) is in.[6] From Freiburg's perspective, no World Cup starter—SC sends nobody to the tournament this time.

Brasileirão—round 18 underway

While Europe takes a club break, the Série A rolls on. Palmeiras leads with 38 points and just one league loss, Flamengo sits 4 back on 34 and won at the Maracanã Saturday 3–0 over Coritiba—Samuel Lino scored twice (10th, 24th), Pedro in between (14th), and this despite Flamengo being thinned by World Cup callups.[8] Coritiba played the final 51 minutes a man down.

Sunday wraps the round: Bragantino–Internacional (16:00 MESZ / 11:00 BRT), Vasco–Atlético-MG (21:00 MESZ / 16:00 BRT), plus the late window around 01:30 MESZ / 20:30 BRT with league leader Palmeiras against bottom-dweller Chapecoense, Cruzeiro–Fluminense, and Remo–São Paulo.[9] Down low, Vasco, Mirassol, Remo, and the points-starved Chapecoense battle the drop zone.

It's Sunday, the thirty-first of May. The club season in Europe is over, and last night it got its final punctuation mark: Paris Saint-Germain defended the Champions League in Budapest.

The final against Arsenal ended one to one. Kai Havertz scored early for Arsenal in the sixth minute, Ousmane Dembélé equalized in the second half from the penalty spot. On penalties Paris held their nerve four to three, after Eze and Gabriel missed for Arsenal. For Paris it's the second title in a row—after Real Madrid, only the second club to defend the trophy in the Champions League era.

For Daniel's club, SC Freiburg, it was a season to frame. Seventh in the Bundesliga and the Europa League final too, which they lost three to nothing to Aston Villa on the twentieth of May. Next year Freiburg gets a Conference League playoff.

At SC the rebuild is already underway. Goalkeeper Mio Backhaus arrives on a club-record deal of around fifteen million from Bremen, and Noah Atubolu leaves the club. Yamamoto is also new, and left-back Berkay Yilmaz stays long-term. The transfer window is open until the end of August.

But the most important thread for the next few weeks is the World Cup. Tonight at quarter to nine our Central European time, or quarter to four in Brazil, Germany plays Finland in Mainz. On the sixth of June comes the dress rehearsal in Chicago against the USA, and on the fourteenth of June the DFB team kicks off in Houston against Curaçao in the tournament.

Federal coach Nagelsmann has named his twenty-six men. Joshua Kimmich leads the team as captain, Manuel Neuer is back in goal, and Leroy Sané is somewhat of a surprise inclusion. Nobody from Freiburg is going to the World Cup this time.

In Brazil the ball keeps rolling. Palmeiras leads the Série A with thirty-eight points, Flamengo stays within four on thirty-four, and won three to nothing at the Maracanã Saturday against Coritiba—despite many players called up to the national team.
