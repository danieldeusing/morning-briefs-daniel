SC Freiburg

A great season ends without a World Cup ticket for their own players

Here lies the curious irony of Freiburg's season: the Sport-Club stood in the Europa League final on May 20 against Aston Villa — lost 0:3 (Tielemans, Buendía, Rogers) in Istanbul, but just getting there was the greatest international achievement in the club's history.[1] And yet: when Nagelsmann announced his 26 for the World Cup on May 21, not a single Freiburg player was included. A team that knocked out half of Europe provides no German World Cup starter — that says much about this team's collective character (no superstar, just a machine) and a little about the depth of the DFB squad.

uefa.com · wikipedia.org

Transfer summer: record at goalkeeper, overhaul elsewhere

Sportingly, Freiburg is reshuffling the deck. The most expensive signing in club history stands between the posts: Mio Backhaus arrives for roughly 12 million EUR (plus bonuses) from Werder Bremen (as of May 23).[2] This puts starting keeper Noah Atubolu under pressure — his departure is deemed likely but not yet official (per baden24, as of June 2, clearly marked as speculation). Also joining is Rihito Yamamoto (24, from St. Truiden), a midfielder for summer (May 27), and veteran Anthony Jung (34) extended (May 28).

On the departure side, stalwart Nicolas Höfler is ending his pro career and moving to the reserves; Merlin Röhl is headed to FC Everton (purchase option exercised). Coach Julian Schuster stays. Germany's official transfer window opens July 1 — the big summer action is still to come.

Bundesliga · Final table 2025/26

The season wrapped up as of matchday 34 (May 16). Bayern claim the title with 89 points and a club goal record of 122 goals, comfortably ahead of Dortmund and Leipzig. Freiburg finish 7th (47 pts, −6) — Conference League qualification secured despite the Europa League final run. Heidenheim and St. Pauli go down (both 26 pts; St. Pauli last on goal difference).

DFB & World Cup 2026 [3][4][5]

Squad locked in: 26 names, Kimmich as captain

Nagelsmann announced his 26 for the World Cup in the USA, Canada, and Mexico on May 21 in Frankfurt. Captain is Joshua Kimmich (108 caps), and Manuel Neuer is in goal — with Alexander Nübel and Oliver Baumann as backup. The offensive spine rests on Musiala, Wirtz, Havertz; in attack, Nagelsmann goes for the Premier League powerhouses Nick Woltemade and Kai Havertz plus VfB goalscorer Deniz Undav. Notably absent: Euro hero Niclas Füllkrug and Cologne's 19-year-old prospect Said El Mala.

Why no Freiburg player? Daniel's club plays collective football without a nominal national-team superstar — the key players (Atubolu in goal, Höler, Grifo) were either outside the expanded DFB circle this season or belong to other federations. Strong as a team, individually (not yet) on the World Cup radar.

Group E: manageable, but with a stumbling block

Germany opens on Sun June 14 at 19:00 MESZ / 14:00 BRT against World Cup debutant Curaçao in Houston — the Caribbean island is the smallest federation ever to qualify. Next comes the Ivory Coast (Sat June 20, 22:00 MESZ / 17:00 BRT, Toronto) and finally Ecuador (Thu June 25, 22:00 MESZ / 17:00 BRT, MetLife). Ivory Coast with its Premier League core is the most dangerous opponent; Ecuador is a defensively compact South American nut. Group topping is the goal — anything else would be a disappointment for this squad.

The bigger picture

The World Cup runs from June 11 to July 19 with 48 teams in 12 four-team groups for the first time; the group stage ends June 27. The opening is Thu June 11 with Mexico vs. South Africa (21:00 MESZ / 16:00 BRT) at Estadio Azteca — the first stadium to host three World Cup openings (1970, 1986, 2026). For Daniel, that means: the prime kickoffs sit in US zones, so German matches fall mostly in late evening in Europe and early afternoon/evening in Brazil.

Brazil · Seleção

Ancelotti's first big statement — and Neymar is in

Carlo Ancelotti announced his 26 for the hunt for the "Hexa" (the sixth title) at Rio's Museu do Amanhã. The headline: Neymar is back — for the first time in 2 years and 7 months, and for the first time under Ancelotti.[6] The bitter part: injured are defender/right-back Éder Militão and attacking players Rodrygo and Estêvão. For warmup, there was a 6:2 win vs. Panama on May 31 at the Maracanã (72,000 spectators); the final test is Sat June 6 vs. Egypt (19:00 BRT / 00:00 MESZ on Sunday) in Cleveland.

It's Tuesday, June 2. Nine days before World Cup kickoff, the ball has come to rest in the leagues — and football news revolves almost entirely around the tournament in the USA, Canada, and Mexico.

The bitterest irony for Daniel as a Freiburg fan: his club reached the Europa League final this season, lost three-nil to Aston Villa on May 20 — but when Nagelsmann named his World Cup squad, not a single Freiburg player was included. A team that knocked out half of Europe provides no German World Cup starter. That says much about this team's collective character.

Sportingly, Freiburg is reorganizing its summer. The most expensive signing in club history stands between the posts: Mio Backhaus arrives for roughly twelve million euros from Werder Bremen. This puts starting keeper Noah Atubolu under pressure, with his departure deemed likely. Julian Schuster remains the coach.

The Bundesliga season is done. Bayern are champions with eighty-nine points and a club goal record of one hundred twenty-two. Freiburg finish seventh and advance to Conference League qualification despite the final run. Heidenheim and St. Pauli are relegated.

The German squad: twenty-six names, captain Kimmich, Neuer in goal. Left out: Euro hero Füllkrug and Cologne's young talent El Mala. Germany opens on Sunday, June 14, at seven p.m. Central European Time against World Cup debutant Curaçao in Houston. In Group E, Ivory Coast and Ecuador await.

In Brazil, Neymar is back. Ancelotti brings him in after two years and seven months to the Seleção, his first call-up under the Italian. Militão, Rodrygo, and Estêvão are injured. Brazil starts on June 13 against Morocco, with a final test against Egypt on June 6 in Cleveland beforehand.

The Brasileirão breaks after round eighteen and returns July 22. Palmeiras lead with forty-one points and just one loss, followed by Flamengo.

In the days ahead, final friendlies dominate, then the opening on June 11 with Mexico vs. South Africa at Estadio Azteca. From then on, it's serious every day.
