DFB & World Cup 2026 [1][2][3]

In the USA, against the USA: the final test on Saturday

The schedule is tight. Nagelsmann's 26 flew from Frankfurt to Chicago on 02.06., where a one-week training camp is underway. The highlight before the real thing: on Sat 06.06. at 20:30 MESZ / 15:30 BRT the test against host nation USA at Soldier Field — the final dress rehearsal, and right away against a team keen to ignite World Cup fever in its own country. After that the DFB moves to its tournament base "The Graylyn Estate" in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, and trains on the Wake Forest University grounds.[2]

Squad confirmed: 26 names, Kimmich as captain

Nagelsmann announced his 26 for the World Cup in the USA, Canada and Mexico on 21.05. Captain is Joshua Kimmich, goalkeeper Manuel Neuer (backup: Nübel, Baumann). The attack hinges on Musiala, Wirtz, Havertz; up front Nagelsmann relies on Newcastle powerhouse Nick Woltemade and Stuttgart striker Deniz Undav. Notably absent: Euro hero Niclas Füllkrug and Cologne's 19-year-old talent Said El Mala.[1]

Why no Freiburg player? Daniel's club plays a collective football without a prominent national-team star — the key performers were either outside the wider DFB circle this season or belong to other federations. Strong as a team, individually (yet) under the World Cup radar.

Group E & the bigger picture

Germany starts on Sun 14.06. at 19:00 MESZ / 14:00 BRT against World Cup debutant Curaçao in Houston — the Caribbean island is the smallest federation ever to qualify. Then against the Ivory Coast (Sat 20.06., 22:00 MESZ / 17:00 BRT, Toronto) and finally Ecuador (Thu 25.06., 22:00 MESZ / 17:00 BRT, MetLife). The Ivory Coast with its Premier League core is the toughest prospect; winning the group is the aim.

The World Cup runs from 11.06. to 19.07. with 48 teams in 12 four-team groups for the first time; the group stage ends on 27.06. It opens on Thu 11.06. with Mexico vs. South Africa (21:00 MESZ / 16:00 BRT) at Estadio Azteca — the first stadium to host three World Cup openings (1970, 1986, 2026). For Daniel that means: German matches mostly late evening in Europe, earlier afternoon/evening in Brazil.[3]

Before everything turns to the World Cup, a look back at the recent finals: Bayern completed the Double — after the championship, also the DFB Cup with a 3:0 over VfB Stuttgart in Berlin on 23.05. SC Freiburg

Record in goal — and a bitter Atubolu farewell

Freiburg sets the summer early: the most expensive signing in club history goes in goal. Mio Backhaus (22) arrives for approximately 15 million euros record fee from Werder Bremen, contract through 2031, effective 30.06. — he surpasses the previous record (Doan, 10.4 million).[7] The twist: this signing costs starter Noah Atubolu his spot. Sporting director Jochen Saier confirmed they told Atubolu before the Europa League final that a transfer was "in progress" — the 24-year-old refused an extension and has since been sidelined. As of 26.05. there are no concrete offers according to fussballtransfers — clearly marked as transfer speculation.[8]

Otherwise: Rihito Yamamoto (from St. Truiden) arrived for midfield (27.05.), veteran Anthony Jung extended (28.05.). Nicolas Höfler ends his pro career, Merlin Röhl heads to Everton. Coach Julian Schuster stays. The German transfer window opens only on 01.07. — the busier part of summer is still to come.

Brazil · Seleção

First US training — and the Neymar question straight away

Brazil held its first training session on US soil on 02.06., at the New York Red Bulls facility, with 25 of 26 players under Carlo Ancelotti. Finally joining were the three Champions League finalists Marquinhos, Gabriel Magalhães and Gabriel Martinelli, who were still in Budapest on 30.05.[9]

The one absence was the most prominent: Neymar trained only individually. The reason is a calf injury from the Santos match against Coritiba (17.05.). CBF team doctor Rodrigo Lasmar confirmed on 28.05. a grade-2 calf strain in the right calf — a partial muscle tear, more serious than the fluid buildup first reported by Santos — with an estimated recovery of 2 to 3 weeks. This means he will miss both warmup tests (Panama and Egypt); whether he makes the World Cup opener on 13.06. is open. Ancelotti has not ruled out World Cup participation — the staff are working to get him fit during the group stage —, but it is not assured.[10] For Daniel as a Brazil supporter, the suspense question of the week: will he make it back during the group stage?

It's Wednesday, June third. Eight days before the World Cup kickoff, preparations shift across the Atlantic: Germany has been in Chicago since yesterday, Brazil completed its first training session on US soil in New Jersey.

For the DFB the schedule is tight. Nagelsmann's twenty-six flew from Frankfurt to Chicago on June second, where a one-week training camp is underway. On Saturday, June sixth, comes the final test, and right against host USA at Soldier Field, at half past eight German time. After that, the team moves to its tournament base in Winston-Salem, North Carolina.

The squad was set on May twenty-first. Twenty-six names, captain Kimmich, Neuer in goal. Left out were Euro hero Füllkrug and Cologne's young talent El Mala — and not a single player from Europa League finalist SC Freiburg is on the list. Germany opens on Sunday, June fourteenth, at seven p.m. German time, against World Cup debutant Curaçao in Houston.

In Brazil it all revolves around Neymar. At the first US training he was absent with a calf injury and worked individually only. Team doctor Rodrigo Lasmar of the CBF confirmed on May twenty-eighth a grade-two calf strain in the right calf, with an estimated recovery of two to three weeks. So he will miss both warmup tests against Panama and Egypt. Whether he makes the opener on June thirteenth against Morocco is open. Coach Ancelotti has not ruled out World Cup participation, but it is not assured — it depends on how the healing progresses. That's the suspense question of the week.

At least the three Champions League finalists Marquinhos, Gabriel Magalhães, and Martinelli have joined the Seleção in New Jersey. But Militão, Rodrygo and Estêvão remain injured.

A brief look back at the past season: Bayern claimed the Double. After the championship also the cup, a three-to-nil win over VfB Stuttgart in the final in Berlin with a hat-trick from Harry Kane. In the Champions League, Paris Saint-Germain defended the title, four to three on penalties against Arsenal in Budapest, their second consecutive trophy.

At SC Freiburg comes goalkeeper Mio Backhaus for roughly fifteen million euros, the most expensive signing in club history. That costs starting keeper Noah Atubolu his place, who refused an extension and currently has no concrete offer. Coach Julian Schuster stays. The German transfer window opens only on July first.

The Brasileirão pauses after round eighteen and returns July twenty-second. Palmeiras leads with forty-one points and only one defeat.

In the coming days the final two warmup tests on Saturday, then the opening on June eleventh with Mexico versus South Africa at Estadio Azteca. From then on, it's serious football every day.
