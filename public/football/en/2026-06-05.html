SC Freiburg: without the DFB badge — but broadly on the World Cup stage

The honest truth first: not a single SC Freiburg player is in the German World Cup squad. Julian Nagelsmann announced his 26-man roster on 21.05 — and Matthias Ginter, with 52 international caps and the World Cup title from 2014 the most experienced candidate in the Breisgau, came up empty. From Freiburg's perspective a frustration: Ginter was one of the most reliable center-backs in the league this season. Nagelsmann prefers Rüdiger and younger options in defense — sportingly defensible, still a sting for SC fans.[1]

The consolation: Freiburg is represented at the World Cup through four other nations after all. Philipp Lienhart travels with Austria (first World Cup participation since 1998), Ivan Matanović with Croatia, Yuito Suzuki with Japan, and Johan Manzambi with Switzerland. For a club of Freiburg's stature, this international reach is a strong signal — the scouting department hits the mark.[2]

Transfer thread: rebuild for 26/27 already underway

The summer window is open, and Freiburg is working on the new squad in parallel with the World Cup break. The first new arrival is confirmed: Rihito Yamamoto, 24, joins from Belgian top-flight side St. Truiden. Sporting director Klemens Hartenbach calls him a "versatile midfielder"; the transfer fee is around 7 million euros according to kicker (transfermarkt.de cites up to 8 million euros). Yamamoto has been in St. Truiden's starting XI consistently and contributed 5 goals this season — a typical Freiburg move: seasoned league player, no hype name, immediately available.[3]

On the departures side, the counterpoint to the World Cup story: Johan Manzambi could become the SC's record sale — precisely the player who is now heading to the World Cup with Switzerland and can drive his market value further. Added to that, several loan players return (Dinkci, Adamu, Weißhaupt, Wagner, Muslija and others) and strain the squad depth. Rumor status: 04.06; only Yamamoto (arrival) and the expiring loans are concrete.[4]

Context Yamamoto plus the returning loan players create a full midfield pool. The real lever of the summer is the Manzambi question: a record transfer would free funds for a targeted reinforcement, instead of just managing depth.

German Team: 4:0 opener, now the real test

The 4:0 against Finland on 31.05 in Mainz was a sovereign start to World Cup preparation — but against an opponent of this caliber it says little about tournament form. The actual test is tomorrow against hosts USA (Sat, 20:30 MESZ / 15:30 BRT, Soldier Field Chicago, RTL): a World Cup participant with home-field atmosphere, perfect for calibrating the starting XI for the Curaçao opener.[5]

Nagelsmann's roster blends youth and experience: Joshua Kimmich remains captain, Manuel Neuer returns as number one (ahead of Baumann and Nübel), in front of them the creative axis Musiala and Wirtz. The youngest player is 18-year-old Bayern prospect Lennart Karl. After moving to the base in Winston-Salem (from 08.06), only one thing matters: the opener against Curaçao on 14.06.[6]

Brazil: Neymar in the squad — but injured, Ancelotti chases the Hexa

Daniel's adopted home is anxiously awaiting the final test: Brazil vs. Egypt (tomorrow Sat, 19:00 BRT, Huntington Bank Field Cleveland, live Globo) is the dress rehearsal before the World Cup begins. Kickoff is prime time for Daniel — for German eyes it would be 00:00 MESZ in the night leading into Sunday.[7]

The big story with a twist: Neymar is in the World Cup squad but will miss the Egypt game. At 34, the captain made the cut under Carlo Ancelotti for the first time after two years and seven months — but the CBF confirmed on 04.06 a calf strain (grade 2 muscle injury in the right calf): Neymar is not traveling to Cleveland and remains in New Jersey for treatment. He is not being cut from the World Cup roster — the goal is fitness for the 13.06 opener. The Italian is building around Neymar, Vini Jr., and Endrick a squad that should claim the sixth World Cup title (the "Hexa"), and sees the tournament deliberately "without a clear favorite." Brazil opens on 13.06 against Morocco (Group C, plus Scotland and Haiti) at MetLife Stadium.[8]

Hexa: Short for "hexacampeonato" — the sixth World Cup title. Brazil has five (last in 2002) and has been waiting since then to add a sixth to the collection. "Hexa" is almost a rallying cry in Brazil.

It's Friday, June fifth — six days until the World Cup kicks off in Mexico City. The league season is over, Bayern are champions for the thirty-fifth time, and all of football is now focused on one date: tomorrow, the final dress rehearsal before the tournament begins.

Germany plays tomorrow evening against hosts USA in Chicago, at eight thirty German time, that's three thirty in the afternoon Daniel time, live on RTL. The team already won the first test, four to zero against Finland in Mainz. But Finland means little — the USA with home advantage are the real test, before the action kicks off on the fourteenth against Curaçao.

Nagelsmann's squad has been set since the twenty-first of May. Kimmich stays captain, Neuer returns as number one, in front of them the creative axis Musiala and Wirtz, and the youngest is eighteen-year-old Lennart Karl. In Group E, after Curaçao come the Ivory Coast and Ecuador.

For Freiburg fans there's a bitter piece of news and a bright one. Bitter: not a single Freiburger is in the German squad, Matthias Ginter wasn't picked despite a strong season. Bright: four Freiburgers are heading to the World Cup in shirts of other nations — Lienhart with Austria, Matanović with Croatia, Suzuki with Japan, and Manzambi with Switzerland. For a club of this size that's a strong signal.

Meanwhile the transfer window is already open. Freiburg have signed Japanese midfielder Rihito Yamamoto from St. Truiden for around seven million euros. And Manzambi, who's heading to the World Cup with Switzerland right now, could become the club's record sale.

Brazil also takes a final test tomorrow, against Egypt in Cleveland, at seven o'clock in the evening Daniel time, live on Globo. The big story is Neymar: at thirty-four he made the World Cup squad for the first time in two and a half years under coach Carlo Ancelotti — but a grade-two calf strain keeps him from the Egypt test, he's not traveling to Cleveland and stays in treatment. He's not being dropped from the squad though; the goal is fitness by the opener. With Vini Junior and Endrick, Brazil is chasing the sixth title, the Hexa. The World Cup opener against Morocco is on the thirteenth of June.

So tomorrow the big double test, and on Thursday Mexico kick off the tournament against South Africa. In the next brief we'll see how Germany and Brazil came through their final rehearsals — and whether the starting lineups for the World Cup opener are now set.
