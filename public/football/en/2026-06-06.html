Germany: World Cup shock for Karl — and a test meant to settle things

Bad news on dress rehearsal day: Lennart Karl will miss the World Cup. The 18-year-old Bayern youngster, listed yesterday as the squad's youngest, suffered a muscle bundle tear in his left front thigh during the final training session before the USA test in Chicago — the DFB confirmed the exit on Friday. Karl said it felt "indescribably painful to have to miss the biggest tournament." Nagelsmann moved quickly and called up Assan Ouédraogo (RB Leipzig, 20) — a remarkable vote of confidence in the next German midfield talent.[1]

Muscle bundle tear: A more severe muscle injury — not just a few fibers tear, but an entire bundle. Healing typically takes several weeks, so the World Cup is off the table for Karl.

From the match itself (today Sat, 20:30 MESZ / 15:30 BRT, Soldier Field Chicago, live on RTL) Nagelsmann expects the final signal for the starting lineup against Curaçao. Oliver Baumann is expected in goal — Manuel Neuer is still in "load buildup" after his calf injury and hasn't trained fully with the team at camp; Nagelsmann is deliberately resting him for the tournament start. Up front, the trio Musiala–Wirtz–Havertz is expected to start together for the first time since November 2024, with Havertz as center forward.[2]

The expected lineup (all information "expected"): Baumann – Kimmich, Tah, N. Schlotterbeck, Raum – F. Nmecha, Pavlović – Sané, Musiala, Wirtz – Havertz. After moving into base in Winston-Salem (starting 08.06) only the opener against Curaçao on 14.06 matters.[2]

SC Freiburg: without DFB badge — but well represented on the World Cup stage

The honest truth remains: not a single SC Freiburg player is in the German World Cup squad. Matthias Ginter, the 2014 World Cup winner and the most experienced candidate in Freiburg, came away empty when Nagelsmann announced his squad on 21.05 — defensible on sporting grounds, but a sting for SCF fans nonetheless.[3]

The consolation: Freiburg is represented at the World Cup through four other nations. Philipp Lienhart goes with Austria (first World Cup appearance since 1998), Igor Matanović with Croatia, Yuito Suzuki with Japan, and Johan Manzambi with Switzerland. For a club of Freiburg's size, this international spread is a strong signal — the scouting department is hitting.[3]

Transfer Thread: Manzambi stays non-committal, Yamamoto locked in

As the World Cup approaches, the summer window is open. Johan Manzambi himself has eased transfer concerns: at the Swiss national team's press conference he said "Freiburg fans have nothing to worry about for now — he is still a Freiburg player at the moment, but left a later move open." This takes the possible record transfer off the hot-topic list for now — though a strong tournament could push his market value higher. Statement as of 04.06.[4]

What is locked in is the signing: Rihito Yamamoto, 24, comes from Belgian first-division club St. Truiden (29 of 30 matches, 5 goals, 6 assists). Sporting director Klemens Hartenbach calls him a "versatile midfielder"; the fee is around 7 million euros according to kicker. Yamamoto is already the second summer arrival — goalkeeper Mio Backhaus came before him. A typical Freiburg one-two: established league players without hype, ready to play immediately.[5]

Brazil: Neymar in squad — but injured, Ancelotti chases the Hexa

Daniel's adopted home is buzzing for the final test: Brazil against Egypt (today Sat, 19:00 BRT, Huntington Bank Field Cleveland, live on Globo/SporTV) is the dress rehearsal before the World Cup starts. Kickoff is at prime time for Daniel — for German eyes it would be 00:00 MESZ in the night going into Sunday.[6]

The big story with a damper: Neymar is in the World Cup squad but will miss Egypt. At 34, the captain finally made the cut under Carlo Ancelotti — but the CBF diagnosed a calf strain (Grade 2 muscle injury in the right calf) on 27.05 with two to three weeks of healing time: Neymar is not traveling to Cleveland and is staying in New Jersey for treatment. He won't be cut from the squad — the goal is fitness by the opener on 13.06. Ancelotti instead tests with the expected lineup around Vini Jr., Raphinha and center forward Igor Thiago; Brazil opens on 13.06 against Morocco (Group C, also Scotland and Haiti) at MetLife Stadium.[7]

Hexa: Short for "hexacampeonato" — the sixth World Cup title. Brazil has five (most recently 2002) and has been waiting since to add to the collection. "Hexa" is almost a battle cry in Brazil.
