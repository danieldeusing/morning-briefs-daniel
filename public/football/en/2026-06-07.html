DFB: nail-biter in Chicago — but the dress rehearsal paid off

It wasn't a showcase, but in the end exactly what Nagelsmann needed: Germany won the final warm-up before World Cup 2026 with a 2:1 victory (1:1) over hosts USA at Chicago's Soldier Field. Kai Havertz headed in a Kimmich free-kick delivery for an early lead just 100 seconds in. But then the DFB side let a stunning volley from Antonee Robinson (37.) take the wind out of their sails — only Leroy Sané restored the deserved win in the 57th minute.[1]

What stands out is the streak behind it: this was the ninth consecutive win — no German team has ever gone into a World Cup this successfully. "We've now won nine times in a row," Nagelsmann stressed, but acknowledged the team took time to settle into the conditions after Robinson's goal. The German press rightly calls it a "nail-biter" — the early going was impressive, but the middle spell was shaky.[1][2]

A lot is now set for the starting XI against Curaçao on 06.14: Oliver Baumann started in goal because Manuel Neuer is still ramping up from his calf injury and being spared for the tournament. Germany lined up with Baumann – Kimmich, N. Schlotterbeck, Tah, Brown – Nmecha, Pavlović – Sané, Musiala, Wirtz – Havertz. Sané stepped in for injured Lennart Karl on the right, Nathaniel Brown defended on the left ahead of David Raum — two personnel decisions Nagelsmann can now enter the tournament with in good standing.[3]

SC Freiburg: no DFB stamp — but four men on the World Cup stage

Nothing has changed in Freiburg's World Cup balance, and it remains bittersweet: not a single SC player made the German squad. Matthias Ginter, the most experienced Breisgau candidate with a 2014 World Cup title, went home empty from Nagelsmann's nomination. Instead, the club is represented across four other nations: Philipp Lienhart (Austria), Igor Matanović (Croatia), Yuito Suzuki (Japan) and Johan Manzambi (Switzerland). For a club of this size, the international spread is strong scouting credentials.[4]

Meanwhile, the summer window rolls on quietly. Signing Rihito Yamamoto (24, from St. Truiden) is done — he plays the number-10 role behind the striker, exactly the position Manzambi has been filling. This fuels sale talk around the 20-year-old Swiss; Manzambi himself downplayed it recently: he has no concrete "dream club" and is focused on playing. Yamamoto is the second summer signing after goalkeeper Mio Backhaus — a typical Freiburg double strike.[5]

Brazil: 2:1 over Egypt — but Cleveland leaves a wound

Daniel's adopted home has passed its final test: Brazil beat Egypt 2:1 at Cleveland's Huntington Bank Field. Bruno Guimarães poked in the lead in the 6th minute, Mostafa "Ziko" Zizo equalized right after a defensive error (10.), before Endrick six minutes into the second half (51.) scored the winner after a Raphinha cross. The result is fine — the display was a mixed bag with ups and downs.[6]

Bad news overshadows everything: Wesley, AS Roma's right-back, had to come off injured. Already in the 16th minute he grabbed his left groin, was replaced by Danilo and cried on the bench — then he limped out of the stadium. Ancelotti confirmed the worry: Wesley will undergo tests today (Sun, 06.07), a squad removal cannot be ruled out. The FIFA replacement deadline runs until 06.12, exactly 24 hours before the opener. For Daniel as a Brazil resident, this is the story of the day.[7]

Ancelotti himself caused talk: at halftime he made eight substitutions and left only Danilo, Douglas Santos and Raphinha on the pitch — a complete team rotation that many fans criticized as a confusing experiment. And Neymar? He remains the open question: with a Grade 2 calf injury (right, diagnosed 05.27), the captain didn't travel to Cleveland at all and is working on his fitness in New Jersey for the tournament.[8]

Hexa: Short for "hexacampeonato" — the sixth World Cup title. Brazil has five (last in 2002) and has been chasing the next one ever since. "Hexa" is almost a rallying cry in Brazil.
