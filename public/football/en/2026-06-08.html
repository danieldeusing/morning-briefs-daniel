SC Freiburg: Ginter out, four others in

For SC, this World Cup is a lesson in fortune and chance. The club is as present at the world's biggest tournament as it rarely is — four released professionals — and yet the one everyone here expected is missing: Matthias Ginter.

The Ginter pain

Ginter, 32, had a strong season and helped Freiburg reach the Europa League final (0:3 against Aston Villa in Istanbul). Yet Nagelsmann cut him from the 26-man squad. The national team coach himself called the decision tough: on the pitch, Ginter had "nothing to apologize for," but he had "the disadvantage of never being part of the squad in these three years." Ginter responded calmly: "I couldn't have done more." When he returned to Breisgau, fans celebrated him with chants — and booed Nagelsmann.[1]

Four on the plane — but no German

While the DFB door stayed closed for Freiburg, others opened: Philipp Lienhart (Austria), Igor Matanović (Croatia), Johan Manzambi (Switzerland) and Yuito Suzuki (Japan) are heading out. Manzambi kicks off against Qatar on Sat 06.13, Suzuki against the Netherlands on Sun 06.14, Matanović with Croatia taking on England in Dallas, Lienhart with Austria facing Jordan. For a club of Freiburg's stature, that's a strong World Cup pedigree — the international academy and scouting work pays off.[2]

Germany: Kimmich leads, Neuer returns

Nagelsmann named his 26 men on 05.21. Joshua Kimmich (Bayern, 108 caps) is captain, Manuel Neuer is back in goal unexpectedly — his first tournament since the Euro 2024 quarterfinal against Spain. It's a mix of the Bayern block (Kimmich, Musiala, Tah, Goretzka, Pavlović, young Lennart Karl) and Liverpool star Florian Wirtz.[3]

Group E is manageable on paper — Curaçao (smallest World Cup participant ever), the Ivory Coast (the toughest nut, three-time African champions) and Ecuador. Kickoff of the opener is Sunday evening German time, for you a pleasant 14:00 BRT in the early afternoon.[4]

Brazil: Neymar returns, Ancelotti chasing the Hexa

The Seleção is Brazil's national team — five-time world champion, more than any other country. The "Hexa" would be the sixth title, which all of Brazil has been waiting for since 2002.

Under Italian coach Carlo Ancelotti, Neymar (34) returns to the squad — his first call-up under Ancelotti, after two years and seven months away. Brazil is in Group C with Morocco, Haiti and Scotland and kicks off on Sat 06.13 against Morocco in New Jersey, 00:00 MESZ (Sun) / 19:00 BRT — for you the ideal Saturday evening time. Matches follow against Haiti (06.19, Philadelphia) and Scotland (06.24, Miami).[5]

World Cup 2026: What kicks off Thursday

It's the first World Cup with 48 teams and 104 matches, spread across the USA, Canada and Mexico. The opener is on Thu 06.11 at 21:00 MESZ / 16:00 BRT with host Mexico against South Africa at the Estadio Azteca in Mexico City — the first stadium ever to host three World Cup openings. The tournament runs through 07.19; the final is at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey.[6]

For your calendar Most matches run at very convenient times in Brazil — afternoon through evening BRT. Germany's matches on 06.20 and 06.25 start at 17:00 BRT, early evening; the opener against Curaçao is even earlier at 14:00 BRT.

It's Monday, June eighth. The leagues are silent, the Brasileirão is paused — and yet football was rarely this close. In three days, Thursday, Mexico and South Africa will kick off at the legendary Azteca Stadium the first World Cup with forty-eight teams. Kickoff for you in Ribeirão Claro is four p.m.

For SC Freiburg this summer is bittersweet. Four professionals are heading to the World Cup — Philipp Lienhart with Austria, Igor Matanović with Croatia, Johan Manzambi with Switzerland, and Yuito Suzuki with Japan. But not one in a German shirt. Because Matthias Ginter, who had a strong season, was cut by national team coach Nagelsmann. Nagelsmann himself called the decision tough; Ginter simply said he couldn't have done more. When he returned to Breisgau, fans celebrated him — and booed the national team coach.

Germany itself plays Sunday against Curaçao in Houston, for you at two p.m. in the early afternoon. Captain is Joshua Kimmich, and surprisingly Manuel Neuer returns in goal — his first tournament since the European championship in twenty twenty-four. Then come the Ivory Coast and Ecuador.

Brazil plays Saturday evening, your time, against Morocco in New Jersey. Under coach Carlo Ancelotti, Neymar is back, after more than two years away. All of Brazil dreams of the Hexa, the sixth title. The Seleção is in group C, alongside Morocco, Haiti, and Scotland.
