Germany: workmanlike win in Chicago, but no Neuer

Germany's final warm-up before the tournament came through — 2–1 against the USA on Saturday in a sold-out Soldier Field in Chicago (63,636 fans). It was a job done, not a statement.

Havertz early, Sané seals it

Kai Havertz headed Germany ahead in just the 2nd minute. Antonee Robinson equalized for the hosts with a spectacular volley from around 21 meters (37th), before Leroy Sané made it 2–1 in the 57th minute following a Havertz assist. Oliver Baumann (Hoffenheim) was in goal — regular keeper Manuel Neuer was rested due to a calf issue. Nagelsmann took away a deserved but unspectacular win: solid structure, few moments of brilliance in the final third.[1]

SC Freiburg: four on the plane, one painfully missing

For SC, this World Cup is a lesson in luck and circumstance. The club will be well represented — four players released — yet the one everyone expected is absent: Matthias Ginter.

Four paths to the tournament — none in German colors

Philipp Lienhart (Austria), Igor Matanović (Croatia), Johan Manzambi (Switzerland), and Yuito Suzuki (Japan) are heading there. Manzambi opens things on Sat 06/13 with Switzerland against Qatar in Santa Clara (9:00 PM MESZ / 4:00 PM BRT), and Suzuki on Sun 06/14 with Japan against the Netherlands. Matanović — signed in the summer for 8 million euros from Eintracht Frankfurt and the club's most expensive striker ever — travels with Croatia. For a club of Freiburg's size, that's a strong World Cup showing.[3]

World Cup 2026: What begins on Thursday

It's the first World Cup with 48 teams and 104 matches, spread across the USA, Canada, and Mexico. The tournament opens on Thu 06/11 at 9:00 PM MESZ / 4:00 PM BRT with host Mexico against South Africa at the Estadio Azteca in Mexico City — the first stadium ever to host three World Cup openings. Right after (4:00 AM MESZ Fri / 11:00 PM BRT) comes South Korea–Czech Republic in Guadalajara. The tournament runs through 07/19; the final is at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey.[4]

For Daniel's calendar Most matches run at friendly times for Brazil — afternoon to evening BRT. The World Cup opener Thursday starts at 4:00 PM BRT, and Germany's first match is even earlier at 2:00 PM BRT. Only Brazil matches drift deep into the evening (7:00 PM BRT and later).

Brazil: warm-ups won, Neymar the headache

On the pitch, things look good for Ancelotti — Brazil cruised through both friendlies. But the headline belongs to someone who didn't play.

2–1 vs Egypt, before that 6–2 vs Panama

In the last test on Saturday in Cleveland, Brazil beat Egypt 2–1: Bruno Guimarães struck early (7th), and Endrick came off the bench to head home the winner seven minutes after entering. A week prior, the Seleção had demolished Panama 6–2. The fly in the ointment: right-back Wesley had to come off injured in the first half — a scare just before the opener.[5]

Neymar: in the squad, but hurt

Contrary to early celebration, Neymar (34) is named to the roster but is sidelined with a Grade-2 calf strain — two to three weeks out. He missed both warm-ups and will almost certainly miss the opener against Morocco (Sat 06/13). Ancelotti stays unfazed: "If he's not fit for the first match, he'll be fit for the second." Asked if he would have named Neymar knowing the severity of the injury, he replied dryly: "If my grandpa had wheels, he'd be a car." A recent MRI shows "good progress" per the CBF — a group-stage return is in the cards, just not at kickoff.[6]

Tuesday, June 9th. Two days until Mexico and South Africa open the first World Cup with forty-eight teams, Thursday evening in the legendary Azteca Stadium. Kickoff is four p.m. your time in Brazil. The final friendlies are done, and they told us a lot.

Germany won two to one against the USA in Chicago, in front of more than sixty-three thousand fans. Kai Havertz headed in the opener in the second minute, Leroy Sané sealed it in the fifty-seventh. It was a working win, not a standout performance. Manuel Neuer didn't play, by the way — Oliver Baumann was in goal — but Neuer should be fit by Sunday.

Germany opens Sunday against Curaçao in Houston, two p.m. your time in the early afternoon. Then Côte d'Ivoire and Ecuador follow. From a Freiburg angle, it's bittersweet: four players are heading to the World Cup — Lienhart with Austria, Matanović with Croatia, Manzambi with Switzerland, and Suzuki with Japan — but not a single one in a German shirt, because Matthias Ginter was left out.

Brazil looked sharp: two to one over Egypt on Saturday, Bruno Guimarães scored early, young Endrick came off the bench to seal it. The week before there was even a six to two romp over Panama. But the real story is Neymar — and not the good kind.

Neymar is in the squad, but he tore his calf, two to three weeks out. He missed both friendlies and will likely miss the opener against Morocco Saturday evening your time. Coach Ancelotti is staying calm: if not the first game, then the second. Brazil plays Saturday night your time against Morocco in New Jersey and is chasing the Hexa, the sixth title.
