SC Freiburg — the goalkeeper conflict is the summer story

Off-season, but drama in goal: record signing vs. academy product. No Freiburg player at the World Cup.

Backhaus displaces Atubolu

With the signing of Mio Backhaus (22) from Bremen — base fee around twelve million euros, per Bild up to 15 million plus bonuses, in any case a club record — Freiburg has turned its goalkeeper hierarchy upside down.[1] Incumbent starter Noah Atubolu (23), academy product and former U-21 national player, sees himself displaced: his agents have told the club they are working "intensively on a summer transfer." Atubolu prefers the Premier League, with Brighton and AC Milan named as interested parties — but no concrete offer has yet arrived.[1]

For a club that has built its identity on smart academy development, this is delicate balance: Freiburg is investing record money in a position it has cultivated for years. When asked about Backhaus after the Europa League final, Atubolu was guarded: "The message is also new to me … I can't say much about it."[1]

Yamamoto confirmed, Röhl out

The second signing is Japanese midfielder-all-rounder Rihito Yamamoto (24), for around 7–8 million € from Belgian side Sint-Truiden — confirmed 27.05.[2] In season 25/26 he managed 5 goals and 8 assists in 38 games; sporting director Hartenbach calls him "truly a midfielder all-rounder." On the departures side, Merlin Röhl has moved permanently to the Premier League with Everton.

Brazil — warm-up won, concern on the right

Group C: Brazil · Morocco · Haiti · Scotland. Opener at the best BRT prime time.

2:1 over Egypt — and a painful injury

In the final test before the tournament, Seleção beat Egypt 2:1 on 06.06. in Cleveland (before 64,311 spectators). Bruno Guimarães scored in the 6th minute after winning the ball high up the pitch, an Egyptian named Zico (oddly named after the Brazilian legend) equalized, before Endrick made it 1 in the 51st after a Raphinha assist.[3] The downside: right-back Wesley had to come off injured after 15 minutes and was cut from the World Cup squad — Danilo is now the leading candidate for the right fullback role.

Morocco reached the 2022 semifinals — not a warm-up game but the group's toughest test right away. Then Haiti and Scotland as outsiders.

WC opening — Mexico vs South Africa at the Azteca

Today 21:00 MESZ / 16:00 BRT · Estadio Azteca, Mexico City · Group A.

The 2026 World Cup will be opened tonight where World Cup history has been made twice before: at the Azteca Stadium. Host Mexico faces South Africa — and this is a mirror of 2010: back then South Africa opened as hosts against Mexico, the legendary 1:1 with Siphiwe Tshabalala's wonder goal and Rafael Márquez's equalizer.[5]

The form contrast is stark: Mexico is unbeaten in eight games (6 wins, 2 draws since November 2025), most recently a 5:1 against Serbia, led by 17-year-old wonderkid Gilberto Mora (tournament's youngest player) and veteran keeper Guillermo Ochoa (40). South Africa, by contrast, has been winless in four games (two draws, two losses, just three goals) — coach Hugo Broos visibly frustrated. Coach Javier Aguirre (third tenure) starts Mexico as clear favorite, buoyed by home advantage.[5]

DFB team — starting XI takes shape

Base in Winston-Salem · Opener Sun 14.06. vs Curaçao · all times MESZ / BRT.

Neuer fit, Brown ahead of Raum, Sané for Karl

Three days before kickoff, Nagelsmann's picture clarifies. Manuel Neuer (40), who missed the tests due to calf problems, trained fully in Winston-Salem on Wednesday for 2.5 hours — the "clear plan" is that he starts against Curaçao.[6] On the left defense, surprisingly Nathaniel Brown (Eintracht Frankfurt, 22) has come through: he started both World Cup tests, with David Raum settling into a substitute role ("I accept my role. When I come on, I give everything.").[7] To fill the gap left by injured Lennart Karl in the attacking trio, Leroy Sané is expected to slot in; Germany is expected to line up in a 4-2-3-1.[6]

sports.yahoo.com · sportschau.de
4-2-3-1: One of the most common formations — four defenders, then two "sixes" screening in front, then three attacking midfielders behind a lone striker. Think of it like a pyramid: broad, stable foundation at the back, a point at the top. Advantage: stability in midfield plus creativity feeding the striker.

Curaçao first — the underdog opener

Germany face Curaçao on Sunday (Houston, 19:00 MESZ / 14:00 BRT, ARD/MagentaTV). The Caribbean island (FIFA rank 82, around 156,000 people) is the smallest nation in World Cup history, making their first finals and mostly made up of Dutch-trained players. Germany arrive with nine consecutive wins (most recently 4:0 vs Finland, 2:1 vs USA) as overwhelming favorite — a "Dushi Boys" win is quoted at around +4500 odds.[8] After this, the real tests: Ivory Coast and Ecuador.

It's Thursday, June eleventh, and tonight the opening whistle of the World Cup falls. Host Mexico opens the tournament against South Africa at the legendary Azteca Stadium in Mexico City. Kickoff is at nine p.m. Central European time, four p.m. Brazil time — prime living-room time for Daniel.

This opening match is a mirror of twenty ten, when South Africa opened as host against Mexico in the famous one-one draw featuring Siphiwe Tshabalala's wonder goal. This time the roles are reversed. Mexico goes in as clear favorite, unbeaten for eight games, with seventeen-year-old wonderkid Gilberto Mora. South Africa, on the other hand, has been winless in four games.

At SC Freiburg, the summer drama is a goalkeeper conflict. The club signs keeper Mio Backhaus from Bremen for around twelve million euros — a club record. This displaces academy product Noah Atubolu, the previous starting goalkeeper. Atubolu's agents are working on a transfer, he prefers the Premier League, but no concrete offer yet exists. If he stays, an internal rivalry looms. No Freiburg player made the German World Cup squad, by the way.

With the German national team, the starting eleven is taking shape. Manuel Neuer, who missed the friendlies due to calf issues, trained fully again on Wednesday and is expected to start against Curaçao. On the left defense, twenty-two-year-old Nathaniel Brown of Eintracht Frankfurt surprisingly came through, with David Raum accepting a bench role. For injured Lennart Karl, Leroy Sané is expected to fill the attacking midfield position. Germany starts on Sunday in Houston against Curaçao, the smallest nation in World Cup history, at seven p.m. Central European time, two p.m. Brazil time.

Brazil won their warm-up game on June sixth, two to one against Egypt in Cleveland. Bruno Guimarães and Endrick scored. The downside: right-back Wesley was injured and cut from the squad; Danilo moves in to replace him. Coach Carlo Ancelotti says he knows his starting XI for Morocco and pins particular hopes on the pair Raphinha and Vinicius Junior. The Seleção start Saturday Brazil time against Morocco, the twenty twenty-two semifinalist — not a warm-up but the group's toughest test right off the bat.
