WC Opener — Mexico 2-0, but the Azteca talks about the red cards

Group A · Estadio Azteca, Mexico City · 11.06.2026. First WC day complete.

Quiñones early, Jiménez emotional — and in the end eleven against nine

The World Cup 2026 began exactly where Daniel had hoped for it: at prime watching time with goals. Mexico beat South Africa 2-0. Julián Quiñones struck in the 9th minute — and wrote a small footnote, because it was the tournament's first goal, scored by a CONCACAF player in the opener.[1] The 2-0 came in the 67th minute via Raúl Jiménez (35) — a strike with history: Jiménez had suffered a fractured skull years ago, and he dedicated his first World Cup goal with a look skyward to his late father.[1]

What shaped the match, though, were three red cards. South Africa fell apart: Sithole saw red in the 49th minute (foul on Gutierrez), Zwane followed in the 52nd (striking Alvarado in the face) — South Africa finished with nine men. To keep things balanced, Mexican César Montes was sent off near the end (90', hip check). Bottom line: a commanding home win, carried by the Azteca cauldron, but with plenty of discipline to discuss.[1]

South Korea turns around Group A's second match

In Group A's second match in Guadalajara, South Korea trailed Czech Republic at first: Ladislav Krejci scored in the 59th minute. But Hwang In-Beom equalized in the 67th, and Oh Hyeon-Gu sealed the 2-1 win eleven minutes from the end.[2] Remarkable: Group A is already tightly bunched after just one matchday — both winners on three points, both losers on zero. For Daniel as a German fan with no direct rooting interest on Day 1, it was entertaining nonetheless: two matches, five goals, three reds.

SC Freiburg — Atubolu finds no buyer, the dispute hardens

Off-season. Record signing Backhaus is here, the former starter keeper is stuck.

Newcastle was hottest lead — none remained

The goalkeeper conflict, already simmering since the Backhaus transfer, turns bitter for Noah Atubolu (24). His stated goal is a move, but according to reports, there is no concrete inquiry yet. Newcastle United long seemed the most likely destination — a solid offer never materialized.[3] In the worst case, Atubolu spends season 26/27 on the bench before his contract expires in summer 2027.

The tone is awkward: the club allegedly accuses Atubolu of behaving "not respectfully enough" in negotiations — and there is "no going back" and no compromise.[4] For a club that defines itself through smart academy products, that's an unusually harsh conflict with a homegrown player.

DFB XI — Neuer fit, the starting lineup takes clear shape

Base Winston-Salem · Kickoff Sun 14.06. vs. Curaçao · all times MESZ / BRT.

Expected XI: 4-2-3-1 with Wirtz as No. 10

Manuel Neuer (40), who missed the WC warm-ups with a calf issue, trained in full contact again and is Nagelsmann's clear No. 1 for the opener.[5] In front forms a 4-2-3-1: Joshua Kimmich as right back, a double six of Aleksandar Pavlović and Leon Goretzka, Florian Wirtz as No. 10, Jamal Musiala on the left and Leroy Sané on the right behind a lone striker.[6] Injured Lennart Karl is replaced in the squad by called-up Assan Ouédraogo.[7]

bundesliga.com · fourfourtwo.com
Double six: Two defensive midfielders side-by-side directly in front of the back line — the "six" is the shirt number that the central shield-bearer used to wear. Think of them as two bouncers at the club door: they clean up in front of your defense and feed the ball forward. Advantage against attacking opponents: the middle is doubly covered, one player can push up while the other holds back.

Curaçao first — underdogs, but not a walk-over

Germany kick off on Sunday in Houston (19:00 MESZ / 14:00 BRT) against Curaçao, the tournament's smallest nation. Then come the sterner tests of Group E: Ivory Coast (20.06., Toronto) and Ecuador (25.06., New Jersey).[5] Three days before kickoff, Nagelsmann's plan looks set — the talk is no longer about the system, just nuances on the flanks.

It is Friday, the twelfth of June, and the World Cup has completed its first matchday. Host Mexico has won the opener against South Africa two to zero — but everyone will talk mainly about three red cards.

Julián Quiñones scored in the ninth minute and thus tallied the tournament's very first goal. Raúl Jiménez made it two to zero in the sixty-seventh minute, a strike with history: Jiménez had suffered a fractured skull years ago and dedicated his first World Cup goal to his late father. After that, South Africa lost composure, received two red cards, and finished with nine men. Just before the end, Mexican César Montes was sent off as well.

In Group A's second match, South Korea turned around a deficit and won two to one against Czech Republic. That means Group A is complete after one matchday: Mexico and South Korea each have three points, Czech Republic and South Africa each have zero.

The weekend gets serious for Daniel. Brazil kicks off Saturday evening Brazilian time against Morocco, the semifinalists from twenty twenty-two — so right away the group's toughest test. Coach Carlo Ancelotti says he has locked in his starting XI and relies especially on the pair Vinicius Junior and Raphinha. Kickoff is at seven p.m. in Brazil, in Europe not until Sunday at midnight.

The German XI plays Sunday in Houston against Curaçao, at seven p.m. German time, two p.m. in Brazil. Manuel Neuer is fit again after his calf trouble and a clear number one. Nagelsmann presumably has them lining up four-two-three-one, with Florian Wirtz as the number ten, Jamal Musiala on the left, and Leroy Sané on the right. Assan Ouédraogo is called up to replace injured Lennart Karl.
