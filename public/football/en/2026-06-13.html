Brazil — it starts today, and the opener packs a punch

Group C · MetLife Stadium, New Jersey · Kickoff Sun 00:00 MESZ / Sat 19:00 BRT. Daniel's must-watch.

Ancelotti's XI set — Vinicius and Raphinha as the core

Carlo Ancelotti has named his starting eleven and highlighted the attacking duo of Vinicius Jr. and Raphinha, whose partnership impressed in warm-ups.[1] The much-cited preview lineup: Alisson; Danilo, Marquinhos, Gabriel, Alex Sandro; Casemiro, Bruno Guimarães; Raphinha, Lucas Paquetá, Vinicius Jr.; Matheus Cunha.[2] The match is refereed by Slovenian official Slavko Vinčić.

Two personnel moves affect the right flank: Wesley is ruled out for the entire tournament with a muscle injury from the warm-up friendly against Egypt — Danilo steps in, with Ederson even tested as emergency cover on the wing.[2] And Neymar is dealing with a Grade 2 calf strain that forced him out of the last two friendlies; if he's not ready today, the team is targeting match 2 against Haiti on 19.06.[3]

No walkover Morocco was the fairy tale of 2022 — the first African team to reach the World Cup semis. With Achraf Hakimi, Noussair Mazraoui, and Sofyan Amrabat, they have a European-schooled backbone on the pitch, even if the Atlas Lions have injury worries of their own. If you want Brazil to face their toughest group-stage test on day one, this is it — Haiti and Scotland come later. Oddsmakers and previews slightly favour Brazil (Sports Mole tips 3:1), but "slight" is the word against this opponent.

SC Freiburg — new lead in goalkeeper poker: Real Sociedad chases Atubolu

Off-season thread, continued. Update since Fri Newcastle was yesterday's story — today a Spanish name enters the fray.

San Sebastián over Newcastle? Matarazzo is pushing

In the stalled goalkeeper dispute over Noah Atubolu (24), a new suitor emerges: Real Sociedad is linked with a summer transfer, with reported market value around 25 million €, and deal probability estimated at roughly 15 % as of 09.06.[4] The driver is Real coach Pellegrino Matarazzo — the former Bundesliga manager (Stuttgart, Hoffenheim) took over the Basques in December and wants Atubolu as his starter, especially since their current No. 1 Álex Remiro is in talks with Napoli.[5]

The situation itself hasn't eased: Freiburg signals no willingness to sell, and no binding offer is on the table. Atubolu, for his part, is openly hoping for a Premier League club — Chelsea is named as his preference, and the English league is his dream.[6] Newcastle, yesterday's hottest lead, is thus just one of several names, without any turning into a done deal yet.

World Cup Friday — Canada claims first point in history, USA crushes Paraguay

Opening matchday 2 · Groups B & D underway · 12.06.2026.

Canada 1:1 Bosnia — a late point with symbolic weight

Co-host Canada drew 1:1 with Bosnia-Herzegovina — and it was historic: Jovo Lukic put Bosnia ahead in the 21st minute before substitute Cyle Larin equalized in the 78th minute. It is Canada's first-ever World Cup point — in their two previous appearances (1986, 2022) the team had lost all six matches.[9] On home soil, that minor success becomes a statement.

espn.com · olympics.com

USA 4:1 Paraguay — Balogun brace, record for the US men

The USA made their statement in Los Angeles: an early own goal from Bobadilla (7th), then a brace by Folarin Balogun (31st and just before halftime) to make it 3:0, later another from Gio Reyna. Paraguay pulled one back in the 73rd. The 4:1 is the highest World Cup win for the US men ever — they've never scored more than three goals in a single World Cup match before.[10] An opening statement from the hosts that instantly pressures Australia and Turkey in Group D.

Germany — facing Curaçao tomorrow, Neuer is ready

Base in Winston-Salem · Opener Sun 14.06. in Houston · all times MESZ / BRT.

Neuer as clear No. 1, the system is set

Manuel Neuer (40), who sat out both friendlies against Finland and the USA due to calf problems, is fit to start against Curaçao according to Nagelsmann and remains the undisputed No. 1.[11] In front, the familiar 4-2-3-1 takes shape: Joshua Kimmich at right-back, a defensive double from Pavlović and Goretzka, Florian Wirtz at No. 10, Jamal Musiala on the left and Leroy Sané on the right behind a lone striker.[12] Curaçao is the smallest nation in World Cup history — tactically an outsider, but the opener is meant to build confidence before tougher tests (Ivory Coast, Ecuador).

bundesliga.com · cbssports.com
Double pivot: Two defensive midfielders side-by-side directly in front of the defence — the "six" is the old shirt number for the centre-back. Think of it like two bouncers at the club door: they clean up in front of the defence and launch the ball upfield. Advantage: the centre is doubly protected, one can step up while the other holds position.

It is Saturday, June thirteenth, and for Daniel, this is the day he has been waiting for. Tonight, Brazil time, the Seleção kicks off the World Cup — and they face Morocco, the two thousand twenty-two semifinalist.

Coach Carlo Ancelotti has named his starting eleven and is counting on the attacking pair of Vinicius Junior and Raphinha. On the right side of defence, however, there are concerns: Wesley is out of the tournament with a muscle injury, and Danilo steps in to replace him. Kickoff is at seven p.m. Brazil time, and in Europe, that's midnight on Sunday.

Morocco is no warm-up opponent. With Achraf Hakimi, Noussair Mazraoui, and Sofyan Amrabat on the pitch, they have a European-trained frame. The oddsmakers slightly favour Brazil, but slightly is the word against this opponent.

On Friday, the World Cup continued. Co-host Canada drew one-all with Bosnia — and it was historic. The substitute Cyle Larin equalized in the seventy-eighth minute, and it is Canada's very first World Cup point ever. In their two previous appearances, they had lost all six matches.

The USA, on the other hand, crushed Paraguay four to one. Folarin Balogun scored twice in the first half alone, and later Gio Reyna added another. It is the highest World Cup victory for the US men ever — before now they had never scored more than three goals in a World Cup match.

Tomorrow it is Germany's turn. Julian Nagelsmann's team starts on Sunday in Houston against Curaçao, the smallest nation in World Cup history, at seven p.m. German time, two p.m. Brazil time. Manuel Neuer is fit after his calf troubles and is the clear number one. Germany will line up in the familiar four-two-three-one with Florian Wirtz at number ten, Jamal Musiala on the left, and Leroy Sané on the right.
