Germany's World Cup opener: today vs. Curaçao

Kickoff 19:00 MESZ / 14:00 BRT — no result yet at deadline

This afternoon (US time), the World Cup begins for the DFB side: at NRG Stadium in Houston, Germany faces Curaçao in Group E, kickoff 19:00 MESZ / 14:00 BRT. When this newsletter went to press, the match hadn't started yet — ESPN and FOX Sports listed it as "Pre-Match." So there's no result here: inventing a final score would be the exact opposite of what this newsletter stands for. Tomorrow's brief will bring the verified final score.[1]

On paper, it looks like a clear favorite's task. But the old World Cup wisdom applies: the opening match is never the one where a top favorite shines, but the one where they show their nerves. Brazil just gave us a lesson (see opposite). For Germany, the priorities are simple: three points and no injuries out of the opener — the rest of the group will be tougher.

Curaçao: the smallest World Cup nation in history

Curaçao — a Caribbean island with about 156,000 inhabitants, autonomous part of the Kingdom of the Netherlands — is the smallest nation ever to play a World Cup finals match. That's no reason for arrogance: the squad is built on players with Dutch Eredivisie and professional experience, and a deep-sitting underdog waiting for the counterattack is exactly the scenario where a favorite can get bogged down.

Deep block: Picture a team that parks almost entirely in its own half — like a bus in front of the goal. They give up their own play and wait for the one quick counterattack. For the favorite, that means: lots of possession, but no space, and a single mistake can be costly.

SC Freiburg: transfer window is open

Bundesliga paused, market not — as of 14.06.2026, source: transfermarkt.de

Record goalkeeper and a striker from Belgium

Freiburg has made the costliest transfer in club history: goalkeeper Mio Backhaus (22) arrives for 12.0 million € from Werder Bremen to take over the gloves long-term. The club also signs Japanese center forward Keisuke Goto (21, 10.0 million € from Anderlecht) — recently on loan at Sint-Truiden with 10 goals and 5 assists in 28 games — plus midfielder Rihito Yamamoto (24, 7.0 million € from Sint-Truiden).[3]

On balance, despite the purchases, that's a net loss of only 3.2 million € (incomings 29.0 million €, outgoings 25.8 million €). The reason: one big sale finances almost everything.

transfermarkt.us

Röhl brings in 25 million € — and comes right back

The clever window construction: Merlin Röhl (23) transfers from loan to permanent deal for 25.0 million € to Everton — and returns immediately on loan to Freiburg. In plain English: Freiburg pockets the fee, keeps the player on the pitch for next season, and has essentially paid for the Backhaus-Goto pairing with his own transfer. Junior Adamu (25) also left for 800,000 € to Schalke 04.

Brazil: bumpy start — 1:1 vs. Morocco

Vinícius rescues the draw

At MetLife Stadium (East Rutherford, NJ), the Seleção couldn't get past a 1:1 tie with Morocco on 13.06. Ismael Saibari (21') put Morocco ahead, but Vinícius Júnior (32') equalized before halftime. For Brazil, an opening draw is a rare stumble — the expected masterclass didn't materialize.[4]

Why the draw counts: Like in skat: the first hand isn't the whole game, but whoever stumbles early has to score under pressure later. Brazil needs wins over Haiti and Scotland now — a second draw and the Round of 16 wobbles.

Matchday 1 results (Groups A–D)

While Germany and Brazil command the spotlight, the group stage is already in full swing:
