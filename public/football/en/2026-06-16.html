Germany · 7:1 in World Cup Opener

Group E, Matchday 1 · Sun 14.06.2026, Houston · Source: Sky Sports / ESPN

That was a statement. Against World Cup debutant Curaçao — the smallest nation ever to compete in a tournament — Germany delivered a 7:1 in Houston. Felix Nmecha opened after just six minutes, then the machine ran: Nico Schlotterbeck (38.), a Havertz penalty in first-half stoppage (45.+5), then Musiala (47.), Brown (68.), Undav (78.), and Havertz again (88.). Comenencia's equalizer (21.) was merely a footnote. Kai Havertz with a brace as the evening's top scorer.[1]

Worth noting: a 7:1 against an underdog tells you more about efficiency in attack than defensive solidity — but that very goal tally is what Germany has often lacked lately. With +6 goal difference, Germany tops Group E after matchday one, level on points with Côte d'Ivoire (1:0 vs. Ecuador), but ahead on goal difference.

Brazil · Grinding 1:1 Against Morocco

Group C, Matchday 1 · Sat 13.06.2026, MetLife Stadium · Source: ESPN / Wikipedia

No walkover for the Seleção. Morocco took the lead through Ismael Saibari in the 21st minute before Vinícius Júnior equalized in the 32nd — a fine finish. After that, goalkeeper Alisson kept the point with a late double save. From Brazil's perspective, too little return for all that possession: Morocco sat compact and forced Brazil to work the halfspace.[4]

Halfspace: the strip between the center and the sideline — not quite center, not quite wing. Picture the field in five vertical lanes: the second and fourth lanes are the halfspaces. From there, you can play passes inward or toward the goal line, which is why modern teams tuck their creative players right there. Morocco locked those zones — and Brazil's playmakers found no way through.

The table situation is awkward: Scotland won their opener 1:0 vs. Haiti (McGinn, 28th) and top Group C. Morocco and Brazil both have one point — separated only by fair play points, and Brazil sits lower at −2 (Morocco 0). Translation: after matchday one, the Seleção are mathematically in third place.

Fair Play ranking: When two teams are identical in everything — points, goal difference, goals — one of the final tiebreakers is conduct on the pitch: yellow and red cards each cost points. Like traffic tickets — misbehavior drops you down. Brazil picked up more cards against Morocco and so sits behind on that measure.

SC Freiburg · Quiet Summer Weeks, One In, One Out

Transfer window open (May–Aug) · Source: bundesliga.com · as of 11.06.2026

Arrival: Keisuke Goto

Freiburg has signed Japanese striker Keisuke Goto (21) from RSC Anderlecht — fee undisclosed, contract signed 11.06. Goto comes well-recommended: 10 goals and 5 assists in 28 league appearances on loan at Sint-Truiden last season. He's the third Japanese player in the squad — after Rihito Yamamoto and Yuito Suzuki — as the club clearly expands its pipeline into Japanese scouting.[5]

Departure: Junior Adamu → Schalke 04

Austrian striker Junior Adamu moves to Bundesliga-promoted FC Schalke 04, base fee €800k, contract through 30.06.2029. Adamu made 67 competitive appearances for Freiburg with 7 goals and 4 assists, and spent 2025/26 on loan at Celtic (Scottish double). A player who never quite broke through at Freiburg — the sale clears space up front for Goto.[6]

2025/26 Season Summary Freiburg finished the Bundesliga 7th with 47 points (13 W, 8 D, 13 L, 51:57 goals) and qualified for Conference League 2026/27. However, they lost the Europa League final on 20.05., falling 0:3 to Aston Villa — runners-up in European competition, but the bigger trophy slipped away. Season top scorer Igor Matanovic (11 league goals, 15 across all competitions). World Cup squad includes Philipp Lienhart (Austria), Yuito Suzuki (Japan), Johan Manzambi, and Igor Matanovic.[7]

It's Tuesday, June 16th, and the football calendar is dominated by the World Cup in the USA, Canada, and Mexico. For you, that means no Freiburg match, no Bundesliga — that wrapped mid-May — and the Brazilian league is paused until July 19. Only the tournament matters.

Germany's national team made a statement in its opener. Against World Cup debutant Curaçao in Houston, they posted a seven to one victory. Felix Nmecha scored after just six minutes, and then the machine rolled on. Kai Havertz got a brace, plus goals from Schlotterbeck, Musiala, Brown, and Undav. With that goal difference, Germany tops Group E, level on points with Côte d'Ivoire. The next match is Saturday against those same Ivorians, at twenty-two hundred hours German time — five p.m. your time in Brazil — in Toronto.

Brazil, though, struggled. Against Morocco, they could only draw one to one. Morocco went ahead early, Vinícius Júnior equalized in the thirty-second minute, and keeper Alisson had to make a double save late on to earn a point. In the standings, that's tough luck: Scotland leads Group C after a win, and Brazil sits third — level on points with Morocco but behind on fair play points because the Seleção picked up more cards. A win against Haiti on Friday is essential.

Tonight your time, the matches continue. France play Senegal at four p.m. Brazil time — a match with history, because Senegal beat France in the opening game of the two-thousand-two World Cup. Later come Iraq against Norway and, deep into the night, world champions Argentina against Algeria. As we went to press, those matches hadn't kicked off yet, so no results to report.

At your club, Freiburg, the summer window brought quiet movement. Japanese striker Keisuke Goto, twenty-one years old, arrived from Anderlecht — the third Japanese player in the squad. In return, Junior Adamu heads to promoted Schalke for eight hundred thousand euros. Reminder: Freiburg finished seventh in the Bundesliga, qualified for the Conference League, and made it to the Europa League final, losing three to nothing to Aston Villa.

Tomorrow the group stage rolls on, with Portugal, England against Croatia, and Austria — where Freiburg's Philipp Lienhart is in the squad. And the weekend brings the crucial matches: Brazil against Haiti on Friday, Germany against Côte d'Ivoire on Saturday. Until then.
