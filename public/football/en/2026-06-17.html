Germany: 7-1 masterclass to open

Seven goals, seven goal-scoring moments — Germany's highest World Cup win in years

It could have been a nervous opener: World Cup debutant Curaçao (the small Caribbean island, the lowest-population nation ever to play in a World Cup) actually equalized through Livano Comenencia in the 21st minute — the island's first-ever World Cup goal. Then Germany ran wild at the NRG Stadium in Houston: 7-1, final score.[1]

Felix Nmecha opened the scoring in the 6th minute, Nico Schlotterbeck headed in shortly after (38th), Kai Havertz converted a penalty just before halftime (45+5) and added his second in the 88th. Between them, Jamal Musiala scored (47th) plus substitute impact: Nathaniel Brown (68th) with goal and assist, Deniz Undav (78th) with goal and two assists.[2]

Why +6 matters: In World Cup group play, goal difference decides ties when teams are level on points. Think of the group like a savings account — every goal is credit that can secure a knockout spot in a tight race. Germany's +6 after one match is a cushion.

Only against an organized, physical block like the Ivorians will you see whether Germany's offense finds solutions without open space. Record-breaking night: Messi, Mbappé, Haaland

Messi ties Klose's timeless World Cup record

Last night in Kansas City, Lionel Messi delivered what five prior World Cups never gave him: a World Cup hat-trick. As title-holder Argentina beat Algeria 3-0, the 38-year-old scored three — making him the oldest player ever to score a hat-trick at a World Cup.[10]

His first goal (17th minute, a long-range strike) was his 16th World Cup goal overall — exactly the mark of Miroslav Klose, the sole previous World Cup goal-scoring record holder. Messi now stands level with the German atop the all-time World Cup scorer list.[11]

espn.com · abcnews.com

Mbappé past Fontaine, Haaland finally at the World Cup

Two other stars made history: Kylian Mbappé scored twice as France beat Senegal 3-1 and now sits at 14 World Cup goals — new French record, past Just Fontaine's 13 from 1958.[12] And Erling Haaland debuted for Norway (4-1 over Iraq) in the country's first World Cup match in 28 years — also with a brace.[13]

Austria after 36 years — Arnautović in 90+12

This morning (Daniel's time) we had a result: Austria beat World Cup debutant Jordan 3-1 at Levi's Stadium in San Francisco (kickoff 15:00 MESZ / 10:00 BRT) — Austria's first World Cup win since 1990, after 36 years. Romano Schmid (21st) and an own goal (76th) set it up, Marko Arnautović converted a penalty in the 12th (!) minute of stoppage time for the final score.[14]

It's Wednesday, June seventeenth, mid-group stage of the twenty twenty-six World Cup in Canada, Mexico, and the United States. Day two is underway, and German fans have plenty to celebrate.

Germany made a statement: seven-one over dark-horse Curaçao in Houston. Kai Havertz with two, plus goals from Nmecha, Schlotterbeck, Musiala, and strong substitutes Brown and Undav. Curaçao managed their first-ever World Cup goal. Germany leads Group E with a plus-six goal difference. The real test comes Saturday against Ivory Coast, level on points, at ten p.m. German time, five p.m. Brazil time.

Yesterday belonged to the record-breakers. Lionel Messi scored his first World Cup hat-trick, three-nil against Algeria. At thirty-eight, he's the oldest player ever to score a hat-trick in the World Cup. His first goal was his sixteenth World Cup goal overall, tying Miroslav Klose's all-time record.

Kylian Mbappé also made history: fourteen World Cup goals, a new French record, surpassing Just Fontaine. And Erling Haaland made his World Cup debut with Norway, four-one over Iraq, with a brace. Austria won three-one over Jordan this morning, their first World Cup victory in thirty-six years, sealed by an Arnautović penalty in the twelfth minute of stoppage time.

No win yet for Brazil. The Seleção drew one-one with Morocco, with Vinícius equalizing. Scotland surprisingly lead the group. On Friday, at ten p.m. Brazil time, a win is mandatory against Haiti.
