SC Freiburg — Transfer window

As of 19.06.2026, Source: transfermarkt.de

While the league rests, SC Freiburg's transfer department is the only thing moving — and it's moving fast. The headline arrival is Mio Backhaus (22), goalkeeper from Werder Bremen, for reported 12 mln EUR. According to transfermarkt.de, this is the most expensive transfer in club history — for a keeper, underscoring long-term planning at the position. Added to that: Japanese striker Keisuke Goto (21) from RSC Anderlecht for roughly 10 mln EUR; Goto is also in Japan's World Cup squad and playing in this tournament.[1]

On the outgoing side, the biggest hit still brings cash: Merlin Röhl (23) joins Everton permanently for 25 mln EUR. The attacking midfielder was on loan in England since September 2025; with Everton's secured Premier League survival, the purchase option was triggered. For Freiburg, it's a clean gain — an academy product selling for double Backhaus's price. Also out: striker Junior Adamu (25) to Schalke 04 for around 800,000 EUR, and Rihito Yamamoto (24) returns from his Sint-Truiden loan and is now permanently on the books at roughly 7 mln EUR.[1]

Data caveat All transfer fees are transfermarkt.de estimates (as of 19.06.), not officially confirmed by Freiburg. Backhaus reports vary: transfermarkt.de says 12 mln EUR, other German media report around 15 mln EUR plus up to 3 mln EUR in bonuses — the reliable range is 12–15 mln EUR. For Yamamoto, it's unclear whether it's a permanent buy or end of loan — the source lists both.

Context: this is a typical Freiburg summer — solid, deliberate, no hubris. Backhaus and Goto are both young; with Röhl's 25 million, the net balance is actually strongly positive. Four Freiburg players are also in World Cup squads: Philipp Lienhart (Austria), Igor Matanovic (Croatia), Yuito Suzuki (Japan), and Johan Manzambi (Switzerland) — plus new arrival Goto for Japan.[2]

World Cup 2026 — the German angle [3][4]

Group E · After Matchday 1 (19.06.2026)

The opener couldn't have gone better: Germany thrashed Curaçao 7:1 (14.06., Houston). Felix Nmecha struck in the 6th minute, Kai Havertz scored twice (including a penalty), plus goals from Schlotterbeck, Musiala, Brown, and Undav. That goal difference is gold in a tight group.

Tomorrow evening it tightens: Germany – Ivory Coast, Saturday 20.06., 22:00 CEST / 17:00 BRT in Toronto. The Ivorians won their opener 1:0 vs Ecuador (Diallo, 90') and also have three points. Both teams face a direct shootout for group top — a draw likely helps both, a win secures first place and a potentially easier knockout run.

It's Friday, June nineteenth, and football is now running entirely on World Cup time. The Bundesliga and Brasileirão are both on break, so everything today is about the World Cup in the USA, Canada, and Mexico. It's Matchday 2 of the group stage, and four matches are lined up for tonight — none have kicked off yet as of this brief.

The main event for us here in Brazil: the Seleção plays Haiti, kickoff at half past two Central European Summer Time, which is half past nine Brazilian time, in Philadelphia. Brazil opened disappointed with just a one-to-one draw to Morocco, with Vinícius Júnior scoring. Against outsider Haiti, a win today is essential, otherwise the group gets tense.

At the same time, Brazil's other two group rivals face each other, Scotland against Morocco. For the Seleção, a draw there would be the best outcome because it keeps both rivals at distance.

The strongest match of the evening starts early: USA versus Australia in Seattle at nine o'clock Central European Summer Time, four o'clock in the afternoon here. Both won their openers, the winner today takes the group lead.

And tomorrow it turns German: Germany plays Ivory Coast, Saturday evening at ten Central European Summer Time, five o'clock Brazilian time, in Toronto. After an impressive seven-to-one win over Curaçao, the group title is within reach. The Ivorians have also won their first match, so it's a real final for first place.

At SC Freiburg the transfer window is running hot. The club is bringing in goalkeeper Mio Backhaus from Bremen for twelve million euros, the club's record transfer. Plus Japanese striker Keisuke Goto from Anderlecht for ten million. On the other side goes Merlin Röhl permanently to Everton for twenty-five million because Premier League survival triggered the purchase option. On balance a clearly positive result.

The Bundesliga season is wrapped: Bayern is champion with eighty-nine points and a club record of one hundred twenty-two goals, Freiburg finished seventh and play in the Conference League. In Brazil Palmeiras lead the frozen table with forty-one points.

Tomorrow we'll focus mainly on the German match and whether Brazil got the win they needed against Haiti. See you then.
