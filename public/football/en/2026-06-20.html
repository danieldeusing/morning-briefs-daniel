Germany – Ivory Coast: tonight is about the Round of 16

Preview — Kickoff 22:00 MESZ / 17:00 BRT, BMO Field, Toronto (Group E, Matchday 2). Match not yet underway at time of publication.

This is the match of the day for Daniel. Both teams won their opening fixture and sit on three points atop Group E — whoever wins tonight will have a Round of 16 spot essentially locked up.[1] Germany comes in with momentum: 7:1 against Curaçao was a masterclass (more below). Ivory Coast, meanwhile, scraped past Ecuador with a late goal in the 90th minute — a finish by substitute Manchester United winger Amad Diallo — 1:0, the latest goal the Ivorians have ever scored at a World Cup.[2]

Bookmakers have Germany heavily favored (Moneyline around −195, roughly two-thirds win probability).[3] But beware: Ivory Coast has individual quality and a compact defensive shape — they held a clean sheet against Ecuador for long stretches. If Germany gets careless and runs into a counter-attack, it could get tight.

What matters today A win puts Germany through with six points (barring an extremely unlikely goal-difference scenario). A draw keeps it alive until the final match against Ecuador on Thursday (June 25). A loss leaves everything open.

7:1 against Curaçao — Germany is all-time World Cup top scorer

June 14, 2026, NRG Stadium, Houston · Matchday 1

Germany set a record in its opener: with the 7:1 victory over Curaçao, they surpassed Brazil as all-time World Cup top scorers — the national team now holds 239 goals, the most of any nation (Brazil follows with 238).[4] Remarkably, it was tight for a long time: Curaçao, the smallest nation ever to qualify for a World Cup, equalized via Comenecia (21st minute) to make it 1:1 and hung on until the 38th minute.[5] Then the floodgates opened.

The scorers: Nmecha (6th), Schlotterbeck (38th), Havertz by penalty (45+5), Musiala (47th), Brown (68th), Undav (78th), and Havertz again (88th).[6] Kai Havertz with two, Jamal Musiala proving decisive once more — the attack looks in form. That's what makes tonight's test against defensively compact Ivory Coast so intriguing: can the team's quality shine through against an opponent that closes down space?

Brazil 3:0 over Haiti — Cunha and Vinícius Júnior deliver

June 19, 2026, Lincoln Financial Field, Philadelphia · Matchday 2

Things went smoothly for Daniel's second home yesterday: The Seleção beat Haiti 3:0. Matheus Cunha scored twice (23rd, 36th), Real Madrid's Vinícius Júnior added one shortly before the break (45+3).[7] Cunha's brace carries historic weight: it was only the 10th time ever that a Brazilian scored two goals in a single World Cup half — Neymar last did it in 2014 against Cameroon.[8] Raphinha had actually scored early, but the goal was disallowed for offside (12th).[7]

After a sluggish 1:1 opener against Morocco, this is a release valve. Brazil now sits at four points — tied with Morocco atop Group C, but with superior goal difference (see table).

Neymar: the race against time

Status as of June 19, 2026

The big question mark around the Seleção remains Neymar. A calf strain has sidelined him for both group matches — he sat on the bench against Morocco and missed Haiti entirely, staying in New Jersey for rehab.[9] Coach Carlo Ancelotti is deliberately managing his return with an eye toward the knockout rounds. His target comeback is the Round of 16; his participation in the final group match against Scotland on Wednesday (June 24) is in doubt.[10] That the team looked so assured without him eases some pressure — but he'll be vital once things get tight in the knockout stages.

Also today · Surprises in the tournament

Alongside Germany's match, Group F's pivotal showdown unfolds today: Netherlands – Sweden (19:00 MESZ / 14:00 BRT, Houston).[11] Sweden steamrolled Tunisia to open 5:1 — Viktor Gyökeres and Alexander Isak, two of the tournament's best strikers, stood out.[12] The Netherlands managed only a 2:2 draw against Japan.

The biggest surprise comes from Group H: Spain could not break through Cape Verde, finishing 0:0 — the co-favorite stumbled, and after Matchday 1 the group sits wide open with all four teams on one point each.[13] Group G is equally balanced: Belgium, Egypt, Iran, and New Zealand all drew with each other, all on one point.[11]
