Brazil leads — but Scotland will force the title-clincher

Group C · as of 21.06.2026

3–0 over Haiti: Cunha double, Vini seals the deal

Brazil dispatched Haiti 3–0 on Friday (19.06.) in Philadelphia with ease. Matheus Cunha bagged a brace (23rd, 36th), Vinícius Júnior added the third deep into the first-half injury time (45.+3). The Seleção now sits on four points from two games (one win, a 1–1 draw against Morocco in their opener) and leads the group on goal difference.[1]

Wikipedia · Group C

Wednesday is the title-clincher: Scotland for top spot

This is where it tightens, and that's the story. Brazil and Morocco both sit on four points, with Scotland lurking on three. Brazil cannot be eliminated anymore (even a loss leaves them on four points, Scotland can muster at most six — the group will be decided by goal difference and head-to-head), but winning the group remains wide open. And that matters: top spot usually means a supposedly easier run to the quarter-finals in the tournament bracket.

Scotland–Brazil kicks off on Wednesday, 24.06., at 00:00 MESZ / 19:00 BRT in Hard Rock Stadium in Miami Gardens — for Daniel in Brazil a pleasant evening kick-off time. Morocco take on already-eliminated Haiti in parallel, so should deliver. Brazil must win or at least protect its goal difference.[1]

Germany: Round of 32 secure, now the group crown

Group E · as of 21.06.2026

Undav's late double sends Germany past Ivory Coast

Drama: Germany trailed Ivory Coast 1–0 in Toronto on Saturday (20.06.) through Kessié (30th), before Deniz Undav struck twice — the leveler in the 68th, the winner to make it 2–1 in the 90.+4. With the last-minute victory, Germany sits on six points, has won Group E and is safely through to the Round of 32 as group winners.[2][3]

Wikipedia · Group E · Al Jazeera

Final group match against Ecuador — a formality

With the Round of 32 already sealed, the closer against Ecuador (Thu 25.06., 22:00 MESZ / 17:00 BRT, MetLife Stadium) is just about the group crown — already secured — and maintaining rhythm. German head coach-style, there will likely be rotation — a good chance to manage workload without a must-result. Their opener was a gala performance: 7–1 over Curaçao (Havertz brace, Musiala, and others).[2]

It's Sunday, June twenty-first. The Bundesliga and the Brasileirão are paused for the World Cup in the USA, Canada, and Mexico. For Daniel, two things matter today: Germany has advanced to the next round, and Brazil faces a genuine title-clincher.

Germany has secured the Round of 32 as group winners. Against Ivory Coast in Toronto on Saturday, the team trailed through Kessié, but Deniz Undav turned the match around and scored in the fourth minute of injury time to make it two to one. Six points, Group E won. The last group match against Ecuador on Thursday at five p.m. Brazil time is just a formality.

Brazil leads its group. The three-to-zero win over Haiti was dominant, with a brace from Matheus Cunha and a goal from Vinícius Júnior. But the table is tight: Brazil and Morocco both have four points, Scotland three. On Wednesday at seven p.m. Brazil time in Miami, Brazil faces Scotland for first place in the group. Brazil cannot be eliminated, but topping the group means an easier path through the tournament bracket.

Tonight four group matches kick off: Spain against Saudi Arabia, Belgium against Iran, Uruguay against Cape Verde, and New Zealand against Egypt. At deadline, the results are not yet in, so this brief lists only the kick-off times, not match reports.

Already settled today: Japan's four-to-zero win over Tunisia — Tunisia is knocked out. In Group F, the Netherlands and Japan are now level on points. Alongside Germany, Mexico and the USA have also qualified.

At Freiburg there's transfer activity during the break: Goalkeeper Mio Backhaus is arriving from Werder Bremen, young striker Keisuke Goto was signed for around ten million euros, and first-choice keeper Noah Atubolu is rumored in kicker magazine to be eyeing a move to England — still unconfirmed.
