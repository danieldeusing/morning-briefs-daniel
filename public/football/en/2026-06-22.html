DFB Team: Undav delivers the goods

It looked like it was heading for a cliffhanger. The Ivory Coast led in Toronto through Franck Kessié (30.), Germany struggled early on — until head coach Julian Nagelsmann made changes at halftime, bringing on Nadiem Amiri and Deniz Undav. This pair turned it around: Amiri's cross allowed Undav to volley in the equalizer in the 68th minute, and in stoppage time (90+4) Undav finished off a Felix Nmecha assist to seal the 2:1.[1]

With the substitute's brace, Germany advances to the Round of 16 after just two matches — joining host USA and Mexico among the first teams to qualify a game before the end. Group E is led decisively with 6 points and a 9:2 goal record. The opening 7:1 demolition of Curaçao padded the goal difference nicely.[1][2]

Context The performance wasn't convincing, but the result certainly was. That the goals came off the bench is a luxury problem for Nagelsmann — impact subs matter more in knockout football than a sluggish first half. Before the final group game, he can now rotate without worry.

SC Freiburg: Season done, squad in flux

For Freiburg, the 2025/26 Bundesliga season is old news: 7th place, 47 points (13 wins, 8 draws, 13 losses, 51:57 goals) — enough for Conference League qualification, the third European tier. Solid result, but no surprise like in some past campaigns. Now the transfer window (open May–August) is in full swing, and the sporting director and coaching staff are reshaping the squad noticeably.[3][4]

Biggest departure: Midfielder Merlin Röhl (23) is heading to the Premier League — Everton. Per footballplace.co.uk, a mandatory clause triggered after Everton secured survival; the fee sits at roughly 23–25 million €. That's cash Freiburg needed — but one less starter in midfield.[4]

Three came in: Striker Keisuke Goto (21, from RSC Anderlecht, 10 million €) — the Japanese forward scored 10 goals in 28 games on loan at Sint-Truiden last season and added 5 assists. Goalkeeper Mio Backhaus (22, from Werder Bremen, ~12 million €, est.) and defensive midfielder Rihito Yamamoto (24, from Sint-Truiden, 7 million €). Net transfer balance so far sits at roughly −2.7 million € (≈ 29 million in spending, ≈ 26.3 million in revenue).[5]

Mandatory purchase clause: A condition in a loan deal that triggers an automatic buy if something agreed upon happens — here: Everton stays in the top flight. Like a rent-to-own option that converts itself to ownership once a box is checked. The selling club (Freiburg) can't hold on to the player anymore.
Assessment Clear reshaping in breadth and youth: two Japanese prospects plus a young keeper as a bet on the future. Röhl's sale stings on the pitch but is almost a financial necessity — with the near-breakeven balance, room remains for another midfield move by end of August. The window is still open for over two months.

Brazil: Final push for top spot

The Seleção has the Round of 16 locked up, but Group C is tight: Brazil and Morocco both at 4 points, Brazil ahead on goal difference (+3 vs. +1). Opening day brought a 1:1 with Morocco (Vinícius Júnior scored in the 32nd), followed by a commanding 3:0 over Haiti. Haiti is the first team eliminated from the World Cup.[6]

The group final takes place on Wednesday, 24.06., 19:00 BRT (00:00 MESZ on Thu 25.06.) in Miami Gardens: Scotland – Brazil. Fits perfectly into Daniel's Brazilian evening schedule. Scotland (3 points, off their first World Cup win since 1990 vs. Haiti) are still alive and have to win — Brazil usually just need a draw to top the group. Winner of Group C gets a potentially easier path in the Round of 16.[6][7]

Why it matters A win secures first place and thus the better bracket side. If Brazil lose unexpectedly, Morocco (playing Haiti simultaneously) could leap ahead — then a tougher Round of 16 opponent looms. Not a disaster, but a turning point.

It's Monday, June twenty-second. The Bundesliga is asleep, the Brasileirão is paused — but the World Cup is in full swing, and Daniel has three pieces of good news.

First: Germany advances to the Round of 16. Against Ivory Coast it was sluggish, the Africans ahead through Kessié. But coach Nagelsmann brought Deniz Undav off the bench, and he turned it around single-handedly. First the equalizer in the sixty-eighth minute, then the winner deep in injury time. Two to one, and Germany is through a game before the end. Group E is led decisively with six points and nine to two in goals.

The last group game against Ecuador on Thursday evening, ten p.m. German time, five p.m. in Brazil, airs on ARD — but it's just about the group title and the seeding. Nagelsmann will likely rotate heavily.

The second piece of good news comes from Brazil. The Seleção leads their group, tied with Morocco at four points each, ahead only on goal difference. One to one against Morocco with a goal from Vinícius Júnior, then a clear three to nothing against Haiti, who are the first team out.

The group final is Wednesday evening at seven p.m. Brazil time. Perfect timing for Daniel's Brazilian evening. Scotland are alive and need to win. Brazil usually just need a draw for top spot.

And third, at Daniel's beloved club: SC Freiburg finished the season seventh with forty-seven points, enough for Conference League qualification. In the transfer window, Freiburg sell Merlin Röhl to England's Everton for roughly twenty-three to twenty-five million euros. In return come three new faces: the Japanese striker Goto, goalkeeper Backhaus, and midfielder Yamamoto.

Tonight the ball rolls four times in the USA. The standout: Argentina versus Austria with Messi, seven p.m. German time. Plus France against Iraq and late at night Norway against Senegal and Jordan against Algeria.
