Germany: Mission accomplished, a bitter loss [1][2][3]

Two wins, nine goals — already through

Germany has secured the new Round of 32 (Round of 32) before the final group match. After a 7:1 victory over Curaçao in the opener (14.06., Houston), there followed a far tighter 2:1 win against Ivory Coast (20.06., Toronto). Group E standings: 2 matches, 2 wins, 9:2 goals, 6 points — first place, well clear of Ivory Coast (3), Ecuador (1) and Curaçao (1).[1]

Against Curaçao it was a masterclass: Nmecha (6.), an own goal by Comenencia (21.), Schlotterbeck (38.), Havertz by penalty (45.+5) and after the break Musiala (47.), Brown (68.), Undav (78.) and Havertz again (88.). Against robust Ivory Coast it played out differently — after Kessié's opener (30.), Deniz Undav turned it around with a brace (68., assist Amiri; 90.+4, assist Nmecha) in injury time.[2]

Schlotterbeck misses rest of World Cup

The downside: Nico Schlotterbeck suffered a medial ankle ligament rupture against Ivory Coast. The DFB confirmed his exit for the rest of the tournament — a major loss for central defence, who started in Toronto before being substituted at halftime for Antonio Rüdiger.[3]

Note Before the tournament, Bayern's 18-year-old Lennart Karl was ruled out with a thigh muscle tear (in training); replacement Assan Ouédraogo (RB Leipzig, 20) was called up. A formal replacement for Schlotterbeck had not been confirmed by deadline.

Final group match & the road ahead

On Thursday, 25.06., 22:00 MESZ / 17:00 BRT, Germany faces Ecuador in East Rutherford (New York-New Jersey Stadium) — sport-wise without pressure since the group is won. The big picture: Group stage after matchday 2 [6]

The new 48-team format: From each of the 12 groups, the top 2 plus the 8 best third-place finishers advance to the Round of 32 — 32 teams, then Round of 16, quarterfinals, semifinals, final.

Round of 32: an additional knockout round that didn't exist in earlier tournaments. Because 48 instead of 32 teams compete, an extra round is needed before the traditional Round of 16 begins.

It's Tuesday, June 23rd, and in the middle of the World Cup in the USA, Canada and Mexico, there's one big piece of good news for German fans: Germany has already advanced.

Two matches, two wins, nine to two goals. First a seven to one win over Curaçao, then a tight two to one win over Ivory Coast that Deniz Undav turned around with two late goals. With that, Germany is group winners in Group E and safely into the new Round of 32 — the extra knockout round that's appearing for the first time at this first 48-team World Cup.

The downside is Nico Schlotterbeck. The centre back suffered a medial ankle ligament rupture against Ivory Coast and is out for the rest of the tournament. Before the World Cup, young Bayern player Lennart Karl was already ruled out with a thigh muscle tear; his replacement Assan Ouédraogo was called up.

Sport-wise the final group match on Thursday against Ecuador is pressure-free — kickoff is ten p.m. our time, or five p.m. in Ribeirão Claro. The question is whether Undav plays from the start after his brace. The Round of 32 then happens on Monday, the 29th of June, in Boston; the opponent is still to be determined.

In the broader picture, matchday two in Groups K and L is today — Portugal versus Uzbekistan, England versus Ghana, Panama versus Croatia and Colombia versus DR Congo. At deadline in the afternoon the results weren't in yet, so there are no final scores here, just kickoff times.

Alongside it the tournament's storylines: Lionel Messi is now the sole all-time World Cup goalscorer with eighteen goals, Norway is back in the knockout stage for the first time in almost thirty years — while Belgium with only two points and Cristiano Ronaldo's Portugal are stumbling after a tepid draw.
