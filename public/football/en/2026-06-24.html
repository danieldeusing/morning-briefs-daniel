Brazil: A stumble with a safety net

Scotland 1:0 Brazil — and the Seleção advances anyway

Brazil has lost its final group match: Scotland wins 1:0 and delivers the Seleção's first defeat of this World Cup. The kickoff was late (18:00 local Miami time = 19:00 BRT / 00:00 CEST overnight into Thursday), the result is confirmed via FOX Sports' results page, but it's fresh at deadline — we couldn't confirm the goalscorer cleanly from any source, so we're deliberately not naming them.[1]

A stumble, not a fall: the defense wobbled for the first time in the tournament, but the knockout round is essentially booked.

Germany: Group victory in the bag

6 points, 9:2 goal difference — and the knockout stage is confirmed

The DFB side has controlled Group E with two wins: 7:1 against Curaçao (14.06., Houston), 2:1 against Ivory Coast (20.06., Toronto). Both are enough for 6 points from two matches and — with a 3-point lead and +7 goal difference — early group victory. The knockout stage is locked in, regardless of the final group match.

Denis Undav leads the team scorers with 3 goals, Kai Havertz has 2. In the tournament-wide Golden Boot race, though, others lead — Messi (5), Mbappé and Haaland (4 each) — and Undav ranks with 3 in the chasing pack.[2]

SC Freiburg: Squad overhaul in the open window

29 million euros in — the keeper plan is set

While the World Cup runs, Freiburg is rebuilding the squad for 2026/27. Three signings are locked in (fees per transfermarkt.de): Mio Backhaus (keeper, 22) from Werder Bremen for 12 million euros (Transfermarkt valuation; media report up to ~€15m, officially undisclosed), Keisuke Goto (centre-forward, 21) from RSC Anderlecht for 10 million euros, Rihito Yamamoto (defensive midfielder, 24) from Sint-Truiden for 7 million euros. That's 29 million euros in investment — for Freiburg's scale, an aggressive summer statement, especially the two Japanese signings.[3]

On the departure side, the big one is Merlin Röhl, who moves permanently to Everton for 25 million euros after his loan — by far the summer's biggest sale. Plus Robert Wagner (Dynamo Dresden, 850k euros), Junior Adamu (Schalke, 800k euros), Noah Weißhaupt (Darmstadt, 500k euros); veteran Nicolas Höfler (36) leaves on a free to the U23, Max Philipp and Daniel-Kofi Kyereh released without fee. All told, a transfer balance that's almost even despite the 29-million outlay (net around −1.85 million euros).[3]

The Atubolu drama: Backhaus isn't here by chance

The Backhaus transfer has a backstory: Freiburg's starting keeper Noah Atubolu is on the move. Per sport1.de (as of June), his advisors are pushing for a summer sale, Atubolu's contract runs through 2027 — sell now or risk a free-agent void next year. Suitors: Premier League (Brighton mentioned), plus AC Milan, Atlético Madrid and Real Sociedad (where ex-Freiburg coach Matarazzo works). As of 24.06. nothing is signed — but the fact that Freiburg has already brought in Backhaus as replacement speaks volumes.[4]
