SC Freiburg — rebuilding for 2026/27

Transfer window open until 31.08. — Status 25.06.2026, transfermarkt.de

Daniel's club is the real summer story, not the World Cup. Freiburg signs three players and lets its biggest talent leave for serious money. Mio Backhaus (22, goalkeeper) arrives for €12M from Werder Bremen — was their number one, Keisuke Goto (21, forward, Japan) for €10M from RSC Anderlecht — recently on loan at St. Truiden where he scored 10 in 28 matches. Plus Rihito Yamamoto (24) for €7M from Sint-Truiden.[1]

On the exit side: Merlin Röhl (23), recently on loan at Everton, now moves permanently for €25M to England.[1] Plus smaller sales — Robert Wagner to Dresden (€850k), Junior Adamu to Schalke (€800k), Noah Weißhaupt to Darmstadt (€500k) — and contract expirations for Höfler, Philipp and Kyereh.

Bottom line: Revenue €27.15M, spending €29.0M, net −€1.85M.[1] My take: smart. Freiburg sells Röhl at peak value and reinvests nearly all of it into two young, proven attacking options from Belgium — exactly the profile the club has thrived on for years. Backhaus targets a different gap: he comes as goalkeeper competition and depth for the number-one spot, not offense. The open question is replacing Röhl in central midfield — that's a separate puzzle for Freiburg. Late June also sees loans returning: Berkay Yılmaz (Nürnberg), Eren Dinkçi (Heidenheim) and Florent Muslija (Düsseldorf) come back — squad planning is still in motion.

World Cup tonight: Ecuador – Germany

Group E, Matchday 3 · Kickoff 22:00 CEST / 17:00 BRT · MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford · Status 25.06.2026, sportschau.de

Result pending Kickoff is after deadline (22:00 CEST / 17:00 BRT). This briefing has no final score — if you read a number, you read it elsewhere. Last verified: Germany 2 matches, 2 wins, 9:2 goals, 6 points.[2]

Germany is already confirmed as group winners after the 7:1 thrashing of Curaçao and the 2:1 win over Ivory Coast — tonight is purely about rhythm, load management and table aesthetics before the knockout round.[2] Head coach Julian Nagelsmann said he would "not experiment" and trust the proven lineup.[2]

Ecuador goes in under pressure: just 1 point from two matches (0:1 vs. Ivory Coast, goalless 0:0 vs. Curaçao). Don't discount them — the South Americans finished second in qualifying, ahead of Brazil and Uruguay.[2] The ref is Tori Penso.

More important than tonight's result is Monday's lens: As group winners, Germany meets one of the best third-place teams in the Round of 32 (Match 74) — on Mon 29.06., 22:30 CEST / 17:30 BRT at Gillette Stadium in Foxborough.[3]

Round of 32: Picture the tournament like a playoff bracket that runs one round longer this time. Because 48 teams compete instead of the usual 32, there's a bonus round before the traditional Round of 16 — the Round of 32, from 28.06. through 03.07., with 16 extra matches. Only after that comes the classic Round of 16.[3]

Brazil 3:0 Scotland — Group C dominated

Group C, Matchday 3 · played 25.06.2026, Miami · espn.com

Brazil won its World Cup group. In the 3:0 against Scotland, Vinícius Júnior bagged a brace: early 7th minute with a tap-in to an empty net (he rounded the keeper and finished), just before halftime in 45+3 with a header for 2:0.[5] Matheus Cunha made it 3:0 around the 60th minute.[5] Vini is now among the group-stage goal-scorers — following the 3:0 vs. Haiti (Matheus Cunha double) where he also scored, and the 1:1 opening draw vs. Morocco.[5][6]

Brazil and Morocco both ended Group C on 7 points; on better goal difference (+6 vs. +3), Brazil advances as group winners to the Round of 32 — on Mon 29.06., 19:00 CEST / 14:00 BRT at NRG Stadium in Houston, against the Group F runner-up.[3][6]

It is Thursday, the twenty-fifth of June, in the middle of the World Cup group stage. A double day: Germany plays its final group match tonight, and at the same time, SC Freiburg is actively restructuring its squad in the transfer window.

First, tonight's match. At ten p.m. our time, five p.m. in Rio de Janeiro, Germany faces Ecuador at MetLife Stadium. And here is the key point: at deadline, the match had not kicked off yet, so there is no result in this briefing. That is intentional. Germany has already qualified as group winners after the seven-to-one victory over Curaçao and the two-to-one win over Ivory Coast, nine goals in two matches. Coach Nagelsmann wants not to experiment, according to the sports news. What matters more is Monday.

Because this World Cup has forty-eight teams for the first time, and thus a completely new round: the Round of Thirty-Two. Germany plays there on Monday, the twenty-ninth of June, at ten-thirty p.m. our time, five-thirty p.m. in Brazil, at Gillette Stadium near Boston, against one of the best third-place finishers.

But for Daniel as a Freiburg fan, the transfer thread is the real talking point. The club signs three players: Mio Backhaus from Werder Bremen for twelve million, the Japanese striker Keisuke Goto from Anderlecht for ten million, and Rihito Yamamoto from Sint-Truiden for seven. In return, the biggest talent moves on: Merlin Röhl transfers permanently to Everton for twenty-five million euros. The bottom line is a loss of just under two million. My assessment: a smart deal. Freiburg sells at the peak and reinvests in young, proven attackers. Bitter for Daniel: despite a strong season, no Freiburg regular player made the World Cup squad — even captain Christian Günter was not called up; David Raum plays his position.

From Brazil there is already a result. The Seleção won its group, three to nothing against Scotland. Vinícius Júnior scored twice — early with a tap-in to an open goal and shortly before halftime with a header — Matheus Cunha scored the third. Brazil and Morocco both had seven points, but Brazil's better goal difference made them group winners. They also play on Monday in the Round of Thirty-Two, at seven p.m. our time, two p.m. in Brazil, in Houston.

The Bundesliga season is complete: Bayern champions with eighty-nine points, Freiburg a solid seventh with forty-seven, Heidenheim and St. Pauli are relegated. Brazilian league play is paused due to the World Cup since June eleventh; Palmeiras leads after eighteen rounds with forty-one points, resumption is around July twenty-second.
