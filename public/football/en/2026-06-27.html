SC Freiburg — Transfer Thread

Summer window open · Status 27.06.2026, source: transfermarkt.de + badische-zeitung.de

Röhl makes the leap — and funds the window

The biggest move is done: Merlin Röhl moves permanently to Everton, fee €25 million. The attacking midfielder was only on loan since September 2025 — the English side triggers the purchase option. For the 23-year-old it's the expected next step; for Freiburg it's the sale that opens the entire summer budget.

Sportingly the departure hurts — Röhl was a tireless box-to-box player, exactly the type Freiburg's intense play demands. But €25 million for a player you essentially picked from your own region in 2024 is excellent work by the sporting director. The question is: do you replace quality with quality, or with quantity?

transfermarkt.de

Three arrivals: striker, goalkeeper, midfielder

Freiburg responds broadly. Up front comes Keisuke Goto (21, Japan) from RSC Anderlecht for €10 million — a young striker, clearly a development and prospect transfer. In goal SC Freiburg signs Mio Backhaus (22) from Werder Bremen for €12 million, a keeper with Bundesliga experience and plenty of room to grow. And in midfield Rihito Yamamoto (24, defensive midfield) from Sint-Truiden for €7 million fills exactly the gap Röhl leaves behind.

Striking: two of the three newcomers are Japanese. Freiburg is clearly doubling down on the Japanese market — good value, high training discipline, and a scouting network that clearly has the region wired. Yamamoto is the most functional transfer: a specialist defensive midfielder as a direct answer to the Röhl departure.

badische-zeitung.de · sportschau.de · transfermarkt.de

Departures & Net balance: deliberately slight minus

Alongside Röhl, Freiburg clears house: Junior Adamu (25) permanently to Schalke 04 (€800k), Noah Weißhaupt (24) to Darmstadt 98 (€500k), Robert Wagner (22) to Dynamo Dresden (€850k), Yann Sturm (21) on loan to Eintracht Braunschweig, and veteran Nicolas Höfler (36) on free transfer to the reserve squad.

Balance Revenue approx. €29 million, expenses approx. €27.15 million — net roughly −€1.85 million. For Freiburg's standards that's active: you sell a top player dear and invest nearly all the money in younger talent rather than hoarding it. Solid summer work — the real test comes when Goto and Backhaus have to deliver.

World Cup 2026 — Brazil & Germany in the Round of 32

FIFA World Cup 2026 · Status 27.06.2026, source: cbssports.com + Wikipedia

Seleção through Group C unbeaten

Brazil completed group play without defeat and wins Group C with 7 points (2 wins, 1 draw, 7:1 goals). After the 1:1 opener vs. Morocco (13.06., MetLife Stadium), came a 3:0 over Haiti (19.06., Philadelphia) and a dominant 3:0 over Scotland (24.06., Miami). Just one goal conceded in three games — the defense holds, the attack was sluggish against deep blocks but efficient.

In the Round of 32 on Mon, 29.06., Japan awaits (Group 2nd in Group F, 5 points), kick-off 19:00 CEST / 14:00 BRT at NRG Stadium in Houston. For Daniel the perfect afternoon kickoff in Ribeirão Claro — and a match with trap-game potential: Japan is tactically disciplined and sharp on the counter.

Germany wins Group E — Paraguay drawn for Round of 32

Germany advances as Group E champion in the knockout round: 6 points from 2 wins and 1 loss, with 10:4 goals the best attack among group winners so far. One loss in the mix, but the goal difference speaks to an attack that packs a punch.

The opponent in the Round of 32 is Paraguay, on Mon, 29.06., 22:30 CEST / 17:30 BRT at Gillette Stadium in Foxborough. For Daniel the late afternoon/early evening slot in Brazil — very doable. On paper Germany is the favorite, but Paraguay is a classic knockout team: compact, physical, built on set pieces and counters. No walkover.
