SC Freiburg: a transfer playing this very afternoon

Summer window open · Status 29.06.2026, source Transfermarkt

Goto in the wrong jersey — and Freiburg is watching

Here's the loveliest connection of the day: Keisuke Goto, 21, Freiburg's new striker since June 11th (transfer fee reportedly between €10 and €13 million, from RSC Anderlecht), is in the Japanese World Cup squad — and Japan plays at 14:00 BRT today against Brazil. Daniel's new club gets to see his new signing live in the Round of 32, only still wearing the national team jersey instead of Freiburg's. Goto comes off a strong loan spell at Sint-Truiden: 10 goals and 5 assists in 28 matches of the Belgian Jupiler Pro League.[1]

bundesliga.com

The full window: three in, one out for 25 million

Freiburg made three paid acquisitions this summer: alongside Goto the goalkeeper Mio Backhaus (22, from Werder Bremen, fee undisclosed ~€12–15M) and defensive midfielder Rihito Yamamoto (24, from Sint-Truiden, €7M — not in the World Cup squad). The biggest departure is Merlin Röhl: his loan from Everton ends, the club now makes it permanent for €25M.[2]

Bottom line, the window is nearly balanced — around €29M spent, approximately €27.15M raised, net roughly −€1.85M. Plus minor departures: Robert Wagner (Dynamo Dresden, €850k), Junior Adamu (Schalke 04, €800k), Noah Weißhaupt (Darmstadt 98, €500k), Nicolas Höfler internally to the second team.[2] To put it plainly: this is solid Freiburg workmanship — realizing a €25M sale and funneling the money into three younger, development-ready players without straining the budget. Whether Goto and Yamamoto hit the ground running will depend on preseason; on paper, the squad hasn't gotten weaker.

Germany–Paraguay: two absences, one wall of defense

World Cup Round of 32 · Foxborough · Kickoff 22:30 MESZ / 17:30 BRT · Preview, not report

Germany must break through a wall

Germany won Group E with 6 points (tied with Ivory Coast). Today it faces Paraguay — and that's the trickiest opponent you could draw in a Round of 32: an opponent built on defense. Paraguay managed just 1.1 xG across three group games and kept two clean sheets.[5]

xG (Expected Goals): a probability calculation for shooting chances. A value of 1.1 across three entire matches means: Paraguay created so little that, on average, only about a single goal was "deserved." This is no attacking team — it's a fortress watching for counters and set pieces.

Personnel-wise, the DFB has problems: Nico Schlotterbeck out with an ankle injury, Nathaniel Brown with a muscle injury. The Moroccan Jalal Jayed will referee.[6]

espn.com

How Nagelsmann likely lines up

The expected starting XI in a 4–2–3–1 (per ESPN, subject to late changes): Neuer — Kimmich, Tah, Rüdiger, Raum — Nmecha, Pavlovic — Sané, Musiala, Wirtz — Havertz. Against a deep-sitting Paraguay, the question isn't whether Germany has the ball, but whether Sané/Musiala/Wirtz find that final gap. My hunch for the script: a grinding 1–0 or 2–0, in which a set piece or a Wirtz moment breaks the deadlock. But: kickoff is only this evening — this is preview, not report. How it went is in tomorrow's brief.[6]

Freiburg in the German squad: Atubolu yes, Ginter no

Two Freiburg notes at the edges of the World Cup squad: goalkeeper Noah Atubolu is in — as one of four keepers, backup behind Neuer, but included.
