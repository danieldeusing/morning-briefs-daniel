SC Freiburg — Transfer Summer

As of 2026-06-30 · Sources: scfreiburg.com, transfermarkt.de, ligainsider.de

Röhl record sale: €25 million to Everton

Merlin Röhl is gone for good. Premier League club FC Everton had secured a purchase obligation last winter for €25 million — and it kicked in the moment Everton preserved their top-flight status. The loan became a permanent transfer, and it's the most expensive sale in Freiburg's club history.[1]

For the sporting director it's a double-edged deal: Röhl, at 23 still young, came through the academy and had become a regular in midfield. But €25 million is a sum Freiburg cannot turn down — especially since the club had already planned for the gap before the deal was done.

ligainsider.de · scfreiburg.com

Goto arrives — fresh from the World Cup

The headline signing: Keisuke Goto, 21, Japanese striker. Freiburg are bringing him in for around €13 million from Belgian top-flight side Anderlecht; the move was confirmed on 2026-06-11.[2] Goto was in Japan's World Cup squad — yesterday's 1–2 loss to Brazil ended his tournament (the Japanese goal was scored by Kaishū Sano, not Goto), but Freiburg are getting a striker with World Cup experience and room to grow.

scfreiburg.com

Goalkeeper question: Backhaus in, Atubolu out?

The goalkeeping situation is getting crowded. Freiburg are signing Mio Backhaus (22) for around €12–15 million from Werder Bremen (Transfermarkt basis €12 million, Bild/Sky report €15 million + bonuses)[3] — and that deal puts Noah Atubolu (24) in a very uncomfortable position. Atubolu wants to leave, but according to bulinews there is no concrete offer for him yet. The risk: he spends all of 2026/27 on the bench until his contract runs out.[4]

Rumor check Earlier this year, figures of €20–30 million were floated for Atubolu — none of that has been confirmed, and no concrete new offer is on the table. Until a credible buyer emerges, it remains speculation.
transfermarkt.de · bulinews.com

The balance sheet: slightly in the red, but intentionally so

Add Rihito Yamamoto (24, defensive midfield) for €7 million from Sint-Truiden. In total, Freiburg spent around €29 million on incoming transfers (Goto €13 + Backhaus €12 + Yamamoto €7) and brought in €27.15 million from sales (dominated by Röhl). The net balance is roughly −€1.85 million — for a club that usually runs surpluses, a deliberate investment in squad quality.[3]

Alongside the usual end-of-season business: Robert Wagner (€850k to Dynamo Dresden), Nicolas Höfler down to the reserve side, Maximilian Philipp and Daniel-Kofi Kyereh out on free transfers.

World Cup 2026 — Germany out, Brazil through

Round of 32 · As of 2026-06-30 · Sources: cbssports.com, espn.com, sbs.com.au

1–1, then 3–4 — the first-ever World Cup penalty exit

Foxborough, last night (22:30 MESZ / 17:30 BRT). Paraguay took the lead through Julio Enciso in the 42nd minute, Kai Havertz equalized after the break with a header from a Wirtz cross (54th). It stayed 1–1 through extra time — and Germany lost the shootout 3–4.[6] It is the first penalty shootout defeat in the entire history of the DFB at a World Cup — a record that held for decades, broken at the worst possible moment.[7]

Paraguay's keeper Orlando Gill was the hero: he saved efforts from Havertz and Woltemade. The decisive miss came from Jonathan Tah, who sent his penalty over the bar; José Canale converted the winning kick for Paraguay.[6]

VAR controversy Germany thought they were through as early as the 105th minute: Tah headed in, but the goal was disallowed after VAR review — Waldemar Anton was adjudged to have obstructed keeper Gill. Instead of a winner in extra time, it went to penalties — where Tah then missed. It couldn't be more heartbreaking.[6]

It's Tuesday, June 30th, and for German fans it's a morning of reckoning. Germany are out of the World Cup — and for the first time in the entire history of the DFB, they have lost a penalty shootout at a World Cup.

Last night in Foxborough, at ten-thirty Central European Summer Time — half past five in the evening in Brazil — Paraguay struck first through Julio Enciso in the 42nd minute. Kai Havertz headed in an equalizer after the break, but no more goals came — not in regular time, not in extra time. It got even worse: in the 105th minute Jonathan Tah headed the ball in, but VAR ruled it out because Waldemar Anton was judged to have obstructed keeper Orlando Gill. So instead of a golden-goal winner, it went to penalties. Gill saved from both Havertz and Woltemade, and Tah — who had already seen his headed goal disallowed — blazed the decisive spot-kick over the bar. Germany lost three to four.

Head coach Nagelsmann was unsparing: this is now the third consecutive early exit, and Germany no longer belong among the top-class teams. The debate about the DFB's path has only just begun.

Things went better for Brazil, Daniel's adopted home. The Seleção edged Japan two to one in Houston. Kaishū Sano put Japan in front in the 29th minute, Casemiro leveled in the 56th, and Gabriel Martinelli sealed it in the sixth minute of stoppage time. Close, but Brazil are through to the quarter-finals. A footnote worth noting: Japan's squad included Keisuke Goto — Freiburg's new striker.

Tonight brings three more Round of 32 matches. Ivory Coast face Norway at 19:00 MESZ, two in the afternoon in Brazil. France take on Sweden at 23:00 MESZ, six in the evening Brazilian time. And Mexico versus Ecuador kicks off at three in the morning European time. The winner of France versus Sweden will meet Paraguay in the quarter-finals.

At Freiburg, the transfer window is wide open. Merlin Röhl has permanently joined Everton for twenty-five million euros — the most expensive sale in the club's history. In comes 21-year-old Japanese striker Keisuke Goto for around thirteen million from Anderlecht, plus goalkeeper Mio Backhaus for somewhere between twelve and fifteen million euros from Bremen. That Backhaus signing has left Noah Atubolu in a bind: he wants to leave, but no club has come calling yet.

The Bundesliga is done and dusted. Bayern won the title with eighty-nine points and set a record of one hundred and twenty-two goals. Freiburg finished seventh and are in the Conference League next season. In Brazil, the league is on pause for the World Cup until July 23rd; Palmeiras lead with forty-one points.
