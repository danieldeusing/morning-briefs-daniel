SC Freiburg — the window is open

For Daniel's sports club, this marks the truly exciting phase of summer: Starting July 1, Bundesliga clubs can officially buy and sell players, with the window remaining open through August 31.[1] Freiburg wrapped up the 2025/26 season in 7th place with 47 points — solid, but tinged with disappointment after losing the Europa League final to Aston Villa (0–3 in Istanbul). The seventh-place finish does secure Conference League qualification for 2026/27.[2][3]

Arrival: Keisuke Goto

The biggest confirmed signing is already locked in: Freiburg acquired Japanese striker Keisuke Goto (21) from RSC Anderlecht in mid-June. The transfer fee stands at roughly €10 million per media reports — the club itself has made no official announcement of a figure.[4] Goto was part of Japan's 2026 World Cup squad, a young center forward with pace and an eye for goal. For a club that has often lacked cutting edge up front (only 51 league goals), he's exactly the type needed.

Rumor: Atubolu reportedly wants out

Rumor No confirmed deal yet. What follows is transfer speculation, not fact.

Goalkeeper Noah Atubolu (24) reportedly does not want to extend his contract (which runs through June 2027) and is seeking a move this summer — several sources have reported this since spring. Interested clubs mentioned include Tottenham, Real Sociedad, AC Milan, and Atlético Madrid; his market value is around €25 million. Nothing is confirmed: as of July 1, there is no completed transfer.[5] A sale would be painful for Freiburg from a sporting standpoint — Atubolu is their clear number one — but financially a window they can scarcely afford to pass up.

Departures and other moves

On the outbound side, there has been activity: striker Junior Adamu (25, Austria) switched to Bundesliga newcomers Schalke 04 (base fee €800k, contract through 2029), and Noah Weißhaupt joined Darmstadt 98.[6][7] Media have also reported Robert Wagner (to Dynamo Dresden), Yann Sturm (to Eintracht Braunschweig), and the arrival of goalkeeper Mio Backhaus from Bremen — these three have yet to be fully confirmed through official channels but are considered to be far advanced.[8] Still in play: interest from several clubs in prospect Dzenan Pejcinovic (21), with Benfica said to have made an offer.

Summer schedule

Training camp kicks off on July 12; the pre-season camp runs July 22–31 in Schruns, Austria, with friendlies against Derby County (July 24 in Imst) and Greuther Fürth (July 31) on the agenda.[9] The season opener at Europa-Park Stadium is August 15; the Conference League playoff draw takes place August 3.

Germany out — the first World Cup penalty exit

It is the tournament's defining disappointment from a German perspective: On June 29, the DFB team exited in the Round of 32 against Paraguay. 1–1 after extra time, then 3–4 on penalties.[12] Julio Enciso headed Paraguay into the lead in the 42nd minute; Kai Havertz equalized with a header in the 54th. Extra time produced no breakthrough — and penalties turned into a nightmare: Goalkeeper Gill saved from Havertz and Woltemade, Tah's attempt sailed over the bar, while Canale converted the deciding penalty.[13]

Notably: Germany dominated the match, holding roughly 75 percent possession and firing 21 shots (7 on target vs. Paraguay's 4), but made little of their dominance.[14] Historically, the defeat is singular — it is the first time ever that Germany has lost a penalty shootout at a World Cup.[15] The DFB has announced it will conduct a full review; Paraguay faces France in the Round of 16 next, having beaten Sweden 3–0.

World Cup 2026 — today and Brazil's Round of 16

Round of 32 / Round of 16: Because this World Cup is being played with 48 teams for the first time, there's an extra round compared to past tournaments — after the group stage comes 32 teams (Round of 32), then 16, then eight. Germany is out in the first knockout round; Brazil advances to the next.

Brazil defeated Japan 2–1 on June 29 and advanced to the Round of 16 — facing Norway on July 5.[16] A downside for the Seleção: Lucas Paquetá picked up a thigh injury against Japan and is doubtful for the Round of 16.[17]

Wednesday, July first, brings two very different stories for German football: the heartbreak of an early World Cup exit, and the excitement of the summer transfer window opening today.

First, the pain. Germany is out of this World Cup after losing to Paraguay on penalties. The match ended one to one after extra time, then Paraguay won the shootout four to three. Julio Enciso headed Paraguay ahead, Kai Havertz equalized, but when it went to penalties, both Havertz and Woltemade were stopped by the goalkeeper, and Tah shot over the bar. This is historic: it's the first time ever Germany has lost a penalty shootout at a World Cup. The DFB says they'll conduct a review.

But today also marks a fresh start for Freiburg and the Bundesliga. The transfer window opens July first and runs through August thirty-first. Freiburg finished seventh with forty-seven points, which qualifies them for the Conference League. Their big signing is Japanese striker Keisuke Goto, twenty-one years old, who arrived from Anderlecht for roughly ten million euros.

There's also a rumor we have to flag: goalkeeper Noah Atubolu wants to leave, and clubs like Tottenham and AC Milan have been mentioned. But there's no confirmed deal yet — this is speculation, not done.

Tomorrow the Deutsche Fußball Liga announces the full fixture schedule for the new season. Freiburg will play their opening match on a Sunday; the opponent will be confirmed with the schedule release. The season starts August twenty-eighth.

In Brazil, the Seleção advanced to the Round of sixteen after beating Japan two to one. They face Norway on July fifth, though Lucas Paquetá is questionable after a thigh injury. The Brazilian league is on pause, with Palmeiras leading on forty-one points.

So tomorrow becomes crucial: the fixture schedule. And we'll be watching to see if the Atubolu rumor turns into something real.
