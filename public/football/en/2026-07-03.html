SC Freiburg — Preparation underway, Goto as record signing

Off-season · Per kicker.de as of 06/26 / scfreiburg.com-adjacent reports, 07/03/2026

Where Freiburg stands now

No competitive matches, but preparation for 2026/27 under Julian Schuster is in full swing. SC has already played friendlies vs. Racing Strasbourg in Colmar (the exact result is inconsistent across sources, so we're omitting the scoreline). The first team training session of the new season is set for Sunday, July 12, 11:00 AM CEST / 6:00 AM BRT at the Europa-Park Stadion; the summer training camp runs July 22-31 in Schruns, Austria (Montafon).[1]

Squad activity in the summer window

Arrivals: The standout move is Keisuke Goto (21), Japanese international, for around €10M from RSC Anderlecht — 10 goals and 5 assists in 28 matches last season. Also arriving is goalkeeper Mio Backhaus from Werder Bremen. Departures: Junior Adamu joins Schalke 04, Yann Sturm to second-division Eintracht Braunschweig. Backup goalkeeper Jannik Huth (32) and Anthony Jung have signed extensions.[2]

Rumor, as of 07/03 Reports suggest starting keeper Noah Atubolu wants to leave — no concrete offers yet. Philipp Lienhart (29) is being linked with Lazio. Both are unconfirmed rumors.
kicker.de · scfreiburg.com

Recap: the strongest season in club history

Sportingly, 2025/26 was a breakout year: 7th place and the club's first European final ever — the Europa League, lost 0-3 to Aston Villa at Beşiktaş Park in Istanbul. Because the final was lost, there was no bonus qualification; as 7th-place finishers, Freiburg enters the Conference League qualification rounds (not the league phase directly) in 2026/27.[3]

World Cup 2026 — Germany out, knockout heats up

Round of 32 (06/28–07/03) · Round of 16 begins 07/04 · Source: Sky Sports / ESPN / goal.com, 07/03/2026

The shock: Germany falls to Paraguay

On June 29 in Foxborough, Massachusetts, it's over for Germany early: 1-1 after extra time, 3-4 on penalties vs. Paraguay — the biggest upset of the tournament so far. Julio Enciso put Paraguay ahead, Kai Havertz equalized in the 52nd minute. In extra time, VAR overturned Jonathan Tah's header for a push (102'). Then Tah himself skied the decisive penalty.[6]

Nagelsmann "I'm not someone who walks away. It's not the first time this happens, and there are some things that need to change today." A coaching discussion is now open, but no decision has been made.

Round of 32: The 2026 World Cup has 48 teams instead of 32 for the first time. So before the traditional round of 16, there's an extra knockout round with 32 teams — hence "round of 32." Lose here and you're out. For Germany, this was the end of the line.

It's Friday, July third. No Bundesliga match, no Freiburg kickoff — yet the loudest football day of the summer, because the World Cup in the United States, Canada, and Mexico is in knockout mode. Today the first knockout round with 32 teams ends, tomorrow the round of 16 begins.

The bad news first: Germany is out. On June twenty-ninth, the German national team lost to Paraguay one-one after extra time and then three-four on penalties. Kai Havertz equalized in the second half, but in extra time the video referee overturned Jonathan Tah's header. And it was Tah who then hit the decisive penalty over the bar. It's the biggest shock of the tournament so far. Coach Julian Nagelsmann said afterwards he's not someone who walks away — the coaching debate is open, but nothing is decided.

For Daniel in Brazil, the tournament continues. Carlo Ancelotti's Seleção are in the round of 16 — after a two-one comeback against Japan. On Sunday, July fifth, Brazil plays Norway, at ten p.m. Central European Summer Time, that's five p.m. in Brazil, at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey.

The most compelling round of 16 match for us comes Saturday: Paraguay versus France, at eleven p.m. our time, six p.m. in Brazil. Germany's conqueror against one of the title favorites.

At Freiburg, preparation for the new season is underway. The club has played friendlies against Racing Strasbourg, the first team training session is scheduled for July twelfth, the training camp in Austrian Schruns runs from the twenty-second to the thirty-first of July. The costliest new signing is Japanese international Keisuke Goto for about ten million euros from Anderlecht.

Looking back on the completed Bundesliga season: Bayern won their thirty-fifth championship and the cup — a double, in the final three-nil over Stuttgart with a hat trick from Harry Kane. Freiburg finished strong in seventh and even played in a European final for the first time, but lost nil-three to Aston Villa. At the bottom, Wolfsburg dropped down after twenty-nine years in the league via the relegation playoff.

Brazilian league play is resting for the World Cup. Palmeiras lead with forty-one points, matchday nineteen kicks off again on July twenty-second.

Tomorrow and the day after it's all about the World Cup round of 16 — and on Sunday we're rooting for Brazil against Norway. See you then.
