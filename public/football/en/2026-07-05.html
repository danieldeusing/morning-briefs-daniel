Brazil in the tournament · Tonight against Norway

Here on the ground, this is the match of the weekend: Brazil faces Norway today at 22:00 CEST / 17:00 BRT at MetLife Stadium near New York.[1] The 5pm kickoff falls right in the middle of Brazilian Sunday evening — half the country will be in front of the TV. Norway, with Erling Haaland as its headline threat, is no pushover, but the Seleção enters the match as favorites.

What's on the line: the winner advances to the quarterfinals (July 9–11). For Brazil, that would mean a match in one of the US stadiums in Boston, Los Angeles, Miami, or Kansas City — the exact opponent depends on how the rest of the round of 16 plays out. Losing isn't an option; from this round on, whoever loses is out.

Context Norway is a wild card at this World Cup: individually world-class thanks to Haaland, but inexperienced as a team in knockout football at a major tournament. Brazil's main job is controlling Haaland's runs in behind — if the Seleção leaves too much space at the back, a single long ball can flip the match. Brazil arrives as favorites, not as a sure thing.

World Cup 2026 · The round of 16 — without us

Yesterday the knockout stage continued, and two winners are confirmed: Morocco beat Canada 3-0 — Amine Ounahi scored twice (50th and 82nd minute), the third goal came in stoppage time.[2] And France won 1-0 against Paraguay: Kylian Mbappé converted a penalty in the 70th minute — his 19th World Cup career goal.[3] Both are through to the quarterfinals, where they'll face each other directly (more on that below).

For the German fan, the Paraguay result leaves a bitter aftertaste right now: Paraguay is the exact team that eliminated Germany — and they came close to another upset against France. A painful reminder: Germany lost the round of 32 on June 29 against Paraguay 1-1, 3-4 on penalties. Havertz equalized late, a Tah goal was disallowed by VAR, and in the shootout Havertz, Woltemade, and Tah all missed; José Canale buried the decisive penalty for Paraguay.[4]

Round of 32: Because 2026 is the first World Cup with 48 teams instead of 32, there's one extra knockout round compared to before. 32 teams start in the first elimination round, and the 16 winners move on to the round of 16. Think of it as an extra opening cup round: Germany didn't get past this new first hurdle — meaning they went out earlier than would even have been possible in the classic 32-team World Cup format.

What that means for this brief: there's no German team left to follow. The focus here on the ground is on Brazil — and on the question of how far Paraguay, the team that beat us, can go in the upper half of the bracket.

The bracket · Where things go from the quarterfinals

The quarterfinals run from Thursday (July 9) to Saturday (July 11), entirely in the US: Boston opens on Thursday, Los Angeles follows on Friday, Miami and Kansas City close it out with a Saturday double-header.[7] One matchup is already set: QF1 = France vs. Morocco in Foxborough near Boston — yesterday's two winners face each other immediately.[8]

After that, things move fast: the semifinals are on July 14 in Dallas and July 15 in Atlanta, the final on July 19 at MetLife Stadium near New York — the very same stadium where Brazil plays tonight.[7]

Upper / lower bracket half: A knockout bracket is split into two halves. Teams in the same half can only meet each other in the semifinal; the two halves only collide in the final. Brazil and Paraguay are in the same half — so the question "how far can the team that eliminated us go?" is, here on the ground, the same question as who Brazil might still meet on the way to the final.

SC Freiburg & Bundesliga · Summer break, transfers only

In Germany, no ball is rolling — it's deep summer break, and the 2026/27 season doesn't start until late August. So there's no Bundesliga table to print: no match has been played yet, any table would be an empty shell. Freiburg news is therefore running entirely through the transfer window.

On the departure side, several players are leaving SC. Confirmed is the move of striker Junior Adamu — after his loan spell at Celtic, he's heading to FC Schalke 04 (reported as of June 11).[9] On top of that, according to consistent reports, two more departures: Noah Weißhaupt is moving to SV Darmstadt 98, midfielder Robert Wagner to second-division Dynamo Dresden.[9] These latter two reports come from aggregator and search results (transfermarkt / besoccer), not from a cleanly re-pulled primary source — so read with caution until the club confirms officially, as of ~June 23.

Context As long as the World Cup is running, the transfer window is the only Freiburg storyline. The pattern fits the club: targeted squad moves rather than big names, plus rotation between the first and second divisions (Wagner to Dresden). The overhaul can only really be assessed once the club officially confirms the departures and arrivals, and preseason shows who fills the vacated spots. Until then: reported, not set in stone.

The German transfer window is open until August 31 — the deadline by which Freiburg and everyone else must finalize their squads. League storylines will gradually return with preseason; until then, the tournament in the US sets the pace.
