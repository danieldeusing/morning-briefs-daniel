Germany: out in the round of 32 — the early knockout

Let's start with the pain that hits closer to home. The four-time world champion was eliminated a full round before the actual "big" round of 16: in the round of 32 on Monday, 06/29, against Paraguay. 1-1 after 90 minutes — Julio Enciso put Paraguay ahead in the 42nd minute, Kai Havertz equalized in the 54th — then the penalty shootout. And there, everything that could go wrong for the DFB team did: Havertz, Nick Woltemade, and Jonathan Tah all missed, and José Canale buried the winner in sudden death to make it 4-3. Germany out, a historically early exit.[1][2]

Round of 32 vs. round of 16: The 2026 World Cup has 48 teams, so there's one more knockout round than before. First 32 teams (round of 32), then 16 (round of 16), and only then the 8 quarterfinalists. Germany didn't even make it into the last 16 — an early exit for a four-time world champion.

Penalty shootouts are a coin toss with nerves — but three misses from three regular starters say more than bad luck. Havertz and Tah bear responsibility, and the fact that it was the most experienced takers who faltered will shape the DFB debate all summer. For Daniel as a fan, the sober takeaway is: no German quarterfinal to root for, and the next real national-team fixture isn't until after the summer break.

Brazil: Haaland kills the Seleção's party

And then, on Sunday, the second blow — this one right on the host country's doorstep. Brazil lost its round of 16 match against Norway 1-2, at New York New Jersey Stadium in East Rutherford. The man of the match: Erling Haaland, who scored both Norwegian goals — in the 79th and 90th minute. Brazil's consolation came from Neymar via a stoppage-time penalty. Ancelotti's remodeled Seleção, considered one of the title favorites, was stopped in the last 16.[3][4]

This is the kind of exit that stings: Brazil had possession and territorial dominance, but Norway only needed two moments — and Haaland was perfectly placed for both. A lesson in efficiency over possession. For the mood here in the country, the World Cup feels essentially over; the kiosks and bars in Ribeirão Claro will be noticeably quieter starting today.

Efficiency vs. possession: Possession measures who has the ball; efficiency measures who scores from few chances. Norway let Brazil have the ball and waited for two opportunities — Haaland converted both. Like a boxer who blocks for 30 rounds and then lands two punches. More possession is no advantage if you don't convert it into goals.

Who else went out — and who's still in

Germany and Brazil aren't alone. The round of 16 also claimed the USA (1-4 against Belgium), Portugal (0-1 against Spain, goal in the 90+1'), and Mexico. England won a thriller 3-2 against Mexico — a Bellingham brace plus a Kane penalty, and about 40 minutes of that with a man down after Quansah's early red card. France beat Paraguay 1-0 via an Mbappé penalty.[5][6][7]

What's left of the tournament for us?

Without Germany and Brazil, the World Cup becomes a neutral pleasure — and there's plenty to enjoy. Spain looks the most complete (a stoppage-time 0-1 win over Portugal, now facing Belgium in the quarterfinals). France brings individual class with Mbappé. And Norway is the story of the tournament: Haaland is carrying a team that struggles defensively but stays ice-cold up front. My pick for what to keep watching: Norway–England on Saturday — the emotional encore for anyone whose heart is still with Brazil.
