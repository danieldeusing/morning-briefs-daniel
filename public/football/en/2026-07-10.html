SC Freiburg — the record-breaking summer

The transfer window (open until 08/31) is turning Freiburg into a net seller on a record scale right now. According to Sky Sport and kicker (as of 07/08–09), Newcastle United and Freiburg have agreed on an overall package worth around €60m for Johan Manzambi — plus bonuses on top, according to Fabrizio Romano. That would be not only the most expensive sale in the club's history, but also the most expensive transfer of a Swiss player ever.

Not yet official The deal is fully negotiated but not yet completed — Manzambi is right in the middle of the World Cup with Switzerland (quarterfinal against Argentina on Sat. 07/11). Until the medical and the official announcement, it remains "reportedly," not fact.

On the other side of the ledger, Freiburg bought early: goalkeeper Mio Backhaus (22) arrives for a base fee of ~€15m (plus bonuses up to €3m) from Werder Bremen (deichstube.de) — a club-internal record in its own right — plus striker Keisuke Goto (21) for around €10m from RSC Anderlecht. Both are done deals. The future of goalkeeper Noah Atubolu remains open: according to ligainsider, despite interest (including Real Sociedad, Atlético, Milan) no concrete offer is on the table yet.

Purchase option: When a player is loaned out, the contract can include a "purchase option" — the borrowing club may sign him permanently afterward for a set fee, but isn't obligated to. An "obligation to buy" would make it mandatory. For former SC loanee Merlin Röhl, exactly that happened: Everton triggered the purchase option — the move has been official and permanent since 07/06/2026, for a fee of around €25m (per Get German Football News). For Freiburg, it's one of the most expensive sales in club history.

Bundesliga — 25/26 is in the books

The season wrapped up after Matchday 34 (05/16) and relegation playoffs (05/21 & 05/25). Bayern defended the title comfortably with 89 points and a goal difference of 122:36. Freiburg finished a solid 7th (47 pts) — just missing out on Europe. At the bottom, Wolfsburg went down for the first time in 29 years (lost the relegation playoff to Paderborn), while Heidenheim and St. Pauli went down directly. Schalke, Elversberg, and Paderborn come up for 26/27. Play resumes in mid-August.

2026 World Cup — finals week

The quarterfinals are underway. France opened proceedings and beat Morocco on Thu. (07/09) in Foxborough 2-0: Kylian Mbappé scored the 1-0 (his 20th World Cup career goal, having missed a penalty earlier in the match), Ousmane Dembélé added the 2-0 — his fifth goal of the tournament (cnn.com, espn.com). Les Bleus are the first team through to the semifinal.

For Daniel's two nations, the tournament is over. Germany was eliminated as early as the round of 32, against Paraguay: 1-1 after extra time (Havertz 54', Enciso 42'), then 3-4 on penalties on Mon. (06/29, Foxborough) — the DFB side's first-ever World Cup loss on penalties (wikipedia). Brazil went out a round later: 1-2 against Norway on Sun. (07/05) in East Rutherford, Erling Haaland with a late brace (79', 90'), Neymar's penalty (90+10') came too late (espn.com). It's Brazil's weakest World Cup showing since the round-of-16 exit in 1990.

The DFB side — and now Nagelsmann?

After the historic penalty-shootout exit, the coaching question is open. Julian Nagelsmann himself rules out resigning: "I'm not someone who runs away," he told Sportschau. The DFB is staying tight-lipped: president Neuendorf says he doesn't want to "move straight on to business as usual after a setback like this," while Rudi Völler calls Nagelsmann "probably the right man" but qualifies that he's "not the DFB alone" (eurosport.de). According to a Sky report, Jürgen Klopp is circulating as a possible successor — explicitly speculation, nothing confirmed.

Brazil — Ancelotti stays, league heads for restart

Despite the World Cup debacle, Carlo Ancelotti carries on: his contract with the CBF runs through 2030, and the players backed him in a letter (cnnbrasil). "Today the pain is great. But the confidence in what we're building doesn't change," said the Italian. Série A has been on pause since Round 18 (05/30–31) for the World Cup and returns with Round 19 in the week of 07/22 — more than 50 days of standstill. Palmeiras leads (41 pts).

It's Friday, July tenth. The Bundesliga is on its summer break, but the World Cup in North America is heading into finals week — and for us, it's already over: both Germany and Brazil are out.

France got things started yesterday in Foxborough, beating Morocco two-nil. Kylian Mbappé scored the opener — his twentieth World Cup goal, even though he'd missed a penalty earlier in the game. Ousmane Dembélé made it two-nil. That puts France through as the first semifinalist.

Germany went out a round earlier, in the round of thirty-two against Paraguay. After a one-one draw in extra time, the team lost three-four on penalties — the very first World Cup shootout loss in German history. Head coach Nagelsmann isn't planning to step down; he says he's not someone who runs away. The German federation is staying quiet, and according to Sky, Jürgen Klopp is being floated as a possible successor — but that's pure speculation.

Brazil was eliminated by Norway, one-two. Erling Haaland scored twice late, and Neymar's penalty in stoppage time came too late. It's Brazil's weakest World Cup since nineteen ninety. Coach Carlo Ancelotti is staying on regardless — his contract runs through twenty-thirty, and the players publicly backed him.

Tonight the action continues: Spain against Belgium, at nine p.m. our Central European time, four p.m. here in Brazil. Tomorrow brings Norway against England and Argentina against Switzerland. The semifinals are Tuesday and Wednesday, and the final is Sunday, July nineteenth, at MetLife Stadium.

Over at Freiburg, the most expensive sale in club history is taking shape. Johan Manzambi is set to move to Newcastle United in a package worth around sixty million euros — that would be not just a club record, but the most expensive transfer of a Swiss player ever. It's not official yet, though, since Manzambi is right now playing the quarterfinal with Switzerland. On the buying side, Freiburg moved early: goalkeeper Mio Backhaus for around fifteen million from Werder and striker Keisuke Goto for ten million from Anderlecht are both done deals.

The Bundesliga season is wrapped up. Bayern are champions with eighty-nine points, and Freiburg finished a solid seventh with forty-seven points. At the bottom, Wolfsburg was relegated for the first time in twenty-nine years. In Brazil, Série A stays paused until the week of the twenty-second of July, when round nineteen picks up. Palmeiras leads the table.
