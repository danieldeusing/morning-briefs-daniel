SC Freiburg — the record-breaking summer

The transfer window (open until 08/31) is turning Freiburg into a net seller on a record scale right now. According to media reports (including sportschau.de/SWR and the kicker transfer ticker, as of 07/10), Newcastle United and Freiburg have agreed on an overall package worth around €60m for Johan Manzambi (20). That would be not just the most expensive sale in club history, but also the most expensive transfer of a Swiss player ever.

Not official yet The deal has been negotiated through, but not yet completed — personal terms and the medical are still pending, and Manzambi is in the middle of the tournament with Switzerland (quarterfinal against Argentina tonight). Until the official announcement, it stays "per reports," not fact.

On the other side of the ledger, Freiburg shopped early: goalkeeper Mio Backhaus (ex Werder Bremen) arrives for a base fee of around €12–15m (depending on the source), alongside the presentation of new signings Rihito Yamamoto and Keisuke Goto. Going the other way: striker Junior Adamu moves for just under €1m (plus bonuses up to €1.5m) to Schalke 04, Yann Sturm to Eintracht Braunschweig (contract through 2029), and Robert Wagner's loan to Dynamo Dresden has been made permanent. Still open: keeper Noah Atubolu (24), who wants to move but is starting preseason regardless.

And that preseason starts tomorrow: kickoff on Sun. 07/12, with a training camp in Schruns, Austria from 07/22 to 07/31 (including a friendly against Derby County in Imst). The competitive-fixture start comes via the DFB-Pokal — the 1st round sends Freiburg away to third-division side Fortuna Düsseldorf (drawn 06/06, played 08/21-24). As last season's seventh-place finisher, Freiburg is also in Conference League qualifying.

Conference League qualifying: Think of it as the waiting room before European competition — as seventh-place finishers, Freiburg isn't seeded straight into the group/league phase but has to get through a qualifying playoff round first. Win it, and the club is in the competition; lose it, and the European season is over before it really starts.

Bundesliga — 25/26 is in the books

The season has been over since matchday 34 (05/16). Bayern defended the title comfortably with 89 points and a goal difference of 122:36. Freiburg landed a solid 7th place (47 pts), narrowly securing Conference League qualifying. At the bottom, Heidenheim and St. Pauli went straight down, and Wolfsburg (16th) also lost the relegation playoff. The ball won't roll again until 08/28 — kickoff is Bayern vs. Stuttgart, 20:30 MESZ / 15:30 BRT; the transfer window is open until 08/31.

2026 World Cup — finals week

The quarterfinal round is nearly done, and the first semifinal pairing is set: France–Spain on Tue. 07/14 (21:00 MESZ / 16:00 BRT, Arlington). France beat Morocco 2-0 (Thu. 07/09) to become the first semifinalist; Spain followed with a 2-1 win over Belgium, with Mikel Merino scoring late (Fri. 07/10). Tonight decides the final two tickets: Norway–England (23:00 MESZ / 18:00 BRT, Miami Gardens) and Argentina–Switzerland (03:00 MESZ Sunday night / 22:00 BRT, Kansas City) — the latter featuring Freiburg's Manzambi.

For both of Daniel's nations, the tournament is already over. Germany fell in the round of 32 to Paraguay: 1-1 after 120 minutes (Enciso 42', Havertz 54'; a goal by Tah in extra time was ruled out after VAR), then lost 3-4 on penalties on Mon. 06/29 in Foxborough (sportschau.de, fussballdaten.de) — the first shootout Germany has ever lost at a World Cup. Brazil went out a round later: 1-2 vs. Norway in the round of 16 (Sun. 07/05, East Rutherford), Erling Haaland with a brace, a Neymar penalty coming too late (zdfheute.de). It's Brazil's weakest World Cup since 1990.

Round of 32 vs. round of 16: Because of the 48 teams, the 2026 World Cup has one more knockout round than before. After the group stage, 32 teams advance first — they play the round of 32 (where Germany went out). Only after that comes the round of 16 with 16 teams (where Brazil went out). In short: Brazil made it one round further than Germany.

DFB — Nagelsmann out, Klopp taking over

The coaching question left open after the penalty-shootout exit is now settled — and differently from how it looked right after the game. Julian Nagelsmann (38) has left the DFB: the board and supervisory board unanimously dissolved his contract, which had actually run through 2028, on 07/03, after Nagelsmann himself had asked for it the evening before (dfb.de, sportschau.de). As his successor, the federation now wants Jürgen Klopp — he's the top choice and, according to consistent reports, has signaled basic willingness, though a contract has not yet been signed (zdfheute.de).

Context: Klopp brings a level of star power and standing that almost no other available coach can match — but he hasn't been on a touchline since leaving Liverpool, spending his time instead in a corporate role at Red Bull. Until the federation and Klopp reach an agreement, this is a wish, not an appointment. Manage expectations accordingly.

It's Saturday, July eleventh. The Bundesliga is on its summer break, and for us the World Cup in North America is long over — Germany and Brazil are both out. Still, today has two hot topics: a record transfer in Freiburg and a settled coaching question at the German federation.

In Freiburg, the most expensive sale in club history is taking shape. Johan Manzambi is set to move to Newcastle United for a package worth around sixty million euros — that would be not just a club record, but the most expensive transfer of a Swiss player ever. Officially, though, the deal isn't done yet: the medical is still pending, and Manzambi is still playing tonight with Switzerland in the quarterfinal against Argentina.

On the other side, Freiburg shopped early. Goalkeeper Mio Backhaus arrives for around twelve to fifteen million from Werder Bremen, along with new signings Yamamoto and Goto. Junior Adamu, meanwhile, is heading to Schalke. On Sunday the team already kicks off preseason, with a training camp coming up in late July in Schruns, Austria.

At the German federation, the big personnel question is settled. Julian Nagelsmann has left the job — the board and supervisory board dissolved his contract, which had actually run through twenty twenty-eight, on the third of July, at his own request. As his successor, the federation now wants Jürgen Klopp, who has signaled basic willingness. Nothing is signed yet, though — until then, it's a wish, not an appointment.

At the World Cup, the first semifinal is set: France against Spain on Tuesday, at nine p.m. Central European time, four p.m. here in Brazil. France beat Morocco two to nothing, and Spain beat Belgium two to one on a late goal from Mikel Merino. Tonight decides the final two tickets: Norway against England, and Argentina against Switzerland.

A reminder of how our two teams went out. Germany lost already in the round of thirty-two against Paraguay, after a one-to-one draw, three to four on penalties — the first shootout a German team has ever lost at a World Cup. Brazil went out a round later against Norway, one to two, with Erling Haaland scoring twice. Because of the larger field this time, there's one extra knockout round, which is why the first round is now called the round of thirty-two.

The Bundesliga season is in the books. Bayern are champions with eighty-nine points, Freiburg finished a solid seventh with forty-seven points, securing Conference League qualification. Play doesn't resume until the twenty-eighth of August, Bayern against Stuttgart. In Brazil, Série A is still on pause until the week of the sixteenth to the twenty-second of July, when Round nineteen picks up. Palmeiras leads.
