SC Freiburg — season kickoff and record-breaking summer

For the Sport-Club, the new season starts today: preseason kickoff on Sun. 07/12, followed by a training camp from 07/22 to 07/31 in Schruns, Austria (sportschau.de). At the same time, the transfer window (open until 08/31) is in full swing — and Freiburg is turning into a net seller on a record scale.

The big name is Johan Manzambi (20). According to consistent reports, the club and Newcastle United have reached a "full agreement" on a transfer; the "Magpies" are said to be paying around €60m (news.de). That would be the most expensive sale in club history.

Not official yet A "full agreement" means the deal is negotiated through but not completed — until the official announcement (medical, signature), it stays "per reports," not fact. At least Manzambi's tournament is now over after Switzerland's quarterfinal exit to Argentina, so yesterday's scheduling conflict is moot.

There's more movement besides: new signings are midfielders Rihito Yamamoto (VV St. Truiden) and Keisuke Goto (RSC Anderlecht, 21). On the way out, striker Junior Adamu (most recently on loan at Celtic) moves to Schalke 04, Yann Sturm to Eintracht Braunschweig (baden24.de). Back from loan spells in Breisgau: Eren Dinkci, Berkay Yilmaz, Noah Weißhaupt, Robert Wagner and Florent Muslija.

The competitive-fixture start comes via the DFB-Pokal: the 1st round sends the team away to third-division side Fortuna Düsseldorf, played 08/21 to 08/24 (scfreiburg.com). As last season's seventh-place finisher, Freiburg is also in Conference League qualifying. Before that, the club had already made history: the first European final in club history, the 2025/26 Europa League final, though Freiburg lost it 0-3 to Aston Villa in Istanbul on 05/20/2026 (uefa.com).

Conference League qualifying: Think of it as the waiting room before European competition — as seventh-place finishers, Freiburg isn't seeded straight into the group/league phase but has to get through a qualifying playoff round first. Win it, and the club is in the competition; lose it, and the European season is over before it really starts.

Bundesliga — 25/26 in the books, restart on 08/28

The season has been over since matchday 34 (05/16). Bayern defended the title comfortably with 89 points and a goal difference of 122:36 — the 35th German championship. Freiburg landed a solid 7th place (47 pts), narrowly securing Conference League qualifying. At the bottom, Heidenheim and St. Pauli went straight down, and Wolfsburg (16th) also lost the relegation playoff; Schalke 04, SV Elversberg, and SC Paderborn 07 came up. The ball won't roll again until Fri. 08/28 — kickoff is Bayern vs. Stuttgart, 20:30 MESZ / 15:30 BRT; the transfer window is open until 08/31 (dfl.de).

2026 World Cup — the semifinals are set

The quarterfinal round is complete, and both semifinal pairings are set. France (2-0 over Morocco, Thu. 07/09, Foxborough) and Spain (2-1 over Belgium, Fri. 07/10, Inglewood; Mikel Merino scored in the 88th minute) got things started. Overnight, the last two tickets were decided: England beat Norway 2-1 after extra time (a.e.t.) — Jude Bellingham with a brace, the winner coming three minutes into extra time after Andreas Schjelderup had put Norway ahead early (espn.com). And Argentina ground down Switzerland 3-1 a.e.t. (Mac Allister 10', Álvarez 112', Lautaro Martínez 120'+1; Ndoye for Switzerland), with Breel Embolo sent off in the 72nd minute for a second yellow (a dive) after a VAR review (espn.com). That ends the tournament for Freiburg's Johan Manzambi.

That sets up the semifinals: France–Spain (Tue. 07/14, 21:00 MESZ / 16:00 BRT, Dallas/Arlington) and England–Argentina (Wed. 07/15, 21:00 MESZ / 16:00 BRT, Atlanta).

Round of 32 vs. round of 16: Because of the field expanding to 48 teams, the 2026 World Cup has one more knockout round than before. After the group stage, 32 teams advance first — they play the round of 32 (where Germany went out against Paraguay). After that comes the round of 16 with 16 teams (where Brazil's run ended against Norway). In short: Brazil made it one round further than Germany — though both went out earlier than hoped.

DFB — Nagelsmann out, Klopp set to take over

The round-of-32 exit triggered a shake-up at the DFB. Germany lost 1-1 to Paraguay after 120 minutes (Julio Enciso 42', Kai Havertz 54') and went out 3-4 on penalties on Mon. 06/29 — the first World Cup shootout a German team has ever lost (skysports.com). A few days later, Julian Nagelsmann (38) drew the consequence: on Fri. 07/03, the DFB and the head coach parted ways after a multi-hour meeting with the executive board and sporting director Rudi Völler — officially a resignation (sportschau.de). As his successor, Jürgen Klopp is now very close: at a meeting lasting more than four hours with DFB president Bernd Neuendorf and vice-president Hans-Joachim Watzke in New York, both sides agreed on the key points of a possible contract — reportedly running through the 2030 World Cup — as the DFB announced on Sat. 07/11. Nothing has been signed yet, though; the only thing still open is the release from Red Bull, where Klopp is under contract as global head of football through 2029 (espn.com · rte.ie).

Context: Klopp brings a level of star power that almost no other available coach can match — but he hasn't sat on a bench since leaving Liverpool, spending his time instead in a corporate role at Red Bull. The key points are agreed — but until Red Bull's release comes through and the contract is signed, this remains an agreement in principle, not an appointment. So temper expectations for now.

Brazil — Seleção out, league heading toward restart

For the Seleção, the World Cup has been over since the round of 16: 1-2 against Norway (Sun. 07/05, East Rutherford), Erling Haaland with a brace, a Neymar penalty in stoppage time coming too late (espn.com). It's Brazil's earliest World Cup exit since 1990. Focus now turns to the league: Série A has been on pause since the 18th round for the World Cup — more than 50 days of downtime — and restarts in the week of 07/16-22 with Round 19. Palmeiras leads comfortably with 41 points, with Flamengo (34, one match in hand) lurking behind.

It's Sunday, July twelfth. The Bundesliga is on its summer break, and for us the World Cup in North America is long over — Germany and Brazil are both out. Still, today has three storylines: the World Cup semifinals are now set, Freiburg's season kicks off, and the coaching search at the German federation just took a big step forward.

At the World Cup, the quarterfinal round is complete. Overnight, England beat Norway two to one after extra time, with a brace from Jude Bellingham, and Argentina beat Switzerland three to one, also in extra time. That sets both semifinals: France against Spain on Tuesday, and England against Argentina on Wednesday — both at nine p.m. Central European time, four p.m. here in Brazil. The final is Sunday at MetLife Stadium.

For Freiburg, the new season starts today. Preseason training begins on the twelfth of July, with a training camp in Schruns, Austria, later this month. At the same time, the club's biggest sale ever is taking shape: Johan Manzambi is set to move to Newcastle United for around sixty million euros. There's a full agreement in principle, though it's not officially completed yet. At least Manzambi's tournament with Switzerland is over now, so yesterday's scheduling headache is gone.

On the incoming side, new arrivals include Yamamoto and Goto, while Junior Adamu heads to Schalke and Yann Sturm to Braunschweig. The competitive season opens with the German Cup, away at third-division Fortuna Düsseldorf in late August. And it's worth remembering: this year Freiburg played its first-ever European final, in the Europa League, though the club lost it three-nil to Aston Villa in Istanbul back in May.

At the German federation, the coaching picture just got clearer. Germany, remember, was eliminated in the round of thirty-two by Paraguay on penalties — the first shootout loss a German team has ever suffered at a World Cup — and Julian Nagelsmann resigned days later. Now Jürgen Klopp is very close to taking over: after a meeting of more than four hours in New York with the federation's president and vice-president, both sides agreed on the key points of a contract, reportedly running through the 2030 World Cup. Nothing is signed yet, though — the only thing still open is Klopp's release from Red Bull, where he remains under contract.

Brazil went out a round earlier, in the round of sixteen against Norway, with Erling Haaland scoring twice. It's Brazil's earliest World Cup exit since nineteen ninety. The domestic league resumes in the week of July sixteenth through twenty-second, with Palmeiras leading the table.

The Bundesliga season itself is fully wrapped up: Bayern are champions with eighty-nine points, Freiburg finished a solid seventh, securing Conference League qualifying. Play doesn't resume until August twenty-eighth.
