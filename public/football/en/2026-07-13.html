SC Freiburg — the rebuild summer

Squad planning in the transfer window · as of 07/13/2026

The historic season is in the books: 7th place in the league, the club's first-ever European final (0-3 vs Aston Villa in Istanbul, 05/20) and the German Cup semifinal (1-2 a.e.t. vs Stuttgart, 04/23). Now the squad is being visibly rebuilt — and the two loudest headlines concern the goal and Johan Manzambi's future.

Arrivals: Backhaus in goal, Gotō up front — both official

Mio Backhaus, goalkeeper and former Werder U21 international, is officially on board (announced 05/23) — and reportedly the most expensive signing in club history. Neither club has confirmed the fee; around €15 million is being reported (Deichstube), and per spox plus up to €3 million in performance-based bonuses. Also joining is Keisuke Gotō (21), a Japanese national-team forward, from RSC Anderlecht (official since 06/11). The reported ~€11 million figure comes from journalist Sacha Tavolieri; the club has not confirmed a number.

scfreiburg.com · deichstube.de · spox.com · bundesliga.com

Departures: the Manzambi mega-deal — agreed, but not yet signed

Johan Manzambi (20) is on the verge of Freiburg's record sale: Newcastle United and SC Freiburg are said to have agreed a fee of around €60 million, with personal terms reportedly settled too. But careful: per the reports (Yahoo, Tagesspiegel, as of 07/11–12), the move is not yet officially signed. Manzambi reportedly wanted to wait until after Switzerland's World Cup exit before putting pen to paper — and that came on Friday (07/11, 1-3 in the quarterfinal against Argentina). The brake is off now; the signature could follow any day. Until then, it remains a rumor with a very concrete number, not a done deal.

What is confirmed, on the other hand, is the departure of Junior Adamu to newly promoted Schalke 04 (official 06/12, contract through 2029). There's a minor source discrepancy on the fee: around €800,000 per club-adjacent figures, while another source cites just under one million, with bonuses up to ~€1.5m — so a low single-digit sum either way.

Preseason & Europe: Schruns, Crystal Palace, Conference League

Training kicked off yesterday (07/12). Next up is the training camp in Schruns, Austria (07/22–31) with friendlies against Derby County (in Imst) and SpVgg Greuther Fürth (in Schruns). The club has not officially locked in exact match dates yet — one aggregator listed 07/24 (Derby) and 07/31 (Fürth), but that's unconfirmed. The season opener comes on 08/15 at the Europa-Park Stadion against Crystal Palace.

Conference League playoff: the Conference League is Europe's third-tier club competition; the playoff is the last qualifying stage right before the league phase — seeding runs on the five-year coefficient (roughly: how well a club has performed in Europe over the last five years). Freiburg gets in via 7th place in the league and enters at the playoff stage: draw on 08/03 in Nyon, two-legged tie on the 20th and 27th of August.

World Cup 2026 — the final week without you

Final rounds in the USA, Canada & Mexico · as known 07/13/2026

For a German fan in Brazil, this World Cup ended bitterly on two fronts: Germany was eliminated in the round of 32 by Paraguay (1-1, then 3-4 on penalties, 06/29) — an exit for the four-time world champion that the press treated as a shock. And Brazil was gone in the round of 16: 1-2 against Norway (07/05, East Rutherford), with Erling Haaland scoring twice (79'/90'); Neymar's penalty in the tenth minute of stoppage time was mere consolation.

The quarterfinals then delivered the expected heavyweights: France 2-0 Morocco (07/09), Spain 2-1 Belgium (07/10), England 2-1 Norway (07/11) and Argentina 3-1 Switzerland (07/11). That sets the final four — and with them the semifinal pairings: France–Spain on Tuesday (07/14, Dallas) and England–Argentina on Wednesday (07/15, Atlanta), both at 9:00 PM CEST / 4:00 PM BRT. The third-place match is in Miami on Saturday (07/18, 11:00 PM CEST / 6:00 PM BRT), and the final is on Sunday (07/19) at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford (9:00 PM CEST / 4:00 PM BRT).

The Freiburg thread running through the tournament: Manzambi

A small comfort for Black Forest hearts: Freiburg's Johan Manzambi made a mark at this World Cup with Switzerland — three goals and two assists before the quarterfinal exit against Argentina (07/11). That very performance is exactly what has fueled his market value and Newcastle's interest — the World Cup stage was the amplifier behind the €60 million rumor.

It's Monday, July 13th, and the World Cup in North America is heading into its final week. For you as a German fan in Brazil, this has been a tournament without a horse in the race: Germany already went out in the round of 32 against Paraguay, on penalties, three to four. And the Seleção was eliminated in the round of 16 against Norway, one to two, with a brace from Erling Haaland.

What's left are four big names. On Tuesday, France and Spain meet in Dallas, and on Wednesday, England and Argentina face off in Atlanta — both at nine p.m. Central European Summer Time, which is four p.m. in Ribeirão Claro. The third-place match is in Miami on Saturday, and the final is on Sunday at MetLife Stadium near New York, again at four p.m. Brazilian time.

A small Freiburg thread ran through the tournament anyway: Johan Manzambi scored three goals and set up two more for Switzerland, before it ended on Friday in the quarterfinal against Argentina. That very performance is exactly what stirred up all the buzz around his transfer.

Because back home in Freiburg, the real summer is underway. Newcastle United and the club are said to have agreed on around sixty million euros for Manzambi, with personal terms reportedly settled as well. But heads up: nothing has been signed yet. Manzambi reportedly wanted to wait until after the World Cup exit to put pen to paper, and that exit has now happened. So the signature could come at any moment, but as of today it still hasn't gone through.

On the incoming side, more is locked in: goalkeeper Mio Backhaus is arriving from Werder Bremen and is, according to media reports, the most expensive signing in club history, with a reported fee of around fifteen million euros plus bonuses. Joining him is Japanese striker Keisuke Gotō from Anderlecht for around eleven million. Leaving the club is Junior Adamu, who's moving to newly promoted Schalke.

Training kicked off yesterday. Next comes the training camp in Schruns, Austria, from the twenty-second through the thirty-first of July, with friendlies against Derby County and Greuther Fürth. The season opener at the Europa-Park-Stadion is set for August fifteenth against Crystal Palace. Internationally, Freiburg continues through the Conference League playoff, with the draw on August third in Nyon.

The Bundesliga itself is quiet. The season wrapped up long ago, with FC Bayern winning the title in dominant fashion with eighty-nine points and a new scoring record of one hundred twenty-two goals. Freiburg finished the season in seventh place. The new season doesn't start until August twenty-eighth, fittingly with Bayern against Stuttgart.

In Brazil, the Brasileirão is on hold after matchday eighteen because of the World Cup. Palmeiras leads with forty-one points, while Chapecoense sits at the very bottom. The league returns on July twenty-second, with some matches already happening on the sixteenth and seventeenth. The cup continues starting in August, with the final not until December as a single match.

What matters this week: the two semifinals on Tuesday and Wednesday, the final on Sunday — and the question of whether Manzambi's signature with Newcastle finally dries. We'll check back in tomorrow.
