SC Freiburg — the transfer U-turn and a keeper on the way out

Squad planning in the open transfer window · As of 2026-07-14

Freiburg has its own summer drama running while the World Cup plays out in North America. Two stories dominate: the record sale of Johan Manzambi flipped destinations overnight, and starting keeper Noah Atubolu is visibly preparing for an exit.

Manzambi: no longer Newcastle, but Aston Villa — ~€70 million

Update since yesterday The €60 million agreement with Newcastle that had been reported for days now appears to be outdated: according to Sky and Badische Zeitung (as of July 12), SC Freiburg and Aston Villa have agreed on an overall package worth around €70 million including add-ons — it would be Freiburg's record sale. Villa reportedly outbid Newcastle's basic agreement before it was finalized.

For all the precise figures: nothing is signed yet. The reports describe a complete agreement, but the medical is still pending and the move is not officially confirmed. His market value has been fueled by this World Cup: Manzambi (20) scored three goals and set up two more for Switzerland before being eliminated in the quarterfinal against Argentina (July 11). The World Cup stage was the amplifier under the price tag.

sport.sky.de · badische-zeitung.de

Atubolu training separately — Backhaus steps into goal

Noah Atubolu was missing from the squad at the start of preseason: the number-one keeper is training individually, while his already-signed successor Mio Backhaus (from Werder Bremen) works with the head coach on the pitch. Atubolu's contract runs through June 30, 2027, but the club is preparing for a sale; Premier League newcomer Hull City was most recently named as a suitor. Alongside Backhaus, Freiburg also introduced new signings Rihito Yamamoto and Keisuke Gotō — the squad overhaul runs deep.

sport1.de · comunio.de

DFB-Pokal & preseason: away trip to Düsseldorf, Schruns training camp

The draw (June 6) sends Freiburg to an away match at Fortuna Düsseldorf in round 1 of the DFB-Pokal — Düsseldorf now plays in the 3rd division — with the fixture scheduled for the window Aug 21–24. Until then, preseason continues: training kicked off on July 12, followed by the training camp in Schruns, Austria (July 22–31) with friendlies against Derby County (July 24) and Greuther Fürth (July 31), plus Crystal Palace on Aug 15. Internationally, Freiburg enters as league seventh-place finisher via the Conference League playoff.

Conference League playoff: The Conference League is Europe's third-tier club competition; the playoff is the final qualifying stage right before the league phase. Freiburg qualifies via 7th place in the Bundesliga and enters only at this stage — draw in early August, home-and-away tie in late August.

The German national team — Nagelsmann out, Klopp incoming

Coaching shake-up after the World Cup exit · As of 2026-07-14

The World Cup exit against Paraguay set off a chain reaction at the DFB. On the night of the elimination, Julian Nagelsmann still said he would like to continue, "if the federation wants it." Four days later, on July 3, he resigned — after a three-hour crisis meeting (July 2) with DFB president Bernd Neuendorf and Rudi Völler. His parting words: it pained him deeply that the team had disappointed the fans.

Jürgen Klopp is standing by as his successor. According to matching reports (July 11), the DFB and Klopp have agreed in principle on a contract through the 2030 World Cup — only one detail remains open, but a decisive one: the release from Red Bull. The signing isn't planned until after the tournament, once Klopp finishes his role as a MagentaTV pundit. On salary, per Bild, he's expected to come in slightly above Nagelsmann's roughly €7 million a year — which would make him the best-paid national team coach ever.

Why Klopp can't simply sign: Klopp holds a full-time position at Red Bull as "Global Head of Soccer" (since leaving Liverpool in 2024). Like any employee under contract, he needs his employer's sign-off to leave early — so the DFB first has to reach an agreement with Red Bull boss Oliver Mintzlaff on a release. That's exactly where the final knot sits; per Sportschau, it's expected to be resolved by early next week at the latest.
