SC Freiburg — the transfer summer, while the World Cup rolls on

Squad planning in the open transfer window · As of 2026-07-15

For Daniel, this is the real headline: while the World Cup heads into its final stages in North America, the market is buzzing in Freiburg. A record sale is close to completion, and the squad has been reshaped in several spots.

Manzambi to Aston Villa — Freiburg's most expensive sale

After the collapsed Newcastle deal (the ~€60m package never went through because Manzambi withheld his final commitment), Sky reports that SC Freiburg and Aston Villa have agreed on a total package worth up to roughly €70 million including bonuses. That would make the 20-year-old Swiss international by far the most expensive departure in Freiburg's club history.

Despite all the specific numbers: the move is still not official — reports describe a complete agreement, but it has not been formally finalized as of July 15. His market value has been fueled by his showing at this World Cup: Manzambi scored three times and set up two more for Switzerland — in just four appearances before the run ended in the quarterfinal. The World Cup stage acted as an amplifier under the price tag.

Fee "including bonuses": The ~€70m isn't a fixed wire transfer, but a base fee plus bonus tiers that only get paid out once certain things happen — a number of appearances, goals, or a later Champions League qualification for Villa, for example. Like buying a car with optional extras: you only hit the sticker price if all the add-ons come together. That's why it says "up to ~€70m," not "€70m fixed."
sport.sky.de

Arrivals and departures: Backhaus, Yamamoto, Gotō join — Adamu leaves

Freiburg has refreshed the squad in several positions. New in goal is Mio Backhaus (from Werder Bremen), in midfield Rihito Yamamoto (roughly €7 million, from St. Truiden in the Belgian league), and up front Japan's World Cup forward Keisuke Gotō. Gotō's fee is put at roughly €10 to 11 million — though the exact amount and the selling club are reported inconsistently (kicker.de puts it around €10m, as of July 12), so treat it with caution.

On the departure side, besides Manzambi there are two confirmed moves: Junior Adamu is heading to Schalke 04 (base fee just under €1m, with realistic bonuses up to ~€1.5m), and Robert Wagner has now made his loan at second-division side Dynamo Dresden permanent.

World Cup 2026 — today's second semifinal, Spain already waiting

Final rounds in the USA, Canada & Mexico · As of 2026-07-15

The knockout stage is racing toward its close. Spain is through to the final: yesterday La Roja beat France 2-0 at AT&T Stadium in Arlington (Dallas area) — Mikel Oyarzabal converted a penalty in the 22nd minute, Pedro Porro added a second in the 58th. It's Spain's first World Cup final since their 2010 title.

Tonight decides the second finalist: Argentina takes on England at Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta, kickoff 9:00 PM CEST / 4:00 PM BRT (3:00 PM local time). Both sides cruised through the quarterfinal — Argentina beat Switzerland 3-1, England beat Norway 2-1. The third-place match is Saturday in Miami (11:00 PM CEST / 6:00 PM BRT), with France taking that spot as the semifinal 1 loser; the final follows Sunday (July 19) at MetLife Stadium near New York (9:00 PM CEST / 4:00 PM BRT).

Bar-stool pick Pure opinion, not fact: both semifinalists look evenly matched, but in a tight game I lean slightly toward Argentina — big-game composure and tournament experience often tip the scales in semifinals, and history weighs on England against Argentina. A toss-up with the thinnest of edges. And whoever wins tonight, in the final I still see Spain, with their possession-based rhythm, as the favorite.

World Cup exits — Germany and Brazil long since home

Looking back at the knockout rounds · As of 2026-07-15

For a German fan in Brazil, it's a doubly bitter tournament — both favorite teams are out early, long before tonight's fight for a final spot.

Germany: knocked out in the round of 32 by Paraguay

Germany fell in the round of 32 to Paraguay: 1-1 (Enciso 42', Havertz 54'), then 3-4 on penalties after extra time. In extra time, VAR ruled out a supposed German winning goal — a controversial call; in the shootout, Jonathan Tah among others missed the decisive attempt. The match fell on June 29/30 (sources give inconsistent dates).

Brazil: knocked out in the round of 16 by Norway and Haaland

Brazil was out in the round of 16: 1-2 against Norway on Sunday (July 5) at MetLife Stadium. Erling Haaland scored twice in the second half, Neymar pulled one back from the penalty spot in stoppage time. Per CNN Brasil, it's an exit that further extends the Seleção's longest title drought — a tournament to forget for the football nation.
