SC Freiburg — one departure done, one in the waiting room

Transfer window open · as of 07/17/2026

Röhl: the clause snapped shut

Merlin Röhl's loan at Everton has turned into a permanent transfer — not because anyone renegotiated, but because a built-in trigger fired. The fee, per transfermarkt, is around €25m, with a new contract through 2029. There's no cleanly sourced hard confirmation date; reports place the completion in early July.

Mandatory-purchase clause: A loan deal with a built-in automatic trigger — once condition X happens, the borrowing club must buy the player permanently, no choice involved. Like a rental car with fine print: drive more than 10,000 km and the car becomes yours, bill included, automatically. For Röhl, condition X was survival in the league — Everton finished 13th in the Premier League, the condition was met, and the loan turned into a purchase.

Context: per sport1, this is the most expensive completed sale in Freiburg's club history. That record will likely only last days, though — see below.

transfermarkt.us · sport1.de

Manzambi: €60m, and still not a fact

Johan Manzambi, 20, a Swiss international, is reportedly headed to Aston Villa for a sum of "just over €60m." Fabrizio Romano reported on Monday, 07/13, a verbal agreement with Freiburg and a verbal agreement with the player and his agency on personal terms — Villa has thereby overturned an earlier Newcastle arrangement. Since then, the deal has moved considerably further: reports say a five-year contract has been negotiated, and on Thursday, 07/16, Manzambi completed his medical at Aston Villa. Romano predicted Thursday morning that an announcement would follow "in the next few hours."

Reported, not done Even a completed medical is not yet a done deal: as of this morning, 07/17, neither Freiburg nor Villa has announced the transfer — and the club statement is what counts. Everything above lives in transfer reports, not a club announcement. This is now a very far-advanced rumor with a very good source — but Romano's "next few hours" passed yesterday morning, so wait for the paperwork before you re-plan Freiburg's midfield.

Why the price: Manzambi produced 3 goals and 2 assists in 4 matches at this World Cup, as Switzerland reached the quarterfinals. Before that, his 2025/26 season brought 47 appearances, 7 goals, and 9 assists — for a Freiburg side that finished seventh and reached the Europa League final. My take: for a 20-year-old with this profile, a €60m fee isn't a moon price, it's a market price. Still, it'll sting that Röhl and Manzambi are both leaving in the same summer — that's half the midfield.

What's coming in, what's going out

The counter-move is already underway. Arrivals per transfermarkt: goalkeeper Mio Backhaus (22) from Werder Bremen for €12.00m, striker Keisuke Gotō (21) from RSC Anderlecht for €10.00m, defensive midfielder Rihito Yamamoto (24) from Sint-Truiden for €7.00m, plus two promotions from the reserve team (Rouven Tarnutzer, Karl Steinmann) on free transfers.

Departures besides Röhl: Robert Wagner to Dynamo Dresden (€850k), Junior Adamu to FC Schalke 04 (€800k), Noah Weißhaupt to Darmstadt 98 (€500k). Bottom line: Freiburg sells high and buys in bulk — the classic pattern. Whether it works out this time hinges on whether Gotō turns out to be a Bundesliga-ready striker or a project.

No competitive matches are on the calendar. Friendlies are scheduled against Derby County, SpVgg Greuther Fürth, and Crystal Palace; fixed on the calendar is the doubleheader against Racing Strasbourg on Sat 08/08 in Freiburg — 12:30 PM CEST / 7:30 AM BRT and 3:30 PM CEST / 10:30 AM BRT.

DFB — 19 days after the exit, and still no coach

As of 07/17/2026

Update since yesterday Nothing official. For completeness, here's the state of play, because it explains the final weekend you're watching without Germany: On Monday, 06/29, the World Cup ended in the round of 32 against Paraguay. 1-1 after 120 minutes — Julio Enciso scored in the 42nd minute, Kai Havertz equalized with a header in the 54th. Then 3-4 on penalties. Per kicker, Jonathan Tah, Havertz, and Nick Woltemade missed three of Germany's six attempts; José Canale converted the decisive one for Paraguay. A Tah goal in the 102nd minute had already been disallowed by VAR. The match was played at Gillette Stadium near Boston.

On Friday, 07/03, the DFB announced the split from Julian Nagelsmann — formally at his own request, raised in a confidential conversation with federation leadership. The caveat matters: ZDF writes that Nagelsmann agreed to the "face-saving solution" that, per consistent media reports, had been urged on him a day earlier at a crisis meeting at DFB headquarters — Thursday, 07/02, in Frankfurt, with Neuendorf, Völler, Rettig, and Watzke. "Own request" is the wording here, not the actual process: a dismissal dressed up as a resignation. His contract would have run through EURO 2028.

Reported, not confirmed Jürgen Klopp is seen as the near-certain solution: per ran (citing Fabrizio Romano), DFB president Bernd Neuendorf reached an agreement on Klopp's exit clause in a meeting with Red Bull chief Oliver Mintzlaff, and a presentation "could come as soon as next week." As of today, 07/17, the DFB has confirmed nothing — no board sign-off, no announcement. Until that happens, Germany is a football world power without a head coach.

It's Friday, July seventeenth. The World Cup is heading into its final weekend, and you're watching as a neutral. Germany has been out since June twenty-ninth, Brazil since July fifth. What's left is still a massive weekend — and tonight the Brasileirão kicks off right on your doorstep.

The final is Sunday: Spain against Argentina, at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey. Kickoff is nine o'clock German time, four in the afternoon for you in Ribeirão Claro. A Sunday afternoon, no alarm clock needed. Spain reached the final with a two-nil win over France, Argentina with a two-one win over England. My read: Spain is the better team over ninety minutes, Argentina the better team over a whole tournament. That smells like extra time.

A day earlier, on Saturday, France and England play for third place. At Hard Rock Stadium in Miami Gardens, eleven at night German time, six in the evening for you. Two semifinal losers, a match without fear — and that's usually exactly what makes it entertaining.

Tonight at eight o'clock Brazilian time, Fluminense hosts Red Bull Bragantino at the Maracanã. It's a makeup match from round nineteen, and the first competitive game for both clubs after forty-five days of World Cup break. Fluminense sits third with thirty-one points, Bragantino fifth with twenty-nine. Hulk is registered and set to make his official debut. Up top, Palmeiras is pulling away with forty-one points.

At SC Freiburg, one departure is done and another is hanging in the air. Merlin Röhl has moved permanently to Everton, for around twenty-five million euros. That went through a mandatory-purchase clause — an automatic trigger in the loan deal that fires once a condition is met. Here, the condition was survival in the league — Everton finished thirteenth in the Premier League, which made the purchase mandatory. Per sport1, it's the most expensive completed sale in Freiburg's club history.

The second departure is Johan Manzambi, to Aston Villa, for just over sixty million euros. Important: this is a report, not a fact. Fabrizio Romano reported a verbal agreement on July thirteenth — with Freiburg and with the player. Since then, things have moved further along: reports say a five-year contract has been negotiated, and on Thursday, the sixteenth of July, Manzambi completed his medical. Still, as of this morning, neither Freiburg nor Villa has announced anything. Until the club statement lands, it's a very far-advanced rumor with a very good source, nothing more. The price makes sense given his tournament: three goals and two assists in four matches, as Switzerland reached the quarterfinals.

At the DFB, nineteen days after the exit, there's still no head coach in place. Julian Nagelsmann left on July third, formally at his own request — though in reality, the federation had urged him to resign a day earlier, at a crisis meeting at DFB headquarters, so it was a dismissal dressed up as a resignation. His contract would have run through the twenty-twenty-eight European Championship. Jürgen Klopp is reportedly all but certain, with his Red Bull clause said to be resolved and a presentation floated for next week. The DFB has confirmed none of it as of today.

The Bundesliga is still asleep. The DFL's master schedule sets kickoff for August twenty-eighth, with Bayern hosting Stuttgart at eight-thirty in the evening German time, three-thirty for you. Until then, last season's final table remains the anchor: Bayern champions with eighty-nine points, Freiburg seventh, Wolfsburg relegated through the playoff after twenty-nine years.

Monday could bring either a Klopp announcement or just another report about one. And by the next brief, the World Cup champion will be decided.
