World Cup 2026 — Spain crowns itself, Germany falls apart at the penalty spot

It's settled: Spain are world champions. In the final at East Rutherford, La Roja beat Argentina 1-0 after extra time on Sunday evening, with Ferran Torres scoring in the 106th minute. Kickoff was 21:00 CEST / 16:00 BRT at MetLife Stadium. It's Spain's second World Cup title — the first came in the 2010 final in Johannesburg. Over the course of the tournament, they were the deserved winner: composed on the ball, with the clearest idea of how they wanted to play, and in the end calm enough to crack open a tight final in extra time.[1]

Round of 32: This World Cup was the first with 48 teams instead of 32. From twelve groups of four, the top two plus the eight best third-place teams advance — that's 32 teams in the first knockout round. Think of it like a cup tournament that starts one round earlier: before the round of 16, there's now an extra round of 32, and that's exactly where Germany's tournament ended.

For the DFB, it became a new summer trauma. The group stage (Group E) still went to plan — a 7-1 win over Curaçao, 2-1 over Ivory Coast, and a 1-2 loss to Ecuador were enough to advance as group winners. Then came the exit in the round of 32 against Paraguay (Mon, Jun 29, Gillette Stadium in Foxborough): 1-1 after extra time, 3-4 on penalties. It was the first World Cup penalty shootout a German team has ever lost — the myth of the reliable tournament nation is broken.[2]

The fallout didn't take long: Julian Nagelsmann and the DFB terminated the contract by mutual agreement on Jul 3 — Nagelsmann himself asked to be released after a multi-hour conversation.[3] The succession is open. Jürgen Klopp is being touted as the top candidate; DFB chief Hans-Joachim Watzke put the base probability at "greater than 50 percent," but made clear the matter is "definitely not decided yet." Klopp is contracted through 2029 as "Head of Global Soccer" at Red Bull, with no exit clause. In short: hotly rumored, but not signed — until the DFB makes a name official, it stays a rumor, not a report.[4]

SC Freiburg — squad overhaul and a fresh start in the Montafon

For Daniel's favorite club, the summer break is anything but downtime. Training started on Sun, Jul 12 at the Europa-Park-Stadion, and starting tomorrow the team heads to a training camp in Schruns in the Montafon (Jul 22–31). Two friendlies are on the schedule there, against Derby County and Greuther Fürth; the exact dates are per the preliminary schedule.[5]

On the transfer market, the squad has turned over noticeably. The headline story: Johan Manzambi (20), last season's midfield gem, is moving to Aston Villa — of all clubs, the very one that spoiled Freiburg's Europa League final 0-3 in May. Transfermarkt puts the base fee at around €60m, with add-ons pushing it up to €70m according to 90min — either way, the biggest sale in club history. On top of that, Merlin Röhl is moving to Everton for ~€25m, and Junior Adamu to Schalke 04 for ~€0.8m.[6]

The reinvestment is targeted: the new No. 1 goalkeeper is Mio Backhaus (22, from Werder, ~€12m), joined by striker Keisuke Goto (~€10m) and defensive midfielders Yannick Engelhardt and Rihito Yamamoto (~€7m each). The bottom line is a hefty transfer surplus of +€51.15m (€87.15m in income against €36m in direct spending across 13 signings). My take: sound business sense, but Manzambi's departure leaves a creative gap that four solid signings won't automatically close — preseason will have to show who's distributing the ball in the final third.[6]

In Europe, Freiburg's 2026/27 path only runs the long way round. As 7th place finisher, that's not enough for the Europa League or Champions League — only for the Conference League playoff, the qualifying round for Europe's third club competition, which the team has to get through over two knockout legs just to reach the league phase at all. First leg Thu, Aug 20, second leg Thu, Aug 27 (dates per preliminary schedule), with home advantage drawn on Aug 3. In between comes the 1st DFB Cup round away at Fortuna Düsseldorf in the window of Aug 21–24 — the exact date and kickoff time are still to be announced.[5][7]
