DFB — Klopp Becomes Head Coach Tomorrow at 11

Source: sportschau.de, article from 07/23/2026, 11:55 · officially confirmed is the date, not the signature

The Date Is Set: Friday, 11:00 CEST / 06:00 BRT

After three weeks of back-and-forth, it's official: on Friday, 24.07, at 11:00 CEST / 06:00 BRT, Jürgen Klopp is set to be presented at the DFB headquarters in Frankfurt as Julian Nagelsmann's successor. He is expected to receive a contract through 2030 — that is, past the 2028 Euros and up to the next World Cup.[1]

This closes out a process that played out pretty messily in public. Nagelsmann's contract was terminated on Fri 03.07, four days after the World Cup exit; the DFB announced in the same statement that it would seek talks with Klopp.[2] On 19.07, Klopp's agent still denied reports of an agreement — a day later Klopp himself confirmed he had "found an agreement and arrangement with Red Bull," and that "basically nothing stands in the way anymore."[3] This is exactly how weeks emerge in which nobody knows what's actually true. Starting tomorrow, whatever gets said at the podium is what counts.

The Debut Counts Immediately: 24.09 in Amsterdam

Klopp's first match is Thu 24.09, 20:45 CEST / 15:45 BRT, in Amsterdam against the Netherlands — Nations League.[1] Not a gentle introduction against a lesser opponent, but straight into a neighborhood duel with points on the line.

Nations League: Instead of loosely drawn friendlies, UEFA sorts all national teams into leagues by strength and has them play each other in groups — like a school league where the strongest classes stay among themselves, the best move up, and the weakest get relegated. For Klopp, that means his first match is a competitive fixture that counts in the standings.

What He Inherits — and Why the Goalkeeper Question Is Open

The inheritance is a round-of-16 exit: a 1-1 draw after extra time, 3-4 on penalties against Paraguay, Mon 29.06 in Foxborough. Nagelsmann's side failed at the spot: Tah put Germany's sixth penalty over the bar, and José Canale sealed the elimination.[4] On top of that, Manuel Neuer (40) has definitively ended his national-team career; the round of 16 was his last international.[5]

On Neuer's retirement: sourced only via a kicker.de search snippet; we could not verify the exact interview date (approx. 03.–05.07) against the original.

My read: the squad isn't the problem, the hierarchy is. Klopp inherits a team without an undisputed goalkeeper, without a settled captain, and with a generation that has now fallen short of expectations in two straight tournaments. That's exactly what he's good at — assigning roles and charging up a group emotionally. Whether it's enough tactically, we won't see before September at the earliest.

SC Freiburg — a Goalkeeper Stays Behind, a Record Departure Is Set

Transfer window open (May–Aug) · tribuna.com returned a 403 today; the Atubolu report is reconstructed from several specialist outlets, so we deliberately do not print a verbatim club quote

Atubolu Isn't Traveling

SC Freiburg has been working since Wed 22.07 at its training camp in Schruns/Austria (until Fri 31.07) — but without Noah Atubolu (24). The goalkeeper is staying in Freiburg and running his own program there; according to matching reports from today, the club and the player jointly decided this so that talks with interested clubs can be better coordinated — board member Jochen Saier confirmed the reason.[8] Aston Villa and AFC Bournemouth from the Premier League, as well as Juventus, have been mentioned.[9]

That's a remarkably open admission. Clubs usually deny things until the last second; Freiburg instead acknowledges that its starting goalkeeper is on his way out — and has already brought in Mio Backhaus as a potential successor. This looks like an orderly handover, not an emergency.

Manzambi: Aston Villa, £59.5m — Newcastle Is History

Johan Manzambi (20, Switzerland) left the club on 17.07 for Aston Villa. The fee: £59.5m (≈ €70m) — the most expensive transfer in Villa's club history.[10] According to Sky Sports, Villa's Champions League participation was the deciding factor.[10] Manzambi arrived as a World Cup discovery: three goals for Switzerland on the way to the quarterfinals.[10]

Superseded Until mid-July, Newcastle United was considered the favorite; a fee of around €60m was reported. These reports have been obsolete since 17.07 — Manzambi is a Villa player. Anyone who finds a Newcastle headline is reading an outdated version.

Where Freiburg Is Coming From — and Where It's Headed This Season

The 2025/26 season ended in 7th place with 47 points (13 W / 8 D / 13 L, 51-57 goals) — and with the biggest individual achievement in club history: the first-ever European final. In the Europa League final on Wed 20.05, SC lost 0-3 to, of all teams, Aston Villa; the road there ran through Genk, Celta Vigo, and Braga.[11] Top scorer was Igor Matanović with 11 league goals.[11]

For 2026/27, 7th place isn't enough for the Europa League: Freiburg has to go through the Conference League playoff, first leg Thu 20.08, second leg Thu 27.08.[12]

Conference League Playoff: European club football has three tiers — Champions League at the top, Europa League in the middle, Conference League at the bottom. Anyone not directly qualified has to first get through a two-legged knockout duel: win it, and you play in Europe come fall; lose it, and your European season is over in August. Freiburg is right in the middle of exactly that duel.

On top of that, there's the first competitive assignment: in the 1st round of the DFB-Pokal, SC has to travel to third-division side Fortuna Düsseldorf, in the window Fri–Mon 21.–24.08.[13] According to reports, new additions to the squad besides Backhaus include Rihito Yamamoto, Keisuke Goto, and returnee Yannik Engelhardt (from Como 1907).[14]

It's Thursday, July twenty-third. There's really just one big story in football today, and for once it's not a rumor: Jürgen Klopp is set to be unveiled as Germany's head coach tomorrow morning.

The appointment is scheduled for eleven in the morning German time — six a.m. here in Brazil — at the federation's headquarters in Frankfurt. Klopp is expected to sign a contract through twenty thirty, running past the next Euros. That's according to Sportschau, in a report published this morning. What's confirmed so far is the date, not the signature — we'll see that tomorrow.

His first match comes sooner than you'd think, and it's no friendly: September twenty-fourth in Amsterdam against the Netherlands, kickoff at eight forty-five in the evening German time, three forty-five in the afternoon here. That's a Nations League fixture, so it's competitive from the very first whistle.

What he's inheriting is no easy hand. Julian Nagelsmann departed in early July, four days after Germany's round-of-sixteen exit against Paraguay — a one-one draw after extra time, lost three-four on penalties. And Manuel Neuer has now retired from international football for good. Klopp takes over a team without an undisputed starting goalkeeper and without a clear leadership hierarchy. Rallying a group emotionally, though, happens to be exactly what he does best.

Over at SC Freiburg, a quieter version of the same story is unfolding. The club has been training since Wednesday in Schruns, Austria — but without goalkeeper Noah Atubolu. He's staying home because he wants a move, and the club is being upfront about it. Aston Villa, Bournemouth, and Juventus have all been mentioned as suitors. Freiburg has already lined up Mio Backhaus as a likely successor, which suggests an orderly plan rather than a scramble.

The club's biggest departure, meanwhile, is already done. Johan Manzambi now plays for Aston Villa, for fifty-nine point five million pounds — roughly seventy million euros — making him the most expensive signing in Villa's history. One important note: earlier reports linking him to Newcastle are now outdated; the move to Villa was finalized on July seventeenth. Manzambi was one of the breakout stars of the World Cup, scoring three goals for Switzerland.

On the field, Freiburg just wrapped up an oddly strong season: only seventh in the league with forty-seven points, but the first European final in club history. In the Europa League final on May twentieth, they lost nil-three — to Aston Villa, of all teams. This coming season, the club has to fight through the Conference League playoff, with the first leg on August twentieth and the second a week later. Before that comes the cup, an away trip to Fortuna Düsseldorf in the window of August twenty-first through twenty-fourth — though that pairing so far comes from just a single source.

In Brazil, play has resumed after the World Cup break, and Palmeiras is pulling away at the top: forty-four points from nineteen matches, only one loss, seven points clear of Flamengo. Flamengo, though, has a game in hand. One honest caveat on the table: neither source site shows an update date, so this snapshot is pegged to matchday nineteen. Nothing's happening in the Copa do Brasil this week — the round of sixteen doesn't start until the weekend of August second.

Tomorrow's briefing will lead with two things above all: Jürgen Klopp's first words as head coach — and, if Freiburg's friendly against Derby County goes ahead, our first look at the lineup without Manzambi.
