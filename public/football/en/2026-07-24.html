SC Freiburg — the Record Departure Is Settled, First Test Today

Transfer window open (May–Aug) · Sources: scfreiburg.com, kicker.de, tagesspiegel.de, transfermarkt · New signings are club-confirmed without figures, Atubolu move remains at the rumor stage

Schruns, 20th Year in a Row — and Derby County Today

SC Freiburg has been working since Wed 22.07 at its training camp in Schruns/Austria (through Fri 31.07) — already the 20th time at the same location.[1] The first test is today: against Derby County, 16:00 MESZ / 11:00 BRT at the Belly-Arena in Imst/Tyrol.[1] Because kickoff isn't until the afternoon, the result was not yet available at press time — we'll follow up tomorrow. The second test follows Fri 31.07, 14:00 MESZ / 09:00 BRT against SpVgg Greuther Fürth in Schruns.[1]

In terms of personnel, World Cup returnees Philipp Lienhart and Igor Matanović (joining the squad on Saturday) are missing from the camp, along with the injured Florent Muslija; four U23 players are traveling as part of the 30-man squad.[1]

Manzambi: Around €70m to Aston Villa — Club Record

Johan Manzambi (20, Switzerland, a Freiburg academy product) left the club on 17.–18.07 for Aston Villa. The fee: a base of around €60m plus up to €10m in bonuses — the most expensive sale in Freiburg's club history, with the previous record at €25m apiece (Kevin Schade, Merlin Röhl).[2][3] Villa beat out Newcastle United in the process, which already had an agreement worth around €60m.[2] Sky Sports puts the deal at £59.5m — which is in line with the euro figure.

Transfer fee with bonuses: Part of the amount isn't paid right away — only if certain conditions are met later, such as a number of appearances, goals, or the buyer qualifying for the Champions League. Like a purchase price with performance installments: the base amount is guaranteed, but Freiburg only gets the €10m "bonus" if Manzambi really makes an impact at Villa.
Superseded Anyone who finds a Newcastle headline about Manzambi is reading an outdated version: Villa pulled off the deal in mid-July, and Manzambi has been a Villa player since 17.07.

New Signings Confirmed, Goalkeeper Question Open

For 2026/27, the club has officially welcomed new signings Mio Backhaus, Rihito Yamamoto, and Keisuke Goto — welcome videos are running on scfreiburg.com.[4] The club hasn't given transfer fees or exact contract dates so far, so we're not printing any figures here. The future of goalkeeper Noah Atubolu remains open: he continues to train in Freiburg, while, according to the club, "talks with interested clubs can be better coordinated" from there — a concrete move hasn't been confirmed and remains at the rumor stage.[5]

My read: with Backhaus, a possible successor is already between the posts, and Manzambi's record sale fills the coffers. This looks like an orderly handover, not a fire sale — Freiburg can plan this summer from a position of calm.

DFB — Klopp Becomes Head Coach Today, the Cup Awaits Freiburg

Source: sportschau.de (national team, as of 24.07); World Cup exit via dfb.de + Al Jazeera; cup draw via kicker.de + f95.de + baden24.de. At press time, the presentation was imminently scheduled, meaning the signing was announced but not confirmed.

At 11, the Rumor Becomes an Office

After weeks of back-and-forth, today's the day: on Friday, 24.07, at 11:00 MESZ / 06:00 BRT, Jürgen Klopp is set to be presented at the DFB headquarters in Frankfurt as Julian Nagelsmann's successor — with a contract through 2030, meaning beyond Euro 2028.[8] Nagelsmann's contract had already been terminated on Fri 03.07, four days after the World Cup exit.[9]

Klopp's first match counts immediately: Thu 24.09, 20:45 MESZ / 15:45 BRT in Amsterdam against the Netherlands — Nations League, not a friendly.[8] (Fixture not independently confirmed at press time — the UEFA and DFB schedules were unreachable.)

Nations League: Instead of loosely drawn friendlies, UEFA sorts all national teams into leagues by strength and has them play each other in groups — like a school league where the strongest classes stay among themselves. For Klopp, that means his debut is a competitive match that counts in a standings table.

The Legacy: the World Cup Exit Against Paraguay

Why a new coach at all? Germany was eliminated at the 2026 World Cup in the first knockout round (round of 32) by Paraguay: 1-1 after extra time, 3-4 on penalties, on Mon 29.06 near Boston/Foxborough.[10] It was the first time the German national team lost a World Cup penalty shootout.[11] Julio Enciso had put Paraguay ahead with a header (42'), Kai Havertz equalized after the break; in the end, luck simply wasn't on Germany's side in the shootout.[10]

My read: the squad isn't the problem, the hierarchy is — no undisputed goalkeeper, no settled captain, two straight tournaments below expectations. Charging up a group emotionally happens to be exactly Klopp's biggest strength; whether it's enough tactically, we'll see no earlier than September. Spain, incidentally, won the World Cup, 1-0 after extra time against Argentina (Ferran Torres, 106'), on Sun 19.07.[12]

DFB-Pokal: Freiburg Must Travel to Fortuna Düsseldorf

Freiburg's first competitive assignment of the new season is the cup: in the 1st round, they play away at Fortuna Düsseldorf, in the window Fri–Mon 21.–24.08. The draw took place on Sat 06.06 at the German Football Museum in Dortmund.[13] It's a reunion: last season, Freiburg won the 2nd round at the same venue.

It's Friday, July twenty-fourth. Football's day comes down to two big things, and both happen today: this morning, Jürgen Klopp becomes Germany's head coach, and this afternoon, SC Freiburg plays its first test of preseason.

First, the DFB. At eleven in the morning German time — six a.m. here in Brazil — Klopp is set to be presented at the federation's headquarters in Frankfurt as the new head coach. He's expected to sign a contract through twenty thirty, running past the next Euros. That's according to Sportschau, as of today. One important caveat: at press time, the appointment itself was set, so the signing was announced, but it hadn't been confirmed yet.

So why a new coach at all? Germany went out early at the World Cup, already in the round of thirty-two, against Paraguay. One-one after extra time, then a three-four loss on penalties, on June twenty-ninth near Boston. It was the first time a German team had ever lost a World Cup shootout. Julian Nagelsmann's contract was terminated four days later. Spain, by the way, won the World Cup, one-nil after extra time against Argentina.

Klopp's first match counts right away, and it's no friendly: September twenty-fourth in Amsterdam against the Netherlands, kickoff at eight forty-five in the evening German time, three forty-five in the afternoon here. That's a Nations League fixture, so it's competitive from the start.

Over at SC Freiburg, the club's biggest departure is already done. Johan Manzambi now plays for Aston Villa. The fee: a base of around sixty million euros plus up to ten million in bonuses, so roughly seventy million total — the most expensive sale in the club's history. Newcastle had also been bidding but came away empty-handed. Anyone who still finds a Newcastle headline is reading an outdated version.

Freiburg is currently training in Schruns, Austria — the twentieth year in a row at the same location. This afternoon at four o'clock German time, eleven here, they play their first test against Derby County. Because kickoff falls after our deadline, the result wasn't available yet — we'll have it tomorrow. A second test follows on July thirty-first against Greuther Fürth.

Two more personnel notes: the club has officially welcomed new signings Mio Backhaus, Rihito Yamamoto, and Keisuke Goto, but without naming any fees or contract dates — so we're deliberately not printing numbers there. And goalkeeper Noah Atubolu apparently wants to leave, though no specific club has been confirmed. The first competitive assignment of the new season is the cup, away at Fortuna Düsseldorf, in the window of August twenty-first through twenty-fourth.

The Bundesliga itself is still on hiatus. The new season doesn't start until August twenty-eighth, with Bayern against Stuttgart. In last season's final table, Bayern won the title comfortably with eighty-nine points, and Freiburg finished seventh. Heidenheim and St. Pauli were relegated directly, and Wolfsburg went down through the relegation playoff.

In Brazil, play has resumed after the World Cup break, and Palmeiras is pulling away: forty-four points from nineteen matches, only one loss, seven ahead of Flamengo. Flamengo, though, has a game in hand. One honest caveat about the table: the source sites don't show an update date, so this snapshot comes from somewhere around matchday nineteen or twenty.
