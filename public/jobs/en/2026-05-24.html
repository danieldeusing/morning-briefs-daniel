Fresh inflow through the agencies only picks up at the start of the week, so today the board shows the standing pipeline, swept more broadly than yesterday: sixty-three live-verified listings, sorted by a fit score of zero to one hundred against Daniel's CV. Nineteen of them carry fresh IDs, the rest are still-open high-fit roles.

At the top, everything stays as it was. In the freelance field, infoteam leads with a fit of eighty-nine — Java seventeen and twenty-one, Angular seventeen, Kafka and Kubernetes, exactly the Volkswagen VU3 stack, eighty to ninety percent remote out of Nuremberg.

On the employee side, the weekend's two bullseyes hold steady at eighty-eight. Aleph Alpha, Germany's frontier lab for enterprise AI out of Heidelberg, is looking for a senior Java backend developer for its industrial vertical — exactly Daniel's core stack of Java and Spring Boot, not Python research on language models. Right beside it, Camunda: fully remote in the EMEA region, agentic automation, Java and Spring meeting large language models and retrieval pipelines.

New in the upper field, iSAX climbs to eighty-six with a full stack of Java, React, TypeScript and Spring Boot, fully remote — the strongest new entry of the day, with several more iSAX slots following right behind. Alongside it, a dense band of CAES, both BridgingIT projects and a public-sector EGVP full-stack role, all high-remote between eighty-five and eighty-four.

In the niche, VARIUS remains the smart-energy hit at eighty-four, plus a Node-RED and MQTT project and a C-plus-plus OPC-UA role — Daniel's protocol premium at the application level, not the kernel. Three listings remain visible only for transparency: a Java project requiring a security clearance, a Brazilian domestic role, and a Bern role requiring relocation — all three ruled out for Daniel.
