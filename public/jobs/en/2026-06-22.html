Market: first rate decline since 2016 persists [1][2][6]

Behind the full board lies a cooling market. The Freelancer-Kompass 2026 from freelancermap (survey Nov. 2025–Feb. 2026, n≈5,400, plus >340,000 anonymized DACH profiles) measures an average rate of 103 €/h — the first decline since 2016 (down from 104 € the previous year). More important than the single euro is the sentiment: 49% report a worse pipeline than last year, 52% cite uncertain utilization as the biggest risk.

Pipeline / order book: How many project inquiries are coming in and waiting in the queue. Like the water level at a mill: plenty of flow and it grinds steadily; cut off the supply and you have to negotiate on rate just to keep the wheel turning.

Context for twiceD: the Java platform average across all experience levels is ~88 €/h per freelancermap (range 51–123 €/h across 14,330 Java profiles). Daniel's senior rates on the board (90–115 €/h, niche 110–140 €/h) sit firmly in the top third. The eight new listings today also signal that the pipeline is filling for Q3 — six contracts with long durations (12 to 60 months). In a soft market the right move is not to cut the rate but to lean into the niche (IoT/Automotive), where supply is thin and the premium holds.
