Public Tenders (TED EU)

Public contracts with documented mandatory criteria — which is why a genuine partner-gap analysis is possible here. twiceD is a solo/2-person setup; any threshold above that capacity requires a partner.

TED tender: When a German government agency wants to procure software worth more than roughly €220k, it must advertise EU-wide — through "Tenders Electronic Daily." Think of it as a public auction where the winner isn't the cheapest bidder but the one who demonstrably meets all requirements: the agency writes in hard minimums upfront (annual revenue, team size, references, certifications). Anyone who fails to meet even one of these cannot bid at all.

Python Services — Federal Administration ⚠️ Partner

The Federal Procurement Office of the BMI is awarding a framework contract for Python IT services for the wider federal administration (Lot 2). Maximum framework value €22,159,550, deadline Thu 02.07.2026, 11:30 CET. The stack (Python) fits twiceD's backend tooling profile — but no fewer than five mandatory criteria are far beyond solo capacity: ISO-9001 certification, at least €6M annual revenue in software development, 42 Python specialists per year, and three reference projects each worth at least €100k. This isn't a "one gap" case — it's a consortium case.

Partner provides Proof of minimum annual revenue €6M + ISO-9001 certificate + staffing capacity for 42 Python FTE/year + three federal/agency references ≥ €100k. twiceD could realistically participate only as a sub-contractor to a large prime bidder — not as the lead bidder.

AWplus — Web UI + PostgreSQL/PostGIS, BAF Langen ⚠️ Partner

The Federal Aviation Office (BAF) in Langen is tendering the further development of "AWplus" — facility protection under § 18a LuftVG: a web interface plus PostgreSQL/PostGIS database, hosted at ITZBund. Value €670,000, deadline Tue 21.07.2026, 11:00 CET (CPV 72261000/72240000). Stack-adjacent: web development + PostgreSQL are a direct CV match, PostGIS (geodata) is learnable — Java/Angular are not explicitly required. The gap is smaller than the BMI contract, but two mandatory criteria are missing.

Partner provides Proof of €800k revenue over three fiscal years (solo twiceD falls below) + 2 public-sector references of ≥ 100 person-days each (twiceD has no comprehensive agency references). At least 4 FTE and €2M/€1M liability insurance are additionally required — feasible as a partner model with an established public-sector service provider.

What the board is saying today

The contract market is sober and stable: 78 verified listings, of which 51 are freelance, and by far the largest cluster is Daniel's bread and butter — Java + Spring Boot + Angular, 100% remote. The top 7 (fit 85–90) are practically interchangeable: the same stack combination, the same Frankfurt/Munich end clients, the same 90–120 €/h band. Anyone pitching today has choice, not scarcity.

Two patterns stand out. First, unusually long durations: iSAX (41 months), BridgingIT (45), 1st solution (46), and Kinteo (47 months pure Angular) are not three-month gap-fillers but quasi-permanent positions via freelance contract — valuable for planning security but carrying concentration risk since they lock up capacity for a long time.

Second, the OAuth2/Security premium. The top listing (iSAX, fit 90) explicitly centers on auth — OAuth2, FIDO2, AWS Cognito — and that's Daniel's strongest differentiator. Alongside it: the SINC JOSE/JWT crypto SDK (fit 85) and Camunda's Identity role. Where security is the core, rates are negotiable upward, not downward.

Freelance market: first dip since 2016

The Freelancer Compass 2026 from freelancermap (survey 2025-11-17 – 2026-02-08, around 5,400 participants) delivers the market headline of the day: the average hourly rate of all DACH freelancers in 2026 stands at €103/h — down from €104/h in 2025. That sounds like noise, but it is the first decline since the study began in 2016; after years of steady growth, stagnation is showing for the first time.

For Daniel, the context matters more than the headline. First: the often-cited €94/h is the average for the category IT Development (Web/Mobile/Software) from the 2025 study — not a Java-specific figure. Daniel's target (€100/h+ for Java/Angular, 110–140 for niches) is clearly above this broad average, which today's board bands (90–130 €/h) confirm.

Second: Java is the second-most-requested skill on freelancermap, right behind SAP. Demand for exactly Daniel's stack therefore remains high — the rate dip is a market-wide effect, not a Java effect. Project volume has settled at around 90,000 projects (2020 levels, after the 2022 peak of ~132,000).

Average rate vs. sub-index: "€103/h" is like the average price of all cars in Germany — from economy to luxury sedan. "€94/h IT Development" is the average for one segment, and "Senior Java Remote" is a specific, well-paid model within it. Applying the overall average to a single senior-Java rate would be like pricing an S-Class against the average of all cars on the market.
