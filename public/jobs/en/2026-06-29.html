The core stack is now a buyer's market [1][2]

47 of 50 listings are Java/Spring/Angular freelance in the DACH region — and 16 score fit ≥ 83. That's not good news in the classical sense: when Daniel's bread-and-butter profile is posted by the dozen, availability is no longer a constraint that justifies a premium. The lever shifts from "can I deliver?" to "why me and not the other twelve?".

The cleanest hit is iSAX GmbH & Co. KG (Fit 90): Java/Angular, AWS, Microservices, 80% remote, 48 months duration — Frankfurt am Main.[3] Stack 40 of 45 plus near-perfect duration; this is the entry to dock on first. Behind it a dense row of interchangeable Java/Spring backend and fullstack roles (iSAX #2, teclift, RED, VARIUS, SINC, infoteam, HyPlus …), almost all 100% remote, all in the same researched 90–120 €/h band.

ANÜ (Arbeitnehmerüberlassung): A broker formally hires you and "loans" you to the end customer — like temp work for IT professionals. You're not a classic freelancer on your own account, but a fixed-term employee of the broker. Several listings (DOS, Computer Futures, ITech) run on this model — for a Brazilian GmbH director, a different tax and social insurance framework than a pure work-for-hire, so check before you sign.

Verdict: Volume there, differentiation tight. Hold 90 €/h floor on standard Java; oversupply carries no premiums. If you're pitching now, go for iSAX FFM (long duration = predictable) or straight into the niche below.

Premium lives in the IoT/Automotive niche

Two listings stand out from the commodity band, and both deserve the hot marker: Keil GmbH (Fit 86) seeks Java/Spring Boot for telemetry/IoT with MQTT, remote DACH, researched rate 100–130 €/h*.[4] And Computer Futures (Fit 83) has a Java role in the e-mobility/automotive space, 100% remote, also 100–130 €/h*.[5]

Why these are the only two entries where a markup is defensible: IoT protocol integration and automotive software are seldom posted in the DACH region, and Daniel has real references in the OEM space — the VW sales platform (VU3, Java/Spring/AWS, since 2021) and the GigE Vision Linux driver for Mercedes-Benz & Bosch (40 Gbit/s throughput). That's not "I can learn it," it's "I shipped it." Exactly there the 10–30 €/h premium over the standard Java rate holds.

MQTT: An extremely lean messaging protocol for devices with little power and bandwidth — think a radio that sends short codes instead of full sentences. In telemetry (vehicle telemetry) and IoT, MQTT ships sensor and status messages to a central broker, which forwards them to analytics systems.

Verdict: Rate negotiation recommended here, not floor defense. On both, bring the OEM references into the upper band (130 €/h) — the niche justifies it, the standard Java market doesn't.

Verdict: All four are stack-capable, but none is a recommendation — either residency conflict or rate below floor. Deliberately not pitched.
