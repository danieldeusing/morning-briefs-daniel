Market signal: VW decides today, the industry cuts broadly [2][3][4][5]

Volkswagen's supervisory board convenes today at 2:30pm in Wolfsburg. According to ad-hoc-news.de, a restructuring plan is up for discussion that could involve a reduction of up to 100,000 jobs worldwide as well as the closure of four plants.[3] A report from personalwirtschaft.de (citing Spiegel) specifically names Zwickau, Emden, Hannover, and Neckarsulm — staggered (Zwickau and Emden from 2031, Hannover 2032, Neckarsulm/Audi 2034), affecting around 40,000 positions; a Bild report is said to additionally cite 55,000 to 70,000 further positions beyond the already agreed cut of 50,000.[4] The figures vary considerably between sources (40,000 vs. 55,000–70,000 vs. "up to 100,000") — that's the usual pre-meeting noise ahead of a supervisory board session, not a confirmed single number. A final decision is not expected today; IG Metall and the works council have announced an action day for Wolfsburg, including a rally at the executive tower.

This isn't an isolated headline. A Horváth study from 06/30/2026 finds that 60% of surveyed German companies plan to cut staff by year-end; alongside VW, it names BMW (roughly 7,700 positions, margin forecast lowered to 1–3%, China weakness plus US tariffs) and British American Tobacco (5,500 positions cut worldwide, a further 3,500 outsourced by year-end, affecting Hamburg and Bamberg among others).[2] Overall, the study suggests roughly 100,000 jobs could be lost across industry — a figure that coincidentally matches the VW single-company number, but refers to a different (industry-wide) population.

The older counterpoint still holds, even if it's no longer fresh: per Bitkom (as of 02/13/2026, so about five months old), Germany still lacks more than 109,000 IT professionals — even as demand for highly qualified experts drops by nearly 20%.[5] Translated: the broad IT skills shortage persists structurally, but the demand curve at the top (exactly where Daniel's senior profile sits) is flattening. What this means for today's board: enterprise Java/Spring remains the most robust segment at the core of demand, while the industry around it — automotive foremost — is visibly cutting staff. Selection remains the lever, not the markup.

ISO 27001 / BSI C5: A certification that confirms a company has organized its IT security to a recognized standard — access rights, backups, incident response, all documented and signed off by an external auditor. Like a roadworthiness inspection for a car, just for information security. For a solo GmbH, the certificate is rarely a capability problem but a cost-and-lead-time one — an audit typically takes months and costs five figures, which makes it, alongside the minimum-revenue threshold, the most common partner trigger.
