Intriguing: the forward-deployed wave

Nine Intriguing entries, and they tell the same story: the AI companies are building out their forward-deployed teams across Europe. On top, JetBrains with a Principal Forward Deployed Engineer – AI-Native Software Development (Fit 82, Amsterdam/Berlin/London/Madrid/Munich/Prague/Warsaw, no salary range published). Behind it, Cohere (FDE Agentic Platform, UK/Europe remote), Cognition — the makers of Devin — with a Partner Deployed Engineer Europe, and Cursor/Anysphere with an AI Adoption Engineer EMEA. Plus n8n (Sr AI Engineer, Remote Europe) and Owkin.

Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE): a developer who sits half at their own employer, half at the client, fitting the employer's product into the client's systems — half developer, half consultant. At AI companies that means getting the company's own agentic tools running at the enterprise customer, rather than researching new models.

The common denominator isn't foundation-model research but the applied side — exactly the field the employee track marks as a "yes," and one that connects to Daniel's agentic-coding experience (Claude Code, MCP). Salary ranges are mostly missing from the purely frontier-lab roles; their Fit score is deliberately carried by the intrigue component, not by the rate match.

Project Tenders (TED EU)

The TED search across CPV 72*, place of performance Germany, published from July 1, 2026 onward, returned 420 notices (still 87 in the narrower window from July 17 onward). Six stack-adjacent DE procedures were reviewed with full requirement text. Result up front: no direct hit today. Three are listed below as ⚠️ Partner, three fall out — the path to the first three doesn't run through an application, but through a bidder who already clears the formal threshold.

TED & eligibility criteria: TED (Tenders Electronic Daily) is the EU-wide official gazette where public authorities publish their contracts — a bulletin board where the state pins up what it wants built. Anyone who wants to bid has to provide eligibility evidence up front: minimum revenue, reference projects, certificates, named staff. It's exactly these formal hurdles — not the code — that a solo operation trips over.
