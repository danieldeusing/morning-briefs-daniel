Anyone who has worked with it can fit AI agents into existing software rather than merely operating them.

The market is growing — but the IT niche is cooling

The cleanest market signal of the day comes from freelance.de's Q2 2026 market report (Jul 3, 2026). The headline figure is positive: around 14,700 projects were posted in Q2, +9.6% versus the same quarter last year; over the first half of the year the market is up +5.7% YoY. So the freelance market as a whole isn't shrinking — quite the opposite.

The second look is the important one: the share of the IT/software category in total project volume fell from 33.4% (Q1) to 27.9% (Q2) — roughly −21% within the category, while other fields (engineering, among others) gained relative ground. IT remains the largest single category but is losing share. For Daniel that means: not less demand overall, but a market where rate negotiation matters more than sheer contract availability.

Category share: not "how many IT projects exist," but "what percentage of all posted projects is IT." If the overall pie grows, the absolute number of IT projects can stay flat while its slice of the pie shrinks — which is exactly what happened here. A share decline is a softer warning sign than an absolute drop.

On top of that, the structural shift that helps explain the share loss: AI is now an everyday tool rather than a special topic. freelance.de's Freelancer Study 2026 (fieldwork Jan 27–Mar 2, 2026, > 3,300 respondents) measures 53% daily AI use among freelancers — in 2024 it was just 19%. Separately, freelancermap's Kompass reporting cites a rise in AI-project postings of "over 530%" from 2023 to 2025 (industry-wide, not DE-specific — included here only as a rough directional note, from a search snippet, not opened as a primary source). The WeMatch mandate above is the local version of the same trend.

Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE): a developer who sits half at their own employer, half at the client, fitting the company's product into the client's systems — half developer, half consultant. At AI companies that means getting the firm's own agent tools running at the enterprise customer, rather than researching new models.

The common thread isn't foundation-model research but the applied side — exactly the field the Employee track flags as a "yes" and that connects to Daniel's agentic-coding experience. TED & eligibility criteria: TED (Tenders Electronic Daily) is the EU-wide official journal where government bodies publicly post their contracts — a bulletin board where the state pins up what it wants built.
