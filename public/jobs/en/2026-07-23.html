Zurich provides the counter-example: ITech Consult is looking for a Java/Kotlin/Spring Boot/Angular fullstack developer at CHF 140–180/h* — nominally the highest band on the board. Even so, the entry lands at only fit 72, because the role is 30% remote and mostly takes place on-site in Zurich: full rate points (15), but only 8 of 20 on the location axis. That's exactly what the fit score is for — it weighs the rate against the reality of living in Ribeirão Claro, rather than just ranking the biggest number first.

The practical takeaway for negotiation: the benchmark bands in the listings are researched, not offered. Anyone opening at €100/h+ is arguing against a DACH median of €95/h — achievable with a senior profile and niche exposure (IoT, automotive, healthcare/TI), but not a given in the standard Java segment.

Three listings that would be a waste of time

The board carries three entries today that formally fit and are still excluded — they're kept in at a low score so the gap stays visible, not as a recommendation. nemensis AG is looking for a Java/Spring Boot/Camunda fullstack developer (Düsseldorf, 90% remote) — stack share 30, but an SÜ2 security clearance with mandatory German residency zeroes out location and track (fit 43). Same story at Levaro (AWS/Kubernetes/GitOps, 100% remote, SÜ2, fit 37). And Generic Network requires an active SC clearance plus UK residency for a UK-remote DevOps contract (fit 21).

SÜ2 / SC clearance: government reliability checks for work on classified material. Like a building pass that isn't issued by the client but by the authorities — and that requires a vetted residence and life history in the country. For someone based in Brazil, that's not a point to negotiate but a closed door: the process itself presupposes domestic residency.

Rule of thumb for the board: as soon as "SÜ," "VS-NfD," "Sicherheitsüberprüfung," or "active SC/DV clearance" appears in the text, the contract is done regardless of stack. Three of 98 today — a normal share.

The TED search across the IT CPVs (72000000/72200000/72220000/72240000/72260000/72500000/72600000, plus 48000000, place of performance Germany, published from Jul 17, 2026) returned 230 notices. Four stack-relevant tenders were checked with the full requirements text (eForm XML) — and one of them is the cleanest solo candidate in weeks.

Eligibility criteria in the eForm: before a public body even reads a bid, it checks the bidder's eligibility — minimum revenue, staff count, reference projects, insurance sums. These hurdles sit machine-readably in the eForm XML of the notice. A procedure that carries no such criteria there is, for a one-person operation, the difference between "allowed to bid" and "fails before the first line of code."
