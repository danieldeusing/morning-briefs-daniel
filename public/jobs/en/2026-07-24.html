The number Daniel is actually negotiating against

The figure haunting every freelancer discussion this year is €103/h. It comes from freelancermap's Freelancer Compass 2026, surveyed between Nov 17, 2025 and Feb 8, 2026 among roughly 5,400 freelancers — and it's an average across all disciplines.[1] For Daniel, that's the wrong anchor. The same source breaks down the IT basket: €95/h DACH average for IT freelancers, topped by SAP and ERP specialists at €120, Data & Analytics at €100 — and at the very bottom, literally named the "low end," software and web development at €90.[2]

A note on sourcing — it differs markedly between these two figures: the headline value of €103/h across all disciplines could be independently cross-checked. The breakdown by discipline — €95 IT overall, €90 software/web development, €100 Data & Analytics, €120 SAP/ERP — on the other hand appears verbatim only in freelancermap's own blog: it's the portal's proprietary analysis, and no independent confirmation of these sub-figures could be found today. They're useful as a directional signal and as a negotiating argument, not as a hard market price. If someone quotes the €90 figure against you in a negotiation, that's exactly the counter to raise.

This isn't a footnote, it's the negotiating baseline. Walking into a conversation with "the market pays 103" cites a figure that folds in SAP consulting and interim management. The honest line is: Daniel's segment is the cheapest in the IT basket, and anything above €90/h is already a premium — justifiable through seniority, niche (IoT, automotive, telematics infrastructure), or scope of responsibility, but not through an average.

The reality check against today's board is correspondingly sober. Of 107 entries, only five carry a rate the provider named itself, and of those only two are freelance: Right People Group pays €70/h for an Angular 21 frontend developer out of Munich (100% remote, Aug 1–Dec 31) — 22% below the segment average, which is why the listing scores only Fit 64 despite a clean stack and a full remote rating. And ProTIP ProjektService names €680/day (≈ €85/h) for Python/FastAPI/Go in the DB Systel environment. Both under 90. The €90–120 bands attached to nearly every listing on the board are researched benchmarks — what's actually posted today falls short of them.

Why €90/h isn't "three times a salaried wage": Indeed lists an average base salary of €67,806/year for salaried Java developers in Germany (512 salary reports, page as of Jul 19, 2026).[3] At €90/h, that works out to 754 billed hours — roughly 19 fully invoiced weeks. Sounds like a 2.7x multiple, but it isn't one: everything above that goes toward health and retirement provision, unpaid leave, gaps between projects while acquiring new work, liability insurance, and running the GmbH. The hourly rate isn't a salary substitute — it's the price including all the risks an employer otherwise carries.
