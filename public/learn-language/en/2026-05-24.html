Politics, Law & Health Vocabulary: to vote, the verdict, get well soon

Sunday is the day of the great institutions — the ballot box, the courtroom and the waiting room. Today thirty-six words from politics, justice and health, twelve of them verbs with full conjugation, each word with at least two parallel example sentences across all four languages. Three refreshers return fittingly under the health theme (impfen, die Spritze, das Fieber). Today's payoff sits in four bundles: the Spanish stem-changing verbs (gobernar e→ie, aprobar o→ue, defender e→ie), the triply irregular sterben/morir, the nasty false friends (Gift ≠ gift, Gericht = justice and dish, Rezept = cooking recipe and prescription) and no fewer than two gender jumps (die Partei → o partido, der Zeuge → a testemunha).

  • FALSE FRIEND das Gift (DE) ≠ gift (EN) — DE Gift is the toxin (PT veneno, ES veneno). EN gift means present. One word, opposite world.
  • FALSE FRIEND das Gericht (DE) means BOTH — the courtroom and the dish (yesterday o prato/el platillo). PT/ES separate them cleanly: o tribunal vs. o prato.
  • FALSE FRIEND das Rezept (DE) is a cooking recipe and a medical prescription (PT receita, ES receta too). EN recipe (cooking) ≠ receipt (proof of payment).
  • GENUS die Partei (DE f) → o partido / el partido (m) — and die Regierung (f) → o governo / el gobierno (m). The gender jumps in translation.
  • GENDER der Zeuge (DE m) → a testemunha (PT) — in Portuguese always feminine, even when the witness is a man. ES stays el testigo (m).
  • CONJUGATION ES stem changes: gobernar (yo gobierno) e→ie, aprobar (yo apruebo) o→ue, defender (yo defiendo) e→ie — but nosotros stays regular (gobernamos, aprobamos, defendemos).
  • CONJUGATION sterben is triply tricky: DE e→i + "sein" (ich bin gestorben), ES morir o→ue with murió (o→u) and muramos, PT participle morto.
  • CONJUGATION unterschreiben & verschreiben are strong in DE — like schreiben: unterschrieb / verschrieb, unterschrieben / verschrieben. PT/ES, by contrast, stay regular.
  • BR ≠ MX vacinar (BR) vs. vacunar (MX) — one vowel separates the vaccinating. And the health post: o posto de saúde (BR, SUS) vs. el centro de salud (MX).
  • IDIOM in den sauren Apfel beissen = PT engolir o sapo ("swallow the frog") = ES hacer de tripas corazón. Having to do something unpleasant.
  • PHRASE Gute Besserung! → PT Melhoras! · ES (MX) ¡Que te mejores!. And when someone sneezes: Gesundheit! → Saúde! / ¡Salud!.
  • REVIEW three health words return: impfen (vacinar/vacunar), die Spritze (a injeção/la inyección), das Fieber (a febre/la fiebre).

Learning tip With thirty-six words: take the twelve verbs first, the nouns almost follow on their own in a second pass. Three patterns to take away. First, the Spanish stem changes: gobernar (yo gobierno) and defender (yo defiendo) jump e→ie, aprobar (yo apruebo) jumps o→ue — but "nosotros" stays regular throughout (gobernamos, defendemos, aprobamos), and in the preterite the diphthong disappears again (goberné, defendí, aprobé). Second, the triply irregular sterben: in German strong with e→i (du stirbst) and with "sein" (ich bin gestorben, not "habe"); in Spanish morir o→ue (muero), in the 3rd-person preterite even o→u (murió, murieron) and in the subjunctive "nosotros" muramos; in Portuguese morrer is regular, only the participle morto is irregular. Third, the two gender jumps: die Partei and die Regierung are feminine in German but become o partido / o governo (masculine); conversely der Zeuge is masculine, but a testemunha is always feminine in Portuguese — even when the witness is a man. And remember the three false friends: Gift is a toxin (EN gift = present!), Gericht is a courtroom and a dish, Rezept is a cooking recipe and a medical prescription. It's Sunday, the twenty-fourth of May — and today is the day of the great institutions in the languages brief: the ballot box, the courtroom and the waiting room. Thirty-six words from politics, justice and health, twelve of them verbs with full conjugation. Every word has at least two example sentences, parallel in German, English, Brazilian Portuguese and Mexican Spanish.

The most important pattern first: the Spanish stem-changing verbs. To govern is gobernar in Spanish — and under stress the vowel e jumps to ie: yo gobierno. Likewise defender, to defend: yo defiendo. And aprobar, to pass a law, jumps o to ue: yo apruebo. But as soon as the stress moves away, the stem settles down again: nosotros gobernamos, defendemos, aprobamos. And in the preterite the diphthong has disappeared entirely: goberné, defendí, aprobé. In Portuguese, by contrast, governar and aprovar are completely regular.

The second pattern is the verb to die — triply irregular at once. In German it is strong, with a vowel change: ich sterbe, but du stirbst, er stirbt; in the preterite starb; and in the perfect with "sein", not with "haben": ich bin gestorben. In Spanish morir is o→ue: yo muero, tú mueres — but in the third person of the preterite it jumps further to u: él murió, ellos murieron, and in the subjunctive the we-form is muramos. In Portuguese morrer stays regular, only the participle morto is irregular. And unterschreiben and verschreiben are strong in German like schreiben: unterschrieb, verschrieb, unterschrieben, verschrieben.

Then the two gender jumps that easily trip you up in translation. Die Partei is feminine in German, but becomes masculine in Portuguese and Spanish: o partido, el partido. Likewise die Regierung: o governo, el gobierno. Conversely der Zeuge is masculine in German — but in Portuguese it is a testemunha and always feminine, even when the witness is a man. In Spanish it stays el testigo, masculine.

With the false friends, special care is needed today — three of them. The German Gift does not mean a present, like the English gift, but a toxin: in Portuguese and Spanish o veneno, el veneno. Second: Gericht in German means both the courtroom — o tribunal — and the dish we learned yesterday as o prato and el platillo. And third, Rezept: in German as in Portuguese and Spanish, both the cooking recipe and the medical prescription — receita, receta. But in English, careful: recipe is the cooking recipe, while receipt is the proof of payment.

Die Spritze, a injeção and la inyección. And das Fieber, a febre and la fiebre. Plus a typically Brazilian word: the public health center is called o posto de saúde in Brazil, the SUS unit, and el centro de salud in Mexico.

Finally, two expressions for the sickbed and for everyday life. When someone is ill, you wish them a good recovery — in Portuguese melhoras, in Mexican Spanish que te mejores. And when you have to accept something unpleasant, in German that is in den sauren Apfel beissen, in Brazilian Portuguese engolir o sapo, to swallow the frog, and in Mexican Spanish hacer de tripas corazón.
