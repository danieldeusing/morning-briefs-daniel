Festa Junina Cuisine: order & cook at the Arraial

Saturday, June 6th — this week's theme is food, cooking, restaurants, and across Brazil the Festa Junina season is in full swing (peak days are June 13th, 24th, and 29th). Last month we covered restaurant basics (ordering, serving, the menu) and cooking fundamentals (baking, grilling, seasoning). Today we go one step further: the signature Junina dishes and the cooking techniques around them. You knead dough for pamonha, soak corn for canjica, simmer quentão, and order pé de moleque at the Arraial stand. 33 entries, 13 verbs with full conjugations, three reviews from May. blablacar.com.br · tudogostoso.com.br

  • FALSE FRIEND PT/EN „a sobremesa" ≠ DE „Sommer" — a sobremesa (PT) means "dessert," not "summer meadow"; and EN dessert (two s's) ≠ desert (the wasteland).
  • FALSE FRIEND DE „das Gericht" — means both "a dish/meal" (PT o prato) and "court" as in law court (PT o tribunal). Figure it out from context.
  • IDIOM BR „descascar o abacaxi" = "to solve a thorny problem," literally "peel the pineapple" — pineapples are tedious to peel.
  • BR-PT a pamonha / a canjica / o quentão — the classic Junina trio: corn dough steamed in a corn husk, sweet corn cream with coconut, and the hot spiced drink with cachaça (alcohol-free version: with grape or apple juice).
  • BR-PT o milho verde — "green corn" = fresh corn on the cob, the foundation of nearly all Junina dishes; in Mexico you say el elote.
  • CONJUGATION ES derretir (melt) is e→i: yo derrito, él derritió, subj. derrita — also nosotros shifts: derritamos.
  • CONJUGATION ES tostar (roast) is o→ue: yo tuesto, él tuesta — but nosotros stays with o (tostamos). ES calentar (heat) is e→ie: yo caliento, nosotros calentamos.
  • CONJUGATION PT refogar (sauté/braise) shows g→gu before e: eu refoguei, que eu refogue — never *refogei.
  • CONJUGATION ES enfriar / PT—watch out esfriar: ES needs the í-accent (yo enfrío, él enfría); BR-PT does NOT (eu esfrio, no accent).
  • CONJUGATION DE melt & burn are strong and take "be" in the perfect: is melted, is burned — never "has".
  • PHRASE PT „o que tem de comer?" = "What's there to eat?" — the sentence you start with at the Arraial stand.

It's Saturday, June 6th, and this week's topic is food, cooking, and restaurants. Across Brazil the Festa Junina season is in full swing, with the peak celebrations on June 13th, 24th, and 29th. Today's vocabulary covers the signature Junina dishes and the cooking techniques around them. Thirty-three entries, thirteen verbs with full conjugations, three reviews from May.

The three words you'll hear at every Festa are pamonha, canjica, and quentão. Pamonha is corn dough cooked in its own corn husk. Canjica is a sweet corn cream with coconut milk and cinnamon; in the northeast they call it mungunzá. And quentão is the hot spiced drink with cachaça, sugar cane liquor. For the kids there's a version without alcohol, made with grape or apple juice.

With the verbs, pay special attention to the Spanish side first. To melt is derretir and it's an e-to-i verb: I melt, he melted. To roast is tostar and it switches o to ue: I toast, but we toast stays with tostamos. And to heat is calentar with e to ie: I heat, we heat.

A Portuguese trap hides in the verb refogar, meaning to sauté or braise. In the first person past a u sneaks in: I browned, not refogei. Same in the subjunctive: that I brown. That's just spelling, to keep the g hard.

On the German side there are two verbs that take "be" in the perfect instead of "have," because they describe a change of state: the butter is melted, the rice is burned. Never "has melted" or "has burned" in the intransitive sense.

Also watch for a fine distinction in to cool down. In Spanish enfriar needs an accent on the i: I cool, he cools. In Brazilian Portuguese esfriar has no accent: I cool, with no accent at all.

Today's practice assignment: go to a nearby Arraial and order in Portuguese. Ask: o que tem de comer aqui? What is there to eat here? Then: I want one sweet pamonha and one quentão without alcohol, please.
