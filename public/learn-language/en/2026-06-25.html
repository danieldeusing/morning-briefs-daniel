School Vocabulary: to learn, to read, the exam

Wednesday, June 25 — weekly theme Family & Daily Life, today's focus: School, Learning & Children's Education. The exact vocabulary you need to talk about your children's schooling in the Norte Pioneiro: attending a parents' evening, discussing homework, telling the professora in PT-BR that your child is curious and attentive. 32 new entries, 14 verbs with full conjugation, 3 reviews from the Organization Day of June 3 (erledigen, sich kümmern, der Schulausflug).

  • FALSE FRIEND DE Gymnasium ≠ gym — the German Gymnasium is the academic secondary school (university-track). EN gymnasium, PT ginásio, ES gimnasio = sports hall / fitness center. "Mein Sohn geht aufs Gymnasium" translates to PT vai para o colégio / o ensino médio, ES va al bachillerato — never ginásio/gimnasio.
  • FALSE FRIEND DE Note = school grade, but EN note = a written note. The school grade is EN grade, PT nota (a match!), ES-MX calificación. The musical note is the same in all four languages — context decides.
  • CONJUGATION DE lesen is a strong verb with e→ie in the present: ich lese, aber du liest / er liest; simple past las, perfect habe gelesen. PT ler is equally irregular: eu leio, você lê, nós lemos.
  • CONJUGATION DE schreiben strong, ei–ie–ie: schreibe / schrieb / habe geschrieben. ES escribir has an irregular past participle: escrito (not *escribido).
  • CONJUGATION DE helfen strong with e→i: ich helfe, aber du hilfst / er hilft; simple past half, perfect habe geholfen. PT ajudar / ES ayudar are regular.
  • CONJUGATION DE vergessen strong with e→i: du vergisst / er vergisst; simple past vergaß. PT esquecer has the spelling shift c→ç before a/o: eu esqueço, que eu esqueça — preserving the soft ç sound.
  • CONJUGATION PT/ES explicar & praticar/practicar carry the c→qu shift before e: eu expliquei / pratiquei, yo expliqué / practiqué, subjunctive explique / pratique / practique. Preserves the /k/ sound.
  • GENDER das Heft (n), die Schultasche (f), der Schüler (m) — three genders. PT: o caderno (m), a mochila (f), o aluno (m); ES-MX: el cuaderno, la mochila, el alumno.
  • BR-PT dever de casa / tarefa de casa — Brazilians use both for "homework"; dever de casa is the more common spoken form. ES-MX: la tarea.
  • BR-PT sala de aula (classroom), a prova (exam), o boletim (report card), o recreio (recess). Teacher = o professor / a professora; Brazilians address their teachers respectfully as "professora".
  • ES-MX calificación, pizarrón, salón de clases — Mexico diverges: school grade = calificación (not nota), board = pizarrón (not pizarra), classroom = salón de clases (not aula). Teacher = el maestro (informal) / el profesor (formal).
  • PHRASE Darf ich auf die Toilette? = Posso ir ao banheiro? (BR-PT: banheiro, not casa de banho) = ¿Puedo ir al baño? (ES-MX: baño, not servicio). The classroom classic for every schoolchild.

Vocabulary: School Verbs

14 verbs with full conjugation · 2 reviews marked · DE · EN · PT-BR · ES-MX

Conjugating: adapting a verb to match the person and the tense — like tailoring a garment to fit whoever wears it. "Ich lese, du liest, wir lasen." Strong verb (DE): the stem vowel changes (lesen → las → gelesen) instead of just adding an ending. Spelling shift (PT/ES): the spelling adjusts to keep the sound consistent — explicar becomes expliquei because a "c" before "e" would otherwise go soft.

It's Wednesday, June 25. This week's theme is Family and Daily Life, and today's focus is school — learning, teaching, and children's education. This is the exact vocabulary you need to talk about your children's schooling in the Norte Pioneiro: discussing a parents' evening, checking homework, or telling the teacher in Brazilian Portuguese that your child is curious and attentive.

Today's brief has thirty-two new entries and fourteen verbs fully conjugated across all four languages. It also includes three reviews from the Organization Day on June third: erledigen, sich kümmern, and der Schulausflug. All words that tie directly into today's school theme.

Watch out for a handful of strong German verbs today. Lesen changes its vowel in the present tense: ich lese, but du liest and er liest; simple past ich las, perfect ich habe gelesen. Helfen works the same way: ich helfe, but du hilfst and er hilft, simple past half. And vergessen: du vergisst, er vergisst, simple past vergaß.

In Portuguese and Spanish, explicar and praticar hide a spelling rule. Before an e, the c becomes qu to keep the hard sound. So eu expliquei, eu pratiquei, and in Spanish yo expliqué, yo practiqué. Never write expliquei with a soft c.

The Portuguese word for forgetting, esquecer, works the opposite way: before a and o, the c picks up a cedilla and becomes ç. Eu esqueço, and in the subjunctive que eu esqueça. That preserves the soft sound.

Two false friends for today. The key one: the German Gymnasium is the academic secondary school, the path to the Abitur. But the similar-sounding word in English, Portuguese, and Spanish means a sports hall: gym, ginásio, gimnasio. If your child attends a Gymnasium, you say o colégio in Portuguese and el bachillerato in Spanish. And the German word Note means a school grade, not a written note; the grade in English is grade, in Mexican Spanish calificación.

A few Brazilian particulars worth knowing. Homework in everyday speech is dever de casa, the classroom is a sala de aula, the report card is o boletim, and you address the teacher respectfully as professora. And the classroom classic every schoolchild needs: Posso ir ao banheiro? In Mexican Spanish it's baño, not servicio.
