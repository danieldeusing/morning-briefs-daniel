Vocabulary: Invoice — faturar, the balance, eu cobro

Monday, June 29th — this week's topic is Business & Finance; today Part VI: Invoices, Payment Methods & Growth. The money vocabulary every freelancer in Brazil uses daily: how to charge a client (cobrar, faturar), issue a nota fiscal, get paid via Pix or boleto, check your balance, and watch your revenue grow. 33 entries, 12 verbs with full conjugation — including the notorious spelling traps paguei (g→gu) and cresço (c→ç) — plus 3 refreshers from the investment Monday on June 15th (invest, yield, earn).

  • FALSE FRIEND PT lucro ≠ EN luck — lucro (PT) means "profit," not "luck." EN luck = DE Glück / PT sorte / ES suerte. The difference matters at month-end.
  • FALSE FRIEND ES cobrar ≠ "cover" — cobrar means "to charge / collect" (money received), not "cubrir" (cover/hide). El médico me cobró 500 pesos = "the doctor charged me 500."
  • BR-PT nota fiscal (NF-e) — Brazil requires an electronic tax invoice called nota fiscal for every sale, not just "fatura." The fatura is the bundled statement (like a credit-card bill); the NF-e is the official receipt per transaction.
  • BR-PT Pix · boleto · parcelar — in Brazil you pay via Pix (instant bank transfer, free), boleto bancário (bank payment slip), or you can parcelar (split into installments — 12x interest-free). Three deeply Brazilian concepts.
  • Conjugation PT/ES pagar → paguei / pagué — spelling trap: the 1st person past tense adds a u to keep the g hard (g→gu). Never *pagei / *pagé.
  • Conjugation PT crescer → eu cresço — before o/a, c→ç: eu cresço, que eu cresça. ES crecer → yo crezco (c→zc). Both only in 1st person present + subjunctive.
  • Conjugation PT investir → eu invisto — stem vowel shift e→i only in 1st person present and full subjunctive: invisto, que eu invista, invistamos. Você investe stays with e.
  • Conjugation ES invertir → yo invierto / él invirtió — double shift: e→ie in present (invierto, but nosotros invertimos), e→i in 3rd person past (invirtió, invirtieron).
  • Conjugation DE wachsen → ich bin gewachsen — strong motion/state verb, perfect with sein, never *habe gewachsen. Ablaut a-u-a: ich wachse → wuchs → bin gewachsen, umlaut du wächst.
  • Conjugation PT negociar → eu negocio (NOT *eu negocia) — 1st person singular ends in -io: eu negocio, você negocia. Common beginner error in BR-PT.
  • Gender der Saldo (m), die Einnahme (f), das Einkommen (n) — three genders. PT: o saldo (m), a receita (f), o rendimento (m); ES-MX: el saldo (m), el ingreso (m), el rendimiento (m). Don't guess gender.
  • Phrase estar no vermelho (PT) = "in the red" = ES estar en números rojos. The opposite: estar no azul / no verde — in the black.

Vocabulary: Computing, Charging & Paying

12 verbs with full conjugation + 3 refreshers · DE · EN · PT-BR · ES-MX

Conjugate: adapt a verb to person and tense — like tailoring a shirt to fit ("I pay, you pay, I paid"). Spelling shift (PT/ES): spelling adjusts to sound. With pagar, an inserted u keeps the g hard (paguei / pagué); with crescer, before o the c becomes ç (cresço), and in Spanish c becomes zc (crezco). Stem-vowel shift: with investir, stressed e→i (invisto); with invertir, e→ie (invierto) — but with nós/nosotros the stem stays bare (investimos, invertimos). Perfect with "to be" (DE): motion and growth verbs form the past with sein instead of haben — ich bin gewachsen, never *ich habe gewachsen.

It's Monday, June twenty-ninth. This week's topic is business and finance, and today focuses on what every freelancer in Brazil faces daily: writing invoices, collecting money, and watching revenue grow.

The money verbs take center stage. Cobrar means to collect or bill — careful, it has nothing to do with covering. Pagar means to pay, and here's the first classic stumbling block: in the past, first person, Portuguese says pa-guei and Spanish says pa-gué, with an inserted u to keep the g hard. Never pagei or pagé.

Growth gets trickier. Crescer in Portuguese shifts to cresço in the I form, with cedilla, and in Spanish crecer becomes crezco in the yo form. Investir gets a vowel shift in Portuguese: the e becomes i, so eu invisto. In Spanish, invertir shifts e to ie, so yo invierto, but in the third person past it shifts to i, so él invirtió.

On the German side, the key verb is wachsen. It's strong and takes sein for the perfect, not haben. It's ich bin gewachsen — never ich habe gewachsen. That's the mistake nearly every learner makes first.

Add Brazil's unique payment ecosystem. The nota fiscal is the official electronic invoice required for every sale in Brazil. Payment flows through Pix, the central bank's free instant transfer, or boleto, a payment slip with a barcode. And almost everything can be parcelado, split into installments, often twelve interest-free.

Three words return from June fifteenth to refresh: investieren, die Rendite, verdienen. They fit perfectly with today's money theme and deepen the vocabulary from investment Monday.

Tomorrow it's all software and technology. Until then: paguei with u, cresço with cedilla, and ich bin gewachsen with sein.
