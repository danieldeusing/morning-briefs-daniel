Antonelli wins, Russell breaks down — engine failure from the lead, fourth win in a row

What a cruel end to a perfect weekend. George Russell had done everything right in Montreal — sprint win, pole, leading the main race — yet on lap 30 his power unit let him down: engine failure, retirement from the lead. The beneficiary was his own teammate Kimi Antonelli, who won the Canadian Grand Prix — his fourth win in a row (China, Japan, Miami, Canada). The 19-year-old blows the championship lead open from 18 to 43 points. Behind him, a superb Lewis Hamilton charged up from P5 and passed Max Verstappen on lap 62 for P2; Verstappen salvaged Red Bull's best result of the season in third. McLaren had a disaster: a strategy misfire on intermediates, Norris retirement (gearbox), Piastri only P11 — zero points. Gabriel Bortoleto was 13th again in the Audi. Next up: Monaco Grand Prix, 5–7 June.

Formula 1 · Canadian Grand Prix

Antonelli inherits the win — and makes it his fourth in a row

It was the race George Russell should have won — and that Kimi Antonelli won. After a thrilling intra-Mercedes duel in the early stages, in which both Silver Arrows passed each other repeatedly, touched once (the stewards saw no reason to investigate) and both locked up, the decision came from outside on lap 30: Russell's power unit failed and he rolled to a stop from the lead. Antonelli took over and drove a controlled win home — +10.7 seconds ahead of Hamilton.

With that, Andrea Kimi Antonelli, 19, has now won four Grands Prix in a row (China, Japan, Miami, Canada) and stands on 131 points. His championship lead has exploded from 18 to 43 points — the largest of the young season. "It was a really fun battle with George, we were pretty much on the limit," he said after the race. What began as Russell's weekend ends as Antonelli's show of force.

Russell's nightmare: from the perfect Saturday to a zero

No driver loses more this weekend than George Russell. He had won the sprint, taken pole and was comfortably leading the main race — until the power unit gave up on lap 30 and forced him off the track. A Virtual Safety Car was deployed for the recovery. Instead of a potential double blow (win plus the championship lead within reach), Russell scores a zero — and his deficit to Antonelli grew from 18 to 43 points in a single afternoon.

Power unit: the complete drivetrain of an F1 car — the combustion engine plus the hybrid-electric parts as one assembly. 2026 is the big regulation-reset year with all-new engines; the technology is young and not yet fully proven, which makes failures like Russell's more likely this season than usual. Like a brand-new car model in its first production year: fast, but not yet fully road-tested.
ESPN race report · Sky Sports

Hamilton hits his stride — P2 fought from Verstappen

The quiet winner is Lewis Hamilton. From grid slot five the Ferrari driver worked his way forward and fought a late duel with Max Verstappen for second place. On lap 62 it happened: Hamilton passed the four-time world champion around the outside of Turn 1 and secured P2 — Ferrari's best result of the season. Verstappen at least salvaged Red Bull's strongest Sunday of the so-far disappointing reset year in third.

Behind the podium, Leclerc finished fourth, giving Ferrari a double strike that moved them past McLaren in the constructors' championship. Isack Hadjar (Red Bull) was fifth, and Franco Colapinto surprised with P6 for Alpine, doubling his season points tally to 15.

Sky Sports Hamilton move · formula1.com lowdown

McLaren's strategy disaster: zero points, Norris out

While Mercedes and Ferrari cleaned up, McLaren had a jet-black day. Both cars started on intermediates in mixed conditions — and then had to switch early to slicks as the track dried. The strategy did not pay off: Lando Norris, the reigning world champion, retired with a gearbox failure, and Oscar Piastri finished only eleventh, just outside the points. For the defending champion's team that means zero points — and dropping behind Ferrari in the constructors' championship.

Intermediates vs. slicks: slicks are smooth dry tyres for maximum grip on a dry track; intermediates have a tread that clears water — good on a damp but not flooded surface. In changing weather the timing of the tyre change is everything: stay on inters while the track dries and you lose seconds per lap; switch to slicks too early and you slide around in the wet.

Bortoleto: P13 again, Audi still in no-man's-land

Especially relevant for Daniel: Gabriel Bortoleto, the first Brazilian full-time driver since Felipe Massa, ended his mixed weekend in 13th — again behind Audi teammate Nico Hülkenberg (P12). Both Audis came home two laps down and outside the points; their chosen intermediate gamble did not pay off here either. Bortoleto stays on 2 championship points in 15th. In Monaco, where one good qualifying lap is what counts, he gets his next chance — the tight street circuit forgives the Audis' pace deficit more readily than a fast track does.

Monaco preview: qualifying decides

After two weeks off, the action resumes on 5–7 June in Monaco — round 6 of 22, classic format without a sprint. The tight street circuit is the opposite of Montreal: overtaking is nearly impossible, which is why qualifying on Saturday (16:00 MESZ / 11:00 BRT) effectively decides the result. The race starts on Sunday, 07.06., at 15:00 MESZ / 10:00 BRT. For Mercedes it is a chance to cement the lead; for Russell, the first opportunity to make amends. And should it rain like it did in Canada, Monaco turns into a lottery.

Endurance · WEC, Le Mans & São Paulo

WEC: BMW still lead — standings frozen until Le Mans

In the FIA World Endurance Championship (WEC), two rounds have run. After Imola (Toyota win) and Spa (BMW one-two), BMW lead the manufacturers' standings — the Spa victory was BMW's first Hypercar win and its first overall win in global endurance racing since Le Mans 1999. In the drivers' standings the BMW crew René Rast / Robin Frijns (#20) lead on 35 points. With no round before Le Mans (13–14 June), the standings are unchanged since Spa.

Hypercar: the fastest class in endurance racing such as Le Mans. Manufacturers build prototypes under a cost-capped rulebook (LMH/LMDh), balanced via Balance of Performance — targeted weight and power adjustments, like a handicap in golf — so different concepts run at similar speeds.

Le Mans draws closer — and São Paulo after that

The next big endurance highlight is the 94th running of the 24 Hours of Le Mans in the race week of 10–14 June at the Circuit de la Sarthe. The race start is on Saturday, 13.06., at 16:00 MESZ / 11:00 BRT. Le Mans is the third WEC round actually run this season. New on the grid: the all-new Genesis Hypercar; the Le Mans weekend also runs a standalone Balance of Performance.

Especially interesting for Daniel: four weeks after Le Mans, on 12 July, the WEC visits with the 6 Hours of São Paulo at Interlagos — the only WEC home round in Brazil and therefore a must-cover date in this newsletter as it draws nearer.
