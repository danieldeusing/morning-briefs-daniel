MotoGP Mugello: Aprilia's home race, Márquez's return [1][2][3]

This weekend belongs to the motorcycles. At Mugello (May 29–31), the most compelling mix of the season collides: Aprilia, leading the championship with Marco Bezzecchi (142 pts) and Jorge Martín (127), races at home — and yet, oddly enough, has never won a MotoGP race there. Bezzecchi has not even podiumed at Mugello as a MotoGP rider. Martín, still sore from crashes in Barcelona, admits he feels "not 100%".[3]

The red counter-punch comes from Marc Márquez (Ducati Lenovo), back after double surgery. A highsider in the Le Mans sprint broke his fifth metatarsal; simultaneously, a loose screw from an old operation was pressing on his radial nerve. Both were fixed in a single procedure.[1] He missed Le Mans and Barcelona, returns Friday — provided Thursday's medical check clears him. Championship position: just P9, 85 points behind Bezzecchi.[2]

Double-surgery injury Highsider: a crash where the rear wheel loses grip, then suddenly finds it again — the rider is catapulted over the bike like a spring-loaded mechanism. Radial nerve: the nerve controlling thumb, index, and middle finger. Marc has carried a screw from a late-2019 shoulder-stabilization (Latarjet) surgery; the 2020 Jerez upper-arm crash bent it against the nerve over time, where it pressed chronically — now it's out.
Also sidelined Álex Márquez (Gresini Ducati) misses two races (Italy and Hungary) after Barcelona crash — neck vertebra fracture and right collarbone break. Michele Pirro, Ducati's Italian test driver, replaces him — fitting for a home GP.

Mercedes under siege: the brother war on the radio [5][6]

Mercedes has a high-class problem: two drivers with the fastest car who won't let each other have the championship. In Canada's qualifying and sprint, Antonelli and Russell collided — Antonelli demanded a penalty for his teammate over the radio, Wolff shut him down with a radio order to stop complaining.[5] In the race, Russell's engine failure on lap 30 defused the situation for now; he was no longer leading when he stopped, and jumped out furious, by all reports.

Wolff's pre-Monaco statement sounds like a crisis meeting: "The fight is on" — both are allowed to race for position — but if they lock wheels again like that, "we turn it down a notch".[6] Translation: team orders. For Daniel: unusual, since Mercedes didn't face this setup in recent years — two equal title contenders in the same car, plus a 19-year-old rookie with no second thoughts.

Team orders: when the team radios an instruction about who passes whom. Sounds unsporting, but it's standard once two drivers compete for the championship and the team wants to avoid both crashing out. Like when two siblings fight over the last pizza slice and Mom says "Antonia gets it first, it's her birthday."

ADUO: the hidden pressure valve — and the first ruling is coming [7][8]

The 2026 engine rule has a built-in safety valve most fans don't see — and it's about to activate: ADUO.

ADUO (Additional Development and Upgrade Opportunities): imagine several teams playing a game, but one has much better uniforms. To keep the game fair, the worse-equipped teams get to buy extra uniform upgrades. That's exactly what ADUO does with engines: if you're at least 2% down on power, you get an upgrade right; 4% down gets you two. It's meant to stop a Mercedes-2014 dominance from calcifying for five years.[7]

The twist: the first ruling comes within two weeks of the Canada GP — i.e., right now, possibly in force by Monaco. Red Bull team principal Laurent Mekies openly expects Honda/RBPT to breach the threshold and earn Red Bull upgrade rights.[8] That puts three things in play simultaneously: the FIA's tighter compression-testing from June 1 (cold plus 130 °C), the ADUO special upgrades, and Mercedes' internal reckoning on how much Antonelli-Russell sparring Wolff can tolerate before then.

Context If Red Bull does get ADUO AND the Mercedes measurement tightening clips some of their advantage, Monaco becomes the first test of the new power balance. Antonelli's 43-point WM lead over Russell stays intact — this is forward-looking, not retroactive.

Verstappen's future pledge — and what it really means [9][10]

For weeks, the four-time world champion threatened to quit F1 after 2026 — because of the hybrid-heavy engines. At the Canada weekend, the commitment came: "Unless something crazy happens — I can confirm I'm staying in Formula 1."[9] The trigger: the FIA has agreed in principle to shift the ICE/electric ratio back in the V6's favor for 2027 — toward 60/40.[10] What Verstappen did not confirm: staying at Red Bull. His contract runs through 2026; he wants to keep options open — including outside F1 (Nürburgring, Daytona).

Brazil watch: Colapinto delivers, Bortoleto seethes [12][13]

For Brazil's diaspora, Canada was mixed: Franco Colapinto (Alpine, with a large Brazilian fanbase) claimed P6 for his career-best F1 result, jumped back in the simulator, and sits on P11 in the championship (15 pts) — Alpine just recorded, by their own account, the best season start since the team's founding in 2021.[12]

Different story for Gabriel Bortoleto (Audi): only P13 at Canada, audibly frustrated in the Portuguese-language press over the intermediate tire strategy on a drying track and Audi's weak start to the season — he sees "no short-term solution." He remains the only Brazilian with a full-time F1 seat since Felipe Massa (2017). Championship: P15, 2 pts.[13]

It's Thursday, May twenty-eighth. Formula 1 is on a race break, but it's not quiet — and the real racing weekend belongs to motorcycles anyway.

MotoGP races at Mugello in Tuscany — home-race weekend for Aprilia, who lead the championship through Marco Bezzecchi and Jorge Martín. Here's a curious stat: Aprilia has never won a MotoGP race there, and Bezzecchi has never even podiumed at Mugello as a MotoGP rider. If this isn't the moment to break that curse, when is?

The big comeback this weekend is Marc Márquez. The record world champion has been out since Le Mans — he crashed as a highsider and broke his midfoot. During surgery, they also took out an old, loose screw on his radial nerve from a shoulder-stabilization surgery in late twenty-nineteen. Double surgery, then. If his medical check clears Thursday, he races again Friday. He's ninth in the standings, eighty-five points behind Bezzecchi — but Mugello is one of his favorite tracks. His brother Álex is sidelined for Italy and Hungary, with Italian Ducati test driver Michele Pirro stepping in.

For Daniel in Brazil, that means early mornings. Saturday's qualifying starts just before six a.m. local time, the sprint at ten, and the actual race Sunday at nine in the morning.

Meanwhile, there's internal theater at Mercedes. In Canada's sprint, things got hot between Kimi Antonelli and George Russell — Antonelli demanded a penalty for his teammate on the radio, Toto Wolff told him to quit complaining. Russell's engine failure on lap thirty saved Sunday, but Wolff just said before Monaco: the fight stays on, but if the two lock wheels again like that, we're turning it down a notch. That's team orders language, and for Mercedes, an unfamiliar problem after years without a real dual championship contender in the same car.

A second F1 story simmers in the background: the ADUO regulation. If your engine is at least two percent down on power, under the new rules you get special upgrade rights — a catch-up mechanism so no Mercedes twenty-fourteen dominance calcifies. The first ruling comes now, within two weeks of Canada. Red Bull team boss Laurent Mekies openly expects his team to qualify for upgrade rights — it might help by Monaco. Verstappen himself confirmed his F1 future through twenty twenty-seven, after the FIA agreed in principle to shift the fuel-to-electric ratio back toward sixty-forty. But his Red Bull future is still open.

In long-distance racing, eyes turn to Le Mans in mid-June after BMW's double win at Spa — the ninety-fourth running, eighteen hypercars, Ferrari unbeaten as defending champion since twenty twenty-three. BMW starts as championship leader; the test day runs parallel to Monaco's weekend.

In the Brazil watch: Franco Colapinto scored his career-best F1 finish with sixth place; Gabriel Bortoleto, meanwhile, griped in Portuguese-language media about Audi's tire strategy and sees no near-term fix. Mugello starts tomorrow — race on Sunday. Till then.
