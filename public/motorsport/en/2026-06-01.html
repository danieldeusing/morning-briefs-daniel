Mugello main race: Bezzecchi wipes away the sprint stumble [1][2]

What went wrong for Marco Bezzecchi in Saturday's sprint, he made right on Sunday. This time the start at feared Turn 1 went cleanly — he led from lap one and cruised home across 23 laps — finishing time 40:57,347, +3,559 s clear of his Aprilia teammate Jorge Martín (P2).[1] This was Bezzecchi's first win at the Italian GP and Aprilia's first top-class victory at Mugello — a double debut for the Noale marque.[2]

Hometown hero Francesco Bagnaia salvaged P3 (+5,098 s) for local pride, just ahead of Ai Ogura (P4) and Fabio Di Giannantonio (P5). Marc Márquez brought home the full race distance from P7 (+10,762 s) one week after double surgery — a genuine fitness test, passed. Sprint winner Raúl Fernández fell to P8 over the longer distance. Three DNFs: Bastianini, Crutchlow, Rins.

Context This is the answer of a championship leader: rather than panic after the sprint mistake, Bezzecchi flipped the whole weekend. The sprint had squeezed his lead to 12 points — the GP win pumps it back to 17. The message to the grid is starker: with Bezzecchi (P1) and Martín (P2) filling the top two, two Aprilias stood alone, and the Ducati armada trailed. The power balance in 2026 has visibly tilted toward Noale.

The race day wrapped up late into the night with the NASCAR Cup Series: Denny Hamlin won the Cracker Barrel 400 at Nashville Superspeedway over 300 laps, ahead of Christopher Bell (P2) and Chase Briscoe (P3); Ricky Stenhouse Jr. and Shane van Gisbergen rounded out the top 5.[4] Qualifying had been washed out by rain — the grid was set by points, which meant veteran Hamlin started up front. IndyCar: Palou wins Detroit — fourth season win, +62 in championship [5][6]

On the Detroit street circuit, Álex Palou (Chip Ganassi, Honda) converted pole into another coup: 71 of 100 laps out front, +3,0584 s clear of Kyle Kirkwood at the checkered flag — his fourth win in the first eight races of the season.[5] Behind him, a veteran podium: Graham Rahal (P3) posted his best result in ages, Pato O'Ward (P4) and Christian Lundgaard (P5) in tow.

IndyCar: North America's top single-seater series — a U.S. parallel to F1, but with a spec chassis (Dallara) and a mix of ovals, street courses, and road courses. More balanced than F1 because aero and chassis are identical for all; the driver makes more difference. That makes Palou's runaway margin even more striking.

The championship math is clear: Palou leads with 327 points, his margin swelling to 62. Kirkwood climbed to P2 (265) with the runner-up finish, bumping David Malukas (248, P3) down.[6] For Daniel, the stark reading: halfway through the season, last year's reigning champion is on track to repeat his dominance.

F1: Race break — Monaco weekend ahead [7]

F1 is on break between Canada (24.05.) and Monaco — no new race standings. It's Monday, June first — the day after a jam-packed race Sunday when all three favorites came through.

The headline comes from Tuscany, and it's redemption. Marco Bezzecchi threw away his pole in the sprint on Saturday — on Sunday he got everything right. This time the start at Turn One stuck, he led from the opening lap and drove the Mugello main race calmly home across twenty-three laps. A margin of three point five five nine seconds over his Aprilia teammate Jorge Martín. It was Bezzecchi's first win at the Italian Grand Prix and, at the same time, Aprilia's very first top-class victory at this venue.

So the championship lead grows from twelve back up to seventeen points: one hundred seventy-three to one hundred fifty-six. What's striking is the order up front — with Bezzecchi and Martín filling the top two, there stood two Aprilias side by side, with the Ducati armada trailing. Pecco Bagnaia salvaged third on home soil. And Marc Márquez, one week after his double surgery, brought the full race distance home in seventh place — the fitness test, passed.

In parallel, IndyCar raced in Detroit, and the favorite won there too. Álex Palou converted his pole into his fourth season victory, led seventy-one of a hundred laps, and won by three seconds over Kyle Kirkwood. His championship lead swells to a stunning sixty-two points. Kirkwood climbed into second place and pushed David Malukas down. Palou is on track to repeat his dominance from last year.

Late in the night, NASCAR Cup Series raced in Nashville. There, veteran Denny Hamlin won the Cracker Barrel 400 ahead of Christopher Bell and Chase Briscoe. Qualifying had been rained out, the grid was set by points.

In Formula One, there is a break before Monaco. The standings are unchanged since Canada: Kimi Antonelli leads by forty-three points over George Russell, Mercedes is seventy-two points ahead of Ferrari. The Monaco weekend starts Friday, June fifth. The race on Sunday starts at fifteen o'clock track time, that's ten a.m. in Brazil — and on this track, qualifying decides almost everything. For Mercedes it will be the first real test of the announced team orders.

In the World Endurance Championship, Le Mans is drawing near. The test day is already June eighth, the twenty-four-hour race starts Saturday, June thirteenth, at sixteen o'clock track time. BMW enters as manufacturer leader — ahead of Toyota and Ferrari.

What comes next: the Monaco weekend starting Friday, and the Brazilian rookie Diogo Moreira, who scored tenth place at Mugello with the day's best Honda result. Until tomorrow.
