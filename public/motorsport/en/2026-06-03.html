The quadruple Sunday: now with Le Mans test [1][2][3][4]

Yesterday it was a triple weekend — today it's a quadruple Sunday. The reason is a question settled overnight: The official Le Mans test day is scheduled for Sunday, 06/07 (10:00–13:00 and 15:30–18:30 CEST, i.e., 05:00 and 10:30 BRT).[1] So it falls on the same day as the three major races. Yesterday's brief still flagged the date as unconfirmed — that uncertainty is now resolved.

For Daniel's calendar, this means: The Sunday morning (BRT) is packed. MotoGP in Hungary starts at 14:00 CEST / 09:00 BRT,[2] an hour later F1 in Monaco at 15:00 CEST / 10:00 BRT.[3] The Le Mans test runs in parallel in the background (not a points event, but first real pace indicators for the big race a week later). The IndyCar provides the finale: The Bommarito 500 on the Gateway oval starts in US Central prime time at 20:00 CDT — that's 22:00 BRT on Sunday evening (03:00 CEST Monday).[4]

For Daniel's calendar Three races plus a test on one day. Practically speaking: MotoGP 09:00 BRT, Monaco 10:00 BRT (nearly seamless to switch), then a long gap until the Gateway oval at 22:00 BRT. The Le Mans test is optional background — setup data, no points.

F1: Monaco opens a brutal stretch [5][6]

F1 returns from its break to the season's most tactically fragile race — and Monaco is more than a one-off GP: It opens a phase of six races in eight weeks.[5] 3.337 km, 78 laps, a result that almost always settles by Saturday's qualifying because overtaking is nearly impossible here. That's exactly what makes Monaco the first real test of Mercedes' team orders.

Team orders: When two drivers of the same team compete for positions, the team can mandate who gets to lead — like a coach pulling two strikers apart so they don't collide at the same goal. Delicate because both also want their own championship.

After the bruising battle in Canada, team principal Toto Wolff reaffirmed the line: the fight continues, but if team points are threatened, they'll "pull the hand brake".[6] Outside voice: Sky commentator David Croft explicitly urges Mercedes against imposing team orders — a title fight between two of their own drivers is too valuable to artificially dampen. Antonelli leads with 131 points, 43 ahead of Russell (88) — the gap widened in Canada through Russell's exit. In Monaco there's no "settled in the pits": whoever qualifies ahead on Saturday usually wins Sunday.

For Charles Leclerc (Ferrari, P3 with 75) it's the home race; Ferrari sits 72 points behind Mercedes. Verstappen (P7, 43) and Red Bull struggle on a track where comebacks without pole are rarely rewarded.

MotoGP: Hungary GP at Balaton Park [7]

Round 8 is the Grand Prix of Hungary at Balaton Park — the second MotoGP visit there (debut 2025), a young track ~85–90 km southwest of Budapest, opened 2023.[7] Because data is scarce, nobody has a true experience advantage.

Championship leader Marco Bezzecchi (173) arrives with momentum: At Mugello he turned the weekend around after the sprint slip, won the main race ahead of Aprilia teammate Jorge Martín (156) — his tenth MotoGP win and the first at home — and pushed the championship gap back out to 17 points. The Aprilia duo has dominated recently and wants to cement the advantage. For Brazil fans: rookie Diogo Moreira (LCR-Honda) chases his next confirmation after P10 at Mugello.

WEC: Le Mans gets a new Hyperpole format [8][9][10]

The 94th edition of the 24 Hours of Le Mans (round 3 of the WEC season) starts Sat 06/13 at 16:00 CEST / 11:00 BRT, finish Sunday afternoon.[8] Before that comes race week: practice and qualifying from Wed 06/10, Hyperpole on Thu 06/11 evening.

New this year: a redesigned Hyperpole format.[9] Instead of one session per class, there are now two tiers, and two more cars per class can advance.

Hyperpole (new 2026): a sharpened qualifying shoot-out for the best grid spots. First a regular quali per class — the fastest 15 move on. In Hyperpole 1 (20 min.) positions 11–15 lock in their spots; the top 10 move to Hyperpole 2 (15 min.) and race solo for pole. More drama, every lap counts.

62 cars start, 18 in the top-class Hypercar — including a trio of Ferrari 499P (defending champs, unbeaten at Le Mans since 2023).[10] New: Genesis (Hyundai's luxury brand) is the first Korean Hypercar team; Porsche is absent after its works withdrawal. For F1 fans: the returnees Logan Sargeant (Proton, Ford Mustang) and Jack Doohan (Nielsen) are in the entry list.

Álex Palou (Chip Ganassi, Honda) arrives as the runaway championship leader: 327 points, +62 over Kyle Kirkwood (265), who moved to P2 in Detroit ahead of David Malukas (248).[12] Palou has won four of the first eight races — on a 1.25-mile oval where track position and fuel strategy are everything, a single mistake can shrink the championship gap faster than on a street course.

It's Wednesday, the third of June — a quiet day on the tracks, the last before an unusually packed weekend.

The day's big news: what was a triple weekend has become a quadruple Sunday. The official Le Mans test day is now confirmed and falls on exactly that Sunday, the seventh of June. Yesterday's brief left the date uncertain — that's now clear. So four things happen at once that Sunday: MotoGP in Hungary at nine a.m. Brazilian time, Formula One in Monaco at ten a.m., the Le Mans test spread across the day, and in the evening at ten p.m. the IndyCar race on the Gateway oval under lights.

In Monaco, Mercedes isn't facing just any race but a brutal six-races-in-eight-weeks stretch. It's also the season's most tactically delicate race because overtaking is nearly impossible and the result almost always settles in Saturday's qualifying. That's exactly why Monaco is the first real test of team orders. Toto Wolff has reaffirmed the line: the fight goes on, but if team points are threatened they'll pull the hand brake. Sky commentator David Croft argues against it. Kimi Antonelli leads with one hundred thirty-one points, forty-three ahead of George Russell.

In MotoGP, the field visits Balaton Park in Hungary for the second time, a young track with no major experience advantage for anyone. Championship leader Marco Bezzecchi arrives with momentum after turning the weekend around at Mugello and winning the main race ahead of his Aprilia teammate Jorge Martín — his tenth victory and the first at home. His lead is seventeen points.

In the World Endurance Championship, Le Mans gets a new Hyperpole format. Instead of one session per class, there are now two tiers: the fastest fifteen move on, the best ten race solo for pole — and two more cars per class can compete. The ninety-fourth edition starts on Saturday, the thirteenth of June, at four p.m. track time, that's eleven a.m. in Brazil. Sixty-two cars are in the entry, eighteen of them in the top Hypercar class. BMW leads the manufacturers ahead of Toyota and Ferrari.

In IndyCar, Álex Palou arrives at Gateway as the runaway championship leader. His lead is sixty-two points and he's won four of the first eight races. The Bommarito Five Hundred returns to prime time, starting at eight p.m. local time, that's ten p.m. in Brazil. But on the oval, track position and fuel strategy decide everything — a single mistake can quickly shrink the gap.

What comes next: starting Friday the triple blow of Monaco, Hungary, and Gateway, plus the Le Mans test on Sunday — then immediately the full Le Mans week with the new Hyperpole. Until tomorrow.
