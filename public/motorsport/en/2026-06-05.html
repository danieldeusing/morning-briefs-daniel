F1: Monaco starts practice — and the brutal phase [1][2]

F1 is back from the break, and Monaco is more than a single Grand Prix: it opens a phase of six races in eight weeks.[1] 3.337 km, 78 laps, a result that almost always falls already on Saturday in qualifying, because overtaking here is nearly impossible. That's exactly what makes Monaco the first real test of Mercedes team orders.

Team orders: When two drivers from the same team fight over positions, the team can order who drives in front — like a coach holding back two strikers so neither mows the other down. Tricky, because both also want their own world title.

After the hard battle in Canada, team principal Toto Wolff restated the red line: the fight goes on, but if the team's points are at risk, they'll apply the "hand brake".[2] Outside voice: Sky commentator David Croft explicitly advises against — a title fight between two of your own drivers is too valuable to artificially dampen. Antonelli leads with 131 points, 43 ahead of Russell (88) — the gap grew in Canada through Russell's engine failure. In Monaco there's no "sorted in the pits": whoever is in front on Saturday usually wins on Sunday.

For Charles Leclerc (Ferrari, P3 with 75) it's a home race — and Ferrari has lately found better rhythm on tight tracks; Lewis Hamilton (P4, 72) sits just 3 points behind Leclerc. Verstappen (P7, 43) and Red Bull struggle on a track that rarely rewards catch-up runs without pole.

MotoGP: Hungary at Balaton Park — and a time correction [3]

Round 8 is the Grand Prix of Hungary at Balaton Park — the second MotoGP visit there (debut 2025), a young track (4.075 km, 17 corners, opened 2023) about 90 minutes from Budapest.[3] Because data is thin, no one has real experience advantage.

Heads up Yesterday's brief listed the race start as 14:00 CEST / 09:00 BRT. The official MotoGP timetable states 13:00 CEST / 08:00 BRT (27 laps). Cross-checked with motorsportscalendar and motogphungary.com. Sunday morning (BRT) shifts forward accordingly.

Championship leader Marco Bezzecchi (173) arrives on a high: at Mugello he turned the weekend around after a sprint stumble, won the main race ahead of Aprilia teammate Jorge Martín (156), and pushed his championship gap back to 17 points. The Aprilia duo has lately dominated the field. In the background, a big ongoing theme: Marc Márquez (Ducati, P8) is still recovering from his double surgery on foot and shoulder (Madrid, 10.05.); brother Álex Márquez is out injured. For Brazil fans: rookie Diogo Moreira (LCR Honda) is chasing confirmation after P10 at Mugello.

WEC: Le Mans gets a new Hyperpole format [4][5][6]

The 94th running of the 24 Hours of Le Mans (round 3 of the WEC season) starts Sat 13.06. at 16:00 CEST / 11:00 BRT, finish Sunday afternoon.[4] Before that is race week: practice and qualifying from Wed 10.06., the Hyperpole Thu 11.06. evening. The test day this coming Sunday (07.06.) is the first real pace check.

New this year: an overhauled Hyperpole format.[5] Instead of one session per class, there are now two tiers, and two more cars per class can advance.

Hyperpole (new 2026): a sharpened qualifying shootout for pole positions. First a regular quali per class — the fastest 15 advance. In Hyperpole 1 (20 min.) positions 11–15 lock in their starting slots; the top 10 move to Hyperpole 2 (15 min.) and run isolated laps for pole. More drama, every lap counts.

62 cars start, including 18 in the top Hypercar class — with a trio of Ferrari 499P (defending champions, unbeaten at Le Mans since 2023).[6] New: Genesis (Hyundai's luxury brand) is the first Korean Hypercar team; Porsche is absent after factory withdrawal. For F1 fans: returning drivers Logan Sargeant (Proton, Ford Mustang) and Jack Doohan (Nielsen) in the lineup.
