MotoGP: Márquez wins the sprint, starts the race from the front [1][2]

The first race decision of the weekend is in: Marc Márquez won yesterday's Hungarian sprint at Balaton Park—from pole, with 1.548 seconds over Pedro Acosta (KTM); Marco Bezzecchi (Aprilia) took third (+2.722).[1] It's Márquez's first win after shoulder surgery and in just his second comeback weekend. Today he starts the main race over 27 laps also from pole (2:00 PM CEST / 9:00 AM BRT)—at press time, the race had not yet run.

Sprint: a short race on Saturday covering roughly half distance, awarding championship points (12 for the winner, descending). Like a warm-up with admission—it counts toward the championship but isn't Sunday's main event.

For the championship, the picture sharpened after the sprint: Bezzecchi extended his lead to 20 points (180 ahead of Martín 160).[2] His sole real rival Jorge Martín managed only P6 in the sprint. Today's main race can shift this gap again—a full race win brings 25 points, a DNF costs everything.

Remarkable from a Brazilian angle: rookie Diogo Moreira (LCR Honda) scored a strong P7 in the sprint and thus into the points—on the young, challenging Balaton circuit, a signal to watch. He enters the main race from P11.

F1: Antonelli on pole in Monaco—race falls at 10:00 AM BRT [3][4]

In Formula 1, the grid is set but not the result: Kimi Antonelli snatched pole in Monaco—1:12.051 and just 0.043 seconds ahead of Max Verstappen.[3] Lewis Hamilton took third. Bitter for the Tifosi: home driver Charles Leclerc touched the wall at the end of Q3 and sits only at P4. The race over 78 laps runs today from 3:00 PM CEST / 10:00 AM BRT (at press time, not yet driven).

Nowhere does pole position count as much as here: 3.337 km, tight barriers, overtaking nearly impossible—the pole winner almost always wins in Monaco. FP3 ran yesterday, led again by Antonelli.

Pole position: the first grid slot, awarded in qualifying to the driver with the fastest lap—like the inside lane in a 100-meter sprint. On low-overtaking tracks like Monaco, it's half the battle for victory.

Monaco is more than a single Grand Prix this time: it opens a phase of six races in eight weeks—and is the first real test of Mercedes team tactics.[4] Championship leader Antonelli (131) and Russell (88) start from P1 and P6; team principal Toto Wolff has announced he will "apply the brakes" if team points are at risk. On a track that rarely rewards comebacks, that could be the story today.

IndyCar: Bommarito 500 tonight—Palou from his fourth consecutive pole [5][6]

IndyCar races the Bommarito Automotive Group 500 on the Gateway Oval (World Wide Technology Raceway near St. Louis) tonight—the 10th running under lights in US primetime.[5] Planned green flag ~8:00 PM CDT on FOX, so for Daniel 10:00 PM BRT Sunday evening (start after the pre-show). 260 laps on a 1.25-mile oval.

In yesterday's qualifying, Álex Palou (Chip Ganassi, Honda) took pole with a two-lap average of 174.353 mph—his fourth consecutive pole this season—ahead of David Malukas (173.244) and Kyle Kirkwood (173.206).[6]

Palou arrives as runaway championship leader: 327 points, +62 over Kirkwood (265), ahead of Malukas (248). He has won four of the first eight races this season. On an oval where track position and fuel strategy are everything, a single mistake can shrink the gap faster than on a street course—the standings below are current through today's race.

Oval: a closed-loop racetrack with only left-hand turns, here 1.25 miles long. Unlike a street course, drafting, tire management, and precise fuel strategy count—racing often happens bumper-to-bumper at over 300 km/h.

WEC: Le Mans test today—first pace one week before the classic [7][8]

Today runs the Le Mans test day—the first real pace check one week before the 24 Hours. 62 cars and 186 drivers turn their first laps on the 13.626 km semi-street circuit, in two three-hour blocks: 10:00 AM–1:00 PM and 3:30–6:30 PM CEST (5:00–8:00 AM and 10:30 AM–1:30 PM BRT).[7] Test times are only partly indicative (different programs, fuel loads), but they give the first clue as to who delivers in the top Hypercar class.

The 94th running of the 24 Hours (round 3 of the WEC season) starts then Sat 06.13 at 4:00 PM CEST / 11:00 AM BRT, finish Sunday afternoon.[8] New this year is a two-stage Hyperpole format in race week (qualifying from Wed 06.10, Hyperpole Thu 06.11 evening). In the field: a Ferrari 499P trio (undefeated at Le Mans since 2023), Genesis's debut (Hyundai's luxury brand, first Korean Hypercar team), and F1 returnees Logan Sargeant and Jack Doohan on the roster; Porsche absent after works withdrawal.

Hyperpole (new 2026): a sharpened qualifying shootout for the top grid slots. First a standard qualifying per class—the fastest advance, then the top spots run in two stages isolated for pole. More drama, every lap counts.
