WEC: BMW's historic pole — Cadillac's record lap disqualified [1][2]

Thursday night's hyperpole delivered the most dramatic qualifying finale Le Mans has seen in years. On his final lap, Jack Aitken put the Cadillac #38 on top with a 3:22.559 — and beat Dries Vanthoor's BMW by 0.005 seconds. Five thousandths over 13.6 kilometers: according to trackside reports, the narrowest pole margin in Le Mans history.[1]

But the celebration didn't last: the stewards deleted Aitken's fastest lap (Stewards Decision No. 133) due to a pit-lane release infraction (the car was released too early). The #38 dropped to P10. BMW benefited: Vanthoor inherits in the #15 (3:22.564) the very first Le Mans pole in BMW factory history — shared with Kevin Magnussen and Raffaele Marciello.[2]

Pit-lane release, explained: At a pit stop, a mechanic releases the car only when the pit lane is clear. Like pulling out of a parking space: whoever exits without watching for traffic risks a collision. In qualifying, that counts as a rules violation — the affected lap is deleted, in this case the pole lap.

For BMW, it's a milestone: in the hypercar era (since 2023), no BMW factory car has ever sat on pole at Le Mans. Worth noting: a fast lap doesn't win 24 hours. Over the distance, tire management, fuel consumption, and reliability matter far more than a grid position — but the block of #15 (P1) and #20 (P4) gives BMW a strong starting point.

WEC: 94th Le Mans — the start is tomorrow [3]

Starting Saturday, the classic itself gets underway: the 94th running of the 24 Hours of Le Mans (WEC round 3) on the 13.626 km Circuit de la Sarthe. 62 cars, 186 drivers — 18 hypercars from eight manufacturers (Aston Martin, Toyota, Cadillac, BMW, Genesis, Alpine, Ferrari, Peugeot), 19 LMP2 and 25 LMGT3.[3] The luxury brand Genesis (Hyundai) is making its Le Mans debut and promptly qualified both cars in the top 9; Porsche is absent after the factory withdrawal.

Defending champion Ferrari won the last three editions (2023–2025) with the 499P and brings three factory 499Ps again — but after qualifying chaos, the fleet is scattered: best Ferrari is the #51 in P8, the #50 in P12, defending champion #83 already out Wednesday in 17th. But grid position matters less than other things over 24 hours. Start Sat 13.06. at 16:00 CEST / 11:00 BRT, finish 24 hours later Sun at 16:00 / 11:00 BRT.
