MotoGP — Márquez' 100th win in Hungary

At the Hungarian GP (race 8) on June 7 at the new Balaton Park circuit, Marc Márquez (Ducati Lenovo) claimed his 100th Grand Prix victory in 42:55.325 — ahead of Pedro Acosta (Red Bull KTM, +1.343 s) and Francesco Bagnaia (Ducati Lenovo, +11.632 s).[8][9]

100th GP win: Across all classes of his career (Moto3/Moto2/MotoGP), Márquez has now won a hundred races — like a striker scoring his hundredth career goal. Only a handful of riders in history have ever reached this mark.

But the biggest championship impact came from a rival crash: In turn 1, Jorge Martín braked too late and triggered a mass pileup that caught five riders — including his Aprilia teammate and championship leader Bezzecchi, plus di Giannantonio, Fernández, and Aldeguer. The FIM race stewards penalized Martín as the cause (double long-lap penalty). Bezzecchi was the victim but remained championship leader. Yet Márquez took 30 championship points from him over the weekend.[10]

It's Sunday, June 14th. In Barcelona, today at 10 a.m. Brazilian time, the seventh Formula One race of the new 2026 season kicks off — and for once, the world championship leader isn't starting from the front.

George Russell claimed pole in qualifying with a time of one minute, fourteen point six seven nine. Lewis Hamilton in the Ferrari was just sixty-four thousandths off — he starts second. Kimi Antonelli, who leads the championship decisively, only starts from third place this time. It was rough for Charles Leclerc: he crashed in the final qualifying session and has to recover from tenth on the grid.

Important: the race result wasn't published at press time. Formula One Dot Com, Motorsport Dot Com, and the official FIA page showed only the starting grid. So we won't name a winner — circulating claims from search summaries aren't verified.

In the world championship, Antonelli leads going into Barcelona with one hundred fifty-six points, ahead of Hamilton with ninety and his teammate Russell with eighty-eight. Five wins in six races — Mercedes is dominating the big regulation reset year, where the new teams Audi and Cadillac are also on the grid, both still at the bottom of the standings.

From MotoGP comes this week's big story: Marc Márquez celebrated his hundredth Grand Prix victory on June 7th in Hungary at the new Balaton Park circuit. Pedro Acosta finished second, Francesco Bagnaia third.

And yet Márquez doesn't lead the world championship. He only moved up from eighth to fifth. Out front is Marco Bezzecchi with one hundred eighty points — but he lost thirty of them in Hungary when his Aprilia teammate Jorge Martín took him out in a first-corner pileup caused by Martín. The stewards penalized Martín; Bezzecchi was the victim but remains championship leader.

Looking ahead: next weekend MotoGP races in the Czech Republic at Brno, the main race presumably Sunday morning at nine o'clock Brazilian time — though the exact time wasn't officially confirmed yet. Formula One takes two weeks off and comes back on June 28th in Austria at the Red Bull Ring.
