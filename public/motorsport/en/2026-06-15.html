Hamilton's first Ferrari win — and Toyota takes Le Mans

Monday, June 15, 2026 · Ribeirão Claro/PR

The racing weekend you've been waiting for: In Barcelona, Lewis Hamilton claimed his first win with Ferrari — his first F1 race for the Maranello team that didn't end in the midfield. Championship leader Kimi Antonelli was running P2 behind Hamilton, but didn't finish the race: four laps from the end, he retired with a technical failure (electronics/oil pressure) following contact. Despite a zero-point Sunday, Antonelli stays championship leader, but his advantage shrinks from 66 to 41 points. Next stop: F1 in Austria, in two weeks, no sprint.

Hamilton's first Ferrari victory

In Barcelona, at the seventh of 24 races in the 2026 season, Lewis Hamilton claimed a win for Ferrari — his first victory in the red car since switching from Mercedes to Maranello last winter.[1] Behind him, George Russell (Mercedes) finished P2 and Lando Norris (McLaren) P3 — the first all-British podium since 1968.[1] Max Verstappen (P4) and Oscar Piastri (P5) rounded out the top 5.

Strategically, Hamilton executed a three-stop, while both Mercedes drivers went for a two-stop. The deciding factor was a Virtual Safety Car (VSC), triggered by Fernando Alonso's broken Aston Martin — hitting at exactly the moment when an extra pit stop cost the least time.[1]

Virtual Safety Car (VSC): a virtual safety car phase where the entire field must slow down together, but no actual safety car comes out. Anyone pitting during a VSC loses far less time than under normal conditions — essentially a half-price pit stop. That made Hamilton's three-stop cheaper than the Mercedes two-stop strategy.

Fastest lap: also Hamilton.[1] Franco Colapinto (Alpine) was penalized 10 seconds post-race (yellow flag infraction) and dropped from P8 to P10.[1]

Antonelli — retires from P2

The pivotal moment for the championship: Kimi Antonelli was running P2 behind Hamilton and retired on lap 62 of 66 with a technical failure (electronics/oil pressure) following contact.[1] Seven drivers total failed to finish — besides Antonelli, Charles Leclerc, Lance Stroll, Fernando Alonso, Valtteri Bottas, Nico Hülkenberg, and Oliver Bearman.[1]

The championship impact: Antonelli stays championship leader despite taking zero points from Barcelona, because he'd won five of the previous six races. But his cushion shrinks from 66 to 41 points — Hamilton gained 25 points with his win plus fastest lap, Russell picked up another 18 with P2.

Reference Under the 2026 scoring scale, P2 points remain 18, P1 is 25; fastest lap has earned no bonus since 2025.

Le Mans 24 Hours — Toyota wins again after four years

At the 24 Hours of Le Mans, the #7 Toyota TR010 Hybrid driven by Mike Conway, Kamui Kobayashi and Nyck de Vries took the win — Toyota's first La Sarthe victory since 2022 and their sixth in factory history.[4] Behind the winning Toyota, the #20 BMW M Hybrid V8 (Frijns/Rast/van der Linde) claimed P2 — BMW returning to the Hypercar class after a long absence, competing since 2026. P3 went to the sister Toyota #8 (Hartley/Hirakawa/Buemi).

Hypercar / WEC: The FIA World Endurance Championship is the pinnacle of sports car racing — eight races per season, driven by factory prototypes in the unified "Hypercar" class (Toyota, Ferrari, Porsche, Cadillac, BMW, Alpine, Peugeot, Aston Martin, Genesis). The season's crown jewel is always the 24 Hours of Le Mans — one race, three drivers per car, completing a lap every few minutes, for a full day straight.

Notable Hypercar retirements: the #38 Cadillac (power steering failure early Sunday morning), a #50 Ferrari 499P (mechanical) and the #17 Genesis GMR-001 (right front suspension).[4] Just behind the Toyota podium sweep, the #12 Cadillac V-Series.R (Nato/Stevens/Deletraz, P4) and the #51 Ferrari 499P (Pier Guidi/Calado/Giovinazzi, P5) rounded out the top five.[4]

formula1.com · gpfans.com

It's Monday, the fifteenth of June. The racing weekend everyone was waiting for is done, and it delivered exactly the two major stories the world expected.

First story: Barcelona. Lewis Hamilton won his first race driving a Ferrari. His first victory since switching from Mercedes to Maranello last winter. Behind him came George Russell and Lando Norris on the podium — the first all-British podium since nineteen sixty-eight. Max Verstappen and Oscar Piastri rounded out the top five.

Strategically it was a three-stop for Hamilton versus a two-stop for the two Mercedes drivers. The deciding factor was a virtual safety car, triggered by Fernando Alonso's broken Aston Martin — hitting at exactly the moment when an extra pit stop cost the least time. Fastest lap also went to Hamilton.

The key moment for the championship: championship leader Kimi Antonelli was running second behind Hamilton and retired four laps from the finish with a technical failure — electronics and oil pressure after contact. Zero points for him. He stays championship leader because he'd won five of the previous six races. But his lead over Hamilton shrinks from sixty-six to forty-one points. Against Russell it's now fifty.

Second story: Le Mans. Toyota won the twenty-four hours — the number-seven car with Mike Conway, Kamui Kobayashi and Nyck de Vries. It's Toyota's first victory at La Sarthe since two thousand twenty-two and their sixth in factory history. Second was the BMW number twenty with Frijns, Rast and van der Linde — remarkable because BMW only returned to the Hypercar class this season. Third was the sister Toyota with Hartley, Hirakawa and Buemi.

Three Le Mans retirements that shaped the weekend: a Cadillac with power steering failure, a Ferrari with mechanical problems, a Genesis with suspension damage. The Genesis is a brand new entry in the class — the retirement isn't surprising, but it's part of the weekend's story.

In the WEC manufacturers' championship, Toyota now leads decisively: one hundred thirty-two points ahead of BMW with ninety-six and Ferrari with sixty-two. Those are the numbers after three of eight season races — Imola, Spa, and Le Mans. An official drivers' championship tally after Le Mans hadn't been published at press time; the figures from the first two races showed Frijns and Rast of BMW tied at the top.

Looking ahead: Formula One now takes a two-week break and returns the weekend of June twenty-sixth through twenty-eighth in Austria at the Red Bull Ring in Spielberg. Classic format with no sprint. The race starts Sunday, June twenty-eighth, at ten a.m. Brazilian time. Three free practices, qualifying Saturday afternoon, seventy-one laps over four point three kilometers.
