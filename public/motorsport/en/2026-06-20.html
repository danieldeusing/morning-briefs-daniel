Formula E: Dennis wins Sanya, Evans loses the lead

At today's Sanya ePrix on China's Hainan island, Jake Dennis (Andretti) drove from pole to victory and led an Andretti one-two: P2 went to Felipe Drugovich, P3 to Pepe Martí (Cupra Kiro).[1] That was the highlight — the rest was chaos.

Title-deciding: championship leader Mitch Evans (Jaguar) endured a crash-filled race and finished only 17th — effectively without points. Other contenders also stumbled: Oliver Rowland crashed out, Edoardo Mortara retired, and Pascal Wehrlein (Porsche) slipped to P14 after penalties.[1] With that, the Formula E title fight is wide open again after a single race day.

Formula E: the FIA's electric racing series — single-seaters with pure electric power, mostly on tight street circuits. Because all cars are technically similar and racing is close, crashes and position changes happen more often than in F1; a bad day for the leader reshuffles the standings faster. Sanya was race eleven of the season.

F1: Hamilton's Ferrari win gives the championship air

The last F1 action was six days ago: at the seventh of 22 races in 2026, Lewis Hamilton claimed his first Ferrari win in Barcelona (Sun June 14).[3] Behind him George Russell (Mercedes) and Lando Norris (McLaren) — the first all-British podium since 1968. At 41 years old, Hamilton was also the oldest F1 winner since Jack Brabham in 1970; his 106th career win, the first in 686 days.[3]

Title-deciding: leader Kimi Antonelli's retirement: he was running P2 when a mechanical failure stopped him four laps from the end — zero points. His lead shrinks to just 41 points (156 to 115).[4]

DNF (Did Not Finish): a retirement — the car doesn't reach the finish. Points are only awarded to the top ten finishers, so a DNF means zero points, no matter how far ahead you were. That's exactly what happened to Antonelli from P2.

MotoGP: Bezzecchi leads into Assen

The MotoGP is also on break until next weekend. After the Hungarian GP (June 7), Marco Bezzecchi (Aprilia) leads the championship with 180 points — 20 ahead of teammate Jorge Martín (160). Followed by Di Giannantonio (138), Acosta (132), and Márquez (108).[8] The Hungary race was won by Márquez over Acosta by just 53 thousandths — the tightest finish of the season; Bezzecchi and Martín crashed out on lap one after a collision.

Next race Dutch TT, Assen (Netherlands), June 26–28 — the same weekend as the F1 Austria GP. Full weekend: F1 in Spielberg, MotoGP in Assen.
