MotoGP Brno: Bezzecchi Ban Clouds the Race Day

The Czech Grand Prix in Brno is today's only major race event — and it was already overshadowed before the start. After Saturday's sprint, championship leader Marco Bezzecchi (Aprilia) was banned for the rest of the weekend: following a crash in the sprint race, he struck a marshal who was trying to retrieve his Aprilia. Race control excluded him from the Grand Prix — Brno starts today with a 20-bike grid.[1]

It's a turning point for the championship: Bezzecchi leads with 180 points, but scores zero today. His pursuers Martín, Di Giannantonio, and Acosta can dramatically close the gap in a single race — and Bagnaia, riding high from the sprint win, is back in the thick of the title fight.

Marshal: volunteer trackside helpers who retrieve crashed bikes, wave flags, and provide emergency aid when needed — the essential safety crew of every racing circuit. Striking a marshal is considered motorsport's most serious off-track offense; a weekend ban is actually lenient.
Still open today The main race runs at 14:00 CEST / 09:00 BRT (21 laps) — that is, after this brief's press time (06:00 BRT). There is no result at this moment; this brief invents none. Top contenders for points are Bagnaia, Ogura, Márquez, Di Giannantonio, and Martín. The result will appear in the next brief.

Brno Sprint: Bagnaia Takes It, Ogura Close

Saturday's sprint (15:00 CEST / 10:00 BRT) was won by Francesco Bagnaia (Ducati Lenovo) in 18:55.527 minutes — his first sprint win in months. Behind him, a tight three-way fight: Ai Ogura (Trackhouse Aprilia) was just +0.241 s back, and Marc Márquez (Ducati Lenovo) took third with +0.794 s.[2] Ogura had earlier claimed pole — with a new Brno track record; Di Giannantonio (P2) and Bagnaia (P3) rounded out the front row.[3]

F1: Off Weekend — Hamilton's Barcelona Win Still Echoes

Formula 1 is dark this weekend — a break between Barcelona (round 7, 14.06.) and the Austria GP (round 8, 28.06.). The last F1 action was huge: Lewis Hamilton won in Barcelona with his first Ferrari victory — the first non-Mercedes win of 2026, ending Antonelli's five-win streak.[5] It was Hamilton's 106th career win, his first in 686 days, and the first all-British podium since 1968 (Russell P2, Norris P3).[6]

Championship-critical was leader Kimi Antonelli's DNF: he retired from P2 four laps from the end with a mechanical failure — zero points. His lead shrunk to 41 points (156 to 115).[7]

DNF (Did Not Finish): a retirement — the car doesn't cross the line. Points go only to the top 10 finishers, so a DNF means zero points, no matter how far ahead you were. That's exactly what befell Antonelli from second place.
