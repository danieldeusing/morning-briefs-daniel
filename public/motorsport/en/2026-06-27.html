Double race day: Spielberg qualifying and Assen sprint today

Saturday, June 27, 2026 · Ribeirão Claro/PR · F1 Austrian GP & MotoGP Netherlands GP live

Today is qualifying and sprint Saturday: Formula 1 is fighting for the grid at the Austrian GP in Spielberg, while the MotoGP sprint is taking place at the same time in Assen. Both sessions run in the morning Brazilian time — and both championships are telling a completely new story in 2026: In F1, the big regulation reset has pushed Mercedes to the front with a 19-year-old at the helm, while in MotoGP, Aprilia is leading the title fight for the first time with three bikes in striking distance. F1 · Austrian GP, Spielberg

Round 8 of 22 · Red Bull Ring · Qualifying today, race tomorrow

Status The qualifying (16:00 CEST / 11:00 BRT) had not run at press time — pole position and starting grid are deliberately open here. Live result: formula1.com. What's solid are the two practice sessions — and they already tell a lot.

Antonelli makes the pace in both practice sessions

Kimi Antonelli (Mercedes) was fastest on Friday in both sessions: FP1 in 1:07.796, FP2 even in 1:07.014 — and both times the field was tight. In FP2, Oscar Piastri (McLaren) was second just 0.237 s back, Lando Norris (McLaren) third 0.325 s back.[1] Max Verstappen (Red Bull) lined up fourth in front of home fans (+0.550 s), Lewis Hamilton (Ferrari) took fifth (+0.597 s). The year's trend holds here too: Mercedes up front, McLaren and Ferrari waiting in the wings, Red Bull struggling.

the-race.com · Status 27.06.2026
2026 regulation reset: Formula 1 has rebuilt almost everything from scratch this season — new chassis rules and new engines ("Power Units"). Think of it like a video game getting a big update: whoever was strong last year doesn't have to be strong anymore, because the ruleset has shifted. That's exactly what you see in 2026 — Mercedes nailed the update, Red Bull (the recent dominant team) is struggling.

What matters today & tomorrow

Spielberg is a short circuit with only a few true corners and three long straights — overtaking is relatively easy here, so pole position is less decisive than elsewhere. For Daniel that means: Even if Antonelli botches the qualifying today (11:00 BRT), tomorrow's race (71 laps, 10:00 BRT) is still wide open. The question is whether McLaren gets closer to Mercedes in race trim than on a single lap.

Toyota leading after Le Mans triumph

The endurance championship (WEC) doesn't resume until July 12 with the 6 Hours of São Paulo at Interlagos — that's Daniel's home race. In the top-class Hypercar manufacturers' standings, Toyota is clear on top after the Le Mans win with 132 points ahead of BMW (96) and Ferrari (62). The Toyota #7 won Le Mans by 10.913 seconds over BMW — the marque's sixth overall victory.[5]

pitdebrief.com · motorsport.com
Hypercar: The fastest WEC class — the prototypes that run for overall victory at Le Mans. Unlike F1 (one driver, one car), two or three drivers share a car here and swap out over hours; both driver crews and manufacturers score points.

MotoGP · Netherlands GP, Assen

Round 10 of 22 · TT Circuit Assen · Sprint today, GP tomorrow

Status Qualifying (Q1/Q2, from 10:50 CEST / 05:50 BRT) and sprint (15:00 CEST / 10:00 BRT) were still running at press time — sprint winners and the grid are open. Live: motogp.com / crash.net.

Bezzecchi back — and immediately fastest

Marco Bezzecchi (Aprilia) missed the last race in Brno due to a ban: After a sprint crash he had struck a track marshal. In Assen on Friday he was straight back on top of the combined practice times (1:31.123), ahead of Raul Fernandez (+0.177) and Pedro Acosta (+0.187).[2] Marc Marquez crashed in FP1, but qualified safely for Q2 as sixth.

crash.net · Status 27.06.2026
Sprint (MotoGP): Since 2023, MotoGP has run a short extra race every Saturday at half distance — today in Assen 13 instead of 26 laps. It has its own points (winner gets 12, half what the main race gives), but no separate championship. For the WM fight, sprint and Sunday points add together — so Saturday is long past being just a warm-up.

Three manufacturers, eight points gap

The title fight is as open as it's been in years: Bezzecchi (Aprilia, 180) leads his teammate Jorge Martin (172), third is Fabio Di Giannantonio on the VR46 Ducati (157). Marc Marquez (Ducati Lenovo, 140) cut the gap with his Brno win down to 40 points.[3] With today's sprint plus 25 points on Sunday the picture could shift dramatically by Monday.

It's Saturday, the twenty-seventh of June, and for motorsport fans today is packed. In Spielberg, Austria, Formula One is racing for grid slots for tomorrow's race, and in the same time window, the MotoGP sprint is happening in Assen, Netherlands. Both sessions run late morning Brazilian time. Important upfront: The results from today weren't in when we wrote this brief. So this is the preview, not the report.

In Formula One, the story of the year is the Mercedes turnaround. This season began with a big rules reset, new cars and new engines, and Mercedes nailed it. The just nineteen-year-old Kimi Antonelli leads the drivers' standings with one hundred fifty-six points, forty-one ahead of Lewis Hamilton in the Ferrari. In both Friday practice sessions, Antonelli was fastest. Red Bull, the dominator of recent years, is struggling, with Max Verstappen finishing only fourth in the second session.

Qualifying in Spielberg starts at eleven in the morning Brazilian time, tomorrow's race at ten o'clock over seventy-one laps. Spielberg is a short track with long straights where overtaking is easier than most places. Even if qualifying doesn't go perfectly, tomorrow's race is still wide open.

In MotoGP, the title fight is as tight as it's been in years. Marco Bezzecchi on the Aprilia leads with one hundred eighty points, just eight ahead of his teammate Jorge Martin, and Fabio Di Giannantonio on the Ducati is third. Bezzecchi was banned last week for hitting a track marshal after a crash, but in Assen on Friday he was immediately fastest again. Marc Marquez crashed in the first session, but has closed the gap recently.

The Assen sprint over thirteen laps starts at ten in the morning Brazilian time, tomorrow's main race at nine o'clock over twenty-six laps. Because sprint and Sunday points both count toward the championship, the standings could shift dramatically this weekend.

And good news for Brazil fans: the endurance championship is heading to the country soon. Next round is the six hours of São Paulo on July twelfth at Interlagos. Toyota leads the manufacturers' standings there after their sixth Le Mans overall victory with one hundred thirty-two points, ahead of BMW and Ferrari.

Then comes race Sunday with the Grand Prix of the Netherlands and the Grand Prix of Austria. Next briefing will show who got pole and the sprint today and how both championships have sorted themselves after the double weekend.
