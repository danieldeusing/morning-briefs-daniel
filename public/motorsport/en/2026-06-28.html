Race Sunday: Russell on pole at Spielberg, Martin in Assen

Sunday, June 28, 2026 · Ribeirão Claro/PR · F1 Austrian GP & MotoGP Dutch GP live today

Today brings two decisions that this brief cannot anticipate: the Austrian GP starts at 10:00 BRT in Spielberg, the Dutch Grand Prix at 09:00 BRT in Assen. This brief is the grid preview, not the results report.

F1 · Austrian GP, Spielberg

Round 8 of 22 · Red Bull Ring · Grid is set, race today at 10:00 BRT

Status The race (15:00 CEST / 10:00 BRT, 71 laps, 4,326 km/lap) was still running at editorial close — the winner is intentionally open here. Live result: formula1.com. What's set is the pole and the complete grid — and yesterday there was plenty of drama.

Russell takes pole, Ferrari locks down front row

George Russell (Mercedes) posted the fastest lap in qualifying at 1:06.113 and will start from pole. Behind him, a Ferrari front row: Charles Leclerc (P2, 1:06.349) and Lewis Hamilton (P3, 1:06.408). Championship leader Kimi Antonelli (Mercedes) lines up in slot 4, with Max Verstappen (Red Bull) in fifth.[1] For the title fight, this is spicy: Antonelli leads the championship, yet his Mercedes teammate Russell starts ahead — if Russell wins today, the 50-point gap in the internal Mercedes battle shrinks.

formula1.com · 27.06.2026

Verstappen crashes in Q3 at Turn 9

On his final Q3 lap, Max Verstappen lost control in front of his home crowd at Turn 9 and hit the barrier — yellows came out. Race stewards examined whether Russell's pole lap was affected by the yellow flags, but cleared it: Russell had not violated the yellow flag rules, the pole stands.[2] Verstappen starts fifth — at a track where Red Bull has historically been strong, but in 2026 trails Mercedes and Ferrari pace.

skysports.com · the-race.com
2026 Regulation reset: Formula 1 made almost everything new this season — new chassis rules and new engines ("Power Units"). Think of it like a video game getting a major update: whoever was strong last year doesn't have to stay strong, because the ruleset has shifted. That's exactly what we're seeing in 2026 — Mercedes and Ferrari nailed the update, Red Bull (the former dominant force) is playing catchup, and a 19-year-old is leading the championship.

What matters today

Spielberg is a short track with few real corners and three long straights — overtaking is relatively easy here, so pole is less decisive than elsewhere. For this brief: Russell isn't home just because of pole, and Antonelli starting from P4 can turn this race around on merit. With warm, dry conditions (up to 31.5 °C Sunday), tire degradation becomes a factor — good news for drivers who are gentle on rubber during the race stint.[3]

MotoGP · Dutch GP, Assen

Round 10 of 22 · TT Circuit Assen · Sprint complete, GP today at 09:00 BRT

Status The Grand Prix (14:00 CEST / 09:00 BRT, 26 laps) was still running at editorial close — the winner is open. Live: motogp.com / crash.net. What's set is the grid and yesterday's sprint.

Martin on pole — Aprilia sweeps rows one and two

Jorge Martin (Aprilia/Trackhouse) claimed pole and leads a historic Aprilia top-4 sweep of the grid: Behind him line up Ai Ogura (Trackhouse Aprilia, P2) and championship leader Marco Bezzecchi (P3).[4] Aprilia occupying the first four starting spots is unprecedented — the brand that spent years as an outsider suddenly sets the standard in 2026. Franco Morbidelli was penalized (three-place grid drop to P16 for impeding Bastianini in practice).

crash.net · 27.06.2026
Sprint (MotoGP): Since 2023, MotoGP runs a short extra race every Saturday over half distance — in Assen 13 instead of 26 laps. It awards its own points (winner gets 12, half of the main race), but there is no separate sprint title. Sprint and Sunday points combine for the championship — Saturday is no longer a warm-up.

Trackhouse-Aprilia scores sprint 1-2 — Martin falls back

Saturday's sprint saw Trackhouse-Aprilia celebrate a double victory: Raul Fernandez won ahead of teammate Ai Ogura (+0.362 s), with Fabio Di Giannantonio (VR46 Ducati) third ahead of championship leader Bezzecchi. Pole-setter Martin dropped from first to fifth.[5] Marc Marquez finished only sixth and for the first time since Mugello lost ground to Bezzecchi. The sprint result does not count for today's main-race grid — racing order is determined by qualifying.

crash.net · motorcycles.news
The "Cathedral of Speed": That's what fans call Assen. It's the only track that has hosted a Grand Prix in every year since the first motorcycle world championship in 1949 — a bit like a stadium that has been running the same cup final nonstop for over 75 years.

It is Sunday, the twenty-eighth of June, and today is race day. In Spielberg in Austria, the Grand Prix starts at ten o'clock Brazil time, and an hour earlier, at nine o'clock, the MotoGP race gets underway in Assen in the Netherlands. Important note up front: both races had not run at the time this brief was written. This is a preview of the starting grids, not the results.

In Formula One, qualifying brought a small earthquake. George Russell in the Mercedes took pole in a time of one-oh-six point one-one-three. Right behind him, two Ferraris: Charles Leclerc and Lewis Hamilton. And the man leading the world championship, Russell's own teammate Kimi Antonelli, sits only in fourth place. For the title fight, that is significant, because if Russell wins today, his points gap to Antonelli shrinks.

Max Verstappen caused the drama. In front of his home crowd, he lost control in the decisive qualifying lap at Turn Nine and crashed into the barrier. Race stewards checked whether Russell's pole lap was affected, but cleared it. Verstappen starts from fifth place.

In the standings, Antonelli continues to lead decisively with one hundred fifty-six points, forty-one ahead of second place Hamilton and fifty ahead of Russell. Mercedes also dominates the team standings well ahead of Ferrari. Spielberg is a short track with long straights where passing is easy, so the race remains open despite the pole.

In MotoGP, Aprilia is making history. Jorge Martin took pole and leads an Aprilia four-way sweep of the grid. The first four places all go to Aprilia — that has never happened before. Behind him his brand partner Ai Ogura and the championship leader Marco Bezzecchi.

Saturday's sprint saw a double victory for Trackhouse Aprilia. Raul Fernandez won ahead of Ai Ogura, and pole man Martin dropped to fifth place. In the standings, Bezzecchi leads with one hundred eighty-six points, just nine ahead of Martin. The title fight is as tight as it has been in a long time. The main race today runs twenty-six laps.

Next week will be exciting twice over. Formula One races at Silverstone in a sprint weekend with an extra race on Saturday, and a week later MotoGP visits Sachsenring in Germany. The next brief will show who won today in Spielberg and Assen and how both championships sort themselves out.
