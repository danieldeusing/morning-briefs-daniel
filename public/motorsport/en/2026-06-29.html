Russell controls Spielberg — Antonelli's lead crumbles

George Russell controlled the Austrian GP at the Red Bull Ring from pole position to the checkered flag — over all 71 laps, never surrendering the lead. It's his first win of the season since the opener. Behind him it was tight: Max Verstappen crossed the line in second with +1.611 s, Russell's Mercedes teammate and championship leader Kimi Antonelli in third with +1.986 s.[1]

The real story is on the standings: before the race, Antonelli led the drivers' championship by 50 points over Russell. Russell's 25 winner points versus Antonelli's 15 for third place shrink the gap to 40 points — the first real dent in the Mercedes internal duel, which Antonelli had controlled firmly so far this season.[2]

A noteworthy footnote: despite P3, Antonelli set the race's fastest lap at 1:10.374 — the pace was there, but the position wasn't. Lewis Hamilton (Ferrari) tried an aggressive three-stop strategy, which "proved unsuccessful," and finished fifth behind Oscar Piastri.[1][3]

Preview: Silverstone Sprint, Russell's home game [4][5]

It won't be a quiet week: in six days, the British GP weekend kicks off at Silverstone — and it's only the second sprint weekend of the 2026 season. The sprint returns to Silverstone for the first time since the debut format in 2021.

Sprint weekend: A shortened second race on Saturday (here 52% of the Grand Prix distance) with its own qualifying. Think of it like a short warmup match with real points — there's only one free practice session (Friday) instead of the usual three, then you go all-in immediately. Little time to fiddle with setup, high potential for surprises.

For George Russell it's the home race — and he arrives with the momentum of the Austrian win and exactly 40 points down on Antonelli. One sprint plus Grand Prix offer up to 33 points in a single weekend: theoretically the stage to narrow the Mercedes duel further. The track (5.891 km, 52 race laps) rewards high speed and aero efficiency.

WEC comes to Interlagos — Daniel's home race [6][7]

Two weeks after Silverstone, the World Endurance Championship (WEC) comes to Brazil: the 6 Hours of São Paulo at Interlagos, Sunday July 12. Start is 06:30 BRT / 11:30 CEST — for Daniel a rare weekend where home start time is also the actual track time, no midnight math needed.

WEC / Hypercar: The world sportscar championship where factory prototypes (Toyota, BMW, Ferrari, Cadillac, Alpine, Aston Martin) compete over many hours with driver changes — like a relay race in the same car. The top class is called Hypercar; both driver lineups and manufacturers are scored.

Toyota arrives as the clear championship leader: after the 24 Hours of Le Mans victory — car #7 with Kobayashi, Conway, and de Vries won by just 10.913 s ahead of BMW — Toyota leads the manufacturers' standings with 132 to 96 points ahead of BMW, followed by Ferrari (62). Cadillac sits here with 32 points in P5 — unlike in Formula 1, the brand scores solidly in sportscar racing.[6][7]

Context The 2026 season is the big regulation reset year (new chassis + power units, new teams Audi/Cadillac) — the balance of power is different from prior years. Mercedes has navigated the overhaul best and leads both F1 championships by a clear margin; Red Bull (P4 constructors) and Cadillac (pointless, 0 points) are struggling. In sportscar racing, by contrast, Toyota is firmly in control after the Le Mans victory.

It's Monday, June twenty-ninth. Race Sunday is settled, and the Austrian Grand Prix has reopened the Mercedes internal championship fight.

George Russell won at Spielberg from pole to checkered flag — over all seventy-one laps, never giving up the lead. It's his first season win since the opener. Behind him, Max Verstappen finished second with just over one and a half seconds back, and behind him Russell's teammate Kimi Antonelli in third.

The key story is on the championship. Antonelli leads the drivers' world championship with one hundred seventy-one points ahead of Russell with one hundred thirty-one and Hamilton with one hundred twenty-five. Before the race, Russell was fifty points behind — the win closes it to forty. Antonelli took consolation in the fastest lap: one ten point three seven four. Lewis Hamilton gambled on three pit stops and finished only fifth.

Cadillac had a black day. Both cars dropped out early, Bottas on lap two, Pérez on lap four, both from overheated brakes. Cadillac remains the only team without a single point, dead last in the constructors' standings. Mercedes, meanwhile, leads the team championship decisively, with almost one hundred points ahead of Ferrari.

After a race-free weekend, the next highlight awaits. From July third through fifth comes the British Grand Prix at Silverstone, and it's only the second sprint weekend of the season. That means a shortened second race on Saturday with its own qualifying and just one free practice. The main race starts Sunday at three p.m. British time, which is eleven a.m. in Brazil. For Russell, it's home turf, with momentum and forty points to make up.

And two weeks later, the world endurance championship comes to Brazil. The six hours of São Paulo run on July twelfth at Interlagos, start time six thirty a.m. local — a true home race for Daniel. Toyota arrives as the clear leader, fresh off the Le Mans win with one hundred thirty-two to ninety-six points ahead of BMW.

Tomorrow stays quiet in motorsport — the next major date is the Silverstone weekend. Until then, the Mercedes duel is worth watching: Antonelli versus Russell, now separated by only forty points.
