Silverstone: the sprint returns [1][2]

Round 9 — and the first sprint weekend there since 2021

Starting tomorrow morning, the grid heads to Silverstone: The British Grand Prix is round 9 of the 2026 season and runs on the 5.891 km Silverstone Circuit over 52 laps.[1] What makes it special: For the first time since 2021, Silverstone is hosting the sprint format again — it's the second sprint of the 2026 season.[2] For the first time this year, all three major junior series are running in parallel: F2, F3, and F1 Academy run the full weekend.[2] For Daniel in Brazil, that works out well — the main race on Sunday starts just after 11 a.m. BRT.

Heads up · Sprint weekend At a normal Grand Prix weekend, only the long Sunday race counts. With the sprint format, there's an additional short race on Saturday (~100 km, points for the top 8) — like a dress rehearsal where real points are on the line. That means free practice shrinks to just one session on Friday, and there's a second, separate qualifying (the "sprint qualifying") just for Saturday's sprint. The regular Sunday qualifying still happens.

Recap: Russell wins in Austria [3][4]

Mercedes double podium, Ferrari flubs the strategy

At the last race before Silverstone — the Austrian Grand Prix (Round 8, Red Bull Ring, 28.06.) — George Russell won from pole, his second victory of the season. Second went Max Verstappen (Red Bull) by +1.6 s, third Russell's teammate Kimi Antonelli (+1.9 s).[3] That put Mercedes back on the podium with both drivers — exactly the pattern securing the team's championship lead.

For Ferrari it was a lost day: Lewis Hamilton could only manage fifth place (125 pts in the championship), Charles Leclerc behind him. A stubborn strategy error under Safety Car cost the team, by the results, any realistic shot at a top-3 finish.[4]

Heads up · Safety Car pit stop When the Safety Car (a slow lead car after an accident) is out front, everyone slows down and bunches up tight. Pitting at exactly that moment is a trick: because others are braking, you lose far less time on the tire change than usual. Miss the window — too early or too late — and you hand away several positions.

F1 championship after Round 8 [5]

The picture before Silverstone: Antonelli leads the drivers' standings with 171 points — his margin over Mercedes teammate Russell (131) is 40 points.[5] Behind him, Lewis Hamilton sits third in the championship (125) and keeps the pressure on from a distance. Red Bull, despite Verstappen's P2 in Austria, sits only fourth — the major 2026 rules reset with completely new cars has visibly shifted the balance toward the Silver Arrows. MotoGP: Martin shifts the lead [6][7]

Assen: Ogura wins for the first time, Martin takes the championship lead

At the Dutch TT in Assen (28.06., the last MotoGP race), there were two storylines. First: Ai Ogura (Trackhouse Aprilia) claimed his first MotoGP victory — making him the first Japanese winner in the premier class since Makoto Tamada in 2004.[6] His teammate Raul Fernandez came second; both Trackhouse riders passed factory Aprilia pilot Jorge Martin nine laps from the end.[6]

Second — and crucial for the championship: Martin still finished third, while rival Marco Bezzecchi crashed out. That was enough for Martin to take the championship lead for the first time since Austin.[7] He now sits at 193 points ahead, just 7 clear of Bezzecchi (186).

No race this weekend The next MotoGP round is the Germany GP at the Sachsenring, 10–12 July — not this weekend. Live motorsport for Daniel this weekend comes from F1 at Silverstone only.

WEC: 6 Hours of São Paulo [8][9]

Home race feeling at Interlagos — next round 12 July

For Daniel, the highlight of the week after next: The FIA World Endurance Championship (WEC) comes to São Paulo — the 6 Hours of São Paulo at Autódromo José Carlos Pace (Interlagos), Round 4 of the season.[8] In the manufacturers' standings, Toyota leads with 132 points, 36 ahead of BMW (96) and 70 ahead of Ferrari (62).[9] The standing is from after Round 3 (24 Hours of Le Mans) — no WEC race has run since then.

Heads up · WEC / Manufacturers The WEC is the World Endurance Championship: races over 6 or 24 hours, with multiple drivers per car trading stints. Unlike F1, the manufacturers' championship is what matters — it's about which brand (Toyota, BMW, Ferrari, Porsche, etc.) racks up the most points over the season. Toyota is ahead, but will need to defend that lead against a strong BMW factory effort at Interlagos.

It's Thursday, July second — the last race-free day before a big weekend. Starting tomorrow morning, Formula One takes to the track at Silverstone.

And Silverstone makes this race something special: for the first time since twenty twenty-one, there's a sprint weekend there. That means not just one race on Sunday, but a short extra race on Saturday with its own points. That comes with just one practice session and a separate qualifying on Friday. For Daniel in Brazil, the timing is good — the main race starts on Sunday at eleven a.m. Brazilian time, which is three p.m. at Silverstone and four p.m. in Central Europe.

Competitively, the picture is clearer than ever. Mercedes leads both championships. In the drivers' title, rookie Kimi Antonelli leads with one hundred seventy-one points as of June twenty-eighth. His margin over his own teammate George Russell is forty points, and forty-six over Lewis Hamilton. In the constructors, Mercedes is ninety-eight points clear of Ferrari.

The last race played a role: in Austria, Russell won from pole ahead of Verstappen and Antonelli. Ferrari blew the strategy call under the Safety Car and threw away any podium chance — Hamilton could only manage fifth.

In MotoGP, the power dynamic has shifted. At Assen on that same Sunday, Japanese rider Ai Ogura took his first win — the first Japanese winner in the premier class since two thousand four. More importantly, Jorge Martin on Aprilia took the championship lead for the first time since Austin, with one hundred ninety-three points, just seven ahead of Bezzecchi. The next MotoGP round, though, isn't until July tenth through twelfth at the Sachsenring — not this weekend.

After that, the week turns into a home race for Daniel: the World Endurance Championship brings six hours of São Paulo to Interlagos, probably on July twelfth. Toyota leads the manufacturers with one hundred thirty-two points. The exact date is still provisional and will be confirmed before the weekend.
