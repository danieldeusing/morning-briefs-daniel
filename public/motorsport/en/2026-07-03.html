Silverstone: the sprint returns [1][2]

Round 9 — and the first sprint weekend there since 2021

The field is now at Silverstone: the British Grand Prix is the ninth round of the 2026 season and runs at the 5.891 km Silverstone Circuit over 52 laps.[1] The special thing: for the first time since 2021, Silverstone is running the sprint format again.[2] It gets started right away this afternoon: after the single free practice in the morning, sprint qualifying at 16:30 BST will deliver the first real decision of the weekend. For Daniel in Brazil, the weekend timing is good — the main race on Sunday starts shortly after eleven in the morning BRT.

ELI5 · Sprint Weekend At a normal Grand Prix weekend, only the long Sunday race counts. With the sprint format, there's an extra short race on Saturday (~100 km, points for the top 8) — like a dress rehearsal where real points are on the line. In return, free practice shrinks to just one session on Friday, and there's a separate qualifying session (the "sprint qualifying") just for the Saturday sprint. The classic Sunday qualifying still happens.

Recap: Russell wins in Austria [3][4]

Mercedes double podium, Ferrari botches the strategy

At the last race before Silverstone — the Austrian Grand Prix (round 8, Red Bull Ring, 28.06.) — George Russell won from pole, his second season victory. Second place went to Max Verstappen (Red Bull), third to Russell's teammate Kimi Antonelli.[3] That put Mercedes on the podium twice again — exactly the pattern that's securing the team's championship lead.

For Ferrari it was a lost day: Lewis Hamilton finished only in fifth place (125 pts in the championship), with Charles Leclerc behind him.[4] This result explains exactly why the outlook into Silverstone looks so lopsided.

ELI5 · Safety Car Pit Stop When the safety car (a slow pace car deployed after an accident) leads the field, everyone drives slower and bunches up tight. That's the perfect moment to pit: because the other cars are crawling, you lose much less time getting new tires than you normally would. If you miss the right window — too early or too late — you lose several positions instead.

Home race at Interlagos — date officially locked in

For Daniel the highlight of next week, and with an update: the FIA World Endurance Championship (WEC) is stopping in São Paulo — the 6 Hours of São Paulo at the Autódromo José Carlos Pace (Interlagos), round 4 of the season. Update from yesterday race date and start time are now confirmed on the official FIA WEC website (listed as provisional in yesterday's brief): Sun 12 July, start 11:30 local time / BRT (16:30 MESZ).[6] Training starts on Friday 10.07. (FP1 11:00, FP2 15:50 local time), qualifying and hyperpole follow on Saturday.[6]

In the manufacturers' championship, Toyota leads with 132 points, 36 ahead of BMW (96) and 70 ahead of Ferrari (62).[7] That standing is from after round 3 (24h Le Mans) — no WEC race has been run since.

ELI5 · WEC / Manufacturers' Championship The WEC is the World Endurance Championship: races run for 6 or 24 hours with multiple drivers per car taking turns. Unlike F1, the focus here is on the manufacturers — which brand (Toyota, BMW, Ferrari, Porsche, etc.) racks up the most points over the year. Toyota is leading but has to defend its edge against a strong BMW factory team in Interlagos.

It's Friday, July third — and finally it's happening. Formula One takes the track at Silverstone, kicking off a sprint weekend.

Silverstone is making its race something special: for the first time since twenty twenty-one, the sprint format is back there. That means not just one race on Sunday, but an extra short race on Saturday with its own points. And this afternoon, the first decision falls with sprint qualifying at four thirty British time — that's twelve thirty in Brazil. Free practice starts at twelve thirty local time, or half nine in the morning for Daniel.

The main race on Sunday starts at three p.m. in Silverstone. For Daniel in Brazil, that's just after eleven in the morning — in Central Europe it's four p.m., a convenient time.

Sportingly, the situation is clearer than ever. Mercedes is running at the front in both championships. In the drivers' championship, rookie Kimi Antonelli leads with one hundred seventy-one points, as of June twenty-eighth. His advantage over his own teammate George Russell is forty points, over Lewis Hamilton forty-six. In the constructors' standings, Mercedes is ninety-eight points ahead of Ferrari. That was also down to the last race: in Austria, Russell won from pole ahead of Verstappen and Antonelli, while Ferrari botched the strategy and Hamilton finished only fifth.

There's news from Brazil: the date for the endurance world championship in São Paulo is now officially confirmed. The six hours of Interlagos start on July twelfth at half past ten in the morning local time — that's half past four in the afternoon in Central Europe. Toyota leads the manufacturers with one hundred thirty-two points, ahead of a strong BMW factory team.

In MotoGP, Jorge Martin on Aprilia took the championship lead after Assen with one hundred ninety-three points, just seven ahead of Bezzecchi. But there's no race there this weekend — the next round is the Germany GP at the Sachsenring from July tenth to twelfth.

So this weekend we're watching Silverstone: the sprint on Saturday, then the main race on Sunday — and whether Mercedes keeps its dominance going in Great Britain.
