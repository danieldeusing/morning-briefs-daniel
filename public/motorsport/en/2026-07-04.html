Sprint pole for Hamilton — race today [1][2]

Yesterday: Ferrari strikes, eleven hundredths decide it

That was close: In Friday's sprint qualifying, Lewis Hamilton (Ferrari) claimed pole position for today's sprint — with a fastest time of 1:28,376, just 0.011 seconds ahead of championship leader Kimi Antonelli (Mercedes, 1:28,387).[1] Third was Max Verstappen (Red Bull, +0.321 s), followed by Charles Leclerc (+0.327 s) and George Russell (+0.357 s). What's remarkable about this grid: precisely at Silverstone, where Mercedes has dominated the season so far, Ferrari suddenly finds pace — and slots Hamilton right up front.

Explainer · Sprint qualifying vs. qualifying At a sprint weekend there are two separate qualifying sessions. The sprint qualifying ran yesterday and only sets the grid for Saturday's short race — Hamilton won that. The regular qualifying today determines pole positions for Sunday's long main race separately. Two races, two starting grids — that's why teams fight for pole twice at a sprint weekend.

Recap: Russell wins in Austria [3]

Mercedes double podium — the pattern behind the championship lead

The last race before Silverstone — the Austrian Grand Prix (round 8, Red Bull Ring, 06.28) — went to George Russell from pole, his second win of the season. Second was Max Verstappen (Red Bull), third Russell's teammate Kimi Antonelli.[3] Once again, two Mercedes drivers on the podium — exactly the pattern that gives the team its commanding championship lead. Ferrari had a rough day: Lewis Hamilton finished only fifth. All the more remarkable that Ferrari now grabs sprint pole at Silverstone.

WEC: 6 Hours of São Paulo — in eight days [5][6]

Home race at Interlagos — date confirmed

The next week's highlight for Daniel: The FIA World Endurance Championship (WEC) comes to São Paulo — the 6 Hours of São Paulo at Autódromo José Carlos Pace (Interlagos), round 4 of the season. Race date and start time confirmed on the official FIA WEC site: Sun 07.12, start 11:30 a.m. local time / BRT (16:30 MESZ).[5] Practice begins Friday 07.10 (FP1 11:00, FP2 15:50 local time), qualifying and hyperpole follow Saturday.[5]

In the manufacturers' standings, Toyota leads with 132 points, 36 ahead of BMW (96) and 70 ahead of Ferrari (62).[6] This is the standing after round 3 (Le Mans 24h) — no WEC race has run since then.

Explainer · WEC / manufacturers' standings The WEC is the world endurance championship: races run 6 or 24 hours, with multiple drivers sharing each car. Unlike F1, the focus here is the manufacturers' championship — which brand (Toyota, BMW, Ferrari, Porsche, etc.) scores the most points over the season. Toyota sits on top and must defend its lead against a strong BMW factory team at Interlagos.

MotoGP: Martin leads — but no race this weekend [7][8]

After Assen: Martin on top of the championship, next race not until 07.10–12

A quick look at the motorcycle world championship: At the Dutch TT in Assen (06.28), Jorge Martin (Aprilia) took the championship lead for the first time since Austin — he finished third while rival Marco Bezzecchi crashed out in lap 2. Bezzecchi went into the gravel at around 200 km/h; hospital checks were fine — Aprilia reported him "fully conscious with normal mobility in all four limbs".[7] Martin now leads with 193 points, just 7 ahead of Bezzecchi (186).

No race this weekend The next MotoGP round is the German Grand Prix at Sachsenring, 07.10–12 — a traditional Marc Márquez stronghold. This weekend, live motorsport for Daniel comes only from Formula 1 at Silverstone.

The championship standings haven't changed; they're still from June twenty-eighth. Looking ahead to Brazil: in eight days, the world endurance championship comes to São Paulo. The six hours of Interlagos starts on July twelfth at eleven thirty in the morning local time, which is half past four in the afternoon in Central Europe. Toyota leads the manufacturers with one hundred thirty-two points, with a strong BMW factory team pressing hard.

In MotoGP, Jorge Martin on Aprilia leads the championship with one hundred ninety-three points, just seven ahead of Marco Bezzecchi. Martin took the lead at Assen when Bezzecchi crashed in the second lap — he came away unhurt. The next MotoGP race isn't until July tenth through twelfth at Sachsenring, not this weekend.

So this weekend it's all eyes on Silverstone: sprint race today, qualifying, main race tomorrow — and whether Ferrari can confirm its new pace or if Mercedes rolls on with dominance.
