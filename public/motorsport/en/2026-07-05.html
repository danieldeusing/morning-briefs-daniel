Silverstone: today's main race [1][2]

52 laps, start 16:00 CEST — the weekend's centerpiece

After a jam-packed sprint weekend, today brings what it's all been building toward: the long main race over 52 laps at Silverstone, round 9 of the 2026 season. The start falls at 15:00 BST — that's 11:00 BRT for Daniel, and 16:00 CEST in Central Europe.[1] Silverstone runs on British Summer Time (BST, UTC+1); BRT sits four hours behind, and Central Europe (CEST) an hour ahead. For Daniel, that's a convenient slot: just after eleven in the morning, no alarm-clock acrobatics required.

There's no result yet — as of this brief's deadline, the race hadn't been run. Yesterday's classic qualifying session (16:00 BST) set the grid for the main race; the short sprint race (17 laps) already ran yesterday at midday. Tomorrow's brief will have the full race result.

ELI5 · What's a "sprint weekend"? Normally a Formula 1 weekend consists of free practice, a qualifying session, and the race on Sunday. On a sprint weekend, there's an additional second, shorter race on Saturday — the "sprint" (17 laps here instead of 52), with its own points for the top 8. That also requires two separate qualifying sessions: a sprint qualifying (sets the grid for Saturday's race) and the regular qualifying (for Sunday's main race). More action, more points, more chaos potential. This year Silverstone runs this format for the first time since 2021.[2]

Recap: Russell wins in Austria [3]

Mercedes double podium — the pattern behind the championship lead

The last scored race before Silverstone was the Austrian Grand Prix (round 8, Red Bull Ring, 06/28): George Russell won from pole, his second win of the season. Max Verstappen (Red Bull) finished second, and Russell's teammate Kimi Antonelli took third.[3] So once again two Mercedes drivers stood on the podium — exactly the pattern securing the team's clear championship lead. Russell's win narrowed the gap to championship leader Antonelli but didn't change the lead itself: the rookie remains clearly out front.

F1 championship after round 8 [4]

Going into Silverstone: Antonelli leads the drivers' standings with 179 points — the lead over his Mercedes teammate Russell (136) is 43 points, and 47 over Hamilton (132).[4] In the constructors' standings the picture is even clearer: Mercedes is pulling away from Ferrari with a 100-point lead (315 to 215). McLaren is third (167), and Red Bull — despite Verstappen's P2 in Austria — is only fourth (118): 2026's sweeping regulation reset, with completely new cars, has noticeably tilted the balance of power toward the Silver Arrows.

WEC: 6 Hours of São Paulo — in eight days [5][6]

Home game in Interlagos — date locked in

Next week's highlight for Daniel: the FIA World Endurance Championship (WEC) stops in São Paulo — the 6 Hours of São Paulo at the Autódromo José Carlos Pace (Interlagos), round 5 of the season. The race date and start time are confirmed on the official FIA WEC site: Sun July 12, start 11:30 local time / BRT (16:30 CEST).[5] Practice kicks off already on Friday 07/10 (FP1 at 11:00, FP2 at 15:50 local time), with qualifying and hyperpole following on Saturday.[5]

In the manufacturers' standings, Toyota leads with 132 points, 36 ahead of BMW (96) and 70 ahead of Ferrari (62).[6] This reflects the standings after round 3 (24 Hours of Le Mans, 06/13) — no WEC race has been run since.

ELI5 · WEC / manufacturers' standings The WEC is the World Endurance Championship: races lasting 6 or 24 hours, with several drivers rotating per car. Unlike F1, what matters most here is the manufacturers' standings — which brand (Toyota, BMW, Ferrari, Porsche…) racks up the most points over the year. Toyota is out front but will have to defend that lead in Interlagos against a strong BMW factory team.
