Silverstone: Leclerc's release [1][2]

First win of the season — and his first at Silverstone

Charles Leclerc won the British Grand Prix — his 9th career win, his first of the 2026 season, and his first ever at Silverstone.[1] George Russell (Mercedes) and Ferrari teammate Lewis Hamilton completed the podium behind him; Lando Norris (McLaren) and Isack Hadjar (Red Bull) followed in fourth and fifth.[2] After several weak weekends, it was a release for Leclerc: "the feeling was back where it needs to be," he said after the race.[1]

The race ran 52 laps and finished behind the safety car: in lap 48, Max Verstappen spun into the gravel at Stowe and retired — race control neutralized the field until the end.[1]

ELI5 · Why one win can flip the title race In Formula 1, the winner gets 25 points, second place 18, and so on. A race where the points leader scores nothing and a rival wins can cut a championship lead in half in a single afternoon. That's exactly what happened yesterday: Leclerc banked 25 points while leader Antonelli got almost none — the gap at the top shrank from 43 to 25 points.
formula1.com · espn.com

Antonelli's drama: from pole to P16

The other story of the day: Kimi Antonelli, the championship leader, had started from pole position — and lost everything. In lap 41, while running second, the front-left wheel shield on his Mercedes failed; he had to make multiple pit stops.[1] He crossed the line ninth on track, but a 5-second penalty for exceeding track limits dropped him to P16.[1][2]

ELI5 · What's a "wheel shield"? The wheel shield is a cover on the suspension that smooths airflow around the front wheel — important for the car's aerodynamics. If it breaks off during a race, the car loses downforce and balance, and the debris forces a pit stop. For Antonelli, that meant dropping from a safe podium finish to the back half of the field.
