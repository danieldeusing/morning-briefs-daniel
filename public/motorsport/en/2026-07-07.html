WEC: home race at Interlagos — this weekend [1][2]

6 Hours of São Paulo, Sun 07/12 — not yet run

The personal highlight of the week for Daniel: the FIA World Endurance Championship (WEC) visits São Paulo — the 6 Hours of São Paulo at the Autódromo José Carlos Pace (Interlagos), Round 4, July 10–12.[1] The race starts Sun 07/12 at 11:30 BRT / 16:30 CEST — the same time as the MotoGP race at the Sachsenring. Practice kicks off already on Fri 07/10, with qualifying and hyperpole on Sat 07/11.[1]

Heads up · no result yet The round has not been run yet. A false "Cadillac wins São Paulo" snippet circulated online under a URL dated 07/12 — a false alarm. A direct check of fiawec.com confirms: countdown timer running, no results posted.[1] Who wins won't be known until after Sunday.

In the manufacturers' standings, Toyota leads with 132 points, 36 ahead of BMW (96) and 70 ahead of Ferrari (62).[2] That's the standing after Round 3 (24 Hours of Le Mans, June) — no WEC race has been run since, so Interlagos will be the first to move the table.

ELI5 · WEC / manufacturers' standings The WEC is the endurance world championship: races over 6 or 24 hours where multiple drivers share each car — like a relay race, but with cars. Unlike F1, the focus here is mainly on the manufacturers' standings — which brand (Toyota, BMW, Ferrari …) scores the most points over the year. Toyota is out front but has to defend its lead against a strong BMW factory team at Interlagos.

MotoGP: Martin takes over, Sachsenring next [3][4]

Sachsenring 07/10–12 — last round before the summer break

In the premier motorcycle class, next weekend brings the German GP at the Sachsenring (July 10–12) — the last round before the summer break.[3] The sprint falls on Sat 07/11 at 15:00 CEST / 10:00 BRT / 15:00 CEST, the race on Sun 07/12 at 14:00 CEST / 09:00 BRT / 14:00 CEST — at the same time as the Interlagos start (11:30 BRT).[3]

And the championship has a new leader: Jorge Martin (Aprilia) retook the top spot in Assen for the first time since Austin (COTA) — with 193 points, but only 7 ahead of his previously leading Aprilia teammate Marco Bezzecchi (186).[4] Bezzecchi crashed out in Assen in lap 2 and scored no points — the third weekend in a row without a score. Fabio di Giannantonio is third (177), Ai Ogura fourth (168), defending champion Marc Márquez (Ducati) only fifth, 40 points behind.[4] There's no talk of Márquez dominance this year.

Context · Assen At the last race (Dutch GP, Assen, 06/26–28), Ai Ogura (Trackhouse Aprilia) won his first MotoGP race, ahead of teammate Raúl Fernández (+2.0 s). Title leader Bezzecchi's early crash-out flipped the championship lead to Martin.

F1 after Silverstone: tight championship, Spa next [5][6]

Silverstone's Sunday — a quick recap

Charles Leclerc won the British Grand Prix on Sun 07/05 (round 9, Silverstone) — his first win of the season, ahead of George Russell (Mercedes) and Ferrari teammate Lewis Hamilton.[5] Championship leader Kimi Antonelli had started from pole but lost everything to a mechanical failure and finished only P16. The win has shifted the standings.

In the drivers' standings, Antonelli still leads, with 179 points — but his lead over Russell (154) is down to just 25 points.[6] Hamilton is third (147), Leclerc up to P4 (108) thanks to the win.

Context A single mechanical failure cost nearly half the championship margin — with many races still to go, the drivers' title is wide open again. The 2026 season is the big regulation-reset year: new chassis and power units, plus new manufacturers Audi and Cadillac on the grid.

On Sunday, July twelfth, the World Endurance Championship stops in São Paulo. The six hours of Interlagos start at half past eleven in the morning local time, half past four in the afternoon in Central Europe. Important: this race hasn't been run yet. A false Cadillac win circulated online, but the official site shows only a countdown and no results. Who wins won't be known until after Sunday. At the same time, MotoGP runs its last race before the summer break at the Sachsenring. The sprint falls on Saturday at three in the afternoon Central European time, the race on Sunday at two in the afternoon — nine in the morning for Daniel.

And the motorcycle world championship has a new leader. In Assen, the previous leader Marco Bezzecchi crashed out early and scored no points — the third weekend in a row. That let Jorge Martin on the Aprilia retake the top spot for the first time since Austin, with one hundred ninety-three points, but only seven ahead of Bezzecchi. Defending champion Marc Márquez is only fifth, forty points behind. There's no talk of Márquez dominance this year.

In Formula One, things stay tight after Silverstone. Charles Leclerc won there on Sunday, while championship leader Kimi Antonelli fell from pole all the way to sixteenth place after a mechanical failure. Antonelli still leads with one hundred seventy-nine points, but his margin over George Russell has shrunk to just twenty-five points. A single mechanical failure cost him nearly half his lead.

The next Formula One round is Spa, on July nineteenth. A small correction on the start time: the race begins at three in the afternoon local time, ten in the morning in Brazil — not the one o'clock slot that circulated earlier.

On the sidelines, two more series: in IndyCar, Pato O'Ward won at Mid-Ohio, and Álex Palou remains in the lead. In Formula E, Mitch Evans leads, with the top four within twenty-seven points.
