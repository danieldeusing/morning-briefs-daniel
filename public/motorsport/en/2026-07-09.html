ELI5 · Hyperpole A two-stage qualifying format: in the first stage, every car in a class runs, and only the fastest advance. The second stage — the Hyperpole — is a separate shootout among those top cars, and only there is pole position decided. Think of it like a semifinal and a final instead of a single race against the clock.

Gresini fields just one bike

Fermín Aldeguer is out for the Sachsenring: he suffered a T7 vertebra fracture in free practice at Assen (06/26) and remains in rehab.[5] Gresini has deliberately opted for no replacement rider in Germany — unlike at the season opener in Buriram, where Michele Pirro stepped in — and will race with only Álex Márquez. [5] Aldeguer has until Silverstone after the summer break in early August to get fit in time for his VR46 switch.

A second rider swap involves LCR: Cal Crutchlow steps in again for the still-recovering Johann Zarco — his fifth outing this season, following Mugello, Balaton Park, Brno and Assen.[6]

Daniel's Sunday: still no conflict
