WEC: 6 Hours of São Paulo — the home race is underway [1][2]

Interlagos, round 4 — the action starts today

Daniel's home race is here: the FIA World Endurance Championship opens the Rolex 6 Hours of São Paulo weekend today with free practice at the Autódromo José Carlos Pace — Interlagos, 4.309 km, 15 corners, round 4 of the season.[1] The first free practice session begins at 11:00 BRT / 16:00 CEST, and the race itself runs on Sunday, 07/12, from 11:30 BRT / 16:30 CEST for six hours, wrapping up around 17:30 BRT. Because the track sits in Daniel's own timezone, local time here is identical to BRT — Europe is five hours ahead.

ELI5 · Hyperpole WEC qualifying happens in two stages: in the first stage, every car in a class runs, and only the fastest advance. The second stage — the Hyperpole — is a separate shootout among just those best cars; only there is pole position decided. Think of it like a semifinal and a final instead of a single timed run.

Formula 1: Leclerc's first Silverstone win [3][4]

British GP, 07/05 — chaotic finish behind the safety car

The last F1 round before the current break, the British Grand Prix at Silverstone (July 3–5), went to Charles Leclerc in the Ferrari — his 9th career win, his first at Silverstone and the first by a Monegasque driver in this race's history.[4] George Russell (Mercedes, +0.427 s) and Lewis Hamilton (Ferrari, +0.772 s) rounded out the podium.

The race ended behind the safety car: Max Verstappen spun into the gravel at Stowe in lap 48, and the resulting safety car ran all the way to the checkered flag.[3] Painful for championship leader Kimi Antonelli: a five-second penalty for exceeding track limits dropped him to 16th in the final classification.[4]

ELI5 · Safety car finish If there's an incident shortly before the end, race control sends out the safety car; all cars must line up behind it and can no longer overtake. If the incident isn't cleared in time, the safety car stays out until the finish — the field crosses the line in order, with no real final sprint. That's how it went at Silverstone: Leclerc's lead was no longer under threat from lap 48 onward.

It's Friday, July tenth. This morning, the World Endurance Championship rolls onto the grid at Interlagos for the first time this season — Daniel's home track, right in his backyard.

The six hours of São Paulo start today with free practice, qualifying follows on Saturday, and the race runs Sunday at eleven thirty in the morning local time, four thirty in the afternoon in Central Europe. In the manufacturers' standings, Toyota leads with a hundred and thirty-two points ahead of BMW and Ferrari. The standings have been frozen since Le Mans in June, and Interlagos is the first race to shake them up again. The midfield is especially tight: Alpine, Cadillac, and Aston Martin are separated by just twelve points.

Formula 1 is on a break right now. The last round, the British Grand Prix at Silverstone, brought a small surprise: Charles Leclerc won in the Ferrari — his ninth career victory, his first at Silverstone, and the first ever by a Monegasque driver in the history of this race. George Russell and Lewis Hamilton rounded out the podium behind him. The race ended behind the safety car after Max Verstappen spun into the gravel in lap forty-eight. It was painful for Kimi Antonelli: a five-second penalty dropped the championship leader down to sixteenth place.

That's done little to change the top of the standings. Antonelli still leads the drivers' championship with a hundred and seventy-nine points, twenty-five ahead of Russell, with Hamilton behind him. Leclerc moved up to fourth with the win. Among the constructors, Mercedes is clearly out front, with three hundred and thirty-three points ahead of Ferrari and McLaren. A circulating aggregator figure of two hundred and forty-four to a hundred and sixty-five is outdated — we're using the official numbers, which line up exactly with the drivers' points.

The next Formula 1 stop is the Belgian Grand Prix at Spa, from the seventeenth to the nineteenth of July, a weekend with no sprint. The race starts on Sunday at three in the afternoon Central European Summer Time, ten in the morning for Daniel.

And the good news for the weekend: MotoGP races at the Sachsenring, but it doesn't overlap with Interlagos on Sunday. The motorcycle race starts at nine in the morning Brazilian time and wraps up after about forty-five minutes, with the Interlagos start not following until eleven thirty. Only on Saturday do the MotoGP sprint and the third free practice session briefly collide.
