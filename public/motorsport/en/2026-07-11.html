WEC: Interlagos Saturday — pole is set today [1][2]

Rolex 6 Hours of São Paulo, round 4 — qualifying afternoon

Daniel's home race enters its decisive day: at the Autódromo José Carlos Pace (Interlagos, 4.309 km, 15 turns), the FIA World Endurance Championship sets the starting grid this afternoon. After the third free practice in the morning (10:10 BRT), qualifying runs — LMGT3 first, then Hypercar (15:25 BRT) — before the Hypercar hyperpole at 15:45 BRT / 20:45 MESZ decides pole.[1] The actual six-hour race follows on Sunday, 12.07., from 11:30 BRT / 16:30 MESZ. Since the track sits in Daniel's timezone, local time here matches BRT — Europe is five hours ahead.

No result before this afternoon A "Cadillac front-row lockout, Lynn on pole at 1:22.570" for Interlagos is circulating in searches — that's from the 2025 race (qualifying on 12.07.2025), not this year. The 2026 hyperpole doesn't run until 15:45 BRT today; until then there is no starting grid. It'll be in tomorrow's brief.
ELI5 · Hyperpole WEC qualifying happens in two stages: first all cars in a class run, and only the fastest advance. The second stage — the hyperpole — is a separate shootout among just those top cars; pole is only decided there. Think of it like a semifinal and a final, instead of a single run against the clock.

Friday: Aston Martin ahead, Ferrari close behind

In Friday's first free practice, the Aston Martin Valkyrie #007 (Tincknell) was fastest with 1:25.457 — just 0.024 s ahead of the Ferrari AF Corse #50 (Fuoco); the second Ferrari #51 (Pier Guidi) followed +0.106 s.[1] A Friday practice session says little about race pace, but it hints at how tight the Hypercar front-runners are at Interlagos.

MotoGP: Sprint Saturday at the Sachsenring [3][4][5]

German GP, 10.–12.07. — Márquez sets the tone

While the endurance championship qualifies at Interlagos, MotoGP runs its final round before the summer break at the Sachsenring. Saturday is the highlight for viewers: first qualifying (05:50 BRT / 10:50 MESZ), then the sprint at 10:00 BRT / 15:00 MESZ in the morning.[3] On Friday, Marc Márquez (Ducati Lenovo) was the day's fastest with 1:19.394, ahead of Raúl Fernández (Trackhouse Aprilia).[4] The Sachsenring has long been considered Márquez territory — Friday confirmed his favorite status.

ELI5 · Sprint The sprint is a short Saturday race over half the distance. It counts toward the championship, but with half points: the winner gets 12 instead of 25 points, second place gets 9, third gets 7 — only the top nine score. Important, but not as heavy as the actual Grand Prix on Sunday.

The championship is a four-way fight

At the top, Jorge Martín (Aprilia) leads with 193 points — just seven ahead of Marco Bezzecchi (186, also Aprilia).[5] Behind them, Fabio Di Giannantonio (VR46 Ducati, 177) and Ai Ogura (Trackhouse, 168) keep the pressure on; Márquez, despite his Sachsenring strength, is only fifth overall (153), 40 points behind Martín. Ogura rides in with momentum after his first premier-class win at Assen — the rookie's charge is one of the stories of the season.
