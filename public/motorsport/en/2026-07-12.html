WEC: 6 Hours of São Paulo — Cadillac ahead, race start today [1][2]

Rolex 6 Hours of São Paulo, round 4 — start 11:30 BRT / 16:30 CEST

Daniel's home race is on: at the Autódromo José Carlos Pace (Interlagos, 4.309 km), the FIA World Endurance Championship's six-hour race gets underway today at 11:30 BRT / 16:30 CEST — round 4 of 8, marking the season's halfway point.[1] Since the track sits in Daniel's time zone, local time here equals BRT; Europe is five hours ahead. The starting grid has been set since yesterday afternoon: in the Hypercar class, Cadillac Hertz Team JOTA has locked out the entire front row.

ELI5 · Hyperpole WEC qualifying runs in two stages. First, all cars in a class go out; only the fastest advance. The second stage — the Hyperpole — is a separate shootout among just those top cars, and only there is pole actually decided. Think of it like a semifinal and a final, rather than one single timed run.

Hyperpole: Cadillac one-two, Alpine close behind

Will Stevens put the #12 Cadillac V-Series.R on pole with 1:23.041 — just 0.048 s ahead of teammate Jack Aitken in the #38.[1] Only behind them came the fastest non-Cadillac: Victor Martins in the #36 Alpine A424 (1:23.108, +0.067 s to pole). Two American cars up front, the works Alpine within striking distance — for a six-hour race the starting grid says little about the outcome, but Cadillac heads into the night with the best possible hand. In LMGT3, Kobe Pauwels in the #23 Heart of Racing Aston Martin took pole (1:33.350).

What's at stake

In the manufacturers' standings, Toyota leads with 132 points ahead of BMW (96) and Ferrari (62).[2] The standings have been static since Le Mans in June — Interlagos is the first race to move them again. The midfield is where it gets interesting: Alpine (38), Cadillac (32) and Aston Martin (26) are separated by just twelve points. Cadillac is on pole and can flip that order today with a podium finish — that's exactly the wager behind this starting grid.

MotoGP: Márquez sweeps the weekend — GP today [3][4][5]

German GP, Sachsenring — race 09:00 BRT / 14:00 CEST

At the Sachsenring, MotoGP runs its final round before the summer break today. The 30-lap Grand Prix starts at 09:00 BRT / 14:00 CEST; this morning's warm-up (04:40 BRT) has already run.[3] The favorite is Marc Márquez (Ducati Lenovo), and he's left no doubt: on Saturday he took pole — his 77th in the premier class, with a new lap record — just six hundredths ahead of brother Álex; Fabio Di Giannantonio completed the Ducati front-row lockout.[4] In this morning's warm-up, Márquez was fastest again (1:20.119); Di Giannantonio (+0.070) crashed late at turn 8 while testing the latest GP26 aero package, but was unhurt.

Sprint: win from start to finish

Márquez won Saturday's sprint leading all 15 laps, by 0.368 s — his 19th sprint win, a record.[4] Behind him came Álex Márquez and Di Giannantonio; Ai Ogura fourth, Raúl Fernández fifth. Championship leader Jorge Martín salvaged four points by finishing sixth, ahead of Francesco Bagnaia.

ELI5 · Why the sprint barely moves the standings The sprint is a short Saturday race over half the distance, and it pays half points: the winner gets 12 instead of 25, and only the top nine score. Márquez won and picked up 12 points — Martín finished sixth and took 4. Net result: Márquez's deficit only shrank from 40 to 32. The big haul gets handed out today, in the Grand Prix.

The catch: a dominant weekend, fifth in the standings

Márquez is out front at the Sachsenring — yet he's still only fifth overall with 165 points, 32 behind Martín (197).[5] That's the story of his season: dominant on individual weekends, but an early deficit that even a clean sweep per weekend only chips away at slowly. At the top it stays tight — Martín (197), Bezzecchi (186) and Di Giannantonio (184) are separated by just 13 points. A win today, going into the break, would give Márquez maximum momentum — but the math still won't get him into the top three.
