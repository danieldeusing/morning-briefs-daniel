Driver market: Verstappen's exit clause is now guaranteed triggerable [1][2]

The math no longer adds up

Verstappen's Red Bull contract contains a clause: if he's not in the top two of the drivers' standings by the summer break, he may exit for 2027 — even though the contract nominally runs longer.[1] The last race before the break is the Hungarian GP on 07/26, and the math is now unambiguous: Verstappen sits at 76 points in seventh place, Russell in second at 154.[7] Only two races remain before Budapest (Spa and Hungary, neither with a sprint) — a maximum of 50 points. Even with two wins he'd reach 126, still behind Russell (154) and Hamilton (147, P3). He can no longer reach the top two — the trigger condition is now mathematically guaranteed to be met.

ELI5 · How an exit clause works A contract clause is like an emergency exit with a condition attached. Verstappen's exit essentially says: "If the car isn't good enough to keep you in the top two by mid-season, you're allowed to leave." The championship standings are the referee. This time the verdict is in early — the points math already rules out the top two, so the exit is unlocked. Whether he walks through it is a completely separate decision from whether he's allowed to.
racingnews365.com

The 72-hour window — and why he'll likely stay anyway

The trigger would fall within a window of roughly 72 hours between the Hungarian GP and the official start of the summer break — only then would teams know whether the contractual trigger had been pulled.[2] Important distinction: "may" doesn't mean "will." Per current reporting, Verstappen intends to stay at Red Bull despite the option and race for the team in 2027. The clause mainly gives him negotiating leverage, not an automatic split. For Daniel, that means the mathematical certainty is the news — the outcome remains open and likely won't become clear until early August.

Speculation boundary That the condition is met is mathematically documented. That Verstappen stays is reporting/analysis (gpblog), not official confirmation — flagged deliberately as such.

Aston Martin: Newey's big package not until Budapest

Technical director Adrian Newey confirmed (07/09) that Aston Martin's extensive upgrade — weight reduction, reworked aero surfaces, new nose — will land on both cars only at the Hungarian GP, not already at Spa.[6] Spa, the longest and fastest track of the year, is considered the team's weakest weekend until then. "We plan to introduce our upgrade in Hungary on both cars," Newey said. In the constructors' standings, Aston Martin sits tenth with just one point — every tenth of a second from the package counts.
