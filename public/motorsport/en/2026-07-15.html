Driver market: the clause kicks in — the outcome stays open [1][2]

The math is done

Verstappen's Red Bull contract contains a clause: if he's not in the top two of the drivers' standings by the summer break, he may exit for 2027.[1] The last race before the break is the Hungarian GP on 07/26 — and the math is unambiguous: Verstappen sits at 76 points in seventh place, Russell in second at 154, Hamilton in third at 147.[4] Only two races remain before Budapest (Spa and Hungary, neither with a sprint) — a maximum of 50 points. Even with two wins he'd reach 126, still behind Russell and Hamilton. He can no longer reach the top two; the trigger condition is now mathematically guaranteed to be met.

ELI5 · How an exit clause works A contract clause is like an emergency exit hatch with a condition attached. Verstappen's hatch essentially says: "If the car isn't good enough to keep you in the top two by mid-season, you're allowed to leave." The championship standings are the referee — and this time the verdict is in early: the points math already rules out the top two, so the hatch is unlocked. Whether he walks through it is a completely separate question from whether he's allowed to.

Does he stay — or go to McLaren? The reports disagree

"May" doesn't mean "will" — and what Verstappen actually does is disputed. Per Motorsport Week, "just about every realistic permutation is on the table — including a long-term deal with McLaren, with money highly unlikely to be an obstacle."[2] The same source cites a detail that has observers taking notice: a planned London meeting between Verstappen's camp and Red Bull's ownership side was cancelled after the British GP. Set against that is McLaren boss Zak Brown's public denial: "I'm very happy with my two racing drivers, Lando and Oscar." McLaren already has two contracts with Norris and Piastri — a Verstappen move would displace one of them. For Daniel, that means the math is the hard news; the outcome isn't.

Speculation boundary Only one thing is documented: that the clause's condition is met (the points math). Where Verstappen drives in 2027 is contradictory reporting — a public denial here, unconfirmed sources there. We're not claiming an outcome; the matter likely won't become clear until around the break (late July/early August).

Hungary: last race before the break, Newey upgrade arrives [9]

The clause deadline — and Aston Martin's big package

The Hungarian GP (07/24–26, race start Sun 07/26 3:00 PM CEST / 10:00 AM BRT) is the last race before the summer break — and therefore the deadline that finally locks in the Verstappen clause math. On the technical side, Aston Martin brings its extensive upgrade there: technical director Adrian Newey has confirmed that a new nose, reduced weight, and reworked aero surfaces will land on both cars only in Budapest, not already at Spa.[9] In the constructors' standings, the team sits tenth with just one point — every tenth of a second from the package counts. "We plan to introduce our upgrade in Hungary on both cars," said Newey, who deliberately declined to put numbers on the expected lap-time gains, since the simulation tools aren't yet well correlated enough.
