Driver market: €8 million rejected — the outcome remains open [1][2]

Today's new detail: a buyout offer that went nowhere

Today's freshest, best-sourced piece is concrete: Red Bull reportedly offered Verstappen around €8 million (£6.83 million) to buy out his exit clause — a deal that would have tied him to the team through 2028. Verstappen turned it down.[2] The reasoning cited by gpblog fits his well-known mindset: having a car capable of winning races matters more to him than "a couple of million euros more or less." The offer indirectly confirms what the clause math already suggested — Red Bull considers the exit real enough to try to buy it out.

ELI5 · Exit clause & "buying it out" An exit clause is like an emergency hatch in a contract that opens under a certain condition. Verstappen's hatch essentially says: "If the car isn't good enough to keep you in the top two by mid-season, you're free to leave." A team can offer to weld that hatch shut — that's exactly what "buying it out" means: money in exchange for giving up the exit right. Verstappen didn't take the money.
gpblog.com

The math is done — the clause is unlocked

That the clause even applies is now certain: if Verstappen isn't in the top two of the drivers' standings by the summer break, he can exit for 2027.[1] The last race before that is the Hungarian GP on 07/26 — and the math is clear: Verstappen sits at 76 points in seventh, Russell is second with 154, Hamilton third with 147.[9] Only two races remain before Budapest (Spa and Hungary, neither with a sprint) — 50 points at most. Even with two wins he'd reach 126 and still trail both Russell and Hamilton. He can no longer reach the top two; the trigger condition is now mathematically guaranteed.

Points basis (Verstappen 76/P7, Russell 154/P2, Hamilton 147/P3; max 2 races × 25 = 50 → cap of 126) from the F1 drivers' standings, see footnote 9.

The buyout would have tied him to Red Bull until 2028; £6.83M ≈ €8M.

Two camps, conflicting signals — nothing official [3][4]

"Closing stages" versus "no seat available"

"Can" doesn't mean "will" — and what Verstappen is actually doing is contested. On one side, rumor sites (PlanetF1, relayed via sportbible) report that Verstappen is "in the closing stages of a lengthy negotiation" over a McLaren move, tied to the idea of a Piastri swap to Red Bull.[3] On the other side stands a double public denial out of Woking: McLaren boss Zak Brown says he has "two drivers in the seats," so he "couldn't offer [Verstappen] a seat in the race car."[3] And Lando Norris waves the reports off with notable calm: "A lot of drivers want to come to McLaren, so I don't know why you just highlight Max" — he says he's "still going to be here for many more years."[4]

Where speculation ends What's confirmed is only that the clause condition is met (the points math) and that Red Bull offered a buyout, which Verstappen rejected. Where Verstappen drives in 2027 is contradictory reporting: unconfirmed "closing stages" sources on one side, a twofold on-record denial (Brown, Norris) on the other. No official confirmation from Red Bull, McLaren, or Verstappen. We are not claiming an outcome.

What the backdrop hints at — Lambiase, a photo, a terse Marko

Three side notes color the picture without settling it. First: Verstappen's longtime race engineer Gianpiero Lambiase is already confirmed to be moving to McLaren (as Chief Racing Officer, official since April 2026) — a trusted ally of his will soon be sitting in Woking.[5] Second: on 07/15, a photo surfaced of a meeting between Helmut Marko, Jos Verstappen, and advisor Raymond Vermeulen in Amsterdam, reportedly leaked deliberately to pile pressure on Red Bull.[6] Third: Marko himself remains tight-lipped — "My visit was private. No idea [about Verstappen's future]. I am not in charge anymore."[7] For Daniel, that adds up to a lot of smoke without a clear shot. A decision is expected around the summer break (late July/early August) — but no date is confirmed.
