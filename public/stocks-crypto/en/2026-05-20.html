Big Tech & the AI trade

The day belongs to Nvidia [Stocks]. After the US close (~4:20pm ET, call 5:00pm ET) come the Q1 FY27 numbers. Consensus: revenue $78.8B, EPS $1.77, data-center revenue ~$73B — that would be ~77–78% year-over-year growth. Nvidia's own guidance was $78.0B ±2%. Blackwell now carries the majority of data-center compute, and the estimated AI-accelerator market share is ~81%.

Guidance: The outlook a company gives for the next quarter — essentially its own weather forecast. On the market it often matters more than the result just reported: a blowout quarter helps little if the company says "next time will be worse."

Why Q2 guidance decides everything: The market already prices ~$86B in revenue for Q2. If Nvidia guides below that, the market reads it as a slowdown of the AI boom — and historically that triggers a sell-off even on a clean Q1 beat. Add the China headwind: the H20 export restrictions have effectively wiped out Nvidia's China share.

Call — Nvidia (NVDA)

Verdict: HOLD through the print, no new entry before the call.

Why: The stock is in a 4-day pullback (−8.4%) and the setup is binary. Fundamentally Nvidia remains the winner of AI infrastructure — but at a $5.2T valuation a lot of perfection is priced in. Tonight's asymmetry is dangerous: a beat plus strong guidance could mean +8%, a weak Q2 outlook −10% or more.

Timeframe: Wait for the concrete event — the earnings call today at 5:00pm ET. After the call and the Q2 number, decide: with guidance ≥ $86B and a pullback from broad market risk-off, an entry is attractive for a 6–12 month hold. Below $86B, wait.

Forecast: Base (55%): Q1 beat, Q2 guidance $84–88B, stock moves ±5%. Bull (25%): guidance > $88B → +8–12%. Bear (20%): guidance < $84B or a China warning → −10%+.

Commodities — oil & gold

Oil remains the geopolitics asset [Commodity]. WTI ~$103/barrel (a second straight losing session, as the market hopes for a US–Iran deal), Brent ~$109–111. Early in May Brent briefly jumped +6% to $114.44 when Iran attacked commercial ships and the US destroyed six Iranian boats. The Strait of Hormuz has been largely blocked since Feb 28, 2026 — per the IMO, ~20,000 seafarers are stranded on roughly 2,000 vessels.

Strait of Hormuz: A narrow channel between Iran and Oman through which a fifth of the world's oil is shipped. Picture the only bridge every bread truck uses to reach your town — block it and the bread isn't gone instantly, but everyone hoards out of fear and the price spikes.

Call — oil exposure

Verdict: ACCUMULATE on dips (energy sector / oil proxy), don't buy into the high risk premium.

Why: The price carries a large fear premium. On days with deal hopes (like today) it falls — that's the better entry window than an escalation spike. As long as Hormuz is unstable, a structural floor stays under oil.

Timeframe: Buy in tranches over the next 4–8 weeks, on days when the price is falling. Hold until either a durable Hormuz solution arrives (then sell, premium gone) or an escalation drives the price toward $130 (then take partial profits).

Forecast: Base (50%): Brent oscillates $100–115 as long as the crisis smolders. Bull-for-oil (30%): escalation → $130+. Bear (20%): real détente → back to $85–90.

Backing: WTI ~$103, Brent ~$109–111 (May 20); Brent spike $114.44 early May; Hormuz blocked since Feb 28. al jazeera

Gold consolidates at high levels [Commodity]. Spot ~$4,531/oz (May 20), short-term oversold (RSI ~28), with a daily range roughly $4,380–$5,100 for May. The structural driver is intact: central banks bought a net 244 tonnes in Q1 (+3% YoY), plus the geopolitical premium from the Iran crisis.

Central-bank gold buying: When countries (China, India, Turkey…) hold gold instead of US dollars in their reserves, it's like a saver moving from a checking account into the vault — a slow but very steady buying program that puts a floor under the gold price regardless of the day's mood.

Spot ETF outflows: A bitcoin ETF is an exchange-traded pot that holds real bitcoin so you can buy it like a stock. When money flows out, the pot's manager has to sell real bitcoin — direct selling pressure on the market, not just sentiment. $635M in one day means real coins were dumped onto the market.
