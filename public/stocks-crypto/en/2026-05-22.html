Big Tech & the AI trade

Nvidia delivered — and got sold [Stocks]. On Wednesday evening after the US close came the Q1 FY27 numbers, and they were a blowout: revenue $81.6B (+85% YoY, consensus ~$78.8B), non-GAAP EPS $1.87 (consensus ~$1.77), Data Center revenue $75.2B on the Blackwell 300 wave. The real bombshell was the Q2 guide: $91B ±2% — the market had expected ~$86B, and Nvidia delivers the number without any China Data Center contribution. Plus a dividend raised 25-fold and a new $80B buyback program. And yet: the stock fell roughly 1.8% on Thursday.

"Priced in": When everyone expects a good result, they buy the stock beforehand — so the price rises ahead of the news. When the good news then arrives, the "fuel" is already spent, and some sell to lock in the gain. That's how a stock can fall on top-tier numbers. That is exactly what has now happened to Nvidia for the fourth time in a row.

Why the fall matters more than the beat: At a $5.32T market value, a lot of perfection is in the price. The biggest tail risk — a cooling of the AI capex cycle — is off the table with the $91B guide. But if even a near-flawless number can't lift the stock, that shows where we are in the cycle: the good news is spent, now the next one counts. The broader market sees it positively anyway — the S&P 500 closed at a record on 21.05 (7,445.72), carried by the 10Y yield easing from its 16-month high (4.69%) to 4.56%.

Commodities — oil & gold

Oil in a peace whipsaw [Commodities]. Brent ~$105.20 (+2.6% today), WTI ~$98.57 (+2.3%) — but that's a rebound, not a trend. On Wednesday the price fell >5% (WTI to $98.26, Brent $105.02) after US President Trump called the Iran negotiations "in the final phase" and a 14-point memorandum to end the war is on the table. At the same time, the Strait of Hormuz has been blocked since 28.02.2026 — a stalemate: Iran declared its own "Persian Gulf Strait Authority" control zone and refuses to let uranium leave the country.

Strait of Hormuz: A narrow strait between Iran and Oman through which about one-fifth of the world's oil is shipped. Picture the only bridge over which all the trucks carrying bread into your city drive — block it, and the bread isn't gone immediately, but everyone hoards out of fear and the price shoots up. A hope of peace lets the hoarding — and the premium — melt away again.

Call — Oil exposure

Verdict: HOLD / deep dips only — the risk premium is unwinding, and Wednesday's simple buy case has weakened.

Why: As long as Hormuz is unstable there is a floor. But the market is now pricing a resolution, and Goldman has cut its Q4 Brent target to $90 (2026 average $85), with normalization of the strait by end of June as the base case. Meaning: anyone buying into the strength here is potentially buying into a shrinking premium. Only a clear pullback (or a collapse of the deal) makes energy attractive again.

Timeframe: No new entry into today's strength. Wait for the Iran negotiation outcome (Trump's "final phase" → a matter of weeks) and the EIA inventory report on 28.05. Accumulate only if the deal collapses and the price escalates toward $115+ (then momentum) or on genuine normalization toward $85 (then value).

Forecast: Base (50%): deal comes, Brent glides toward $90–95 by Q4 (Goldman). Bull-for-oil (25%): deal collapses, ~2 mb/d outage → $115+ (Goldman stress scenario). Bear (25%): rapid full normalization → back to $85.

Evidence: Brent $105.20 / WTI $98.57 (22.05), −5% on 20.05 on peace hope; Hormuz blockade since 28.02; Goldman Q4 target $90. te brent · thestreet

Gold consolidates at a high level [Commodities]. Spot ~$4,530/oz (22.05, −0.3% d/d), practically flat on the week — the Iran peace signals took some safe-haven demand off the table short-term, and the DXY firmed to 99.4. But the structural driver remains intact: central-bank diversification away from the dollar, US fiscal-deficit risk, geopolitics. The big houses are clearly bullish — Goldman year-end target $5,400, JPMorgan $6,000.

Real yield: The interest rate after subtracting inflation. The 10-year US bond yields about 2.16% in real terms. When the real yield rises, gold (which pays no interest) becomes relatively less attractive — when it falls, gold shines. Right now high real yields keep a lid on things while central-bank buying builds the floor: a tug-of-war that holds the price sideways at a high level.

When money flows out, the pot manager has to sell real bitcoin — that's direct selling pressure, not just sentiment. $648M in a single day means: real coins were thrown onto the market.
