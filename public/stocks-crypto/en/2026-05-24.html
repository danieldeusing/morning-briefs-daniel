Big Tech & the AI trade

Wall Street heads into the long weekend at a record [Stocks]. The Dow Jones closed Friday (May 22) at an all-time high of 50,579.70, the broad S&P 500 (7,473.47) notched its eighth straight winning week — the longest streak since late 2023 — and the Nasdaq (26,343.97) its seventh weekly gain in eight. The drivers: a strong Big Tech earnings wave and falling oil, which dampens inflation worries. Today (Sun) and Monday (Memorial Day) stock trading stays closed.

The quiet sensation is called Alphabet [Stocks]. Google's parent is the world's number two: market value $4.60T (as of May 22) — ahead of Apple ($4.54T) and only behind Nvidia ($5.22T). The rest of the Mag-7: Microsoft $3.11T, Amazon $2.86T, Tesla $1.60T, Meta $1.55T. Together the seven weigh around $23.5T — roughly a third of the entire S&P 500. The market is visibly rotating from the "picks & shovels" (chips) to the "users" (cloud, search AI, advertising) — the companies where AI investments are already turning into revenue today.

"Picks & shovels": In the gold rush it wasn't the prospectors who got rich, but the ones selling shovels and pickaxes. Applied to AI: Nvidia sells the "shovels" (chips). But eventually what counts is whether the prospectors — Google, Microsoft & co. — actually find gold with the shovels, i.e. make revenue. That is exactly where the money is moving right now.

Nvidia delivered — and was sold off anyway [Stocks]. Wednesday evening (May 20) brought the Q1-FY27 numbers: revenue $81.6B (+85% YoY, +20% QoQ), data center $75.2B (+92%), gross margin 74.9%, EPS $1.87 (non-GAAP) or $2.39 (GAAP). The Q2 guidance of $91B came in far above consensus — and that without a meaningful China contribution. On top: a dividend raised 25-fold ($0.01 → $0.25) and a new $80B buyback program. And yet the stock fell after the print. The analysts, by contrast, raised their price targets.

"Priced in": When everyone expects a good result, they buy the stock beforehand — so the price rises ahead of the news. When the good news then arrives, the "fuel" is spent, and some sell to lock in gains. That's how a stock can fall on top numbers. That is exactly what just happened to Nvidia.

Recommendation — Nvidia (NVDA)

Verdict: BUY on weakness — fundamental uncertainty is lower after the numbers, but the price isn't higher.

Why: Before Wednesday the setup was binary — now the central question ("is the AI boom slowing?") has been answered with the $91B guidance. That the stock fell anyway is positioning (priced in), not fundamentals. The leverage remains: ~80% market share in AI accelerators, Blackwell ramp, the $80B buyback supports the price. For a 6–12-month horizon, a weak phase on confirmed fundamentals is a better entry window than a euphoria peak.

Timeframe: Accumulate on red days, staggered over the next 4–8 weeks. Concrete risk dates: the PCE print on 28.05 and the first FOMC under Warsh on 16/17.06 — both could push tech down again via yields and deliver better prices. Hold through the Q2 confirmation in July.

Forecast: Base (55%): stock consolidates ±5% around the current $5.2T valuation until the market looks for the next story. Bull (30%): yields fall, rotation into quality → new highs, +10–15% over 6 months. Bear (15%): Warsh hawk + growth worry weighs on the whole Mag-7 → −10 to −15%.

Evidence: Q1 revenue $81.6B / EPS $1.87 non-GAAP / DC $75.2B; Q2 guidance $91B; cap $5.22T (May 22); stock weaker on earnings day despite the beat. SEC 8-K · companiesmarketcap

Commodities — Oil & Gold

Oil ends the week in the red [Commodity]. Brent settled Friday (May 22) at around $103.5, WTI at ~$96.6 — on the week roughly −6% (Brent) and −4% (WTI). The driver remains Iran diplomacy: early in the week the price fell sharply on negotiation optimism. On Saturday (May 23), US President Trump reported a deal was "largely negotiated", including a reopening of the Strait of Hormuz — but Iranian officials immediately disputed it and declared the strait would remain under Iranian control. In fact the strait is still blocked to date. The situation is fluid.

Strait of Hormuz: A narrow waterway between Iran and Oman through which about a fifth of the world's oil is shipped. Picture the only bridge over which all the bread trucks drive into your town — if it's blocked, the bread doesn't vanish instantly, but everyone hoards out of fear, and the price shoots up. A hope of peace lets the hoarding — and the premium — melt away again.

Recommendation — Oil exposure

Verdict: HOLD / deep dips only — the risk premium is unwinding, the simple buy case from last week has weakened.

Why: As long as Hormuz is unstable, there's a floor. But the market is pricing in a resolution, and Goldman has raised its Q4 Brent target to $90 (WTI $83) — notable, because that's a target below the current spot, predicated on the assumption that the Hormuz disruption normalizes. Buying into the strength here means potentially buying into a shrinking premium. Only a clear pullback — or a failure of the deal — makes energy clearly attractive again.

Timeframe: No new entry into a geopolitical spike. Wait for the Iran negotiation outcome (headlines running over the weekend) and the EIA inventory report on 28.05. Accumulate only if the deal collapses and the price escalates toward $115+ (then momentum), or on genuine normalization toward $85–90 (then value).

Forecast: Base (50%): deal path holds, Brent slides toward $90–95 by Q4 (Goldman target $90). Bull-for-oil (25%): deal collapses, supply outage → $115+. Bear (25%): rapid full normalization of the strait → back toward $85.

Evidence: Brent ~$103.5 / WTI ~$96.6 (settle May 22), week ~−6%/−4%; Sat-May-23 headlines (Trump deal vs. Iran denial); Hormuz still blocked; Goldman Q4 target Brent $90 / WTI $83. te brent · investing/goldman

Gold consolidates well below the high [Commodity]. Spot ~$4,517/oz (May 22, −0.6% on the day) — the second straight weekly loss and about 19% below the all-time high of $5,594.82 (Jan 29, 2026). Silver fell more sharply ($75.35, −1.7%). The short-term pressure: expensive oil stoked inflation worries, and a risk-on environment pulls capital out of the safe haven. But the structural driver remains intact: central-bank diversification away from the dollar, fiscal-deficit risk, geopolitics. The big houses stay bullish — Goldman reaffirms $5,400; JPMorgan sees ~$6,000 by year-end (upside scenario $8,000–8,500).

Real interest rate: The interest rate after subtracting inflation. When it rises, gold (which pays no interest) becomes relatively less attractive — when it falls, gold shines. Right now the high US real rates (10Y nominal yield 4.57%) keep a lid on it, while central-bank buying builds the floor: a tug-of-war that holds the price sideways.

Verdict: BUY on dips, hold core position.

Why: Gold is the insurance against exactly this regime — geopolitical risk, unanchored inflation expectations, central-bank diversification. The only real headwind is the high real rate; if the Fed under Warsh turns dovish after all, or deficit risk rises, the path higher is clear. The consensus targets ($5,400–6,000) sit 20–33% above the current price — and the 19% pullback from the January high is a better entry than the high itself.

Crypto — Bitcoin & Ethereum

BTC ~$76,900 [Crypto] (as of May 24; +3.0% 24h, −1.5% on the week, −39.0% from the all-time high of $126,080 on Oct 6, 2025). After a week of selling — BTC dipped toward $74k at one point — the price turns higher over the weekend and is back above $76k. BTC dominance has fallen from ~60% to 58.5%: capital is flowing out of BTC and back into altcoins — the classic sign of a risk-on bounce. But caution: the toughest headwind, the spot ETF outflows, had not yet turned by Friday — there were six straight outflow days through May 22, totaling around −$1.26B (May 18 alone −$649M, May 22 −$105M). Fear & Greed sits at 25 ("Extreme Fear"), slightly lower than yesterday (28) — sentiment isn't following the price yet.

Spot ETF outflows: A Bitcoin ETF is an exchange-traded pot that holds real Bitcoin so you can buy it like a stock. When money flows out, the pot's manager has to sell real Bitcoin — that's direct selling pressure, not just sentiment. Six negative days in a row means real coins are moving onto the market. If the bounce turns, the ETF-flow reversal is the signal to wait for.

A rise in thin holiday liquidity is easy to manufacture and easy to give back. That dominance is falling is a risk-on sign, but not yet a foundation. As long as the ETF flows don't turn for several days, the reliable upside catalyst is missing.

Timeframe: No new buy until (a) the ETF flows turn positive for several days or (b) a dovish Fed pivot arrives — the earliest concrete event for that is the FOMC on 16/17.06; before that the PCE print on 28.05. Caution over the long weekend (Memorial Day 25.05): thin liquidity amplifies crypto moves in both directions. Hold an existing position through the volatility if the horizon is 12+ months.

Gas limit (Ethereum): The cap on how many transactions fit into a block — like the number of seats in a bus. Glamsterdam is meant to enlarge the bus substantially: more people come along, the individual ticket (the fee) gets cheaper. Whether more people actually want to ride — and when the bigger bus even arrives — is the real bet.
