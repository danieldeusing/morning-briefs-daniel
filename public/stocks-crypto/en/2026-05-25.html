Big Tech & the AI trade

Frozen on the record [Stocks]. Because today is Memorial Day, US markets are closed — the tape stands still at Friday's close (05-22): the Dow closed at an all-time high of 50,579.70, the broad S&P 500 (7,473.47) booked its eighth straight winning week — the longest run since late 2023 — and the Nasdaq (26,343.97) its seventh up-week in eight. So there's no new equity tape today; the next move comes Tuesday (05-26). What matters until then is whether falling oil and a possibly hot PCE print carry the record mood or break it.

The quiet sensation remains Alphabet [Stocks]. Google's parent is still the world's number two: market value $4.60T (as of 05-22) — ahead of Apple ($4.54T) and behind only Nvidia ($5.22T). The rest of the Mag-7: Microsoft $3.11T, Amazon $2.86T, Tesla $1.60T, Meta $1.55T. Together the seven weigh about $23.5T — roughly a third of the entire S&P 500. The market is visibly rotating from the "picks & shovels" (chips) to the "adopters" (cloud, search AI, advertising) — the companies where AI investment already turns into revenue.

"Picks & shovels": In the gold rush, it wasn't the prospectors who got rich but the people selling shovels and pickaxes. Applied to AI: Nvidia sells the "shovels" (chips). But eventually what counts is whether the prospectors — Google, Microsoft & co. — actually strike gold with those shovels, i.e. make revenue. That's exactly where the money is moving now.

Nvidia delivered — and was sold anyway [Stocks]. Wednesday evening (05-20) brought the Q1-FY27 numbers: revenue $81.6B (+85% YoY), Data Center $75.2B (+92%), gross margin 74.9%, EPS $1.87 (non-GAAP). The Q2 guidance of $91B was far above consensus — and that without a meaningful China contribution. Plus a dividend raised 25-fold ($0.01 → $0.25, effective 06-26) and a new $80B buyback program. And yet the stock fell after the print. Analysts, by contrast, raised their price targets.

"Priced in": When everyone expects a good result, they buy the stock beforehand — so the price rises before the news. When the good news then arrives, the "fuel" is spent, and some sell to lock in gains. That's how a stock can fall on top-tier numbers. That's exactly what just happened to Nvidia.

Recommendation — Nvidia (NVDA)

Verdict: BUY on weakness — the fundamental uncertainty is smaller after the numbers, but the price isn't higher.

Why: Before Wednesday the setup was binary — now the central question ("is the AI boom slowing?") is answered by the $91B guidance. That the stock fell anyway is positioning (priced in), not fundamentals. The leverage remains: ~80% market share in AI accelerators, the Blackwell ramp, the $80B buyback supporting the price. For a 6–12-month horizon, a weak patch on confirmed fundamentals is a better entry window than a euphoria peak.

Timeframe: Accumulate on red days, staggered over the next 4–8 weeks. Concrete risk dates: the PCE print on 05-28 and the first FOMC under Warsh on 06-16/17 — both can push tech down again via yields and deliver better prices. Hold to the Q2 confirmation in July.

Commodities — oil & gold

Oil tumbles on the Iran framework deal [Commodity]. In Monday electronic trading (05-25), Brent fell ~5.5% to ~$97.8, WTI ~5.6% to ~$91.2 — after already −6% and −4% the prior week. The driver: over the weekend the US and Iran reached a framework to extend the ceasefire — including a demined, toll-free reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, in exchange for sanctions relief ("relief for performance"). Important: it's a framework on principles, not a signed deal. The strait is effectively still mined and blocked; the reopening depends on demining that hasn't begun, and Iranian officials still report "wide differences." So the price slide is pricing the expectation of a solution, not the solution itself.

Strait of Hormuz: A narrow strait between Iran and Oman through which about a fifth of the world's oil is shipped. Picture the only bridge all the bread trucks use to reach your town — block it and the bread doesn't vanish instantly, but everyone hoards out of fear and the price spikes. A peace hope lets the hoarding — and the premium — melt away again.

Recommendation — oil exposure

Verdict: REDUCE / deep dips only — the risk premium is deflating fast; the buy case from two weeks ago is largely gone.

Why: As long as Hormuz is unstable there's a floor — but the market is now actively pricing the solution, and the price has fallen from over $115 to under $100 in two weeks. Buying here means buying into a shrinking premium. Even houses betting on a longer Hormuz disruption see Brent below the current spot by year-end. Energy only becomes clearly attractive again on a sharp pullback — or a collapse of the framework.

Timeframe: No fresh entry into the ongoing de-escalation. Wait for two dated triggers: the EIA inventory report on 05-28 and a concrete signal from the Iran talks (signing vs. collapse). Accumulate only if the framework breaks and the price escalates toward $115+ (then momentum), or on genuine normalization toward $80–85 (then value).

Forecast: Base (55%): framework broadly holds, Brent oscillates $90–98 with a downward drift until demining takes hold. Bull-for-oil (25%): framework breaks, supply disruption → $115+. Bear (20%): rapid full normalization of the strait → toward $80.

Evidence: Brent ~$97.8 / WTI ~$91.2 (Monday print 05-25, −5.5%/−5.6% on the day), prior week ~−6%/−4%; weekend headlines: framework for a 60-day ceasefire extension incl. Hormuz reopening, unsigned, strait still blocked. te brent · axios

Real yield: The interest rate after subtracting inflation. When it rises, gold (which pays no interest) becomes relatively less attractive — when it falls, gold shines. Right now the high US real yields (10Y nominal yield 4.57%) keep a lid on it, while central-bank buying builds the floor: a tug-of-war that holds the price sideways.

Spot-ETF outflows: A Bitcoin ETF is an exchange-traded pot that holds real Bitcoin so you can buy it like a stock. When money flows out, the pot's manager has to sell real Bitcoin — that's direct selling pressure, not just sentiment. Six negative days in a row means real coins moving onto the market. When the bounce turns, the ETF-flow reversal is the signal you wait for.
