Big Tech & the AI trade

Record run, but split in two [stocks]. The broad market is at highs: the S&P 500 closed Tuesday (26.05) at 7,519.12 (+0.61%), the Nasdaq at 26,656.18 (+1.19%) — both at fresh records, carried by tech. Only the Dow eased slightly (50,461.68, −0.23%). Pre-market on Wednesday, futures point higher still. The driver: the US and Iran are wrangling over a deal to open the Strait of Hormuz, and Secretary of State Rubio called an agreement “a few more days" away — falling oil eases the inflation outlook, and the 10-year yield has dropped to a two-week low (~4.48%). Falling rates are a tailwind for tech.

Micron cracks the trillion — the AI story broadens [stocks]. The day's star wasn't Nvidia, but Micron (MU): +19% to a record ~$886.6, the first time above $1.0tn market value. UBS lifted its price target sharply to $1,625 — the memory type HBM, which AI accelerators need, is according to Micron sold out through 2026. AMD also marked an all-time high. That's the real message: the AI capex boom no longer carries Nvidia alone, but the whole supply chain — memory, logic, foundry.

HBM (High Bandwidth Memory): Special memory that sits right next to the AI chip and feeds it data extremely fast — like a giant conveyor belt running straight to the workbench instead of a warehouse at the other end of the hall. Every AI accelerator needs stacks of it; if the memory is “sold out", that's a direct demand signal for the entire AI build-out.

Nvidia exhales after the beat [stocks]. The stock sits at ~$214.86 (−0.2%), practically unchanged since the $215.33 close on 22.05 — the move after the Q1 FY27 numbers (Wed 20.05: revenue $81.6bn +85% YoY, Q2 guidance $91bn, new $80bn buyback) has faded. At $5.20tn the market value remains the largest in the world. The Mag-7 behind it: Alphabet $4.66tn (clear number two, ahead of Apple $4.53tn), Microsoft $3.09tn, Amazon $2.85tn, Tesla $1.63tn, Meta $1.55tn. Together ~$23.5tn — roughly a third of the entire S&P 500.

Recommendation — Nvidia (NVDA)

Verdict: BUY on weakness — the fundamental question is answered after the numbers, but the stock hasn't gotten more expensive.

Why: The central worry (“is the AI boom slowing?") was refuted by Nvidia's $91bn guidance — and Micron's “sold out through 2026" HBM confirms demand along the entire supply chain. That the stock has only gone sideways since is a breather after the pre-earnings run, not fundamentals. The leverage stays: ~80% market share in AI accelerators, Blackwell ramp, $80bn buyback as a price support. For 6–12 months, a weak patch on confirmed numbers is the better window than a euphoria spike.

Timeframe: Stagger over the next 4–8 weeks, picking up on red days. The concrete risk events that can deliver such days: the PCE print on 28.05 and the first FOMC under Warsh on 16/17.06 — a hot PCE or a hawkish dot plot pushes tech down via yields. Hold until the Q2 confirmation in July.

Forecast: Base (55%): stock consolidates ±5% around the $5.2tn valuation ($215) until the market hunts for the next story. Bull (30%): AI capex breadth (Micron/AMD) + falling yields → new highs, +10–15% over 6 months. Bear (15%): hot PCE + Warsh hike signal pushes the whole Mag-7 down → −10 to −15%.

Evidence: Stock ~$214.86 (27.05, −0.2%), flat since the $215.33 close 22.05; Q1 revenue $81.6bn / Q2 guidance $91bn; cap $5.20tn; Micron +19% above $1tn (UBS target $1,625). cnn NVDA · companiesmarketcap · micron

Commodities — oil & gold

Oil keeps falling — the deal moves closer [commodity]. After the brief Tuesday rebound (fresh US strikes near Bandar Abbas), oil eases again today: Brent ~$97 (−3% on the day), WTI ~$90. On the week, Brent is more than 5% in the red. The driver is diplomacy: the US and Iran are negotiating a framework meant to open the Strait of Hormuz toll-free (Iran clears mines, the US loosens the port blockade and sanctions); Secretary of State Rubio says it could take “a few more days". Nothing is signed, the demining has not begun, and points of contention (Iran's frozen funds, a guarantee of free passage) are open. The price swings between deal hope and the chance that it all falls apart.

Strait of Hormuz: A narrow strait between Iran and Oman through which around a fifth of the world's oil is shipped. Picture the only bridge across which all the bread trucks drive into your town — block it, and everyone hoards out of fear, and the price shoots up. A peace hope lets the premium melt away; a collapsed deal brings it right back.

Recommendation — oil exposure

Verdict: NEUTRAL / don't chase — the price is a pure headline bet as long as the framework is neither signed nor collapsed.

Why: The Hormuz premium (weeks ago >$115) is largely priced out, and the market now trades the probability of a signing. That's the two-sided situation where you don't chase: buy on de-escalation and you get caught by the next strike; bet on escalation and you get caught by the next diplomatic headline. Energy only gets attractive again once the binary question resolves — a signing (then further down toward $80–85) or a collapse (then up above $115).

Timeframe: No new entry until a dated trigger brings clarity: the EIA inventory report on 28.05 and above all a concrete signal from the Iran talks (signing vs. failure). Accumulate only if the framework collapses and the price escalates toward $115+ (momentum), or on genuine normalization toward $80–85 (value).

Forecast: Base (55%): stalemate — Brent swings $90–100 depending on the headline, with a downside bias on deal progress. Bear (25%): signing + demining begins → toward $80–85. Bull-for-oil (20%): framework collapses / supply outage → $115+.

Evidence: Brent ~$97 / WTI ~$90 (27.05), −3% day, >−5% week; framework not signed (Rubio: “a few days"), demining not begun; EIA report holiday-shifted to 28.05. te brent · washington post

Gold eases in the risk-on [commodity]. Spot ~$4,489/oz (27.05, −0.4% on the day), silver deeper in the red ($75.35, −2.1%). With the Iran deal approaching and yields falling, the geopolitics premium melts away — leaving gold around 19.6% below the all-time high (~$5,595, end of January 2026). The structural driver stays intact: central-bank diversification away from the dollar, fiscal-deficit risk, geopolitics. JPMorgan sticks with its year-end target of ~$6,000 (though it cut the 2026 average to ~$5,243 on weaker near-term demand).

Real rate: The interest rate after subtracting inflation. If it rises, gold (which pays no interest) becomes relatively less attractive — if it falls, gold shines. Right now the picture is two-sided: the 10Y nominal yield has fallen to ~4.48% (good for gold), but a hot PCE would reignite inflation worries — and with them gold demand as insurance.

Verdict: BUY (dips), hold the core position.

Why: Gold is the insurance against exactly this regime — sticky inflation (3.3% PCE expectation), central-bank diversification, a new Fed chief seen as hawkish who nonetheless can't deliver a rate cut against the inflation. Today's −0.4% pullback is a risk-on reflex on the approaching Iran deal, not a break of the thesis — and exactly the kind of dip you buy. The only real headwind stays the real rate. Buying ~19.6% below the January high is better than chasing the high.

Timeframe: Accumulate now in the $4,350–4,550 range. The next upside signal would be a hot PCE on 28.05 (stagflation fear) or a hawkish but powerless tone at the FOMC on 16/17.06. Long-term hold (12+ months) as a portfolio anchor.

Spot-ETF outflows: A Bitcoin ETF is an exchange-traded pot that holds real bitcoin so you can buy them like a stock. If money flows out, the pot's manager has to sell real bitcoin — that's direct selling pressure, not just sentiment. Six negative days in a row means: real coins are moving into the market. The flow turn is the signal you wait for.

It's Wednesday, the twenty-seventh of May. Wall Street is celebrating: the broad market closed at fresh records yesterday, and futures are pointing higher again today.

Before I dive in, the most important thing up front: this is not investment advice, only education and my opinion for my own research. Markets are risky, especially crypto — a total loss is possible.

The driver of the records is the approaching Iran deal: Secretary of State Rubio says an agreement is only a few days away. But the framework still isn't signed. Oil therefore gives back the brief Tuesday rebound and keeps falling — Brent stands at around ninety-seven dollars, more than five percent in the red on the week. My take on oil: as long as the framework is neither signed nor collapsed, the price is a pure headline bet — I'd neither buy nor sell here, but wait for the decision: a signing sends the price toward eighty, a collapse above one hundred and fifteen.

But beneath the record surface, the bond market is hitting the brakes — and that's the real story of the day. Tomorrow's inflation print is expected with a core rate of around three point three percent, a three-year high. The market prices a rate pause at ninety-nine percent for the Fed's mid-June meeting — but the new bit is: a rate cut this year is practically priced out, the residual probability now tilts toward a hike rather than a cut. The twist: the hot inflation reading is a rear-view mirror on the April oil surge, which has collapsed since. Stocks look forward, bonds look at the sticky core. Thursday decides.

In stocks, the day's star wasn't Nvidia, but Micron: up nineteen percent, above one trillion dollars in market value for the first time, because the AI memory is sold out through twenty twenty-six. That's the good news — the AI boom no longer carries Nvidia alone, but the whole supply chain. Nvidia itself runs only sideways at around two hundred and fifteen dollars after the strong quarter. My view stays: exactly this weakness is the opportunity, I'd pick up Nvidia in stages on red days. Micron, by contrast, don't chase after plus nineteen percent.

Gold goes the opposite way and eases slightly in the risk-on, around half a percent to about four thousand five hundred dollars — the geopolitics premium melts away with the approaching deal. It stands around twenty percent below the January high, and JPMorgan sticks with its year-end target of around six thousand dollars. My take: buy exactly this pullback and hold gold as core insurance.

In crypto, the most striking move: Bitcoin fully decouples from the equity cheer and slips below seventy-six thousand dollars, Ethereum at a bit over two thousand. Sentiment has fallen from thirty-four to twenty-five — into extreme fear territory. What passes for risk appetite on the stock exchange is a flight from risk in the crypto market. But careful: the outflows from the Bitcoin exchange pots ran for six days through Friday, well over a billion dollars, and couldn't turn over the holiday at all — the first new flow print could not yet be confirmed this morning. My take: hold Bitcoin, only buy more at a turn in the flows; underweight Ethereum versus Bitcoin, because its big Glamsterdam upgrade is still outstanding with no date.

In the crypto sectors, the artificial-intelligence tokens were the weekly winner — Fetch up twenty-six, Render up seventeen percent on the week — but they cool off today. NEAR stands almost fifty percent higher on the week after praise from investor Arthur Hayes, but has already worked off the overbought spike. After a run like that I'd lock in partial profits, not buy more. The fun coins barely move.

The next fixed point is the inflation print on the twenty-eighth of May — it will help decide whether the Fed under Warsh stays on hold or the market builds out its hike bet. Until then.
