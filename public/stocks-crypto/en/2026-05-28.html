Commodities — Oil & Gold

Oil: the deal bonus has evaporated [Commodity]. Wednesday, WTI crashed −5.55% to $88.68, Brent fell intraday more than −4.5% below $95 — the driver was Iran state TV saying the country was "obligated to restore commercial traffic through Hormuz to pre-war levels within a month." That same evening the White House called the draft "complete fabrication," Trump denied it, said he was "not happy." Today the price rebounds: Brent $96.12 (+1.94%). On the month, Brent is still −12.97% in the red — the last few days' crash has priced out virtually the entire Hormuz premium. The facts on the ground: one supertanker left the Persian Basin over the weekend (Iraqi crude for China), most of the ~600 stranded there are still stuck. Today the EIA inventory report (holiday-shifted) — it should show if US imports are bringing relief.

Strait of Hormuz: A narrow waterway between Iran and Oman through which about one-fifth of the world's oil ships. Picture the only bridge all the bread trucks to your city cross — if it closes, everyone hoards out of fear, and prices spike. A peace hope dissolves the premium; a collapsed narrative brings it roaring back.

Real interest rate: The rate after subtracting inflation. When it rises, gold (which pays no interest) becomes relatively less attractive — when it falls, gold shines. Right now the wind is mixed: the 10Y nominal yield is 4.50%, expected inflation 3.3% — so real rate around ~1.2%, historically a clear headwind. A hot PCE would push the inflation piece up, easing the real-rate headwind.
