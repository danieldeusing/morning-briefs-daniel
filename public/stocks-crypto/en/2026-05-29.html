PCE MoM vs YoY: The Fed's inflation gauge comes in two layers. The monthly MoM ("month-over-month") shows the momentum of the last 30 days — that's what bond traders price in the first minutes. The annual YoY drags in twelve months of old data. Today YoY (3.3%) hit a three-year high, but MoM (0.2%) was milder than expected — so: the trend cools now, the mountain just looks tall historically. Markets trade momentum, not magnitude.

Commodities — Oil & Gold

Oil: the MOU sits on Trump's desk [Commodity]. Brent is this morning at $92.34/bbl (−0.38% today, −16.36% for the month, YoY +47.1%). The Wednesday–Thursday roller coaster ("Iran TV claim" → "White House: fabrication" → "60-day MOU negotiated") has put the market back in diplomacy's hands — and the next move hangs on one signature. According to US News and Axios, Washington and Tehran agreed on 5/28 to a Memorandum of Understanding on a 60-day ceasefire extension; the core: Hormuz back open without toll, Iran clears emplaced mines in 30 days, in return US port blockade and some sanctions lifted. But: Trump has not yet signed, Iran state media say the text is "not final", VP Vance: "uncertain whether and when the agreement will be finalized". That means: market is pricing ~70% odds of signature within days — a Trump rejection could shove Brent back up $5–8 in minutes.

Memorandum of Understanding (MOU): Not a binding contract, but a written statement of intent two parties sign before writing the actual deal. In diplomacy like a restaurant reservation: it shows the table is held for you, but dining happens only when the reservation is confirmed. Until Trump signs, Hormuz reopening is a reservation — no paid dinner.
