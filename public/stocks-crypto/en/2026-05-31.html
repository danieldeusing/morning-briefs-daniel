Anthropic's $965B — and what Daniel can buy

The weekend's headline is a private-market record: Anthropic closed a Series H round worth $65B at a post-money valuation of $965B — nearly triple the $380B from February and above the prior AI leader OpenAI ($852B, March).[1] Led by Altimeter, Dragoneer, Greenoaks, and Sequoia. Annualized run-rate revenue has crossed $47B — up from just $10B end-2025. Claude Opus 4.8 dropped simultaneously (half the error rate of 4.7 on code, per Anthropic), and the cyber model "Mythos" is set to roll out wider "in coming weeks."[1][2]

Why does this belong in a stocks brief if Anthropic isn't publicly tradeable? Anthropic is private — you can't buy a share. But imagine a gold rush: you can't invest in "the rush" itself, but you can invest in the companies selling shovels, jeans, and railroad track. That's the public AI suppliers here — Nvidia (chips), Amazon & Microsoft (data centers), Alphabet (own models + TPUs). Every dollar Anthropic raises flows into their balance sheets.

The clearest lever runs through Amazon: Amazon put $5B into this round and committed up to $25B total; in return, Anthropic pledges >$100B on AWS over ten years and will build up to 5 GW of new compute on AWS-Trainium chips with Amazon (deal dated 04/20/2026).[3] The oft-cited "1M TPUs / >1 GW" comes from Anthropic's separate Google-Cloud deal (Oct 2025) — Anthropic runs dual-track, AWS-Trainium and Google-TPUs. This is the mechanism by which the private AI boom seeps into public revenue.

The bubble case: circular financing

The mega-round fuels both camps at once. Bulls see validation: $65B at near-a-trillion proves real AI demand. Bears see the opposite — their word of the hour is "circular financing."

Circular financing: Imagine a baker lends his regular customer money so he buys more bread, then books the bread sale as growth. On paper the business hums — but the same money spins one loop, real outside demand is missing. In the AI market: cloud providers invest in Anthropic, Anthropic spends the money with those same cloud providers on chips/compute. Revenue gets booked, the cash stays in the same ecosystem.[4]

The scale makes it serious: AI capex should hit $800–900B in 2026, >$1T in 2027 — cumulative ~$2T by end-2027 — against still-small AI revenues. Microsoft, for instance, reported its first-ever AI run-rate in FY26-Q3 (quarter ended 03/31/2026): $37B (+123% YoY) — well-watched, but still a fraction of the related capex.[4] That's exactly why NVDA and GOOGL gave ground Friday into the record: the market is learning to split "AI demand real" from "supplier valuations too hot."
