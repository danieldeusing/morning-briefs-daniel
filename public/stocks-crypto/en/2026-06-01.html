The SpaceX IPO — the first tradable Musk AI play

The structurally most important story of the month is an IPO: SpaceX filed its S-1 registration with the SEC (05/20) and wants to list on Nasdaq under the ticker SPCX — roadshow from the week of 06/08, pricing on 06/11, trading starts 06/12.[1] The targeted valuation is $1.75 to $2.0T, the raise up to $75B — the biggest offering of the decade. Critical for the AI trade: xAI (Musk's AI firm with the Grok model) was absorbed into SpaceX in February 2026 in an all-stock deal. SPCX is thus the first way to buy Musk's combined space-plus-AI empire on the public market.[1]

Why is an IPO a market story for Daniel? An "IPO" (Initial Public Offering) is the day a previously private firm first sells shares to everyone — before that, only funds and insiders could participate. Think of an exclusive club suddenly selling day passes: from 06/12 onward, theoretically anyone can buy SpaceX shares. That wasn't possible with Anthropic and OpenAI — so the SpaceX IPO is the first moment the private AI boom becomes directly tradable.

The balance sheet calls for caution: in 2025 SpaceX posted $18.67B revenue but took a $4.94B loss; in the first quarter of 2026 $4.69B revenue faced a ~$4.28B loss, with the freshly integrated SpaceXAI segment alone ~$2.47B in operating loss.[2] A near-$2T valuation for a company burning billions is a bet on Starlink scaling, Starship, and Grok — not on earnings today.

Recommendation — SpaceX IPO (SPCX)

Verdict: AVOID on day one — watch, don't buy the open.

Why: The hype is enormous (1,500-investor retail event, "biggest offering of the decade"), the fundamentals are not: $4.9B annual loss on ~$2T valuation. ELI5: an IPO open is like the first day of an overcrowded concert — the entry price peaks with the buzz, not at fair value. Mega-IPOs often open with a "pop" and then fade over weeks (lock-up dynamics, early-buyer disappointment). For a developer without a trading terminal, day-one buying is the worst risk/reward.

Timeframe: Watch the open on 06/12 only; a first assessment earliest after the first full quarterly report as a public company (Q3 2026) — and especially after the typical 90-day lock-up expires (~mid-September), when insiders can sell and true float price becomes visible.

Forecast: Base (55%): SPCX opens with a pop above IPO price, then fades 10–25% in the following weeks — the typical mega-IPO pattern. Bull/upside (25%): holds the opening pop and more if Starlink numbers + Grok momentum carry the story and the broad AI trade stays hot. Bear (20%): > 30% below IPO price within months if the market rejects a $2T valuation for a loss-making conglomerate or risk-off (oil/Iran, see next columns) kills IPO appetite.

Evidence: S-1 filed 05/20/2026, SPCX/Nasdaq, valuation $1.75–2.0T, raise up to $75B, roadshow from 06/08, pricing 06/11, trading starts 06/12 (investing.com/thehill); 2025 revenue $18.67B / loss $4.94B; Q1 2026 revenue $4.69B / loss ~$4.28B (xAI deal Feb 2026).

Oil: the reversal — the Iran deal wobbles

The most striking live signal today comes from oil: Brent sits at ~$94.26 (+3.44% on the day), WTI at ~$90.90 (+4.06%) — a straight reversal of Friday's minus of about −2%.[4] The driver: the provisional US-Iran deal to reopen the Strait of Hormuz wobbled over the weekend. Both sides are swapping amendment proposals to the draft, Trump approval still pending — the Hormuz risk premium the market priced out on Friday is being priced back in.[4]

Why does a deal draft move the oil price so much? The Strait of Hormuz is the narrow waterway through which roughly a fifth of the world's oil is shipped. Imagine the only bridge into a city being opened and closed at random — every rumor about "bridge open or closed?" sends prices for everything in the city flying. That's exactly how the oil market works: it trades not today's consumption but the probability that the "bridge" Hormuz stays open.

On 06/07 the 41st OPEC+ ministerial also meets — following the May decision to raise June production by 188,000 barrels per day.[5] So two opposing forces: OPEC+ pumps more (price-dampening), Iran uncertainty drives prices (price-supporting). The price is thus pure geopolitics noise, no fundamental trend.

Recommendation — Oil exposure (Brent/WTI)

Verdict: HOLD — today's spike is no entry signal, but a warning about volatility.

Why: The +4% day confirms exactly the weekend thesis: the price is a bet whether a peace treaty holds — and it's shaky now. ELI5: buying into an asset that swings −2% one day then +4% the next without real supply/demand changes is gambling, not investing. Someone who bought "the dip" on Friday would be up today — but by luck, not analysis.

Timeframe: Build no position before the OPEC+ meeting (06/07) and especially before a final Iran deal signature. The next clear catalyst is the meeting on 06/07; only after that is reassessment worth it.

Forecast: Base (50%): Brent $85–98, high volatility, sideways range while deal status stays unclear. Bear for oil (30%): < $80 if the deal does get signed and OPEC+ raises production further — then the risk premium falls for good. Bull for oil/upside (20%): > $110 if the ceasefire breaks and Hormuz effectively closes again — long position only after confirmed break, not on suspicion.

Evidence: Brent $94.26 (+3.44%), WTI $90.90 (+4.06%) — both TradingEconomics, 06/01; driver: shaky US-Iran-Hormuz deal, both sides demanding changes over weekend (Friday still −~2% on deal hope, ~6-week low).
