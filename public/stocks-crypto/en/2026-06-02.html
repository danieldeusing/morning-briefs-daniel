Anthropic files — and overtakes OpenAI

The structurally most important story of the day: Anthropic (the company behind the Claude model) confidentially filed a draft S-1 with the US Securities and Exchange Commission on Monday, 06/01 — the first formal step toward going public.[1] The valuation from the fresh $65B Series H round comes in at $965B — and for the first time above rival OpenAI (~$852B from March). A debut beyond the trillion-dollar mark is now the base case, according to accompanying reports, "if markets cooperate."[2]

What is a "confidential S-1 filing"? An S-1 is the document a company files with the regulator to disclose all its numbers before being allowed to go public. "Confidential" means: the SEC gets to review it first, but the public doesn't see it yet. Think of it like a job application you show the hiring manager first, not the whole company — you control the timing. Anthropic keeps the door open to launch "as soon as this fall," without committing to a specific date.[1]

The growth numbers are the real argument: Anthropic expects $10.9B in revenue for the second quarter, with the annualized run-rate to exceed $50B by end of next month — up from $4B in July 2025, an 80-fold increase. And unlike most AI firms: Anthropic is "on track for its first profitable quarter."[2]

"Run-rate" — why not just annualized revenue? Run-rate takes the most recent month (or quarter) and projects it across a year. Imagine you earn $1,000 this month — your run-rate is $12,000/year, even though you've only been earning for a month. For a company just eightyfold-growing, run-rate describes today's speed more honestly than looking back at full-year results.

Recommendation — Anthropic IPO

Verdict: WATCH / AVOID on day 1 — note it, don't buy the open.

Why: Anthropic is fundamentally the cleanest AI IPO story — 80× revenue growth, profitability in sight, clear enterprise/coding lead over OpenAI. That's exactly what makes the hype the risk: a $965B valuation before listing leaves little room for an opening pop. ELI5: An IPO open is like day one of a sold-out concert — the ticket price peaks with excitement, not fair value. Mega-IPOs often open with a "pop" then give it back over weeks (lockup dynamics, buyer disappointment). For a developer without a trading desk, day-1 buying is the worst risk/reward.

Timeframe: Until trading starts (earliest this fall — no fixed date, filed confidentially), just observe. Only after the public S-1 is released (with real loss/margin numbers) and after the first public quarterly report should you reassess the valuation — and watch the SpaceX IPO on 06/12 as a leading signal for AI IPO appetite.

Forecast: Base (55%): listing this fall above $1T; day-1 pop, then typical −10 to −25% over first weeks. Bull / upside (25%): holds the pop and more, if disclosed profitability + run-rate beyond $50B sustains the valuation and the broad AI trade stays hot. Bear (20%): delay or weak start > 25% below offer price, if risk-off (crypto capitulation, oil/Iran) or broad AI correction freezes the IPO window.

Evidence: Confidential S-1 filing 06/01/2026 (anthropic.com); valuation $965B from $65B Series H, first time above OpenAI (~$852B, March); Q2 revenue expected $10.9B, run-rate > $50B by end of next month (80× from $4B July 2025), "on track for first profitable quarter," debut "as soon as this fall" (fortune.com).

The AI IPO race: Anthropic ahead of OpenAI, SpaceX already underway

Anthropic's move-first is also a race: it filed before OpenAI, which is only now preparing its own confidential filing and targets a September listing above $1T.[3] That puts three mega-IPOs in sequence — SpaceX (SPCX, already in roadshow mode, pricing 06/11, trading start 06/12), Anthropic (fall) and OpenAI (~September).

Why this matters for Daniel None of these three names is tradeable today — the private AI boom has been closed to retail. This IPO wave is the moment it becomes tradeable. But the sequence is also a stress test: SpaceX's launch on 06/12 is the first real signal whether the market will swallow trillion-dollar valuations for AI/space. If it goes well, that validates the whole trade and gives publicly traded winners (NVDA, AMZN, GOOGL) tailwind; if it flops, Anthropic and OpenAI and all AI sentiment drag with it.

For stocks already trading, that means: the IPO pipeline is the most important sentiment driver for AI heavyweights over the summer — more important than any single quarter. If you want to play the trade without IPO lottery, you buy the infrastructure winners (see Big Tech column), not the opening auction.

Oil: back in Iran noise — weakening again today

After Monday's pop, oil is rolling over again: Brent sits at ~$93.19 (−1.88% on the day), WTI at ~$90.5 (−1.79%) (TradingEconomics, 06/02).[7] The driver remains the back-and-forth US-Iran talks over reopening the Strait of Hormuz — TradingEconomics explicitly names "ongoing uncertainty around US-Iran talks and shipping through the Strait of Hormuz" as the reason.[7] On 06/07, the 41st OPEC+ ministerial convenes (following May's decision to raise output by 188,000 barrels/day).

Why does one negotiation move oil prices so much? About one-fifth of the world's oil ships through the Strait of Hormuz. Picture the only bridge into a city that opens and closes — every rumor about "open or shut?" sends prices jumping. The oil market doesn't trade today's consumption; it trades the odds that the "bridge" stays open.

Verdict: HOLD — no position, the swings are the warning.
