"Foundry" deal (Apple-Intel): A foundry is a chip factory that manufactures designs for others — like a printing plant that prints books for many publishers without being the author. Until now, Apple almost exclusively used TSMC in Taiwan. That Intel will now also make Apple chips is a massive signal for crashed Intel: a top customer validates Intel's new fabs — hence the double-digit jump.

Oil: Hormuz breathes — Brent loses ~8 % on the week

Brent $80.58 (+0.92 % on 19.06. in thin holiday trade), WTI $77.33 (+0.95 %).[9] Over the week, though, a sharp −~8 %, over 30 days −23 %.[9][10]

Strait of Hormuz: the narrow waterway at the Persian Gulf through which much of the world's oil ships — a single chokepoint, like the neck of a funnel. If it clogs, the price rises from fear; if it opens, the risk premium falls. That's exactly what's happening now.

The driver: a US-Iran Memorandum (signed 17.06. in Versailles, effective 18.06., a 14-point document extending an April ceasefire by 60 days) led US CENTCOM to lift the naval blockade of the strait.[10][11] On Thursday, ~10 mm bbl of crude again moved through or near the strait — including the first Saudi tankers in over three months of conflict.[10] Headwinds remain: follow-up talks in Switzerland were abruptly cancelled, Iran demands transit insurance.[10]

Recommendation — Oil exposure (Brent/WTI)

Verdict: HOLD / neutral — no directional bet here

Why: Two opposing forces cancel out: Hormuz normalization (~10 mm bbl/day flowing again, ~8 % week drop) pushes lower, while cancelled Switzerland talks and Iran's insurance demand can bring risk premium back any moment.[10] In a two-sided situation like this, a directional position is a coin flip — so wait.

Timeframe: Until the next hard headline from the region (no fixed date) or the next OPEC+ signal; until then range-bound ~76–84 $ Brent.

Forecast: Base (≈55 %): Brent holds 77–84 $, the week's drop consolidates. Bear-for-oil (≈25 %): full Hormuz normalization pushes toward 73–76 $. Bull-for-oil (≈20 %): deal failure / new escalation brings premium back, break >88 $. Adverse: a single tanker or military headline can flip the thesis overnight.

Prices as evidence: Brent $80.58 (+0.92 %), WTI $77.33 (+0.95 %), as of 19.06.; week −~8 %, 30-day −23 %.[9][10]

Macro anchor: Warsh's hawkish debut

The FOMC meeting on 17.06. held the funds rate at 3.50–3.75 %, but the Dot Plot tipped: 9 of 18 members project at least one rate hike by end-2026 (Fed Chair Warsh submitted no dot), and the PCE inflation forecast rose to 3.6 % (from 2.7 %).[14][15] At the same time, the Fed struck its forward guidance and replaced it with "data-dependent basis".[14]

Dot Plot & "Forward Guidance": The Dot Plot is an anonymous dot cloud where each Fed member marks where they see the funds rate heading — like each team member quietly posting a guess on the board. When the dots drift up, that means "higher rates likely" (hawkish). "Forward Guidance" was the promise to signal in advance what's coming; dropping it means "we decide meeting-to-meeting based on data" — so less certainty for markets.

Alongside came a strong data print: US retail sales +0.9 % in May (expected +0.4 %) — this supports the hawk case, because robust demand doesn't force rate cuts.[14] It was Kevin Warsh's first meeting as Fed Chair. That's the lever behind everything: a stronger dollar, weaker gold and BTC, caution on high-beta. Next meetings: 28–29.07., 15–16.09., 27–28.10.[14]
