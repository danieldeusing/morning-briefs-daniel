Big Tech: Micron saves the AI-memory narrative [1][2][3]

The blowout in detail

Micron (MU) reported yesterday after US close a quarter that didn't just meet consensus but beat it decisively. Q3 FY2026 revenue: $41.5B (+346% year-over-year). Earnings per share: $25.11 versus expected $20.20 — a 24% beat. Crucially, Q4 guidance of $50B revenue (midpoint), roughly 14.5% above analyst expectations.[1]

HBM / AI memory: An AI chip like an Nvidia GPU only computes as fast as data reaches it. The fast memory right next to the compute core is called HBM (High-Bandwidth Memory) — think of it as a massive truck docked directly at the factory loading dock instead of small delivery vans. Micron is one of three global makers of this specialty memory — and the AI wave is pulling inventory out of their hands.

Pre-market on 6/25 the stock stands +16.8% at $1,224 (close 6/24: $1,048.51). Over the past year MU has roughly sevenfold, market cap now exceeds $1T.[2] The effect radiates outward: S&P-500 futures +0.5%, Nasdaq-100 futures +1.8% before open — the two-day AI-infrastructure selloff is at least halted.[3]

Mag-7 context pre-open

The rest of the Mag-7 shows mixed pre-market — Micron's boost has (not yet) fully permeated. NVDA $201.66 (+1.38%, close $199.00), GOOGL $341.06 (−1.22%), META $556.39, AMZN $233.85 (−0.22%), AAPL $291.77 (−0.48%), TSLA $377.65 (+0.58%). Nvidia's 52-week range spans $151.49 to $236.54 — the stock sits in the upper-middle, next earnings 8/26.[4]

Note: OpenAI's in-house chip and Qualcomm's modular deal are AI hardware — covered in the software brief, not here.

Gold crashes below $4,000 — strong dollar wins [5][6][7]

Below the level for the first time since November 2025

Gold spot trades 6/25 at $3,989/oz (−0.26%), the lowest since November 2025 — about −5% year-to-date and −20% from January 2026 high. The driver is unambiguous: the dollar. The DXY (Dollar Index) sits at ~101.6, the highest in 13 months, and broke above 100 for the first time since May 2025.[5][6]

Gold vs. real yields: Gold pays no interest. When risk-free US bonds suddenly yield more — the 10-year yield sits at 4.42% — gold becomes relatively unattractive, like a no-interest savings account next to one that pays. Rising rate expectations typically drive gold lower. That's exactly what's happening now.

The market now prices a 68% probability of a Fed rate hike in September — a week ago it was 29%.[7] A hike (instead of the long-expected cuts) would be poison for gold and a break of the rate narrative so far.

Recommendation — Gold

Verdict: HOLD (no long pre-PCE) — Why: Near-term rate headwinds dominate: firm dollar, rising hike expectations, rising real yields. Longer-term, gold remains the structural hedge against stagflation and geopolitics, but below $4,000 the catalyst for a long setup is absent. Weakness toward $3,900 is an accumulation zone, not panic — scaled, not all at once. Timeframe: Wait for May PCE print today 8:30 EDT — it decides near-term real-yield direction; then the Fed September meeting. Forecast: Base (55%) volatile range $3,900–$4,100; Bear (30%) < $3,850 if PCE runs hot and drives September-hike odds above 80%; Bull (15%) > $4,150 if PCE comes in cooler than consensus or a geopolitical shock returns. Evidence: Spot $3,989.06 (−0.26%, 6/25), −20% from Jan high; DXY ~101.6 (13-month high); 10-Y yield 4.42% (+0.02pp, 6/25).

AI funding: Anthropic overtakes OpenAI [8][9][10][11]

$65B at $965B — and an S-1 in the drawer

This is the story of this category: not which model codes better (that's ai-dev), but who gets the capital and the valuation. Anthropic closed its Series H on 5/28 for $65B at a post-money valuation of $965B — co-led by Altimeter, Dragoneer, Greenoaks, Sequoia, Capital Group, Coatue, and D1; $15B alone came from hyperscalers, including $5B from Amazon.[8]

In doing so, Anthropic now leads its rival OpenAI ($852B post-money from the March 2026 round of $122B) for the first time in private valuation.[9][10] The difference lies in revenue: Anthropic's ARR crossed $47B in May, driven by a 130% revenue surge toward its first operating profit.

ARR & post-money: ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue) is annualized running revenue — like taking your current monthly pay and multiplying by twelve to see what you earn in a year. "Post-money" is the firm's valuation after fresh capital: if someone puts in $65 for 6.7%, the whole company is mathematically worth $965.

The real headline: Anthropic filed a confidential S-1 (IPO application) with the SEC on 6/1 and targets a Nasdaq listing in October 2026. Investment bankers call a debut above $1T the "base case".[11] OpenAI in turn filed a confidential S-1 on 5/22 (Goldman Sachs + Morgan Stanley), targeting a debut as early as September 2026.[10]

For Daniel as an outsider there's nothing directly tradeable here — both are private. But a dual AI mega-IPO in fall would be one of 2026's biggest market events and the litmus test of whether the public market buys AI at private valuations.

Recommendation — Anthropic / OpenAI (IPO watch)

Verdict: WATCH (still private — not tradeable, note it) — Why: Both are not publicly tradeable; a direct position is impossible today. The relevant trade is the setup: at an October debut above $1T, the question is not "buy?" but "at what price after first hype." AI IPOs in recent years often run hot then correct. Want to buy? Plan the setup now, not impulse-buy on IPO day. Timeframe: Key event is Anthropic Nasdaq IPO in October 2026; before that, S-1 publication (roadshow) reveals real financials for the first time. Forecast: Base (60%) debut near $1T with high first-week volatility, then consolidation; Bull (25%) sustained debut well above $1T if the $47B ARR growth impresses the market; Bear (15%) IPO delay or discount to private valuation if AI sentiment breaks by fall (compare: capex doubts last week). Evidence: Anthropic $965B post-money (Series H, 5/28), ARR > $47B (May), S-1 filed 6/1; OpenAI $852B (March round, $122B), S-1 filed 5/22.

Crypto core: ETF outflows put the lid on [15][16][17]

BTC and ETH falter

BTC stands at $61,641 (−1.6% 24h, −4.2% 7d), market cap $1.235T — that's −51.1% from all-time high ($126,080 on 10/6/2025). ETH at $1,652 (−1.0% 24h, −5.4% 7d).[15]

The driver is not crypto-specific collapse but macro: US spot-Bitcoin ETFs lost $3.4B net in one June week — BlackRock's IBIT alone shed $980M, worst week since launch.[16] With a 10-year yield at 4.42%, risk-free bonds become relatively more attractive than idle bitcoin — portfolio reallocation to bonds, no crypto panic.[17]

Spot-ETF flows: A spot ETF is a publicly traded fund that holds actual bitcoin — buy a share, the fund buys bitcoin. When money leaves, the fund must sell bitcoin. These flow figures are thus a direct pressure/pull gauge on spot price. $3.4B out in one week = sustained selling pressure.

One bright spot on the product side: BlackRock launched on 6/16 the iShares Premium Income Bitcoin ETF (BITA) — the first major US issuer with monthly cash distributions from bitcoin exposure, targeting 15–25% p.a. yield.

Recommendation — BTC / ETH

Verdict: HOLD BTC / HOLD ETH — Why: No fundamental break, but capital rotation to bonds (higher yields) plus sustained ETF outflows keep a lid on it.
