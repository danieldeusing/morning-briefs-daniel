1 · Big Tech: The AI dividing line sharpens

As of Friday close 26.06.2026, 16:00 ET — US markets closed over weekend.

OpenAI IPO & why Nvidia cares: An IPO is going public — the first public stock sale. Imagine the biggest customer of a shovel factory announces it's postponing a planned massive order by two years: the shovel makers (Nvidia, AMD, Micron) sink because the market prices in slower AI investment tempo. A New York Times report that OpenAI is pushing its IPO to 2027 triggered exactly that on Friday.

AI hardware cracks, AI software gains

The OpenAI report hit a jittery market: Nasdaq closed Friday at 25,297.62 (−0.24 % day-over-day), but gave back −4.6 % for the week — the fifth straight loss session.[1] Nvidia fell −1.64 % to $192.53, AMD roughly −3.5 %, Micron roughly −4.5 % (direction values from sector tape, not confirmed closes).[2]

Meanwhile Microsoft shot up +5.71 % to $372.97 — the counter-move. The driver: Michael Burry ("The Big Short") disclosed Dec-2028 LEAP calls on MSFT (strikes in the low $700 range), and institutional money rotated from AI hardware into AI software.[3]

LEAP call: A call option with very long expiration (here through December 2028). Like a reserved ticket: Burry locks in the right to buy MSFT at a fixed price later — a very long-term bet that the stock rises sharply. This is sentiment, not a new fundamental datapoint.
