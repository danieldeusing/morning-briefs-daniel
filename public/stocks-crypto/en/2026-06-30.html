1 · Microsoft: The worst month since the dot-com crash

Stock prices: close 2026-06-29, 4:00 PM ET (stockanalysis.com) — the 06-30 close was not yet available at publication. Monthly performance as of 06-29.

Capex vs. free cash flow — and why it matters here: "Capex" is capital expenditure on long-lived assets like data centers and AI chips. "Free cash flow" is the money left over after all expenses. Think of a baker who buys six new ovens at once: the hope is to sell far more bread later — but until then, the register is empty. That's exactly what's happening at Microsoft: AI spending is exploding while free cash flow shrinks, and the market is suddenly asking whether the ovens will ever pay for themselves.

−21.6% in June — $570 billion in market value wiped out

Microsoft lost roughly 21.6% in June, putting it on track for its worst month since December 2000 — the peak of the dot-com crash.[1] Market capitalization, which stood at around $4 trillion twelve months ago, now sits at $2.65 trillion.[2] On 06-29 the stock closed at $368.57 (−1.18%).[3]

The trigger is a capex concern: capital expenditure rose +63% year-over-year while free cash flow fell −10% — roughly $190 billion in capex is committed for 2026.[4] The AI segment is growing rapidly (annualized revenue run-rate of $37 billion, +123% YoY), but it is precisely the gap between today's billion-dollar outlays and tomorrow's returns that is driving the doubt.[4] The applied-AI side of this story — what Azure/Copilot actually delivers — belongs in the ai-usecases brief; here we cover only the equity angle.

Why joining the Dow matters: The Dow is one of the oldest US stock indexes — a list of 30 major companies that many funds use as a benchmark. 5 · Bitcoin & Ethereum: −19% in June, ETF outflows the driver

BTC: last verified price 2026-06-29 (Yahoo/Fortune); ETH live 06-30 (CoinMarketCap). Crypto trades 24/7.

Why ETF outflows push Bitcoin's price down: A spot Bitcoin ETF is an exchange-traded fund that holds real Bitcoin — you buy it like a stock and indirectly own BTC. "Outflows" mean more investors are leaving than entering; the fund must sell Bitcoin and thereby depresses the price. Think of a revolving door that mostly spins outward — roughly $4.06 billion in net outflows as the June monthly total (worst month since inception) represent sustained institutional selling pressure, not daily noise.

BTC ~$59,686 — roughly $4.06 billion in ETF outflows in June

Ethereum is holding up somewhat better: $1,587 (+1.22% over 24h, but −21.8% on the month), market cap $191.5 billion, rank 2 — roughly 35.6% below the August 2025 high of $4,953.[14] Cited drivers of the sell-off: ETF outflows, the prospect of higher interest rates, a stronger dollar, and rotation into AI stocks.[11]

6 · Gold: Below $4,000 for the first time in 8 months

As of: 2026-06-30 (TradingEconomics spot); macro context per June dot plot.

Gold slipped on 06-30 to $3,986/oz (−0.75%) — below $4,000 for the first time in nearly eight months; a second source confirms at $3,985.88 (difference 0.006%, consistent).[15] On the month the precious metal stands at −11.1%, on the quarter roughly −15% — yet it is still +19.4% year-over-year, far below the January record of $5,608.[15]

Real rates & why they weigh on gold: The real rate is the interest rate after subtracting inflation. When expectations shift toward the Fed hiking rather than cutting, real rates climb — and gold, which pays no interest, costs more in "forgone yield." Like a savings account that suddenly pays decent interest again: the gold sitting in the vault earning nothing looks relatively less attractive. That is exactly what is happening now.

The driver is a hawkish pivot at the Fed: in the June dot plot, roughly half of the 18 committee members favor at least one rate hike in 2026 — the first potentially in September.[16] Goldman Sachs cut its year-end gold target from $5,400 to $4,900, as no Fed cuts are expected anymore.[17] The DXY dollar index was last around 101 (last confirmed reading 06-26, 14-month high of 101.8 on 06-24) — a precise 06-30 value could not be verified.[18]
