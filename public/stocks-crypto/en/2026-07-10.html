Nvidia below the index multiple — and Tesla bounces back

The 09.07. stock closing prices tell a calmer story than this morning's crypto and oil boards. Nvidia closed at $202.78 (−0.66%/−$1.34), trading pre-market on 10.07. at 7:35 ET at $201.70 (−0.53%); market cap stands at ~$4.91 trillion.[1] But the real news isn't the day's loss — it's the valuation behind it: Nvidia has lost roughly $1 trillion in market value since its record high on 14.05.2026 and now trades at about 18x expected earnings — below the forward multiple of the broad S&P 500.[2] The most expensive AI name of the past two years has, by the numbers, become cheaper than the index average.

"18x forward earnings" (forward P/E): The price-to-earnings ratio measures how many years of profit you're paying for when you buy a stock. "Forward" means it's calculated using the expected earnings of the next twelve months, not the past ones. Think of buying a house at "18 years of rent for the apartment" — the lower the number, the faster the earnings pay back the price. Nvidia sitting at ~18 while the broad market sits higher is unusual: normally you pay a premium for the growth market leader, not a discount. Two readings: either the AI capex story has gotten cheap (opportunity) — or the market quietly doubts the expected earnings, making the low multiple a warning sign. A dated catalyst that resolves this only arrives with the Q2 numbers in late August.

The rest of the Mag 7 from the 09.07. close: AAPL $314.82 (prior close $313.39, P/E 37.58, ~$4.6 trillion)[3], META $615.60, MSFT $379.30, GOOGL $354.90, AMZN $241.95.[3] In the semiconductor corner, AMD jumped +5.8% on bullish AI-demand analyst commentary, AVGO (Broadcom) $403.24; for TSM there's no clean daily spot price — only the trailing-year run (+88.48%), which I'm not selling as a current price.[4] And Tesla turned +3.17% to $406.55, back above the $400 mark — after three red sessions, but per the source on "bullish momentum" sentiment, with no dated trigger.[5][6]

That satisfies the price side of my buy trigger, which I set on the eighth and ninth of July. But the flow side doesn't hold up: the numbers from the Bitcoin funds are contested and choppy.
